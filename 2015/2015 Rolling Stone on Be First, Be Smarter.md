# 📰 Rolling Stone | May 2015 Special Issue

💀 *Be First Be Smarter or Cheat*

**The Record That Buried Commuted—and Everyone Who Listened**
Feature by Lena Jerichow

>“We didn’t survive it. We just lived longer than she did.”  
> —Jim Brown
  

**🎸 THE LINEUP**

- Jeremy Evans — Lead guitar, maybe a borderline drunk. Showed up every night. Left most of himself on stage.
- [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff Evans]] — Bass, lyrics, core grief engine. Genius? Probably. Manic? Definitely. Broke two pens mid-interview.
- Jim Brown — Drums, scheduling czar, project manager and mortal risk-taker. Ride or die. Mostly die.
- Bill Davenport — Synths, viola, production wizard, eternal work martyr. Had a bed under the mixing desk.
- Gilley — Rhythm guitar, vocals, group id. Medicated with pussy and pain. Somehow sang like he wasn’t falling apart.
    

🎶 THE TRACKLIST (DECONSTRUCTED)

1. **"March 4"**  
- Piano + Viola instrumental
- John Legend on piano
- Bill on viola
- One minute thirty-two seconds of quiet devastation. Opens the grave.

1. **"Terrified"**
- Lead vocals: Gilley
- Response: John Legend
- Children’s choir (Detroit Gospel Youth) backing vocals
- This track doesn’t cry—it hyperventilates.

1. **"Snowglobe"**
- Piano ballad
- John Legend: lead vocals
- Bill: shrapnel synth layer
- At 3:17, it becomes an epic rock eulogy. It never comes back down.

1. "Gabriel’s Modern Trumpet"

- Acoustic Texas blues
    
- Jeremy Evans: unplugged steel-string
    
- Gilley: vocals cracked with blood
    
- It’s church on a broken porch in July.
    

5. "Thirtysix Degrees Fahrenheit"

- Serene synths
    
- Gilley’s vocals like sleep apnea and prayer
    
- Two minutes and fifty-one seconds of numbness. No beat. No escape.
    

6. "Sin and Death Sitting Till Then at the Gates of Hell"

- 7.5-minute 1980s-synth banger
    
- Avril Lavigne: guest vocals
    
- It’s epic until you read the lyrics. Then it’s absolutely not okay.
    

7. **"Time of Death"**

- Should be upbeat. Should be playable on radio.
- Avril Lavigne: lead and chorus
- But the lyrics are an autopsy. Literally.
- Gilley sings like he’s half-gone.
    

8. **"What They Say About Payback (Batt A, 1/134 FA)"**

- OHIO ARMY NATIONAL GUARD: sampled live fire
- Gilley: unhinged frontman
- No chorus. Just fury and feedback. Most streamed, least replayed.
    

9. **"See the Lights" (Simple Minds cover)**

- Stretched to 8 minutes
- Poisonous
- Sung like a weaponized funeral. Ends in feedback and whisper.
