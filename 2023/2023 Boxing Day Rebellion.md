#### INT. THE REHEARSAL LOFT – DAYTON, OHIO – DEC 24, 2023 – 11:52 A.M.

JIM is pacing. Caffeine shakes, two phones, “O Canada” blaring through a Bluetooth speaker in the corner, just to antagonize Jeff.  BILL is half-dressed, soccer jersey and boxer briefs, hair still wet, scrolling his mentions, reading every DM like it’s a love letter or a threat.  JEREMY’s tuning a guitar, then another, then a third, precision masking panic, flicking through old Jackets highlights on mute.  JEFF is slumped over a keyboard, eyes bloodshot, one hand on a sticky note: “Hi, Reddit!” His face says he wants to set the internet on fire, or maybe just unplug it.  AVRIL is nowhere to be seen. She’s in the city, they say, getting her head right. No one knows what that means. Maybe it means the revolution is real.

---

**KENDALL (V.O.)**

They want a riot, do they?   They want mythology?  I’m watching grown men check their hairlines and their history in the same cracked bathroom mirror.  They’re arguing over who gets to post the next photo, who has to call the mothers, who’s going to break it to Kathy’s old fan club. I’m watching them dare each other to blink.  I am not blinking.

**JIM**
(puts his phone down, grins like a child who’s eaten the last Christmas cookie)
“Rolling Stone says we’re a hostage situation with a rhythm section.  Let’s make it a ransom note.”

**BILL** (half-joking, half-hoping, scrolling)
“Does anyone else feel weirdly turned on by the poutine memes? No? Just me?”

**JEREMY** (not looking up)
“I still can’t believe she said yes.”

**JEFF**
“Give it an hour. They’ll want her blood or her autograph. Maybe both.”

#### INT. FOUR SEASONS HOTEL – COLUMBUS, OHIO – 3:37 P.M. – DECEMBER 24, 2023

The city outside is blue-shadowed, blanketed in exhaust and old snow.
AVRIL sprawls on the bed, knees up, guitar on her lap, hotel notepad torn to hell with half-rhymes and confessions.

Her phone buzzes. She doesn’t answer. Not yet.

She’s breathing fast.  She’s humming something new—*not* for TikTok, not for radio, but for the four guys she has to face in the flesh tomorrow, knowing if she blows it, she’ll be immortalized as the girl who killed a legend.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
It’s never just a gig if you know you can fail.  It’s never just a gig if you know you can bleed.

She sits up, runs a hand through hair that’s too clean, too fresh for what she’s about to do.
She’s terrified. She is. She could bail, go back to safe rooms and safe singles and the certainty of manufactured applause.

But this?
This is the mountain. This is the temple, the sacrificial altar, the high wire with no net.

She starts a voice memo. Her throat catches on the first line.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
I want to write something that makes them *hurt*. I want to make them call me a mistake and a miracle in the same breath.  God, I want to survive this.

Her hands are shaking. She *likes* the feeling.

She texts Jeff: *You said you wanted blood. I’m bringing a fucking transfusion. Don’t coddle me.*

She hits play. She starts to sing.

And outside, in the city, the riot is getting louder.

**That’s why she’s here.**  She’s not here to be adored—she’s here to see if she’s good enough to be *devoured* and still crawl out with something worth singing about.  She is writing her own lyrics because if she fails, she wants the failure to be hers.  She is terrified—and she is alive in a way she hasn’t been since she was seventeen and nobody thought she’d make it.

#### INT. FOUR SEASONS HOTEL – COLUMBUS, OHIO – 7:42 P.M. – CHRISTMAS EVE

AVRIL sits cross-legged on the bed, flannel pajama pants, hair in a rough topknot, blue light from her phone making her look ghostly.  Room service tray, cold fries. Champagne, half-gone.  She is not homesick—she’s never home anyway. But she is *wired*, every sound outside the door making her sit up, stare.

She opens Instagram. DMs: 

> [!Instagram]
> *Avril please come to the window*
> *Saw you at the elevator!*
> *Are you really here?*
> 

There’s a knock.  She freezes—waits. Nothing but her own pulse in her throat. The city out there is humming, full of families, full of people with somewhere to be.

She opens X. Trending:
#AvrilInColumbus
#CanadianKathy
#FeralsFindHer
#NotEvenSantaSafe

Her phone buzzes—a text from a number she doesn’t know:

> [!Message]
> *We love you, Av. You looked sad in the lobby. Need anything? XO FERAL CREW*


**AVRIL (V.O.)**
*It’s not sex. It’s not even threat. It’s invasion. I could be anyone. I could be no one. But to them, I’m a relic they can track. A miracle to trespass.*

She gets another ping. Another. Another.  They found the floor. They’ll know the room by midnight.  

She texts Jim—*You were right. This is fucked. Merry Christmas, by the way.*

He calls. She doesn’t answer.

Cut to:
**INT. HOTEL HALLWAY – NIGHT**

Somewhere, two Ferals are arguing with a security guard.  They have theories, screenshots, a group chat that works faster than any tabloid ever could.

Cut to:
**INT. AVRIL’S SUITE – NIGHT**

She locks the deadbolt, then the latch, then shoves a chair under the knob.  She’s laughing, high-pitched, on the edge of crying, because this isn’t the internet.  This is Christmas, and it’s a *hunt*.

She stands at the window.  Outside, some kid is waving up—holding a sign:
**“WE BELIEVE IN YOU.”**

She closes the curtains, slides to the floor, and finally, finally lets herself sob.  Not for fear. For exhaustion. For the price of being seen.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
*Fame is a ghost town at midnight.  You know everyone’s here, but no one leaves you alone.*

But she’s still here. Still alive. Still the voice the riot wants.  And tomorrow, she gets to walk into rehearsal and face the only people in Ohio who understand—because they used to be *hunted*, too.

Do you want to see the texts with Jim after that?
Or the moment she walks out the door, sunglasses on, eyes full of animal?

#### INT. THE HILTON COLUMBUS – 9:09 A.M. – CHRISTMAS DAY

The hall is empty, but not silent—vent hum, distant ice machine, footsteps echoing from nowhere.  AVRIL in a hoodie and sweats, sunglasses even in the dim light, clutching her phone and room key like they’re rosary beads.

She cracks the door.  Down the hall, by the elevator: SECURITY GUARD, big, bored, clearly from the “don’t even try it” school of protection.  He nods.  She nods back—she hates the ritual, but today she’s grateful.

She starts down the hallway, pretending not to hear her own heartbeat.  Her nerves—jumpy, caffeinated by anxiety, not caffeine.  She’s been here before, in Mazatlán, in Tulum, in those nameless hotels where girls go missing and pop stars turn up as rumors.
She’s not scared.  She’s *tired*.

She just wants coffee.  Something hot and anonymous to cradle.  The *Hilton’s* lobby café is supposed to be open—but it’s Christmas, so who the fuck knows.

She steps in the elevator.  Security rides with her—because that’s the deal now.  A fan is waiting in the lobby, holding a phone up—already streaming.  She pulls her hood lower, walks fast.

---

**INT. HILTON LOBBY CAFÉ – CHRISTMAS MORNING**

The place is half-empty. The staff are wearing reindeer antlers and thousand-yard stares.
She orders a black coffee, eyes darting. A mother and daughter at the next table whisper and pretend not to film.

The barista hands her the cup.
“*Merry Christmas, Avril.*”
It’s not a question.

She smiles, sharp and practiced, and turns to leave—but the kid blocks her path, eyes wide.

**GIRL**
“Are you really singing with Commuted now?”

She laughs, sips her coffee, shrugs.

**AVRIL**
“Guess we’ll find out.”

She escapes back toward the elevator, security flanking her, hot coffee burning her palm.
She wonders what the next tabloid headline will be—*AVRIL LAVIGNE GETS COFFEE, SPARKS PANIC*.

She gets to her room, deadbolts the door, sags against it, sips the coffee, and lets herself laugh.  Out there, she’s spectacle.  In here, she’s just a girl with caffeine and stage fright and no way back but through.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
*They think the hardest part is the music.  It’s not.  It’s surviving the pilgrimage from the 19th floor to the lobby and back, without losing anything sacred.*

#### INT. HILTON COLUMBUS – LOBBY – CHRISTMAS DAY, 9:24 A.M.

The lobby is cathedral-vast, all marble and echo, sunlight slanting in through cold glass, holiday wreaths dying on brass banisters.  AVRIL steps out of the elevator, coffee burning her hand, security trailing discreet but close.

She pauses in the middle of the floor, hearing her own footsteps rattle like dice on a Vegas table.  A nervous energy crackles through her—part terror, part bravado, part “fuck it, let’s see.”

She clears her throat.  She looks at the security guard, who shrugs, half-amused.

Then—she *shouts*, big and ringing, from deep in her chest:

**AVRIL**
O-H!

There’s a heartbeat of silence.  Then—a *roar*, like a choir raised on Coors Light and bad decisions:

**60+ RANDOM OHIOANS**
I-O!

The sound bounces off every surface, shakes the antlers on the baristas, and leaves her blinking, stunned, smiling in spite of herself.

Someone cheers. A stranger in a Santa hat throws up a fist.  A group of construction guys in boots and Browns gear hoot back, grinning like they’ve just been knighted by the Queen of Canada.

A grandma flashes the “I-O” sign with her arthritic hands. A little kid squeals and does a little dance.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
*I could be dead in a thousand cities, But here, I’m alive.  Here, I’m one of the idiots.
Here, the ritual is the law.*

She grins—genuine, open, fearless for half a heartbeat.

She lifts her coffee in a toast.

**AVRIL** (bellowing)
**Merry Christmas, you beautiful weirdos!**

A round of laughter, applause, someone shouts “Welcome to Commuted!” and “O-H!” again, and this time the whole lobby sings it back:
**I-O!**

For one dizzy second, fame feels like *belonging*, not exile. She takes the elevator up, still grinning, and texts Jim:

> [!note]
> *Ohio is fucking nuts. I might actually survive this.*

#### EXT. I-71 SOUTHBOUND – MIDDLE OF NOWHERE, OHIO – CHRISTMAS AFTERNOON – 1:44 P.M.

AVRIL white-knuckles the steering wheel, radio static fizzing as she’s somewhere between “anonymous farm exit” and “a place that might have coffee.”
The winter sun glances off endless fields and mud, wind turbines spinning like they’re laughing at her.

She passes sign after sign—COLUMBUS, XENIA, LEBANON, and the existential nightmare of “Rest Area—33 miles.”  Her GPS chirps: “Continue straight for 76 miles.”
She sighs. *Of course.*

She’s starving, imagining some road-trip miracle—maybe a famous truck stop with clean bathrooms, maybe that rumored Texas place with the cartoon beaver.  Bill had mentioned something about brisket once, in the group chat—she’d thought he was joking.

She rolls past a massive construction site north of Dayton. A bright red sign: “<font color="#ff0000">COMING SOON – BUC-EE’S – HUBER HEIGHTS, OHIO</font>.”

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
What the hell is a Buc-ee’s?

She considers pulling over, but all she sees is mud, hardhats, chain-link fence, and a beaver mascot grinning like it knows every secret she’ll never be let in on.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
Bill, you lied to me.  There is no brisket. There is only pain.

She texts the band group:

> [!note]
> *FYI, Ohio is just corn, construction, and broken dreams.
> If you want me to rehearse, someone better bring food.*


No answer.  She cranks the heat, curses the GPS, and resigns herself to surviving on a bag of gas station pretzels and rage.

Her stomach growls.  She passes a sign for “WILMINGTON – NO SERVICES” and mutters:

**AVRIL**
“Ohio, why do you hate joy?”

#### INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – LATE AFTERNOON

She bursts through the door, cold air and Midwestern defeat trailing behind her.

BILL glances up, doesn’t miss a beat:  “Did you find the Buc-ee’s?”  She glares, drops her bag, hair wild.  

**AVRIL**
It’s not open. It’s not *real.* Nothing is real. I’m going to eat someone alive.

**JIM**
“Welcome to Cincinnati, Avril. Now you’re one of us.”

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
Next time, I’m flying. Or making Bill deliver me brisket in person.

**AVRIL** 
Jeremy? Please don’t say he’s on a beach.

**BILL** (smiling)
Wheels down at Lunken in 35. If the wind doesn’t kill him, he’ll be here for the second verse.

**AVRIL**
How’s your Christmas in the States, Bill?

#### INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OTR, CINCINNATI – 2:33 P.M. – CHRISTMAS DAY

#### INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CONFERENCE ROOM – 2:41 P.M. – CHRISTMAS DAY

AVRIL stands in the doorway, eyes wide, taking in the scene: A battered table scarred with coffee rings and Sharpie graffiti.  A stack of **binders**—two feet tall, easily.  Tabs sticking out like battle wounds.

Song titles sharpied in all-caps: *“LEFT HAND*,” “*BLOODLETTING*,” “*AMELIA’S GHOST*,” “*COME INSIDE ME (ALT VERSE)*.”  Loose papers spilling from the edges.

She cracks open the top binder—Handwritten lyrics, annotated with fuck-you arrows and cryptic, underlined warnings: 

<u>*BRIDGE IS A LIE—ASK JEFF.*</u>
<u>*IF YOU CAN SING THIS SOBER, YOU’RE LYING TO YOURSELF.*</u>

She skims a verse.  The words are sacred and filthy—full of knives, orgasms, and stained glass.  Some pages stick together, gritty with old sugar, as if someone spilled their drink in a moment of inspiration.

She flips to the back:  Chords that snarl.  Progressions in odd keys—F# minor, B7 flat 9, whole sections scrawled sideways in Kathy’s spiky hand.

**AVRIL** (to herself, half laughing, half panicked)
Who the fuck *wrote* this? Is this music or witchcraft?

Bill breezes in, a tin of cookies in hand—red and green sprinkles, lopsided, too sweet.
He sets them down with a flourish.

**BILL**
You thought Jeff and Jeremy were complicated?  Try these on for size.

(chuckles)
We used to dare each other—first one to cry in rehearsal had to buy drinks.  Nobody ever won.

BILL (cont’d, mouth full)
Eat.  Sugar helps. And don’t worry about the blood stains. It’s probably just pen.

AVRIL (eyes gleaming, a little wild)
If this is the entrance exam, I’m ready. But, uh, if I start screaming, just throw cookies until I calm down.

Bill laughs—big, genuine, echoing off the cinderblock walls.

She sits.  Flips another page.  Feels the *weight*—of the band, of history, of what’s expected.  She breaks a cookie in half, crumbles it over a verse, lets the sugar fall like snow.

**AVRIL (V.O.)**
You asked for blood.  You gave me sugar.

Let’s see if I can write something that doesn’t get me killed.

Nothing.

**BILL**
It’s in the other binder. Towards the back.

**AVRIL**

Basically. But once you’re in, you’re in for good. You leave your fingerprints, you never really leave.

**AVRIL** (soft, almost reverent)
Found you.

**BILL**

#### INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OTR, CINCINNATI – 3:11 P.M. – CHRISTMAS DAY

#### INT. BLACK RIBBON STUDIOS – OTR, CINCINNATI – 3:27 P.M. – CHRISTMAS DAY

Now you’re thinking like a rock star.

#### INT. BLACK RIBBON STUDIOS – LIVE ROOM – MID-MORNING – EARLY JANUARY

For a week straight, AVRIL pinches herself every morning—checks the bruise, can’t believe she’s really here.
