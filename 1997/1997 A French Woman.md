## **INT. BILL'S RECORDING STUDIO - OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI - DECEMBER 22, 1997 - LATE AFTERNOON**

Snow accumulates along the windowpanes, opaque and clinging, like a poorly Zambonied hockey rink. The radiator clicks with uneven rhythm, a mechanical stand-in for Gilley's absent pacing. The air is thick with the acrid tang of solder, grounded by dust and the persistent ghost of old takeout containers. Bill Davenport, bare-chested beneath a thermal vest, is immersed in a jungle of cables and half-finished patch bays. He’s sweating profusely, a paradox in winter, like an Armco blast furnace ignited inside a snow globe. The creak of the door interrupts him just before it opens.

**JEREMY (ENTERING)**  
Hey.

**BILL (WITHOUT LOOKING UP)**  
This gonna hurt?

**JEREMY**  
Could. Might. Dunno yet.

He paces. Doesn't sit. His boots thud against the recovered wood floor with each pass, an erratic tempo that feels more like a countdown than movement. There's a restlessness to him that makes Bill glance up from his patch bay jungle—a low-level hum of kinetic disquiet that radiates off Jeremy like heat lightning before a summer storm. His arms twitch at his sides, unsure whether to fold or flail. Jeremy's eyes are wild, flitting like ice cubes near brewing coffee, pupils dilated as if he'd just stared down something ancient and Republican.

**BILL**  
Jesus. What is it?

**JEREMY** 
Jeff flew to California. Stratocaster plant. Anaheim. Corona, but hey…you know.

**BILL (GRUNTS)**  
So? Man's got money, let him burn jet fuel.

**JEREMY**  
It was for Kathy. Birthday present.

**BILL (SITS BACK)**  
Oh, my God. No.

**JEREMY**  
Yeah.

**BILL**  
No.

**JEREMY**  
He had a custom Fender Antigua Strat built. Solidbody. Special Edition.

**BILL**  
Okay? I mean—

**JEREMY**  
He asked them to paint it the color of her cunt.

**BILL**  
Jesus fucking Christ.

**JEREMY**  
His words, not mine.

**BILL**  
That fucker.

**JEREMY**  
And when I asked him why—

**(He stops. Shakes his head. Laughs, but it's the kind you do at funerals.)**

**JEREMY (CONT'D)**  
He said, and I quote: "Like the Titanic, everyone's gone down on Kathy."

**BILL**  (utterly still)  
Holy shit.

A heavy silence descends, charged and unsettling. The radiator ticks erratically, mimicking the staccato pulse of a Geiger counter. Jeremy exhales sharply and lowers himself into the studio's worn-out couch, its cushions collapsing under the dilemma of brotherly love.

**JEREMY**  
I mean. What the fuck.

**BILL**  
Pink as her cunt.

**JEREMY**  
It's not even a *good* pink. It's like Big League Chew and regret.

**BILL**  
He *named* it Titanic?

**JEREMY**  
Titania. Which is worse. Sounds like a fairy with a yeast infection.

**BILL**
That poor woman taking the order.

**JEREMY**  
She laughed.

**BILL** 
Of course she did. That laugh is a sign that she's used to dealing with blithering idiots.

**JEREMY** 
I think she might like her gig. Plus? He showed me a photo. It's a good looking guitar.

**BILL**  
Then we're all going to hell.

They sit with it. With the knowledge. With the guitar.

**BILL (AFTER A WHILE)**  
It's gonna end bloody.

**JEREMY**  
Yup.

## **INT. BILL'S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – DECEMBER 22, 1997 – EARLY EVENING**

The space is a confession booth for gearheads and lunatics. Cable nests tangle like bad dreams under the desk. A Roland sampler bleeds red light in the corner. Bill is on the floor, ass-up under a rackmount, cursing like he’s negotiating with God. Jeremy lounges across a salvaged loveseat that smells like every tour they’ve survived—cigarettes, wet denim, something escaped from the slaughterhouse.

**BILL**

**JEREMY**

**BILL**  
It’s recovered. Second-hand. Liquidation sales, repo lots. Junk from Detroit, Memphis. Dead studios. Dead dreams. Stuff with ghosts in the EQ. You wouldn’t believe the shit that shows up in OTR—these places look like they lost a bet with the 1980s.

**JEREMY**  
You and your bargain bin apocalypses.

**BILL**  
Give me a shot. By the time I’m respectable—

**JEREMY**  
You mean by the time your balls drop.

**BILL**

I’ll wire every wall to scream, run signal chains so filthy they’ll have to invent new sins to classify them. I’ll make the walls cry in stereo, bleed harmonics you can taste in your tonsils. You’ll need a priest to turn it off. And maybe a lawyer.

**JEREMY**  
So… three years.

**(He shifts. That smile goes sour. Something’s changed.)** You know we’re not gonna write anything good for a while, right? Might be dry ‘til Kath’s birthday. Nothing new’s coming out of her. Or Jeff. Or me.

**BILL**  
I got a solution.

**JEREMY**  
Oh god. That’s never the sentence I want from you.

**BILL**  
Too late. I’m already proud of it.

**JEREMY** Fine. Just don't fuck with me. I swear, Bill—if this is another one of your goddamn "sound sculptures" made from broken VCRs and a Speak & Spell, I’m gonna set fire to your compressor rack. I’m serious. No gear exorcisms. No haunted reverb chambers. Just one straight idea for once, please.

## **INT. BILL'S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – DECEMBER 22, 1997 – LATE NIGHT**

Bill stands with a screwdriver in one hand and a dangerous glint in his eye. Jeremy hasn’t moved from the loveseat, but his body’s tilted—like the idea of standing might attack him from behind.

**BILL**  
Alright. I got a fucking idea. Way to get an album out a little sooner than everyone thinks.

**JEREMY**  
If it leans more rock this time, I’m listening. Because for a rock band? We, uh… don’t exactly *cut*. We swirl, we suggest, we brood in reverb like a sophomore poet. Which is fine if you're trying to soundtrack an unmade Gus Van Sant film. But we’ve got amps, we’ve got rage, we’ve got six strings and a blood debt to volume—so how about we *use* it?

**BILL**  
Fuck you, I refuse to apologize or regurgitate on your needs. You want big chords? Join Nickelback. But this—this might actually work out for you.

**(He tosses the screwdriver onto the workbench. Clatter like punctuation.)**

**BILL (CONT'D)**  
I’m talking B-sides. Stuff we never released. There’s gold in that shit if you scrape it right. And those concert masters we made? From the "Implying Nothing" tour?

**JEREMY** The sound guy with the hair and the quiet voice?  What was the guy's name?  Do you even remember?

**BILL**  
That motherfucker was a freak. Captured *everything.* His mix on the Accra show? Practically orgasmic. Raw but precise. Every pickup hum, every mic breath—it all made it in. You could hear the bass strings flex before the note even landed, like they were breathing. The crowd bleed wasn’t a flaw; it was a feature, mixed in just enough to remind you this was live, real, bodies on bodies in time. I played it back once in the car, windows down, full volume, and some guy on Vine Street shouted, "Who’d you hire to fake that?" That’s how clean it was. That’s how *right* it sounded.

**(He steps forward now, lit up. Electric.)**

**BILL (CONT'D)** So. You interested? Or you gonna go home and sulk with a cup of Robitussin and a copy of [_Disintegration_](https://youtu.be/EVOSPb9B1SU?si=V_1mxuzikjCL1iCW)?

**(Beat. Grin.)**

**BILL (CONT'D)**  
Because I’d love to work. Or beat off. Either way, I need a decision. I’ve got cables waiting to be soldered and a loop that’s been haunting me since Tuesday. But I can just as easily lock the door, drop trow, and have a perfectly transcendent night of self-worship to that bootleg of us at the Ogden Theatre. Your call, Evans. Make it count.
