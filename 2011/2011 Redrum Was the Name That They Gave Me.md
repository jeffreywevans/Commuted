**June 14, 2011**  
**Location: Rehearsal space, Vandalia, Ohio**  
**Band present: Bill, Jim, Gilley, Jeremy**  
**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]: Missing**

**INT – DINGY BACKROOM STUDIO – MIDNIGHT**

The room smells like sweat, old cables, and crushed hope. Jeremy’s pacing with a cigarette that’s already burned halfway down. Gilley is in the corner, sitting on a flight case, knees jittering. Jim is at the whiteboard, not writing anything. Bill’s still wearing headphones, but there’s no music.

**JEREMY**:  
He’s not answering. Not texts. Not calls. Not landline. His fucking neighbor said the mail’s stacking up.

**GILLEY** (quiet):  
He took his passport.

**JIM** (flat):  
And the pills?

**JEREMY**:  
Gone.

Beat. No one breathes.

**BILL** (careful):  
We should call someone.

**JIM**:  
We are someone.

**JEREMY**:  
I’m his brother, not his fucking keeper.

**BILL**:  
No, you’re both. That’s the job. You know that’s the job.

**GILLEY** (rubbing hands):  
So what do we do? Wait? Keep hoping?

**JIM** (softly):  
Redrum.

Silence.

**JIM** (more firm):  
He’s not spiraling. He’s not partying. He’s done. If she broke him that bad, and he’s gone this quiet?  This isn’t sulking.  This is final.

**JEREMY** (teeth clenched):  
You think I don’t know what redrum looks like? I found him in the goddamn tub in 2004. I know. I know.

His voice cracks. He punches a chair hard enough to knock it over.

**JEREMY** (snapping):  
She told him she loved him. She made him believe. And then she left him for a guy who sells fucking used cars. He was happy for a goddamn minute. We all saw it.

**BILL**:  
And that scared all of us.

Everyone looks at him.

**BILL** (quietly):  
It did. Admit it. We didn’t know how to handle a Jeff who wasn’t bleeding. And now he is again. And this time… it might be the last time.

Beat. Jeremy lights another cigarette with shaking fingers.

**GILLEY**:  
We need to find him. We need to find him tonight.

**JIM**:  
Then we split up. Everyone takes a zone.  Phone. Text. Cops if we have to. Hospitals.  We don't stop.

**JEREMY**:  
What if we’re too late?

**BILL**:  
Then we finish the record.  We finish the record with whatever’s left.

Everyone stares at him.

**BILL** (final):  
But not before we find him.

They all grab their coats. No one speaks as they leave. It’s 12:34 A.M.  Jeff is somewhere out there.  And so is the thing that broke him.

**June 15, 2011 – 3:22 A.M. EST / 9:22 A.M. CET**  **Location: Oslo, Norway / Vandalia, Ohio**

---

**INT – TIM WRIGHT’S BASEMENT WORKSTATION – OSLO**

Tim Wright, forty-something, wiry, half-clothed in an “Encrypt or Die” t-shirt, hasn’t blinked in ninety minutes. Five monitors. Six open terminals. One room that smells like black coffee, ozone, and moral compromise.

His chat window with Jeremy is blowing up.

**JEREMY** (3:17 A.M. EDT):  
bro come on i wouldn’t ask if i didn’t think he was gonna kill himself  
no one’s heard from him in 36 hours  
he’s not in any hospital  
we need a miracle or a felony. your pick.

Tim mutters something in Norwegian, cracks his neck, and reroutes through a side node in Latvia. It takes some creative ping-routing through Jeff’s seldom-used banking app—one that leaked GPS data under its last update.

**TIM** (to himself):  
You dumb bastard… you never updated.

Three hours, one burner VPN, and an identity theft clause later:

**GPS LOCK:** JEFF EVANS – Springfield, Ohio – 39.927° N, 83.804° W  
**Location:** Mulligan’s Mini Golf & Arcade  
**Time:** 3:21 A.M. EDT  
**Status:** Phone idle. Battery at 14%.

**INT – TIM’S CHAT WINDOW**

**TIM (3:22 A.M. EST):**  
He’s alive. He’s at a goddamn PUTT-PUTT.  Mulligan’s. Springfield. Phone’s low. Move fast.  And Jeremy—  Tell Jeff he owes me 300 Bitcoin for this shit.

**JEREMY (3:23 A.M. EST):**  
bro if you saved his life  
i’ll make you a fucking song

**TIM**:  
You already did. Track 3. The one he never sang live.  Go get him.

---
**CUT TO – MULLIGAN’S MINI GOLF PARKING LOT – 4:01 A.M.**

Jeremy’s Jeep screeches in. The “OPEN” sign still flickers.  Inside, Jeff is slumped over a table near the claw machine.  There’s an untouched hot dog.  A scorecard.  And the kind of silence you only get from a man who already gave up, then got bored waiting to die.

**JEREMY** (quietly):  
You motherfucker.

Jeff doesn’t look up.

**JEREMY**:  
You made me call Tim.

**JEFF** (hoarse):  
I just wanted to remember what happiness used to smell like.  This place… used to smell like her shampoo.  She took me here once.

**JEREMY** (sits):  
Sherry?

Jeff nods, barely.

**JEREMY**:  
You still got your passport?

**JEFF**:  
Yeah.

**JEREMY**:  
Good. 'Cause when Bill finds out you made us lose sleep over mini-golf, he’s putting you on a plane to anywhere else.

FADE OUT as the claw machine clinks to life.  Neither of them win. But they don’t leave empty-handed.

**June 15, 2011 – 5:03 A.M.**  **Location: Bill’s Garage – Cincinnati, Ohio**  

Jeff has just returned from his self-imposed vanishing act. Everyone is stunned. Everyone is angry. But no one’s angrier than Kathy.

**INT – BILL’S GARAGE – EARLY MORNING, PALE BLUE LIGHT THROUGH THE WINDOWS**

Jeff sits on the floor, knees drawn up. He looks like a man who’s just come out the other side of a storm with the wrong limbs missing.

Kathy paces, cigarettes in one hand, rage in the other. Her eyeliner’s smudged like war paint. No one dares speak.

**KATHY**:  
You vanish for 36 hours.  You turn your phone off.  You disappear like you’re trying to make us guess which tree to hang the memorial under—  and all you wanted to do was play NBA Jam?

**JEFF** (hoarse):  
I just… needed to go back. To before.

**KATHY** (cutting):  
Before what, Jeff?  Before you gave your ass to a woman who fucked someone else for a two-year punchline?

Gilley winces. Jim looks away. Bill closes his eyes.

**JEFF** (quiet):  
Don’t do that.

**KATHY** (cold fire):  
Don’t what?  Don’t say what everyone here has wanted to scream since you turned into a ghost with a rhythm section?

**KATHY** (stepping closer):  
You gave yourself to her.  And when she dumped you, you stopped giving yourself to us.  
You stopped writing. You stopped talking.  You gave Sherry Greene your heart and now you wanna give her your obituary?

**JEFF** (choked):  
She made me okay.

**KATHY** (voice cracking):  
That was supposed to be me.

Silence drops like a thunderclap.

**JEFF** (small):  
You never wanted all of me.  You wanted the broken parts. The parts you could play like strings.

**KATHY** (raw):  
Yeah? And guess what?  At least I never left you bleeding in a goddamn parking lot.

Gilley clears his throat.

**GILLEY**:  
I’ll still buy you a roll of quarters, man.  If it keeps you from turning us into a eulogy.


Bill says nothing. He just starts setting up mics. Because he knows:  They’re not okay.  But the tape still rolls.

---
**INT – KATHY’S BEDROOM – LATE MORNING**

Kathy’s curled on her mattress like a wrecked violin. No sound. Just pressure. Her notebook is open, page half-torn from scribbles she can’t finish. The room smells like Sherry. Not sex. Not love.  Strategy.

Her hair’s matted. Her knuckles are white from how hard she’s gripping her pen.

And her thoughts?  Her thoughts are a war crime in progress.

**KATHY** (V.O.):  
She didn’t even moan right.  Faked it with rhythm like she fakes everything else. I’ve heard better orgasms from Gilley in a vocal warmup.

She lights a cigarette with a trembling hand, the match snapping halfway through. She doesn’t care.

**KATHY** (V.O.):  
I thought I’d figure it out.  Get inside her head.  See what she gave him that I didn’t. And what I learned?  Nothing!  Except that she’s a black hole in red lace.  And he gave her everything because he wanted to be emptied.

She flips the notebook, starts scrawling:

*“She’s got a laugh like a bullet,*  
*A mouth like a trap,*  
*And hips that write checks she doesn’t plan to cash.”*

She scribbles it out.

*“I’d take a stiletto to a voodoo doll*  
*And poke her where she made me feel smallest*.”

That stays.

**KATHY** (V.O.):  
I didn’t fuck her to win.  I fucked her to see if I could tear her down from the inside.
But she’s not made of anything soft.  Just smoke, mirrors, and regret. And now?

Now I’ve got her taste on my skin and Jeff still staring at shadows.

The coffee is cold. The lyric book is a grave.  And Kathy lights another cigarette, whispering:

**KATHY**:  
Next time, I bring nap
