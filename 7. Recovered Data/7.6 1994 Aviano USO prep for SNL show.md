"*Your job's a joke, you're broke, your love life's DOA—*"

(They turned to Sarah in unison, mugging shamelessly — Jim doing some weird, stiff-armed Carlton dance, Gilley miming sad, dramatic tears.)

Sarah stared at them like they had both just started bleeding from the ears.

**SARAH** (deadpan)
"You’re sober."

**SARAH** (flat)
"You’re *both* sober."

**GILLEY** (grinning wider)
"That’s the tragedy!"

(He threw his arms up like a game show host presenting a broken prize.)

**JIM** (nodding, serious)
"Sober.  And *still* willing to embarrass ourselves for your entertainment."

Sarah just shook her head slowly, grabbing a nearby flight manual and lifting it threateningly like she was about to throw it at the both of them.

**SARAH**
"Keep it up and I'll tell Courteney Cox we’re bringing you to rehearsal in dog collars."

(That got a new roar of laughter — even a half-grin from Jeff where he sat pretending he wasn’t listening.)

Gilley clutched his heart like she’d just shot him.

**GILLEY** (fake wounded)
"I would *wear it with pride.*"

The C-17 rumbled on, carrying a bunch of idiots halfway across the ocean, a little broken, a little burned out— but somehow still laughing anyway.

---

Sarah leaned her head back against the cold metal wall of the C-17, staring up at the riveted ceiling like it was personally trying to confuse her.

The rumble of the engines, the endless sameness of the dark outside the tiny windows — it was messing with her head.

---

**SARAH**
(muttering)
"Alright, somebody explain this to me."

(She sat up a little, looking around like she was filing a complaint.)

**SARAH**
"How come—"

(she pointed vaguely behind them)

**SARAH**
"We take off from Aviano, and it’s dark."

(she pointed forward)

**SARAH**
"We fly halfway across the damn planet,
back home to Wright-Patt—
and it’s still *dark*?"

---

(She looked around, accusingly, like someone was personally responsible.)

**SARAH**
"This doesn’t match.
This does *not compute.*"

---

Gilley, half-asleep on his duffel bag, cracked one eye open and grinned.

**GILLEY**
(lazy)
"Because we’re chasing the night, darlin’."

---

Jim chimed in from across the bay, voice dry.

**JIM**
"And also because nobody ever properly explained time zones to you in grade school."

---

Sarah flipped them both off without even looking.

**SARAH**
(dead serious)
"No. Nope.
Don’t care.
Not buying it."

---

Jeff, without lifting his head from where he was dozing against a pack, said without inflection:

**JEFF**
"You’re flying west.
Night moves west."

(beat)

**JEFF**
"You’re stuck with it."

---

Sarah groaned, throwing her hands up.

**SARAH**
"So basically—
we’re flying through *space bullshit.*"

---

Gilley grinned wider.

**GILLEY**
"Exactly."

---

Nobody corrected her.

Because honestly, she wasn’t wrong.

---

The C-17 kissed the runway at Wright-Patterson with barely a bump, a smooth touchdown that should have felt triumphant—
but instead felt like nothing more than a tired, heavy exhale.

The inside lights buzzed on harsh and fluorescent.
The hydraulic whine of the rear ramp lowering filled the bay.

Welcome home.
Or something like it.

---

Everyone moved slow, mechanical —
dragging duffels, guitar cases, carry-ons.
Nobody talking much.
The adrenaline was long gone,
burned off somewhere over the Atlantic.

It was 2:00 AM local.
Cold.
Still dark.

The world felt wrong, out of phase, like they'd come back to a place that didn't quite fit anymore.

---

At the bottom of the ramp, they began to scatter.

Jim’s wife was waiting with a station wagon, engine running, breath fogging out of the vents.

Bill hitched a ride with one of the handlers back toward Kettering.

Jeff and Jeremy tossed their gear into a battered rental pickup — barely speaking, but not fighting either.

Kathy had someone from management waiting with a blacked-out Suburban, barely visible behind the fence line.

---

Gilley, meanwhile, had no such plans.

No home-cooked welcome.
No soft landing.
Just a reservation at a shitty hotel off the base perimeter road.

---

He slung his duffel over one shoulder, turned to Sarah, and raised an eyebrow —
*the* eyebrow.

The one that said *you could come too.*

---

Sarah caught it instantly.

She stopped at the bottom of the ramp, boots planted firmly on the tarmac, duffel dragging in the dirt.

She didn’t smile.
Didn’t roll her eyes.
Didn’t even pretend not to notice.

She just stared at him, weighing it.

---

There it was.
The offer.

Warm bed.
Hot body.
Forget tomorrow for a little while.

---

The night air was sharp against her skin.
The weight of exhaustion made everything slow, made every decision seem bigger and dumber.

---

Sarah slung her bag higher onto her shoulder.

**SARAH**
(muttering)
"Go to bed, Gilley."

(She said it without anger.
Without judgment.
Almost fond.)

---

Gilley just shrugged, easy, as if to say *worth a shot,* and turned toward the parking lot.

---

The band scattered into the Ohio darkness,
jet-lagged, raw, and a little older than when they’d left.

Nothing dramatic.
No movie endings.

Just the quiet splintering of people who had carried something heavy together
and now had to carry it alone for a while.

---

Sarah slid her keycard into the worn lock, the light flashing red once,
then green on the second try.

The door groaned open like it didn’t want to let her in—
and honestly, she couldn’t blame it.

---

The room smelled like old HVAC and carpet cleaner.
The lamp by the bed had a lean to it.
The bedspread was floral, and not in a charming way—
more like something designed by a committee of exhausted raccoons.

She dropped her duffel by the door and flopped onto the mattress,
fully clothed, boots on, face-first into a pillow that might’ve doubled as drywall insulation.

---

**SARAH**
(muffled, to no one)
"Fucking *Fairborn.*
*Red Roof Inn.*"

---

She rolled onto her back, staring at the popcorn ceiling like it owed her money.

This was the kind of place where the vending machine still had Tab.
Where the front desk guy looked like he moonlighted as a cautionary tale.

She let out a long sigh that deflated her into the mattress like a dying pool toy.

---

**SARAH**
"Should’ve just fucked Gilley."

(beat)

**SARAH**
"Cotton sheets.
800 thread count.
Soft lighting.
Mini bar.
Hell, maybe even room service."

(She turned her head, staring at the ancient microwave balanced on top of the minifridge like a tower of sadness.)

---

**SARAH**
"Morals."

(She said the word like it had dirt under its nails.)

**SARAH**
"They’re such *pests.*"

---

She kicked off one boot, then the other.
Didn’t bother with the rest.

Just laid there.

So goddamn tired.
So goddamn sober.
So goddamn *honest.*

---

And the worst part?

She’d do it the same way again tomorrow.

---

Sarah stared at the ceiling, the Red Roof Inn light fixture above her humming like a mosquito that never lands.

She hadn’t even changed clothes. Her jeans were digging into her hip, her shirt smelled like C-17 fuel and faint sweat, and she could still feel the grit of Italy behind her eyes.

But the thought wasn’t letting go.

Not jet lag.

Not the band.

*Her.*

Kathy.

---

**SARAH**
(quietly, to the ceiling)
"She’s actually gonna do it."

---

The Playboy pictorial.
It wasn’t a bluff.
Wasn’t a stunt.
Wasn’t some idle threat wrapped in rock-star bravado.

Sarah had seen it in her eyes during that mess hall showdown,
seen it in the curl of her lip when she said *Herb Ritts* like it was a goddamn prophecy.

---

This wasn’t like the 120 Minutes stunt—
where the lighting, the edits, the fog of implication did all the dirty work.
That was rebellion by suggestion.
This?

This would be *clear.*
*Naked.*
*Permanent.*

---

Sarah closed her eyes, exhaling through her teeth.

There’s not gonna be any hiding this one.
Not from the execs.
Not from the media.
Not from the moms in Kansas clutching their daughters’ concert tees.

---

**SARAH**
"What the hell do we *do*?"

(She didn't say it like someone panicking.)

(She said it like someone problem-solving at 2:30 a.m. in a motel bed that might give you tetanus.)

---

Because she couldn’t stop it.
And maybe she didn’t even want to.

But if it went bad?

If it blew up the wrong way?

If Kathy got painted with the same lazy, smirking, sexist brush they used on every woman with ambition and a naked chest?

Then this band—this *movement*—
was going to have a goddamn PR war on its hands.

---

And Sarah?

She was gonna have to figure out how to spin it
before it even hit the presses.

---

She rolled over and stared at the cheap curtains, already reaching for her notepad.

Damage control starts now.

---


Sarah’s pen hovered over the notebook, ink pooling at the tip, untouched.
Her legs dangled off the edge of the bed, one boot still on, the other forgotten somewhere in the corner.

She hadn’t written a single damn word.

Just circled the same thought over and over again.

---

**SARAH**
(soft, bitter)
"But is it really damage control?"

---

She stared at the ceiling again.
That warped motel tile. That humming light. That silence you only get at 3 a.m. in towns nobody dreams about.

---

**SARAH**
"Cindy Crawford does it."

(She said it like it was a fact carved into the side of Mount Sinai.)

**SARAH**
"Gets naked in *Elle,* turns around and sells *Pepsi* to a hundred million people."

(She half-laughed, no humor in it.)

**SARAH**
"Cindy’s known for two things.
Her breast—
and her *other* breast."

---

She dropped the notebook on the nightstand with a dull thud.

---

**SARAH**
"But Kathy?"

(beat)

**SARAH**
"Kathy's got talent.
She's not just selling skin.
She's *creating.*
She's provoking.
She's fucking *uncomfortable.*"

---

She rubbed her eyes hard enough to sting.

---

**SARAH**
"This world doesn't know what to do with a woman like her."

---

She looked out the window, at nothing.

---

**SARAH**
"Too loud for their idea of beauty.
Too smart for their idea of sexy.
Too real for their idea of safe."

---

She leaned back again, staring at the ceiling.

---

**SARAH**
"This doesn’t make sense.
This whole industry doesn’t make sense.
It’s a cruel world.
And she’s walking into it naked with both middle fingers up."

---

And Sarah had to figure out whether she was supposed to shield her…

Or hand her the megaphone.

---


Sarah sat cross-legged now on the stiff motel bed, her boot finally kicked off, her hotel notepad stolen from the nightstand and half-filled with furious scribbles. The Red Roof Inn pen was already starting to sputter, but it’d do.

This wasn’t going to be a press release.

It was going to be a *shield.*

---

At the top of the page, in big block letters:

**NOT DAMAGE CONTROL.**
**ARTISTIC CHOICE.**

---

She underlined it twice. Hard. Enough to dent the paper.

---

**SARAH**
(reading aloud to herself as she wrote)
"This isn’t a scandal.
It’s a *statement.*"

**SARAH**
"Kathy isn’t selling her body.
She’s claiming it."

**SARAH**
"She’s not asking for attention.
She’s showing what *control* looks like."

---

New page.

More ink.

She outlined the rollout:
Exclusive quote.
Intentional photo leak — nothing too revealing, just enough to start the buzz.
Let the fans carry the fire for a week.

Then the interview.

And not with Spin. Not with Rolling Stone.
Something sharper.
Maybe *Wired* — frame it in the future of self-image and media.
Maybe *The New Yorker*, if they had the guts.

---

She tapped the pen against her lip.

---

Let Kathy speak. Unfiltered. No publicist in her ear. No makeup team turning her into a product.

Let her *own it.*

Because Cindy Crawford posed.

But Kathy?
Kathy was making a *declaration.*

---

Sarah paused, breathing through her nose.

The world wouldn’t understand right away.
Most wouldn’t even try.

But this wasn’t for them.

This was for the girl in a bedroom with a guitar,
for the boy scribbling lyrics he’s afraid to sing,
for every misfit who needed to see that power could look like this.

Like her.

---

Sarah set the pen down.

Looked at the page.

And finally, *smiled.*

---

Sarah flipped to a clean page and started writing in a scrawl barely legible to anyone but her.

---

**TO-DO LIST.**

– Flight back to New York
– Call *The New Yorker* — *first,* goddammit. They take *forever* to fact-check
– Reach out to *Wired* — spin it as media futurism, artistic autonomy
– Prep *Time*, *Newsweek* — balance the old media gatekeepers
– Foreign press list:
 – *LA Times*
 – *NHK*
 – maybe the fucking *Australians*
 – find a German outlet that’s not going to turn it into porn

---

She stared at the page for a long second.

Then she muttered:

**SARAH**
(flat)
"All the hell with it."

---

She dropped the notebook onto the nightstand with a limp smack, leaned back, and kicked off her remaining boot.

It arced through the air like a tired little spaceship.

She watched it sail across the room,
watched it land perfectly — heel to heel — on top of the first one.

---

**SARAH**
(dry)
"…Nailed it."

---

She rolled onto her side, pulled the scratchy motel comforter halfway over her legs,
and stared out the dark window at nothing.

No more lists tonight.

No more spins, no more pitches, no more thinking.

---

It was time for sleep.

And the war could wait till morning.

---


Twenty minutes later, she was still wide awake.

Staring at the popcorn ceiling, mind racing like it had overdosed on espresso and bitterness.

Her leg twitched under the covers.
Her thoughts wouldn’t shut up.

Her bra strap was digging into her ribcage.

---

**SARAH**
(growling)
"Oh for *fuck’s* sake—"

---

She reached behind herself, yanked the clasp loose with one practiced snap, and flung the bra across the room like it had personally offended her.

It slapped against the air conditioner unit and dropped to the floor, defeated.

---

She sat up, hair a mess, eyes burning.

The motel light buzzed above like a broken promise.

---

**SARAH**
"Jesus, I'm *so* tired."

---

She looked at the notepad again, still open on the nightstand, her handwriting looping into madness near the end.

She squinted at the words.

---

**SARAH**
(half-laughing, half-ashamed)
"Called the *Los Angeles Times* a *foreign press outlet.*"

(beat)

**SARAH**
"Well, maybe to New Yorkers."

---

She flipped a page, saw a note about "Sydney Herald" or maybe it was the Daily Herald or maybe it was just wishful thinking written in caffeine and sarcasm.

She groaned, flopping back against the pillow, sheets twisted like a straitjacket.

---

**SARAH**
"Why can’t sleep just… *take me*?"

(She rubbed her eyes.)

(Then muttered, too honest to ignore—)

**SARAH**
"If I’d gone to bed with Gilley, he’d have fucked my brains out and I’d be unconscious by now."

Silence.

Then, dry as bone:

**SARAH**
"Lesson learned."
