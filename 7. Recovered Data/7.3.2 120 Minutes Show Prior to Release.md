Kathy’s Reluctance on "Typing Lab"

- Genius: This gives Kathy a mini-arc inside the set itself.
    
She’s raw, magnificent, but fragile.  
She doesn't want to end on "Typing Lab" —  
because that song cuts too close.  
Because that song is too personal.  
Because that song hints at the future.

(Which makes it even more powerful when she does it anyway.)

---

Why "Typing Lab" is Emotional Dynamite for Kathy Espich:

- On the surface:
    
- It sounds clever.
    
- It sounds nostalgic.
    
- "Typing Lab" = "Coming of age" = "First time."
    
- Fans think it's cute, even poignant.
    
- Beneath the surface:
    
- It’s Jeff Evans’ literal true-life loss of virginity.
    
- It’s NOT WITH HER.
    
- Kathy had wanted to be that girl.
    
- Kathy tried — back in the late 80s — to make it happen.
    
- Jeff didn't see it.
    
- Kathy was passed over, unintentionally, but heartbreakingly.
    
- Performing it now:
    
- Kathy is literally being forced to sing the story of Jeff loving someone else.
    
- On national television.
    
- Live.
    
- Smiling.
    
- Even worse:
    
- Everyone will think it’s her story because she’s the one singing it.
    
- It erases her real pain.
    
- It turns her anger and heartbreak into entertainment.
    
---

In Kathy's internal monologue, it would sound like:

"I offered you everything, and you didn’t even know it.  
And now I have to sing about you giving yourself to somebody else,  
in front of the entire goddamn planet."
