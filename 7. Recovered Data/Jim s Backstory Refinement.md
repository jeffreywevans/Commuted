> JIM (V.O.)
> The kit wasn’t much. Not by any serious standard. Not by any music store display. But it was mine. Built it piece by piece from pawn shops, flea markets, garage sales. My dad helped once—hauled the floor tom home in the back of his rusted pickup, acting like it was a barrel of gold. That meant something.
> The bass drum? It was a 22" Tama from 1978. Had to bolt the front hoop back together with scrap metal and duct tape. The wrap was peeling. Looked like someone tried to polish it with sandpaper. But it thumped. God, did it thump. It didn’t sound clean—it sounded like somebody getting punched in the gut. I liked that.
> Snare was Frankenstein’d together from a Pearl shell, a Ludwig throw-off, and tension rods that never quite seated right. The bottom head was warped, so I used a piece of masking tape to hold the snare wires tight. It rattled like hell. Sounded angry. Again—perfect.
> Cymbals? That was a joke. I had one Zildjian crash with a hairline crack I refused to acknowledge. A ride cymbal that used to be a crash—until I dropped it on concrete at Gilley’s. Hi-hats? Mismatched pair. I think the bottom was a Sabian. Top might’ve been from Sears. But if you stomped 'em just right, they sizzled like bacon grease.
> No double bass. Not in 1990. Couldn’t afford it. Didn’t want it yet. This was punk rock and prayer, not prog. Hardware was a nightmare. One of the cymbal stands was missing its wingnut, so I used a lock washer I found in Jeff’s dad’s toolbox. That damn washer outlasted the cymbal it held.
> I tuned by ear. Never owned a drum key that wasn’t half stripped. Tension rods I turned with a flathead screwdriver or—once—a quarter taped to the end of a pencil.
> But here’s the thing. When I sat behind it? When Jeff dropped a bass groove and Kathy hit that distortion pedal and Gilley howled like he was channeling every horny ghost in Clark County? That kit sounded like thunder. It sounded like ambition. It sounded like a fuck-you love letter to every kid who thought music had to be clean to be good.

> The Rack Tom – “Little Bastard”

> The Hardware – “The Holding Pattern”

> Sticks – “Mismatched, Like My Soul”

> Influences

That flam on “In the Air Tonight.” The way he turned pain into precision and then made it explode? Nobody else mattered. Not in 1990. That was the goal: build a kit that could chase that sound. That weight. That echo of judgment from a thundercloud.
