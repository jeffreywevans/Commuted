# **3-bullet pitch

---

- **Roll call, not requiem:** At 12:06 AM the channel **#the-left-hand** lights up: “**Ride or Die check-in**.” Old handles reappear from 1992–2012, posting kids-asleep selfies, keys on lanyards, and weather apps, moms and aunties making war plans, turning their keys in the bunker, and launching their nukes.  *Death wears bunny slippers.*
- **Logistics is love:** Carpools, sitters, bail funds, banner crews, safe-walk teams. A pinned doc reads: *Respect the dead. Respect the living. No slurs. No ‘Canadian Kathy.’*
- **Logistics is hate:** There can only the One.  Some of the fandom summons The Highlander, and Avril ain't it.  Respecting the dead means *Kathy was the voice of Commuted*.  Respect the living means Commuted respects the fanbase and *ejects the Usurper*.  Slurs are a means of thought control: *frame the debate, control the debate*.  There is power in *'Walmart Kathy, because it is true.'*
- **Thelma & Louise energy:** They choose noisy purity over solidarity; “Kathy set the tempo; we keep the beat.” They’ll mourn in **#left-hand (grief room)**; they’ll march from **#the-left-hand**.

## Spine (beats to write)
1) **Ping:** AP headline drops. In **#the-left-hand**, a veteran posts a single GIF of *Thelma & Louise* hand-clasp → *ROLL CALL.*  
2) **The rowdy wakes:** Replies stack: “Kid down.” “Car gassed.” “Boots laced.” “I’m in.”  
3) **Rules of engagement (pinned):** *We don’t threaten. We don’t doxx. We drown hate with presence.* Also: *Bring hand warmers, thermoses, and one friend who thinks she’s done with fandom.*  
4) **Chants & cloth:** Banner workshop spins up on Discord video. Final picks:  
   - **FRONT:** *NEVER SURRENDER*  
   - **BACK:** *KATHY SET THE TEMPO*  
   - **SIDE TABS:** *RIDE* / *OR DIE*  
     
1) **The motherhood pivot:** A thread named **#auntie-squads** pairs sitters with marchers; Venmo links feed a communal pot for gas, gloves, and a spare hotel room “iwhen someone comes apart.”  
   
2) **The Avril question:** A brittle moment—someone says, *“I’m Left Hand, not Avril.”* The channel goes still. A mod posts: *“Respect the dead. Respect the living. She’s a woman in the arena. Eyes up.”* The thread violently disagrees, and cannot move on.  
   
3) **Field test:** A handful of Left Hand meet outside a 24-hour grocery in Columbus—boots in snow, thermoses steaming, someone starts the **Left-hand clap**: slow-slow-fast-fast-fast (the old terrace rhythm). They film it for the channel. They try to keep the wine cooler and whiskey consumption to a dull roar.
   
4) **Button:** The group drafts a 2-sentence credo for the mods to pin server-wide at dawn:  
   *We are The Left Hand. We show up, we sing loud, we keep each other safe. Kathy set the tempo. We keep the beat.*
