
Metro Column: Shared Universes and Shared Headaches

By Clark Kent

I spent the day writing about City Hall corruption. It was, frankly, simpler than trying to follow the Commuted-Muppet-Genesis-Tarantino extended universe.

Phil Collins insists Land of Confusion was not a D&D supplement. Jeff insists it was a Muppet supplement. Jennifer Connelly has graciously agreed to be both Kathy Espich and, apparently, a co-author of imaginary modules.

I grew up in Kansas. Out there, we measured life in acres and bushels, not cinematic universes. When something broke, you fixed it. When neighbors argued, you found a way to sit down at the same table. This… this is something else entirely.

So I’ll just say this, quietly, as I file my copy and push back from the desk:

“Oh, I guess they really aren’t ever going to figure out quite how shared this particular cinematic universe really is.”

And then I went back to the corruption story. Because somebody still has to.

—Clark Kent
