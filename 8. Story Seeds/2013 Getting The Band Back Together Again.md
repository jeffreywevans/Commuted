## **SCENE: “THE READ”

**INT. JIM’S HOME, SPRINGBORO, OH – NIGHT**

It’s quiet here. No music. No TV. Just the click of the furnace cycling on. The soft pop of old hardwood floors settling. JIM sits at his kitchen table. The folder Jeff gave him rests in front of him—thick, heavy, filled with grief. There’s a mug of black coffee beside it.  Steam rising. Untouched.

He opens the folder.

**INTERNAL MONOLOGUE – JIM:**

**"Jesus, Jeff."**

He’s read hundreds of Jeff’s lyrics before. He’s played over every bassline, every weird chord, every stubborn tempo change Jeff ever threw at him.

But this isn’t stubborn. This isn’t clever. It’s… naked. And worse? It’s brilliant.

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
**"How the fuck did you write this."**

He turns the page. Another song. It doesn’t even feel like a song—it feels like a will.  Like Jeff’s confessing everything he’s never said aloud, but not to the band. Not even to Kathy.

To himself.

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
**"This isn’t a record.  This is a goddamn suicide note with a bridge and a chorus."**

Jim’s stomach sinks as he reads.

Song after song. Grief layered over grief. Memories buried under metaphors only Jeff understands—but he understands them now too. He’s known Jeff thirty years. He’s watched Jeff wreck relationships, freeze people out, shut down every time the emotions got too close. And somehow—somehow—this is the best thing Jeff’s ever written.

Better than 69 Reasons. Better than To Show Who We Are. Better than anything.

And Jim has no fucking clue how Jeff pulled it off.

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
"You were supposed to burn out years ago."

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
"You were supposed to quit."

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
"You weren’t supposed to outlive Kathy, let alone write this."

Jim flips another page. A demo notation in the margin: *“Final vocals must sound tired. Leave cracks in the voice.”*

Jim freezes.

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
"He knows."

Jeff knows this isn’t just a sad record. Jeff knows it’s his record to finish—because he won’t be around after it’s done. For the first time in his life—after all the weirdness, the coldness, the staring-off-into-space silence—Jim feels something worse than worry about Jeff.

He feels fear.

Jeff isn’t just grieving. Jeff’s alone now. Really alone.

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
"You built this whole album like a funeral for yourself."

**JIM (CONT’D):**  
"And you handed it to me like a fucking errand."

Jim stares at the pages. Every line, every chord progression, every melody feels like Jeff lighting another match in the dark. And in that kitchen, in that dead-silent room, Jim finally says something aloud—soft, barely audible.

**JIM (whispering):**
*"Don’t you fucking die before I finish this."*

FINAL MOMENT: Jim doesn’t close the folder. He just keeps reading. But now, every note feels like he’s holding a time bomb. Because he knows—without question—this is the last thing Jeff Evans ever intends to leave behind.
