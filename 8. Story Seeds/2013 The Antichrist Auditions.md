*"His lyrics are a goddamn autopsy report. But you know what chaps my ass? Half the time I listen to the stems, and it’s like—he’s written my drum fills for me. Every space, every build, every drop. It’s like he’s already lived through the record in his head and just expects me to play it out."*

Kendall studies him—lips twisted, considering.

**KENDALL:**  
*"You ever think about what that means, Jim? That Jeff’s fills are so precise, so specifically written—it could mean he’s got absolute confidence in you. That you’ll always deliver the song he hears in his head. That’s not control. That’s trust."*

Jim blinks. That hits harder than he expects. He takes a slow breath, looks down at his calloused hands.

**JIM:**  
*"Never thought about it like that."*

**KENDALL:**  
*"You’re the only person in that room who can hold the tempo steady enough for him to fall apart on top of it. He needs you steady, so he can bleed out safely."*

Jim’s eyes go glassy for a moment. He drinks, and nods, and it’s the closest thing to a prayer they’ll ever say together.

**SCENE: “TIES AND LIES”**

**INT. LIVING ROOM – 7:20 AM**

Jim sprawls across the couch, a bowl of cereal balanced on his chest, remote in hand. SportsCenter is flickering, one anchor grinning about nothing, the other faking interest in spring training numbers. Footage rolls—bored rookies, empty stands, even the *Reds* didn’t lose, didn’t win. Just tied. Like kissing your cousin, like finishing a fight with no black eye, like writing a song with no last verse. Kendall stands in the doorway, sipping her own orange juice, squinting at him.

**KENDALL:**  
*"You’re actually watching that? The Reds game? It ended in a tie, Jim. You’d get more drama from re-runs of ‘Antiques Roadshow.’"*

Jim shrugs, not looking away from the TV.

**JIM:**  
*"It’s background noise. The world’s on fire, but nobody in baseball seems to notice."*

She sits down, not letting him off the hook.

**KENDALL (soft, surgical):**  
*"What are you avoiding?"*

Jim’s jaw clenches. He spoons cereal, chews, swallows.

**JIM:**  
*"Nothing. Just tired. Thought maybe I could care about something easy for ten minutes. Instead, it’s a tie game, and I don’t even know who played left field."*

She nudges him with her toe. Not buying it.

**KENDALL:**  
*"You’re not tired. You’re scared. You’ve got $16 million hanging over your head and the only thing the world wants from you is a record that doesn’t suck and a bandmate who doesn’t die. You can say you’re tired. But what are you really avoiding, Jim?"*

He exhales, finally looks at her, eyes rimmed red.

**JIM:**  
*"What if I don’t know how to save any of them? What if all I do is break even?"*

She takes the remote, kills the TV.

**KENDALL (firm, loving):**  
*"Then you break even. And you tell the truth. I’ll write the rest."*

He leans back, lets himself be held by silence, for once.

**SCENE: “FLOOR TOM DIPLOMACY”**

**INT. KENDALL & JIM’S FOYER – 7:42 AM**

Kendall stands, car keys twirling on her finger, hair wild and hungry. Jim’s strapping on a hoodie, shoes untied, the dregs of orange juice left behind in the kitchen. The living room still smells like cereal and defeat.

**KENDALL:**  
*"Jim. If you want a real answer out of Jeff—don’t tiptoe. Next time you’re tracking, use the floor tom. Don’t even finesse it—just boom."*

She slaps the wall for effect.  
**BOOM.**  
He jumps.

**KENDALL (cont’d, lowering her voice):**  
*"You get his attention. Then you ask him if he’s mentally healthy. If he’s suicidal. Don’t sugarcoat it. Jeff’s so literal, if you talk to him in his own language, you’ll get the truth. You might not like it. But you’ll get it."*

Jim grabs his jacket, mind racing.

**JIM:**  
*"You want to drive to his house? Fort Ancient by 9, no problem. But you gotta let me handle this. I’ll ask. You keep your pen in your pocket and your mouth shut. Deal?"*

Kendall grins, feral and proud.

**KENDALL:**  
*"Deal. But if you freeze up, I’m going to start describing your technique in my next essay. Nobody wants to read about the soft hands of Jim Brown."*

He rolls his eyes, but there’s relief there—she’s on his team.

**JIM:**  
*"Floor tom. Got it. Let’s go see if the man wants to keep playing."*

She follows, shoes clacking on hardwood, not a word more. The story’s his to tell, or not. The door closes. The world outside is muggy, July mean, full of things not yet said. But for once, they’re driving straight at the problem, not around it.

**SCENE: “THE DRUMMER RIDES SHOTGUN – EARLY MARCH”**

**EXT. DRIVEWAY – 7:50 AM – RAW OHIO MARCH**

Kendall zips her windbreaker, breath fogging the windshield. The grass is brittle, flecks of old snow lingering in the corners. She climbs into the Civic, heater wheezing. Jim crams himself into the passenger seat, a battered stick bag thrown between his knees.

He’s already pulling out his favorite pair, Vic Firth, hickory, the ones with bite marks. He fidgets, tapping a cold syncopation against the glove box.

**KENDALL (teeth chattering):**  
*"Remind me why we’re driving through arctic hell at sunrise?"*

Jim doesn’t answer. He drums a restless, double-time rhythm, his breath just shy of panic. His shoulders don’t fit the seat. Neither does his mood.

**JIM (fixing his stare out the window):**  
*"You’re a better driver. And I’m gonna need both hands to get my head on straight before we hit Jeff’s door."*

He keeps tapping—left stick, right stick, knee, dash, a muffled rattle as they bounce over potholes.

**KENDALL (softer, knowing):**  
*"You’re more nervous about this than our wedding."*

Jim manages half a grin, but the sticks keep moving.

**JIM:**  
*"Because I can’t solo my way through this. If I lose the beat, there’s no one to pick it up."*

**KENDALL:**  
*"Then drum all the way to Fort Ancient. But when we get there—You talk. No ghost notes, Jim. Just the downbeat."*

He exhales, slumping back, knuckles white on maple. The car rolls out of the drive, heater humming, tires crunching over frost. She drives. He drums.

**Winter’s not over yet. Neither is what’s coming.**

**SCENE: “THE BLACK ALBUM”**

**EXT. FORT ANCIENT – JEFF EVANS’ PORCH – EARLY MARCH, 8:22 AM**

There’s frost in the shadows and slick, gray mud at the stoop. Kendall stands behind Jim, who’s hunched in his coat, drumsticks peeking from his pocket like contraband. Jim rings the bell.

A beat.

The door cracks open: Jeff Evans, hair wild, eyes sharp and hooded from a sleepless night.

**JIM (without preamble):**  
*"Where’s your gun, Jeff?"*

Jeff cocks an eyebrow—frown barely visible.

**JEFF:**  
*"Where it always is. Properly serviced, too. You want to check the magazine, or just the trigger?"*

Kendall’s heart hammers—she doesn’t realize she’s holding her breath until her chest aches.

**JIM (dead calm):**  
*"Are you gonna shoot yourself?"*

Jeff barks out a laugh—real, dark, not unkind.

**JEFF:**  
*"Like Kurt Cobain? Please. First, I’m a bassist. Second, I can’t even grow my hair that pretty. Third, this is the Black Album, Jim. Metallica’s. Not Nirvana’s."*

He steps aside, voice low and even—almost gentle.

**JEFF (cont’d):**  
*"This record is pure grief, but it’s a vessel. Not a prophecy. Not a prediction. I’m not leaving. I’m just trying to carry what you all ran from."*

Kendall feels her pulse slow—breath coming back in increments. Jeff sees it, nods.

**JEFF (wry):**  
*"You can come in, Kendall. You, too, Jim—once you get the mud off those boots. I just paid to have these floors refinished."*

Jim grumbles, scraping his soles against the mat. Kendall steps inside, the house warmer than she remembers, full of music equipment, paperwork, coffee stains, and a living pulse. She’s home—at least, for now.

---

**SCENE: “THE MENTAL HEALTH CHECK”**

**INT. JEFF EVANS’ FOYER – EARLY MARCH, FORT ANCIENT – 8:25 AM**

Kendall’s arms are folded tight, like she’s holding in all the air she gulped in the car. Jim stands awkward, boots finally clean, hands shoved in his pockets, waiting for Jeff to either explode or collapse. Kendall dives straight in, voice braced against something she can’t name:

**KENDALL:**  
*"Jeff, do you even understand what Jim’s trying to say? This isn’t about studio hours, it’s a mental health check. He’s worried about you. We both are."*

Jeff barely pauses, only the smallest hitch in his shoulders before he shrugs it off, half-casual, half-clinical:

**JEFF:**  
*"It’s a mental health crisis check. Duly noted. Look, I get it. You’re both worried. I am not concerned about your concerns, but if I were concerned about your concerns, I’d probably be a better partner to Jim, right?"*

He grins, dry as bone, the kind of smile that leaves paper cuts.

**JEFF (cont’d):**  
*"Don’t take this the wrong way, but I am still vertical, still finishing the album, and still keeping the sharp things pointed away from myself. But if you want to keep doing  wellness checks every few days, I can add it to the tour rider."*

He glances at Jim, who just snorts, eyes shining with both relief and something bitterer.

**KENDALL (exhales):**  
*"You could try saying ‘thank you for caring,’ you know."*

**JEFF:**  
*"You could try believing I don’t need it. But if you do? Fine. Thanks. Let’s go make some fucking music."*

He gestures to the kitchen, a flash of hospitality. There’s coffee on. There’s pain in the walls. But the band—what’s left of it—has finally come inside.

---

**SCENE: “BREAKFAST RITUAL”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – FORT ANCIENT – 8:36 AM**

The table is already littered with the detritus of creative men: lyric sheets, two mugs (one chipped), a bottle of Cholula, cold butter, and a pan with bacon grease still popping. Kendall perches on a barstool, Jim pacing, restless but not as rattled as before.

Jeff stands by the stove, spatula in hand—host by default, short-order cook by necessity.

**JEFF:**  
*"This is becoming a habit, you know. You and Jim now, Bill last week. Moody fuckers, both of you. Is there something about my breakfast that screams ‘therapy with eggs on the side’?"*

He forks out two more strips of bacon, lays them on a plate in front of Jim, who grunts his thanks.

**JEFF (cont’d):**  
*"Bill sat right there, arms crossed, scowling at the cat for ten minutes. All that studio angst, too many unfinished songs, not enough sleep. So I fed him. Bacon solves more than therapy, most days."*

He slides a mug of coffee to Kendall.

**JEFF (grins):**  
*"You two want to join my support group, I’ll make a flyer. But hurry up—I got a call scheduled for ten a.m."*

He lets it dangle, lips twitching.

**JIM (mouth full, grumbles):**  
*"Don’t tell me—"*

**JEFF (leans in, eyes sparkling with mischief):**  
*"Guess who, Brown. Hint: Her name rhymes with ‘shmavril shmavigne.’ Apparently, pop-punk royalty takes my calls now."*

Kendall nearly chokes on her coffee, a half-laugh, half-sob that tells everyone: Yes, this is a crisis. But at least today, they’re having bacon.

---

**SCENE: “JEFF’S STRATEGY”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – FORT ANCIENT – BREAKFAST TABLE**

Steam rises from the coffee. Bacon is devoured. The sun tries, half-heartedly, to push through the grey March sky beyond the window. Jeff is leaned back now, eyes glittering with the first real appetite for conversation Kendall has seen all morning. He’s almost playful, which is always the sign of a bigger storm coming.

**JEFF:**  
*"So here’s the move. Avril’s gonna want to know what to do. She’ll ask for a vibe, a mood, a technical breakdown. I’m gonna tell her: just belt happiness out. Like you’re fifteen, and the sun’s finally come out in fucking January, and you got nothing to lose but your voice."*

He stirs his coffee with a butter knife, barely looking up.

**JEFF (cont’d):**  
*"The whole track should sound like someone mainlined the joy of sunshine, and then ran headfirst into a wall of memory foam. Because that’s the only way to signal to every listener, every critic, every backstabber—  
that we’re not well. Not even a little. And we’re not hiding it."*

He looks at Kendall, then Jim.

**JEFF (finishing):**  
*"This record? Joy is the key. Joy, and pleasure, and the raw relief of actually making something together in a room. That’s what increases Truth. The weirder and happier it gets, the more it tells them:‘These motherfuckers are making music at the edge of grief—and they’re doing it anyway.’  That’s the secret sauce. That’s how you let the light in."*

For the first time all morning, his grin is real. Kendall feels the weight in her chest shift, just a little.

---

**SCENE: “WEAPONS & THE PANTRY”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – POST-BREAKFAST**

Jim’s hand lingers on his mug, the emotional current in the room humming like the aftershock of a minor quake. Kendall is silent, watching the two men re-learn how to be vulnerable without apology.

**JIM (soft):**  
*"Jesus, Jeff. You say stuff like that, it’s gonna make me cry."*

Jeff gives a half-smile, not quite sheepish.

**JEFF:**  
*"I’ve rehearsed that song more than a hundred times in my head, man. Always makes me cry—but only inside. I can’t afford to share it with my kids. They get enough truth. Too much, probably."*

He takes a slow breath, runs a hand through his hair, and pushes himself up from the table.

**JIM (quietly):**  
*"You got any more weapons in the house?"*

Jeff laughs—a bark, all exhaustion and relief.

**JEFF:**  
*"No, Brown. Not unless you count what’s in the pantry."*

He opens the door to the pantry with a flourish.

**JEFF (cont’d, feigning scandal):**  
*"Behold: Scandalous stuff. Rainbow carrots. Kale. Microgreens. The girls are total freaks for vegetables. It’s not right. I find shredded brussels sprouts in my shoes sometimes."*

Jim actually laughs, which feels like an exorcism.

**JIM:**  
*"You’re raising some kind of mutants, Jeff."*

**JEFF:**  
*"That’s the plan. Better mutants than musicians."*

Kendall snaps a photo, quietly, without asking—because this, right here, is how hope gets documented: with laughter and scandalous greens, in a kitchen that survived grief.

**SCENE: “THREE DECADES & FIVE WAYS”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – MORNING, MARCH – POST-BREAKFAST**

Kendall, still barefoot and emboldened by caffeine and relief, cross-examines Jeff the way only a woman with notebook in one hand and no fucks left to give can.

**KENDALL (light, but pointed):**  
*"So, tell me straight, Jeff—when are you finally going to retire? I mean, really. The band, the label, the legacy—when do you step off the stage?"*

Jeff slides a bowl of absurdly fresh microgreens toward Kendall, leans back, and gives her the *look*. The one that says you might as well have asked him about his sex life.

**JEFF (shrugging):**  
*"Never, if I can help it. But from touring? That’s probably what you mean."*

He picks at a carrot, still thinking out loud.

**JEFF (cont’d):**  
*"Rock doesn’t pay like it used to, you know that. Streaming’s a joke unless you’re Taylor Swift, and I’m too old for TikTok thirst traps. What’s left is five guys slicing up every dollar after we finally vote to sever payments to Kathy’s estate. And that’s gonna be a funeral with accountants."*

Jim just grunts. Old news. Kendall scribbles.

**JEFF (getting warmer):**  
*"Streaming pays shit. Always has. Merch and the live gig—that’s where the money is. Always was, always will be, unless some fucking AI hologram figures out how to sweat on fans."*

He grins, wolfish, suddenly alive with the old, manic energy.

**JEFF:**  
*"One of these days, I want to get a TV show made. Tell the real story. The ugly, funny, true one. Tour until I’m 64—call it ‘Three Decades & Five Ways to Go Broke Playing Rock and Roll.’ Mark your calendar. 2035, last show.  
I’ll be grayer than Bill’s beard."*

He looks to Kendall, suddenly serious.

**JEFF (soft):**  
*"You interested in a long-form interview? For your archive, for your project?  On how money really works in a rock band in 2020? I’ll give you the truth. Not the bullshit."*

**JIM (sighs, finally giving in):**  
*"Alright, Kendall. You made your point. You want the story? You got the story. Now let’s get the hell out of his kitchen before he makes us eat quinoa pancakes."*

Kendall knows this is the opening she’s been waiting for. **She’ll take it.** Because the story—the *real* one—just got the green light from the only man who still knows where all the bodies are buried.

**SCENE: JEFF’S KITCHEN, THE PAUSE BETWEEN HARD TRUTHS**

Jeff, fork poking through baby spinach like it personally offended him, looks up at Kendall with a dry, feral honesty.

**JEFF (grim laugh):**  
*"My sex life sucks, Kendall. Jodi hasn’t returned a message since she walked out of Bennett King’s office with her lawyer face on. Joint defense agreement my ass. Would’ve been nice to actually act human with her, y’know? Not just… all this legal bullshit."*

Jim can’t help it—he just stares out the window, as if searching for the last time any of them got laid *without* consequences.

**KENDALL (soft, surgical):**  
*"So what’s your solution, Jeff? Porn? Meditation? Tantric breathing? Or you just get weird with the Roomba?"*

He snorts, and even now, there’s that glimmer of the degenerate rockstar beneath the suit—equal parts bravado and bruised ego.

**JEFF:**  
*"Hollywood blondes, babe. They’re always available—if you’ve got a platinum card and absolutely no shame. But it’s not the same. Never is."*

Jim lets out a sound, a groan, a sigh, something heavy and old as their friendship.

**JIM (resigned):**  
*"Same old Jeff. You’d find a way to fuck your way through an avalanche if you thought you could get a new song out of it."*

Kendall closes her notebook, but not her mind.

**KENDALL (wry, motherly):**  
*"Just don’t bring them to the studio. Or the house. Or anywhere I can see them, alright? I’m not cleaning up after your emotional collateral anymore."*

There’s a hush, not quite awkward, just old. The unspoken truth: Love and sex and hunger for meaning still rule the ruins, long after the headlines fade.

Jeff leans back, exhausted by his own appetites.

**JEFF (quiet):**  
*"Just wish I’d said goodbye better. That’s all."*

**JIM:**  
*"We all do, man. We all do."*

**MORNING—JEFF’S DRIVEWAY, MARCH COLD SUNLIGHT**

Jim and Jeff stand in the gray drip of midwestern thaw, coffee steaming, breath visible, their friendship one fraying cord away from snap or reconciliation.

**JIM (level, poking):**  
*"Two o’clock. Over-the-Rhine. Studio. Bring your genius, bring your baggage, bring your goddamn metaphors about pain and resurrection, but show the fuck up, Jeff."*

Jeff’s fingers curl around his keys, posture loose but eyes sharp—still the band’s shark in lawyer’s drag.

**JEFF (shoulders raised, sardonic):**  
*"OTR is the reason I’ve got a CCW, Jim. Not for interior redecoration—hell, not for personal drama, either. You see what happened last summer? You feel those flames? Neighborhood burned hotter than Jim's take on the snare drum."*

He taps his coat, gesture at the invisible holster.

**JEFF (cont’d):**  
*"Look, I’m not paranoid, I’m practical. You want me in OTR, you get the Jeff that doesn’t get mugged. But I’ll be there. Unless you want to record somewhere else that sucks in a less cinematic way. I hear Sharonville has a Dollar General that does soundproofing for free if you buy a twelve-pack of store-brand cola."*

**JIM (smiling, doesn’t blink):**  
*"We’ve lived through riots, deaths, lawsuits, and Billboard Top 10. We’ll survive Bill’s neighborhood, too."*

Jeff nods, a peace—uneasy, but real—passing between them.

**JEFF (final, resigned):**  
*"All right. Two. But if the next civil war breaks out in the alley behind Bill’s, I want the headline: ‘Commuted: Dies Trying to Record the Saddest Track Ever, Survived by Zero Bubblegum Princesses.’"*

**KENDALL (half-out the car window, note-taking, watching her boys pretend to be men):**  
*"I’ll bring snacks. And a tourniquet. See you there."*

*They break. Not healed, but ready to start the ritual. Another attempt to turn grief into something you can dance to, or at least not shoot yourself over.*

Chemical Saints isn’t just a headline. It’s the ghost in every verse. The band is recording. I am observing, not interfering. No featured artists—don’t ask again. When there’s more I can say, you’ll read it here first. With all my love and necessary cruelty,
