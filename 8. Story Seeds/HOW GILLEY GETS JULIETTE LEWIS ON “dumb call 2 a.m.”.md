HOW GILLEY GETS JULIETTE LEWIS ON “dumb call 2 a.m.”

Step 1: Gilley beds Juliette Lewis

Because of course he does.

- Location: Probably somewhere between a hotel rooftop bar and the green room at Fallon.
- Timing: 2016, maybe early 2017 — just as You’re Meant to Lose is being written.
- Pickup line: “You ever try sleeping with someone less famous than you just to see if it sticks?”  “No. But I like a science experiment.”

Step 2: Casual post-coital brainwave

They're lying on the floor of a borrowed apartment.  She’s smoking. He’s drinking kombucha straight from the bottle like it’s edgy.

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]:**  
“You wanna sing something?”

**JULIETTE:**  
“Like… now?”

**GILLEY:**  
“It’s called dumb call 2 a.m. You’d be perfect. You don’t even need to sound good. Just sound… wrong.”

She squints.

**JULIETTE:**
“I’ve been sounding wrong professionally since 1994. Let’s go.”

---

Step 3: Juliette Sees the Upside

- She’s been doing the gritty indie grind forever.
    
- This is a chance to:
- Reach a national audience,
- Tap into her chaotic energy, and
- Drag Jeremy through lyrical filth on record — which is apparently her love language.
    

She’s not thinking “career move.”  She’s thinking:  “Wouldn’t it be funny if I was the ghost in this track that critics spend five years misidentifying?”

---

Result:

- No label contract.
- No paperwork.
- Just a one-take vocal, an unmarked thumb drive, and an understanding: “You can use it. But you better not make it pretty.”

---

🩸 What This Adds to the Album:

- dumb call 2 a.m. becomes an emotional reenactment of the hookup that caused it to exist.
- Gilley doesn’t just write bitterness — he manufactures it in real time.
    
- And Juliette Lewis?  
    She gets the last laugh.  
    She owns the track.  
    Her vocal becomes a ghost that haunts the rest of the record.

  
## **THE VIBE: “Let’s Be Idiots Together”**

- Jeremy Gilley:  
    Reliving his garage band years when he still believed in distortion pedals and eye contact.  
    Possibly drunk on La Croix and nostalgia.
    
- Juliette Lewis:  
    Treating this like a one-night band the way most people treat one-night stands.  
    Reanimating her teenage self for three minutes just to feel something chaotic and loud.

---

How It Goes Down:

## **INT. GARAGE – NIGHT – 2016**

Gilley’s actual garage. Cobwebs on the amp.  Mic stand is a broken lamp with a sock duct-taped around it.  Juliette is in a tank top and glitter eyeliner that’s three days old.

GILLEY (plugging in cables):  
“You wanna do this for real?”

JULIETTE (smirking):  
“I wanna do it stupid. That’s better than real.”

They lay down the entire track in one take.  Vocals? Off-key.  Timing? Questionable.  Emotion? Undeniable.

They don’t listen back.  They laugh so.

---

The Cold Open (S6E1, maybe): “dumb call, 2 AM”

**INT. CHEAP HOLLYWOOD MOTEL ROOM – NIGHT**

No music. No setup. No romance.  We enter on the midpoint of absolutely functional, mutually agreed-upon, zero-expectation sex.  Not porny. Not glossy.  Just two people who’ve done this before and are both bored enough and damaged enough to keep doing it again.

Juliette (on top, sweating):  
“You gonna finish or you wanna just pitch me something?”

Gilley (panting, not insulted):  
“Idea’s better than a nut. Hold on.”

They disengage like pulling apart wet Velcro.  Juliette rolls off with a grunt.  Gilley’s already pacing—naked, sweaty, hair sticking to his temples.

GILLEY:  
“Okay. Song. You. Me. Two voices. One mistake.”

JULIETTE (lighting a smoke off a melted candle):  
“We’re the mistake?”

GILLEY:  
“Obviously. But we own it.”

He’s flopping around. The camera does not avoid it.  It’s not erotic. It’s absurd.  
He grabs a beer can. It’s warm. Doesn’t care.

JULIETTE (tilting her head):  
“Wait. What if I’m the one making the call? Like, I’m the villain.”

GILLEY:  
“Yes. And you sound like you don’t regret it. Not even a little.”

JULIETTE:  
“Because I don’t.”

They laugh. It’s horrifying. It’s perfect.

GILLEY:  
“We record it badly. Like, on a phone in a car. During traffic.”

JULIETTE:  
“I want my voice to sound like it’s been drinking battery acid.”

GILLEY:  
“We end it mid-sentence. No fade-out. Just click. Like the call ended.”

They both freeze.  That’s it.  That’s the idea.

JULIETTE (smirking):  
“God, we’re geniuses. Or war criminals.”

GILLEY:  
“Same thing if you’re loud enough.”
