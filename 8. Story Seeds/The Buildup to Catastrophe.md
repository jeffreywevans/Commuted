## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – AUGUST 1997 – LATE AFTERNOON**

The air smells of solder, stale Gatorade, and ozone from synth racks left humming too long. Golden light slants through dusty blinds. Everyone’s here, half-dead from rehearsal: Bill crouched by the gear rack, Jeff perched over his bass like a hawk dissecting prey, Jeremy Evans noodling aimlessly, Jim spinning a drumstick like a baton, Kathy slouched in her chair, arms crossed.

And then there’s Gilley — long frame draped across the couch like a cat who owns the place. Glasses glint. He looks lazy, but his eyes are sharp. He’s been waiting for the right moment.

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]** (too casual)
Okay. So. I wrote a song.

The room groans as one.

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|KATHY]]**
Christ, here we go.

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]]** (readjusting his stick)
What is it this time? A ten-minute sex ballad about your chiropractor?

**GILLEY** (grinning)
Nope. Campfire song. Super easy. Crowd eats it alive. Sing-along hook. Done.

He leans forward, suddenly serious, and sings the refrain, voice warm but teasing:

**GILLEY (singing)**
*Oh, we’re feeling good, yeah, we’re feeling right…*

He pauses, smirks.

**GILLEY**
Tell me you can’t hear ten thousand kids screaming that back.

Silence. Then:

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|JEFF]]** (flat, unimpressed)
It’s idiot-proof.

**KATHY** (sharply)
It’s too pop. We’re not Third Eye Fucking Blind.

**GILLEY** (grinning wider)
Right, because nobody here has ever written a chorus people actually remember.

**[[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|JEREMY]] EVANS** (mutters without looking up)
Sounds like campfire Creed.

**[[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|BILL]]** (not looking up from patch cables)
Harmonies are clean, though. Stupid clean. I could stack them twelve deep.

**KATHY**
You’re not serious. You expect me to sing this?

**GILLEY** (leaning back, hands up)
Nope. That’s the beauty. I’m singing it. You sprinkle in harmonies. That’s all.

**KATHY** (deadpan, venomous)
Oh, how magnanimous.

**JIM** (half-smiling, seeing the chaos)
Wait, wait, wait. You actually want to front this one? Full lead?

**GILLEY**
That’s right. My song, my mic.

**JEFF** (eyes narrowing)
And you think this belongs in our set? 1998? After Victory Monument? After *[[11.1.10 The Hilton|The Hilton]]*?

**GILLEY** (smooth, unfazed)
Yeah. Because after all the screaming and blood and fury, the crowd needs one song they can fucking sing with us instead of at us. One moment where it’s not us versus them — it’s *us and them*.

Beat.

Kathy scoffs, shaking her head.

**KATHY**
It’s bubblegum.

**GILLEY**
Nah. It’s gasoline. You just don’t like that I’m holding the match.

**BILL** (chuckling, finally looking up)
You’re an asshole, but he’s not wrong.

**JEFF**
This chorus is beneath us.

**GILLEY** (flatly, mask dropped for a second)
No. It’s *beneath you*. And that’s why it’ll work.

He lets it hang there, then leans back again, smirk reloaded. The act of laziness re-draped.

**JIM** (banging a snare, grinning)
Fuck it. Let’s play it once. If it bombs, I’ll never bring it up again.

**KATHY** (icy)
It’s gonna bomb.

**GILLEY** (quiet, deadly confident)
Then you better pray the crowd doesn’t prove me right.

The room shifts. Nobody wants to admit it, but they know how this story goes: they’ll rehearse it once, hate themselves for it, and by the third show the crowd will scream that dumb chorus louder than anything else in the set. And Gilley will stand there in the spotlight, grinning, knowing he worked harder than any of them to look like he hadn’t worked at all.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – SAME DAY – NIGHTFALL**

Pizza boxes stacked like corpses. A few empty Rolling Rock bottles sweating on the floor. The reel-to-reel still spinning, the smell of hot tubes in the air. They’ve just slammed the last take of *[[11.7.6 Fireside Confession|Fireside Confession]]* and everyone knows it: the whole thing took **one afternoon.** A joke compared to their usual perfectionist rituals.

**BILL** (flat, glaring at the playback)

**GILLEY** (sitting cross-legged on the floor, smug)

**GILLEY (cont’d)**
Think about it: in a club, you let the synth breathe. In an arena, you make it thunder. On TV? You strip it bare. Stadium? You build it into a fucking cathedral.

**BILL** (icy)

**GILLEY** (winking)

**JEFF** (muttering from the couch)

**JIM**

## **EXT. ARROWHEAD STADIUM – KANSAS CITY – JUNE 13th, 1998 – GOLDEN HOUR**

The heat clings like a wet towel. 70,000 fans ripple in anticipation, every bleacher vibrating with beer, sweat, and Midwest chaos. The *Leave Your Key Tour* has been teased for months — delayed, teased again, delayed again — while Jeff disappeared into matrimony and brooding. The band’s fanbase was restless, feral. Tonight was ignition.

Backstage, tension is nuclear.

- **Jeff** is pacing with his bass, jaw tight. Still smug from the wedding, acting like nothing happened. He didn’t invite Kathy, and she hasn’t let him forget it — her anger simmers like a live wire.  
- **Kathy** smokes furiously, silent, glaring at Jeff like she might carve him open with her mic stand.
- **Bill** adjusts his synth rack for the hundredth time, muttering about how *Fireside Confession* is “criminally underproduced.”
- **Jim** is bouncing like a kid on amphetamines, already drenched in sweat, loving the chaos.
- **Jeremy Evans** tunes and re-tunes, trying to hold the nervous energy together.
- And **Gilley?** He’s leaning against a road case, grinning, every bit the cat who knows the canary’s already in his stomach.

**[[5.2. JEFF CREMEANS MODERN REVIEW|CREMEANS]]** (snapping his clipboard shut)
Arrowhead. Biggest crowd yet. Don’t mess it up. 

Jim snorts. Kathy flicks ash. Jeff mutters something about set order. And Gilley, stretching his long arms, finally says it:

**GILLEY**
We open the encore with it. *Fireside Confession.*

The room groans like it’s rehearsed.

**KATHY** (venomous)
You’ve got to be kidding me.

**JEFF** (flat, acidic)
We should’ve buried that demo in Bill’s basement.

**BILL** (deadpan)
You’d be so lucky. I get to reinvent it in front of seventy thousand people tonight.

**JIM** (grinning)
Fuck yes. I’m in.

**JEREMY EVANS** (sotto)
This is gonna be a trainwreck.

**GILLEY** (sharp now, mask off)
No. This is the moment. You’re all thinking about yourselves. The crowd? They’re thinking about the night they’ll never forget. This song is theirs. You’ll see.

Beat. Nobody answers. Kathy glares. Jeff sighs, already retreating into that cold interior fortress. Bill fiddles with a knob like he’s prepping a suicide pact with his synth.

## **EXT. ARROWHEAD STADIUM – NIGHT – ENCORE**

The stage is dark. The crowd roars like a jet engine. Then: a single guitar riff from Jeremy, bright and simple.

Spotlight on Gilley. He steps forward, lanky silhouette against a sea of screaming bodies. His glasses catch the light like fire.

**GILLEY (singing)**
*Oh, we’re feeling good, yeah, we’re feeling right…*

The crowd — seventy thousand throats — answers back instantly.

Bill’s fingers blur across the synth, layering textures he invented on the spot. Jim slams into the beat like he’s riding a bomb. Jeff plays stone-faced but deep down, even he feels the floor shudder. Kathy leans into her mic just enough to lace the harmonies, her glare cutting sideways at Gilley as if to say *fuck you for being right.*

By the third chorus the stadium is shaking. The song — unpolished, simple, almost childish — detonates like a ritual.

**JIM** (shouting mid-beat)
Holy *shit*!

**BILL** (between clenched teeth, still playing)
I hate this fucking song.

**KATHY** (into her mic, sotto)
I *knew* this would happen!

And Gilley? He just soaks it in, arms spread wide like a preacher, a grin splitting his face. The “lazy” frontman who worked harder than all of them to manufacture this exact moment — seventy thousand people chanting his words back at him under the summer sky.

## **INT. BACKSTAGE – ARROWHEAD STADIUM – NIGHT – JUNE 1998**

The stadium roar is still echoing down the concrete halls. Everyone’s sweating, half-deaf, adrenaline-drunk.

Bill is pacing, furious about how raw it sounded. Jeff sits slumped, towel over his head, brooding. Jim’s bouncing off the walls like he swallowed gunpowder. Jeremy Evans leans against a case, still picking quietly at his guitar.

And Kathy? Kathy is incandescent. Hair stuck to her temples, eyes blazing, cigarette trembling in her hand because her body hasn’t come down from what just happened.

She storms into the green room like she’s about to start a fistfight — but instead she slams the door and shouts:

**KATHY**
Greatest fucking thing we’ve ever done.

Everyone blinks.

**BILL** (icy)
Excuse me?

**KATHY**
*Fireside Confession.* It’s perfect. Raw. Singable. Unstoppable. We’re releasing it as a single and I’m directing the video.

Beat of stunned silence. Even Jeff’s head lifts out of his towel.

**GILLEY** (grinning, spreading his arms)
Hell yeah. Go for it.

**KATHY** (leaning forward, voice sharp with intent)
And it’s going to have my fingerprints all over it. The protagonist? A woman.

Jeremy Evans looks up, blinking.

**JEREMY EVANS**
So you’re talking… what? Same-sex couple around the campfire?

**GILLEY** (laughing, clapping his hands once)
Hell *yeah.*

**KATHY** (dead serious, smoke curling around her words)
Yep. And we’re gonna fucking take a flamethrower to “[don’t ask, don’t tell](https://www.britannica.com/event/Dont-Ask-Dont-Tell).” Piss all over President Clinton while we’re at it.

The room freezes. Kathy’s disdain for Clinton is infamous — every betrayal of progressive promise, every triangulated compromise, every moment the administration sold out principle for optics. She spits the words like poison.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
You want raw? You want unforgettable? You want the crowd chanting with us? We burn the whole false piety to the ground. We show girls at the fire. Desire without apology. Love without permission. Let MTV try to cut *that.*

Jim howls with laughter, pounding a chair like a drum.

**JIM**
Jesus Christ, I love this band.

Bill stares at her, then at Gilley. He knows the production headaches are about to quadruple. Jeff rubs his forehead like he’s bracing for a migraine — but he doesn’t argue. Not this time.

And Gilley just grins wider, eyes sharp behind those glasses. He knows when he’s won, and this time Kathy’s not just on board — she’s driving the fucking bus.

**GILLEY** (to Kathy, low, admiring)
Make it yours.

## **INT. CREMEANS’ OFFICE – DAYTON – JULY 1998 – MIDDAY**

Austere, tidy, humming with fluorescent light. Diploma on the wall, Psalm 121 framed above a filing cabinet. A Bible sits on his desk, spine cracked, marked with slips of paper. Jeff Cremeans, buttoned-down Southern Baptist, band manager and financial lifeline, is reviewing contracts with his usual granite calm.

Then the door opens and **Kathy storms in.** She’s still riding the afterburn of Arrowhead — hair pinned up but frizzing loose, dark circles under her eyes from no sleep, fire practically leaking out of her pores. She doesn’t sit.

**KATHY**
I want the video. *Fireside Confession.* I’m directing it.

Cremeans closes the folder, folds his hands, looks at her like she just proposed replacing Jeff’s bass with a chainsaw.

**CREMEANS** (quiet)
The song Gilley sang? The one written in an afternoon?

**KATHY**
The one that detonated Arrowhead. Yeah. That one.

She paces in front of his desk, chain-smoking.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
We’re not doing some stock bullshit. No campfire props, no soft-focus teenage fantasy. We put women at the center. Two of them. Sitting in the glow. Hands on each other. Desire you can’t mistake. And when the chorus hits, they’re singing with the crowd. Chanting. Owning it.

Cremeans doesn’t flinch, but he’s listening.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
We aim a flamethrower straight at “don’t ask, don’t tell.” We take a leak on Clinton’s cowardice. The whole triangulated sellout of queer kids who just want to serve or fuck or love without hiding. We burn that to the ground and dance in the fire.

Beat. Cremeans’ silence is heavy. Kathy shifts, for once a little uncertain.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
Look, I know your faith. I know where you stand. But this isn’t smut. It’s truth. It’s survival. And I’m telling you, this is our chance to carve the line in stone: Commuted doesn’t ask, doesn’t apologize.

She waits, braced.

Cremeans takes a long breath. Reaches into his desk drawer, pulls out a calculator. Taps a few buttons, slow, deliberate. Then he looks up, calm as a pond.

**CREMEANS**
Do you think… about two and a half million dollars ought to cover it?

Kathy freezes. That’s *triple* what she got for *The Tailspin* video.

**KATHY** (stunned, whispering)
Two and a half…?

**CREMEANS** (leaning forward, voice measured)
If you’re going to make history, you don’t do it with scraps.

For a second she doesn’t move. Then she lets out a sharp laugh — half disbelief, half war cry.

**KATHY**
All right then. We’re gonna scorch the fucking earth.

## **INT. ISLAND RECORDS – SARAH MALCOLM’S OFFICE – MIDTOWN MANHATTAN – JULY 1998 – NIGHT**

The blinds are drawn against the neon. Sarah’s office is chaos: printouts, half-drunk coffee, a Lotus spreadsheet open on her IBM. A map of U.S. markets with thumbtacks clutters the wall. **SARAH**

**KATHY**

**KATHY (cont’d)**

**KATHY (cont’d)**

**KATHY (cont’d)**
We don’t preach. We don’t point. We don’t ask. We *show*. And when MTV tries to cut it? Too late. Every kid will have seen it, taped it, memorized it.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
You wanted radio bait. Fuck that. This is a match. We’re dropping it in gasoline. Clinton’s “don’t ask, don’t tell” is bullshit, cowardice dressed as compromise. This video? This is us saying it out loud — but better. Saying it without words.

Silence. Sarah leans forward, resting her elbows on the desk.

**SARAH**
You really think this works at scale?

**KATHY**
I think once kids see two girls in a campfire glow singing our chorus, you won’t be able to put the fire out.

Beat. Sarah smirks — just a flicker. She knows her analytics never predicted *this,* but she also knows instinct when it bites her.

**SARAH**
All right. Friday. I want the rest of the boards. Shot lists. Crew notes. You burn it down, Kathy, but you better leave me something to air.

**KATHY** (grinning, savage)
Don’t worry. You’ll have the ratings to pray over.

She crushes out the cigarette in Sarah’s cold coffee, gathers her boards, and storms out.

Sarah sits alone for a long moment, staring at the ash floating in her mug. Then she murmurs to herself:

**SARAH**
Show, not tell. Christ, they’re going to kill me.

**KATHY**
All right, Cremeans. Where the fuck are you going with this?

**CREMEANS**
I’ll tell you where. I’ll put you on stage in front of eighty thousand people. I’ll make sure this album gets promoted properly. I’ll make sure the checks hit your account on time. And I’ll make sure you don’t kill Jeff, and he doesn’t kill you.

## **INT. HOTEL SUITE – DETROIT – AFTER CREMEANS LEAVES – OCTOBER 1998**

The balcony door clicks shut behind him. Kathy exhales a plume of smoke, long and slow, like she’s trying to purge his voice from the air. The bourbon in her hand is warm. The night feels too still.

**KATHY**
Even my fucking manager.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
Like the rest of the goddamn world.

**KATHY (cont’d)**
I really wish people would let me fuck in peace.

The fire is hers, but she can’t decide whether it’s keeping her warm or burning her alive.

You and Gilley think you’ve got the reins on me. Pigeonhole me, package me, sell me like I’m your circus act.

## **INT. COMMUTED TOUR BUS – LATE MORNING, I-90 EASTBOUND – OCTOBER 1998**

The bus rocks steady down the interstate, but inside it feels like a coffin. Kathy hasn’t spoken since Jeff sliced her open with words an hour ago. She’s in the booth, sunglasses on, cigarette trembling just enough to betray the quake in her hand. The others are keeping their distance — Jeremy hiding in guitar strings, Jim humming nervously, Gilley pretending to nap but clearly listening.

In Kathy’s head, the argument replays on a loop, each syllable a nail in her skin.

*If you believe you’re powerful… you don’t need to attack me like that.*
*You’ve either been in charge… or you’ve not.*
*Weak sort of shit, Ms. S-bitch… go straight to hell.*

Humiliation burns hotter than bourbon. Being shown up by Jeff — her rival in precision, her equal in iron — rattles her to the marrow. God, it’s embarrassing. To be cut down, not with fists or shouts, but with a scalpel. She tried to bulldoze him, tried to scream her way through the wall, and Jeff — calm, cold, merciless — met her with an axe.

She sucks hard on her cigarette, exhales, mutters under her breath.

**KATHY**
Fuck.

Because either she’s been driving this train for seven years — every headline, every firestorm, every stadium singalong — or she hasn’t. Either she’s the architect of her myth, or she’s just the passenger clinging to the wheel while the men steer.

And she *knows* it’s her. Has to be her. She’s sure of it. She’s been driving herself. Always.

But in that moment, when Jeff’s words hung in the air and she realized she’d screamed before she thought, she lost. She lost in front of everyone. She lost the argument.

And for Kathy Espich, losing isn’t just shame. It’s annihilation.

She drags her smoke to the filter, stubs it out, and mutters low, almost to herself, almost a vow:

**KATHY (cont’d)**
Never again.

Kendall’s pen scratches furiously: *Kathy rattled to the core. Humiliated by precision, by logic, by restraint. She bullies with fire; Jeff wields an axe. She swore she drives her own legend — but today, she lost the wheel. The vow forms: never again.
