# **CHAPTER ONE: SET YOUR SPIRIT FREE**

---

## **INT. HOTEL SUITE – 5:29 P.M. – COLLISION COURSE**

They are a tangle of limbs and heat and impact.  Clothes long forgotten. Sheets pulled down.  Nothing elegant. Nothing still.  Just **pure velocity**.

**Sarah** is wild-eyed, mouth open, hair a storm around her shoulders—**plasmatic**.  She surges against him like a live wire: **electric, reckless, demanding**.

**[[5.3 Sarah Malcolm|SARAH]]**
More.  Don’t stop—**don’t you fucking stop**—

And **Gilley**—His hands grip with purpose, his grip iron, his voice a **low boom** barely contained—

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]**
I said you’d pay.

Now **feel it.**

And he drives into her again, again—and it’s **thunder now**, in his chest, in his breath, in the slam of hips and the sound of skin.  No rhythm left—just **rupture**, just **impact**, just **climax inside the collapse**.

**SARAH (screaming)**
God—**Gilley—**

**GILLEY (growling)**
Yeah. Say it.  Say my name when you come.

And she does.  She **shatters**, **detonates**, **tears open**—all at once.  The world white behind her eyelids.  And Gilley?  He follows **like thunder behind the flash**,  splitting through her with a roar that doesn’t echo—it **devours.**

**And in that instant—**There is no press.  There is no band.  There is no tour.  No strategy.  Only the **blinding, holy collapse** of two dangerous people who’ve finally stopped lying.

She draws a little swirl in the notebook margin.  She hasn’t written lyrics since she checked in.  Not because she can’t.  But because she **won’t fake the voice.**  She’ll wait until it comes back **real**.

**The yellow legal pad** sits on the desk between **Bill** and **Jeff Cremeans**.  Coffee’s gone cold.

This stuff is…Christ, it’s dark.

It’s also **the best writing she’s done in years**.

And we can’t just sit on it.

I’m not saying we release it raw.  But if you’re asking me?  The best thing we’ve **ever done**—as a band—Is **write what we know**.

So what do we do?  Bring her in and say, “Hey, remember the most shame-filled spiral of your life? Let’s cut vocals over it?”

No.  We ask her **if she remembers what she wrote**.  And if she wants to **turn it into fire**, or leave it in the ashes.  But if she says yes?  This isn’t just a b-side.  This is a **record**.

They both stare at the pad again.  Still heavy.  Still sacred.

**BILL (quiet)**
She wrote what she felt.  And that’s the only thing we’ve ever built anything real from.

## **EXT. CINCINNATI REHAB CENTER – FRONT PARKING LOT – MIDDAY**

The sun is gentle.  The wind smells like autumn starting to crack through the concrete.  Standing near the curb:

- **Cremeans**, holding a to-go coffee but not sure if he should offer it.
- **Gilley**, pacing like he’s waiting for a movie premiere.
- **Jim**, hands in pockets, oddly still.
- **Bill**, in his usual quiet stance, arms folded, eyes scanning.
- **Jeremy**, sunglasses on, leaning against the van like he doesn’t care—but he absolutely does.

Then the doors slide open.  And **Kathy steps out**.  Not a goddess.  Not a storm.  Just **Kathy**, with color in her face, weight back in her hips, **rested**.   Her hair’s pulled back, simple.  Her hoodie’s clean.  Her eyes?

**Alive.**

There’s **no twitch**.  No caffeine jitters.  No thousand-yard stare.  Just… joy.  A lightness none of them have seen in years.  She’s **chipper**.  **Bright**.  Not bulletproof—just **whole**.

**GILLEY (half-whispering)**
Holy shit.  She looks like she time-traveled from a better decade.

**CREMEANS**
She looks like someone who finally got **eight hours of sleep**.

**Kathy** walks straight up to them.  Smiling. Not fake.  **Real**.

**KATHY**
Y’all look like you’re waiting for me to relapse in the parking lot.

They laugh.  The tension breaks.  Even **Jim** chuckles.

**KATHY (CONT’D)**
Don’t worry.  No coke.  No tears.  Just **clarity**.  And a whole lot of clear brain cells I intend to use against all of you.

**JEREMY**
Are we allowed to hug you, or is that, like, a post-rehab violation?

**KATHY**
You can hug me.  But I bite now.

They all move toward her.  Not like a crowd.  Like a **circle closing.**  And for the first time in months—maybe **years**—**They’re all standing together.**  Whole.  Fragile.  **Ready.**

## **EXT. CINCINNATI REHAB CENTER – MIDDAY – MOMENTS LATER**

The warmth of the reunion wraps around Kathy like a weighted blanket—familiar, solid.  Until it doesn’t.  She smiles, accepts the banter, the half-teasing compliments, the group hug that smells like studio dust and road wear.  And then, softly:

**KATHY**
Where’s Jeff?

He’s not here.

He’s still out of the country.

And maybe buying time.

I was in for **thirty days**, not exiled to Mars.

He knows you’re out.  He knows today’s the day.  We just… didn’t press it.

**KATHY**
Yeah.

*Her smile fades, just slightly.  Not devastation—Just that **deep sting of recognition**: the one person she expected—or maybe feared—**didn’t show.**  And everyone knows she noticed.  But no one dares say more.

**KATHY (softly)**
He’s mad.  Or hurt.  Or both.

**CREMEANS**
Maybe.  But he’s also **giving you space**.  Let’s get you home first.

She nods.  Straightens.  Rebuilds the smile—**but not all the way**.  Because **she noticed**.

## **INT. BAND VAN – HEADING BACK TO THE STUDIO – RADIO ON – EARLY AFTERNOON**

The country gets closure.  I get **nothing**.

He knows this was my worst moment.  He knows today matters.  So either he’s **punishing me** —Or worse—he’s doing exactly what I asked.  He’s **leaving me alone** to “find my way.”

**KATHY (internal)**
How considerate.  How emotionally enlightened.  How fucking **passive-aggressive**.

She doesn’t say it aloud.  But the feeling is there.  That **complicated ache** of being **abandoned and respected at the same time**.  And she doesn’t know yet which will hurt worse—**His silence now**, or **what happens when they finally see each other again.**
