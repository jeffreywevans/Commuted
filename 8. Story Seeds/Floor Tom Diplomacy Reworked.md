**INT. KENDALL & JIM’S FOYER – 7:42 A.M. – LATE SUMMER 2014 – OHIO**

It smells like old coffee and last night’s dreams. Kendall in a tank top, hair still half-furious from sleep, car keys swinging like a metronome. The kind of morning where you want to bite or be bitten.

Jim laces up one shoe, then gives up, hoodie hanging crooked on that thick neck. He drinks orange juice straight from the carton, swallows, winces. There’s a rim of lipstick on the glass. It isn’t Kendall’s.

She watches him—*really* watches, like a cat considering whether to pounce or nap.

**KENDALL:**
*"Jim. If you want to get through to Jeff, don’t tiptoe. Track with the floor tom. Not the snare. Not the ride. Not your church-mouse jazz hands. I want to hear you hit something. I want you to make the walls shake."*

She slaps the drywall.
**BOOM.**
He flinches—then blushes, the color crawling up his neck.

**KENDALL (quieter, lower, cutting):**
*"He’ll tell you. If you talk like him—no soft landing, no therapy-speak. Ask if he’s going to make it. Ask if he wants to die. Don’t do it for me. Do it so you can sleep tonight."*

Jim looks at her. He’s the strong one, but right now she’s the one holding the line.

**JIM:**
*"You want to drive? We’ll get there before the traffic, before he talks himself out of honesty. But this is my ask, not yours. You keep the pen capped and the peanut gallery closed. Deal?"*

She grins—predator, priestess, partner. She’s proud of him, and that’s more dangerous than love.

**KENDALL:**
*"Deal. But if you freeze, I’ll write about your technique. I’ll make you famous for being gentle, and you’ll never get laid again."*

He almost smiles, but she can see the shadow behind it.

**JIM:**
*"Floor tom. No finesse. Just gospel thunder."*

She grabs her boots, stomps them on, leather meeting hardwood. They step out into the July wetness, not holding hands, but more together than any lovers on earth.

The air is mean. The sun already tastes like regret.

They walk out, ready to break something open.

**EXT. KENDALL’S DRIVEWAY – 7:50 A.M. – OHIO – RAW MARCH**

The Civic’s heater wheezes. Her hands are shaking but not from cold.

Jim in the passenger seat, knees around a stick bag, picking at old scars in the wood. The air in the car smells like sweat, cinnamon gum, and the faint threat of something ending.

He drums on the glove box, tap-tap, like Morse code from the bottom of a well.

**KENDALL (teeth on edge, half-smiling):**
*"You sure you want me to drive? You know what happened last time."*

Jim grunts, keeps his eyes on the road.
Left stick. Right stick. Knee. Dash. Anxious music, the rhythm of a man who can’t fuck or fight his way out of this one.

**JIM:**
*"You’re a better driver. And I need both hands free. If I’m going to ask a man if he wants to live, I want my grip steady."*

She lets that sit. The car rolls over frost, wheels thudding through a pothole like a bass drum in an empty club.

**KENDALL (softer, slicing):**
*"You look more nervous than the day we got married."*

He laughs, almost—a cough, a stutter of relief, the edge of a panic attack. But he drums louder.

**JIM:**
*"This isn’t something you can improv. If I drop the beat, there’s no click track, no Bill to bail me out. Just Jeff. And the truth."*

She drives. He drums.

She wants to reach over and touch him. She wants to let him fail. Both at once.

The Civic melts the morning ice, two fugitives in love with disaster.

**The world isn’t safe. Not for them, not for Jeff. But the story has teeth, and they’re driving straight toward the bite.**

**SCENE: “THE BLACK ALBUM”**

**EXT. FORT ANCIENT – JEFF EVANS’ PORCH – EARLY MARCH, 8:22 AM**

Frost crawls up the porch posts, sunlight like old bone on the mud. It smells like thaw, gun oil, and the aftertaste of too many bad dreams. Jim stands stiff, coat zipped to the chin, his breath showing every time he exhales. Drumsticks poking from his pocket, not hidden well enough.

Kendall’s right behind—lips bitten, mind buzzing, wishing she’d fucked him last night just for courage.

Jim doesn’t knock. He rings—an act of faith or surrender, hard to say. The porch creaks. The wind sounds like a snare roll.

The door cracks: Jeff. Eyes rimmed red, wild hair, mouth set hard. Still in yesterday’s t-shirt—black, cracked, something indecipherable scrawled across the chest.

**JIM (blunt, no mercy):**
*"Where’s your gun, Jeff?"*

Not a question, a challenge. Kendall’s blood spikes—she wants to flinch, but holds.

Jeff’s eyebrow climbs. A dangerous, arctic little smile.

**JEFF:**
*"Where it always is. Cleaned, loaded, never lonely. Want me to show you the chamber or do you trust me to keep the safety on?"*

For a heartbeat, it’s just the three of them in the freeze. If anyone moves wrong, something breaks.

**JIM (soft as a hammer):**
*"Are you gonna use it on yourself?"*

Jeff laughs, a sharp bark. Not cruel, not kind. Real.

**JEFF:**
*"You want a Nirvana ending, Brown? Sorry. Wrong band, wrong decade, and I’d rather drown in feedback. This is the Black Album, not Nevermind. Metallica, not martyrdom. I’m here to bury grief, not write my own eulogy."*

He steps aside, arm sweeping the cold away.

**JEFF (gentler, but not apologizing):**
*"Come in. Wipe your feet. Last thing I need is mud on the hardwood. Kendall, you look like you need coffee and a goddamn reason to stay."*

She shivers at that. She steps in.

**SCENE: “THE MENTAL HEALTH CHECK”**

**INT. JEFF EVANS’ FOYER – EARLY MARCH – 8:25 AM**

Inside: warm, cluttered, heavy with the scent of burnt coffee and unspoken shit. The table is littered with lyric sheets, mail, and two half-dead houseplants. It’s both bunker and altar.

Jim stands awkward, boots finally clean, hands clutching the drumsticks like worry beads. Kendall, arms crossed—she has to hold herself together, or she’ll either cry or hit him.

**KENDALL (cuts straight in, because if she doesn’t, she’ll drown):**
*"Jeff, do you hear us at all? This isn’t about the next session. We drove out here because we’re fucking worried you won’t see the end of the album. Or the week."*

A twitch in Jeff’s jaw. For one second, the mask slips.

**JEFF (half turning away):**
*"I hear you. All of it. I don’t need a wellness check, I need everyone to stop making ghosts out of grief. I’m upright, I’m writing, I’m not bleeding in the bathtub. If you want to check on me, add it to the rider, right after brown M&Ms and not before noon."*

He turns, a crooked half-smile, every word a test.

**JEFF (cont’d, a little too sharp):**
*"You care, I get it. Fine. But care enough to let me finish this. The grief’s the fuel, not the fire."*

Jim snorts, relief ugly in his face. He wants to hug him, but that’s not this band, not now.

**KENDALL (voice low, almost sweet):**
*"You could just say thanks. Or fuck off. But don’t lie."*

Jeff looks right at her—sees everything, gives nothing.

**JEFF:**
*"Thanks. For caring. For bringing the floor tom. For not pretending. Let’s go. The album’s not gonna finish itself, and if it kills me, at least the floors’ll be clean."*

He gestures to the kitchen: coffee on, amps humming, the living room ready to become myth.

Kendall’s heart finds its old tempo. She’s not home, but she’s where the story is.

**SCENE: “BREAKFAST RITUAL”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – FORT ANCIENT – 8:36 AM**

Everything here is leftover: crusts of bread, tangled pages of lyric drafts, salt sweat on the mug handles, bottle of hot sauce with the label half-peeled. The sun keeps threatening the window, but the world outside is still cold as a church basement.

Kendall’s perched on a barstool—knees up, mug cradled like she might kiss it for warmth. Jim paces, eating bacon like he’s in training, which, in a way, he is. He keeps glancing at Kendall’s hands, then at Jeff, then at the pan.

Jeff’s at the stove—hair wild, t-shirt two days dead, spatula in one hand like a weapon, like a scepter. He’s the only one who looks at home in this mess.

**JEFF (no pretense, no apology):**
*"This is turning into a pattern, you know. Last week, Bill—arms folded, scowling at the cat like it owed him royalties. Now you two, doing your lost souls routine over my frying pan. You think I don’t notice the way you all show up when your world is on fire?"*

He flips the bacon, voice flat but not unkind. He’s learned the recipe for comfort: heat, fat, silence.

**JEFF (cont’d):**
*"I fed Bill. I’ll feed you. Bacon’s cheaper than a therapist, and twice as reliable."*

He slides a plate to Jim, who grunts and sits. Slides a mug to Kendall, eyes never leaving hers.

**JEFF (smirking):**
*"If you want to form a club, I’ll print the flyer. ‘Sad Bastards Breakfast—eggs, grief, and hot sauce provided.’ But you’d better eat quick. I got a call at ten, and she actually returns my messages."*

Kendall’s pulse jumps. She tries to drink her coffee, but nearly chokes on her own laugh.

**JIM (mouth full, almost smiling):**
*"Let me guess. Canadian royalty? Name rhymes with ‘shmavril shmavigne’?"*

**JEFF (leaning in, eyes sharp):**
*"Bingo. Avril’s calling. Wants to talk track vibes, joy, the whole fucking circus. Me, the gatekeeper of happiness. World’s gone mad."*

Kendall snorts. For a moment, the ache in her chest eases. Jeff keeps cooking, but something inside him is lighter now—a flicker of something stubborn and golden.

**SCENE: “JEFF’S STRATEGY”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – SAME**

The plates are messy. The coffee steams. The sun tries to matter. Jeff’s finally sitting—legs sprawled, back to the cold window, as if daring the world to throw another winter at him.

**JEFF (eyes shining, a little wild):**
*"Here’s how we play it. Avril wants a map. She’ll want a sound, a mood, the trick to making sunshine when you’ve got nothing but clouds. So I’m gonna tell her—fuck technical. Just belt joy. The kind you invent when you haven’t had any for years. Fake it until it feels like your own skin."*

He swirls the coffee, stirs it with a knife, laughs at himself.

**JEFF (cont’d, voice raw):**
*"Every song on this record has to sound like someone mainlined spring, then ran naked through a field of broken glass and memory foam. Like, ‘I survived, motherfuckers, and now I’m singing about it.’ That’s the only way anyone believes us. That’s how we win—by not hiding that we’re *not* okay, but we’re *here*."*

He looks up—at Kendall, at Jim, at himself in the reflection on the microwave door.

**JEFF (soft, true):**
*"Joy isn’t the mask. It’s the weapon. The more pleasure we let in, the more real the record becomes. They’ll listen, and they’ll know—these weird, stubborn bastards are still alive, and they’re not hiding from the wreckage. They’re dancing in it."*

Kendall feels it: the weight in her chest turns into a breath. The story, for a moment, is not about dying. It’s about wanting more.

The plates are empty, but the room is full. *Let there be breakfast. Let there be light.*

**SCENE: “WEAPONS & THE PANTRY”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – FORT ANCIENT – LATE BREAKFAST**

Jim’s hand cups the mug like it’s still warm enough to ward off the last chill of grief. There’s a scent of scorched bacon and black pepper, of something once burned that no one swept up. Kendall sits back, knees pulled up, notebook abandoned for now, mouth still carrying the taste of someone else’s secrets.

Jim’s voice is unguarded, almost breaking—gentle as a bruise.

**JIM (low, honest):**
*"Jesus, Jeff. You say shit like that, you’re gonna make me fucking cry."*

Jeff’s smile isn’t just tired—it’s ancient. He runs his fingers through his hair, the way a man does when he’s counting every gray.

**JEFF:**
*"That’s the problem, Brown. I rehearse these confessions in my head, but I only ever let them out in the bassline. My girls—they get the scraps of the truth. I can’t give them the whole song. It’d wreck them."*

He pushes away from the table—spatula still in hand, just in case he needs to shield himself.

**JIM (eyes narrowed, not quite joking):**
*"You got any more weapons stashed? Real question."*

Jeff laughs, a full-body thing, shoulders shaking. For the first time, it doesn’t sound fatal.

**JEFF:**
*"Just the arsenal in the pantry, brother. You want to see what modern Ohio warfare looks like?"*

He throws open the pantry with a flourish. Inside: rainbow carrots, microgreens, three kinds of mustard, a half-open bag of chia seeds that someone (not Jeff) tried to close with a bread clip.

**JEFF (deadpan, grinning):**
*"Welcome to the freakshow. The girls are on a vegetable rampage. I find Brussels sprouts in my shoes and kale chips in the goddamn dryer. You wanna survive this house? Bring ranch."*

Jim finally laughs—really laughs—and it’s as healing as a bloodletting.

**JIM:**
*"You’re raising mutants, man. Not rock stars."*

**JEFF:**
*"Better mutants than martyrs. Or critics."*

Kendall—always the thief—lifts her phone and snaps a photo. She catches the soft edges of men made safe by daylight, a pantry open, nobody hiding, nobody bleeding. This is how you bottle hope, she thinks: bacon fat, laughter, and green things for the next generation.

**SCENE: “THREE DECADES & FIVE WAYS”**

**INT. JEFF’S KITCHEN – MID-MORNING, MARCH – POST-BREAKFAST**

The mugs are empty but nobody’s moving. Sunlight drips down the counter, catching on the pile of lyric sheets, the bowl of microgreens, the day-old bacon grease. Kendall flexes her toes on the cold tile, pen ready, phone poised.

She’s not playing shy anymore.

**KENDALL (probing, dangerous):**
*"Tell me, Jeff—are you ever going to retire? Band, label, the whole mess? Or does the last Commuted record go out with your will?"*

Jeff pushes his chair back, a slow grin building. He tosses a carrot from hand to hand like a coin.

**JEFF (shrugs, relaxed):**
*"You know better. Retirement’s a tax dodge. As for the money—hell, the only thing standing between us and finally getting paid is the last round of lawyers. Kathy’s estate gets a cut of everything. Always has. But that’s changing."*

Jim leans in, eyes narrowed. He already knows the answer, but he needs to hear it out loud.

**JEFF (voice cool, clinical):**
*"We file the suit, probate court in Hamilton County. Six months, nine tops, and that’s if Kathy’s lawyer gets cute. We win—guaranteed. I’ve got the paper trail, the royalty statements, the contracts Bill’s mom kept in her attic. It’s as open-and-shut as these things get. After that? It’s just us. Finally."*

He bites the carrot, chews. There’s something satisfied, almost ruthless, in his expression.

**JIM (with a half-smile):**
*"So we’re finally free? No more writing checks to a ghost?"*

**JEFF (nods, mouth full):**
*"We’ll still owe the past, but not the paperwork. And if I’m lucky, the judge gets a little nostalgic and clocks the case in time for next year’s tour. Might even get my damn publishing back."*

He looks at Kendall, eyes bright with old fury, new hope.

**JEFF (softening, almost tender):**
*"That’s the business, Weatherby. You want the story—the real one? Start with the math. Start with the blood."*

**KENDALL:**
*"Oh, I will. But I want names, too."*

**JEFF:**
*"You’ll get everything. Just keep the tape rolling."*

Jim stands, stretches, the room lighter now that the worst of it’s on the table.

**JIM:**
*"Let’s go before he gets sentimental and offers us kale muffins."*

Kendall flashes him a smile, already scripting the lead for her archive: *The day the ghosts got evicted.*

**SCENE: JEFF’S KITCHEN, THE PAUSE BETWEEN HARD TRUTHS**

Spinach shoves against the tines of Jeff’s fork like something personal. The fridge hums. The floor is gritty with salt and bootprints. Morning sun slices through the window, illuminating dust and regret.

Jeff’s honesty lands rough as sandpaper.

**JEFF (grim laugh, voice frayed):**
*"My sex life sucks, Kendall. Jodi ghosts every message—lawyer face, steel jaw, walked out of Bennett King’s office and never looked back. So much for a joint defense. Would’ve liked to remember we were animals, once. Not just legal fodder."*

Jim’s eyes drift to the window—gray Ohio sky, the silence of a man sifting through memories of bodies, laughter, sweat, and loss.

**KENDALL (soft, surgical):**
*"So what’s your fix? Porn? Tantric breathing? You do weird shit with the Roomba, Jeff?"*

He grins, wolfish, wounded, pride both armor and confession.

**JEFF:**
*"Hollywood blondes, babe. If you got a platinum card and a ruined reputation, there’s always someone ready to pretend. But it’s transactional. No one’s ever hungry for you, not after all this."*

Jim groans—pain and humor entwined, like men who’ve survived too much.

**JIM (fond, biting):**
*"Same old Jeff. If you thought you could squeeze a hit single out of fucking your way through an avalanche, you’d do it twice just for the lyrics."*

Kendall snaps her notebook closed, eyes never leaving them.

**KENDALL (wry, mother-wolf):**
*"Just keep it out of the studio, and off my goddamn porch. I’m not cleaning up after your collateral damage, not at this age."*

A hush. Not awkward—just the afterimage of honesty. This is love, in their language: truth, mockery, survival. Hunger and loss in equal measure.

Jeff slumps, his appetite finally spent.

**JEFF (quiet, almost breaking):**
*"Wish I’d said goodbye better. That’s the one thing."*

**JIM:**
*"We all do, man. We all do."*

**EXT. JEFF’S DRIVEWAY, MIDWESTERN MARCH, SUNLIGHT UNKIND**

Steam lifts from coffee mugs. The air smells like old snow and new beginnings nobody asked for. Friendship, as brittle and sharp as the morning.

Jim squares up, eyes level, poking at the wound and the future.

**JIM (direct, unwavering):**
*"Two o’clock. Over-the-Rhine. Studio. Bring the genius, bring the baggage, bring your endless metaphors about resurrection. But show the fuck up."*

Jeff keys jangling, posture easy, eyes shark-bright beneath all the exhaustion.

**JEFF (shoulders raised, sardonic):**
*"OTR’s why I got a CCW, Jim. Not for the drama, not for the set lists, but because that neighborhood burns hotter than Bill’s snare work. You want me to show up, you get me, sidearm and all. Unless you want to record in Sharonville—at least the Dollar General does soundproofing with your cola."*

Jim laughs, tired and true.

**JIM:**
*"We’ve survived riots, deaths, lawsuits, Billboard Top 10. We’ll survive Bill’s neighborhood. And your nerves."*

Jeff nods, a peace not spoken but exchanged.

**JEFF (final, resigned):**
*"Two it is. But if Cincinnati burns, I want the headline: ‘Commuted: Dies Trying to Record the Saddest Track Ever, Survived by Zero Bubblegum Princesses.’"*

**KENDALL (leaning out the car, all appetite and documentation):**
*"I’ll bring snacks. And a tourniquet. See you there, boys."*

They split—hearts unhealed, but hands steady. Ready to conjure music from the wreckage, to turn old grief into dance, to fuck with fate and come back for more.

**INT. BILL’S PRODUCTION ROOM – LATE FRIDAY NIGHT**

*The air hums with the electric-fried scent of patch cables, cold fries, and existential dread. Penn Station wrappers litter the desk. All five guys—no Kathy, no Kendall tonight—are wedged into rolling chairs, camped around the LCD, where Fox News, Reddit, and Twitter wage information war in adjacent tabs.*

**JEREMY** (feet up on the amp rack, phone in one hand):
Yo, Bill.  Some dude here says he saw you eating a hot dog on Superior’s shore and claim “the water would bless it.”  Fact or fiction?

**BILL** (not looking up, threading a synth cable):
Kielbasa, not hot dog. And I said “consecrate,” which is just science.

**GILLEY** (slouched, grinning, reading off his phone):
Hey, Jer. Listen to this:
> *“Did Jeff Evans install fluoride mind control in Ghana, or is he just a Midwestern cryptid with a water fetish?”*
Should I respond as “The Real Jeff Evans”? Or like, “Aquaman 937?”

**JIM** (deadpan, eyes flicking between the tabs):
Only if you can spell “hydraulic” right this time.

**JEFF** (quietly):
Please don’t. I’m tired.

**JEREMY** (leaning in, keyboard poised):
What if I post as Avril? She’s stuck in Montgomery Inn drive-thru, lost in a pulled pork fugue.

**GILLEY:**
You gotta make it Canadian. Say something about poutine, or “lake law.”

**BILL** (now engaged):
Better: Skyline Chili, just ordering a Pepsi, waiting for the multiverse to implode.

**JIM:**
If you get us banned, I’m restringing your guitars with catgut.

**JEFF** (crooked grin, finally):
See if you can swap concert tickets for water filters. Freaks’ll believe anything tonight.

Post it. For science.

**JIM** (watching, almost at peace):
I hope Avril’s Uber gets hazard pay.

*Doorbell rings, a burst of cold night air. Avril’s voice from the hallway:*

**AVRIL (laughing, breathless):**
Sorry! I brought poutine and questionable judgment. Which disaster do you want first?

**BILL (shouting):**
Bring it all. We’re hosting the Lake Erie Apocalypse over here.

*Laughter again. Relief, for a second. The world’s on fire, but inside this box of patch cables and bad jokes, they’re all still alive, waiting for the sun.*
