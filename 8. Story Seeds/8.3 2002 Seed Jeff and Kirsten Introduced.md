**INT. PRAGUE – HOTEL U ZLATÉHO JELENA – NIGHT, 2002**
 
A band has just finished playing to a crowd that smelled like clove cigarettes and regret.

JEFF EVANS is 30.  Famous. High. Glittering with sweat.  Living on coffee, adrenaline, and three hours of sleep across five days.

KIRSTEN DUNST is 20.  Post-‘Bring It On’, pre-‘Melancholia’—the girl next door who slipped out the backdoor, barefoot, with your cigarettes.

They meet by accident.  Or because someone dared someone else.  Or because Prague makes sin look like art.

---

**In the Room**

The curtains don’t close right.  There’s one lightbulb.  The bed is not clean.

 **KIRSTEN**:
“You always look like a disaster, or is this a special occasion?”

**JEFF** (grins).
“Depends on how this ends.”

---

**The Sex**

It’s not good.  It’s desperate.  Two people clinging to relevance by touching someone else’s myth.

Jeff moves like he’s proving something.  Kirsten laughs until she doesn’t.

**KIRSTEN**: (gasping)
“You don’t kiss like someone who forgets.”

And Jeff doesn’t answer. Because he won’t forget.  And she never planned to remember.

---

**After**

They sit on the edge of the tub. Her bra in a sink half-full of melted ice.  His back against cracked tile.  Both too awake to sleep, too wrecked to leave.

**JEFF**: (curious)
“Would you ever do this again?”

**KIRSTEN**: (confidently)
“Only in another country.  Where no one knows our names.  And the minibar is actually stocked.”
