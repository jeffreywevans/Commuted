## **INT. INDUSTRIAL CLUB – AMSTERDAM – MIDNIGHT – SEPTEMBER 1997**

The bass pulses through Bill Davenport's bloodstream, an electronic heartbeat aligning itself to the natural rhythm that drums deep within him. The room feels damp, charged, as though its very atmosphere is wired with live circuits and searing neon threads. Shadows sway together like dark, liquid forms—part human, part spectral—caught in the eternal embrace of the techno-industrial beat.

Bill leans casually against a wall coated in graffiti, a chaotic, cryptic mural of rebellion and ecstasy. The silver zipper of his black leather jacket glints briefly under a strobe's erratic eye, a metallic flash amid darkness. His hair hangs loose, cascading to his shoulders like a wild river; strands cling to his forehead, dampened by heat and desire.

Nearby, a woman gyrates—her movements hypnotic, serpentine. Her black dress is nearly sheer, each curve backlit by stuttering lights. She turns, catching Bill’s eye, lips curving into a sly, unspoken invitation. He feels a primal pulse, warmth spreading low, but holds back. The night is young, and the city outside still beckons.

**EXT. CITY STREET – AMSTERDAM – 2:47 A.M.**

Amsterdam sprawls ahead of him, canal waters shimmering like spilled ink beneath flickering lamps. Bill moves through winding streets, his boots striking cobblestones wet with mist, passing figures illuminated only briefly by storefront lights and neon signs, each face momentarily familiar yet eternally unknown.

He stumbles into a tiny bar, its atmosphere thickened by smoke, hops, and whispered promises. An aging bartender pours golden beer, the foam crowning the glass like delicate lace.

Bill takes a sip—bitter perfection dances on his tongue. It tastes like everything he craves: authenticity, history, rebellion.

At the end of the bar, a man with a thick beard watches Bill intently. His eyes are bright, challenging. Bill acknowledges him with a slight nod; it's respectful but distant. The man grins, undeterred, eyes alight with curiosity and hunger.

Bill finishes his beer, savoring the faint burn. He leaves a generous tip, slipping into the night without looking back.

**INT. SIGNAL MAESTRO – TECH SHOP – AMSTERDAM – AFTERNOON – SEPTEMBER 1997**

Sunlight filters weakly through grimy windows, casting shadows across walls lined with synthesizers, sequencers, drum machines—a cathedral built from wires and dreams. The proprietor, a thin man named Hans, guides Bill reverently, fingers caressing knobs and buttons like sacred objects.

Hans demonstrates a new modular synth, its sleek form pulsating with an inner light. He patches cables fluidly, creating alien landscapes of sound.

Bill watches, captivated, then tries it himself. His fingers move instinctively, coaxing forth resonances, gritty textures, warm pads that envelope him like velvet. He buys it immediately, knowing it will carry Amsterdam's pulse home.

**EXT. MARKETPLACE – AMSTERDAM – EVENING**

Bill wanders into a bustling marketplace, fragrant with roasting meat, fresh flowers, and spices. Laughter rings out amid the vibrant chatter. A woman selling fruit—her dark curls wild, eyes sharp—hands him a pear, smiling knowingly. She’s radiant, unapologetically natural, unshaved arms brushed by golden sunset.

He bites into the fruit, sweetness flooding his senses. She leans close, whispering in accented English, “The city's sweetest treasures hide deeper.”

**INT. HIDDEN SPEAKEASY – LATER THAT NIGHT**

At the end of an alleyway, behind an unmarked door, Bill finds himself in a secret bar. Dimly lit, jazz-infused whispers curl around his senses. The bartender, a woman with tattoos climbing elegantly up her arms, pours him something smoky and potent.

She watches him drink, leaning over the bar, unshaven underarms proudly displayed, eyes bold with silent challenge. He meets her gaze, amused and intrigued. She laughs softly, leaning closer to whisper, “I finish at midnight.”

**INT. APARTMENT – NIGHT – SEPTEMBER 1997**

Her apartment is warm, small, filled with vinyl records, plants sprawling chaotically across shelves, and art that shocks yet comforts. Clothes discarded effortlessly, they fall together onto her futon—its softness absorbing their mutual hunger.

Bill trails fingers over her thighs, appreciating the honesty of her body, unaltered by convention. She arches beneath his touch, guiding his hand lower, laughter turning to gasps as they find a rhythm as raw as the music he adores. Their night unfolds into whispered truths and uninhibited union.

**EXT. CANAL – PRE-DAWN**

Dawn touches Amsterdam gently, canals reflecting hues of rose and gold. Bill sits on a bench, body pleasantly sore, mind humming with new music, new experiences. A man in business attire approaches, nervous but hopeful.

He speaks hesitantly, voice accented, offering something Bill recognizes as longing, vulnerability naked in dawn's half-light. Bill, moved yet gentle, shakes his head softly. The man smiles sadly, grateful for compassion if not fulfillment.

**INT. RECORD SHOP – DAY**

Bill spends the day immersed in vinyl stacks, discovering obscure industrial artists and avant-garde composers. He feels grounded in this place where sound and artistry intertwine. Later, he shares a cigarette with the shop’s owner, an old punk with piercings, stories, and unapologetic truths.

**NIGHTCLUB MONTAGE – OVER SEVERAL DAYS AND NIGHTS**

Amsterdam opens itself fully—clubs pounding with techno fury, encounters with strangers who offer everything from illicit substances to forbidden desires. Each night, Bill tastes more of the city's raw, intoxicating honesty. He dances, drinks, laughs; he feels alive as never before.

**INT. HOTEL ROOM – AMSTERDAM – LAST NIGHT**

In his hotel, Bill reflects, the city sprawled beyond windows blurred by mist. Sheets tangled from recent love, body marked by experiences—teeth, fingers, nights of passion and music. Amsterdam’s heartbeat pulses in his chest, vivid and sacred.

He lifts the modular synthesizer, fingers caressing keys. Music pours forth, complex yet clear, resonating with each experience, each face, each taste. The composition emerges effortlessly—raw yet refined, chaotic yet precise.

Amsterdam is not merely memory but melody, etched deep within Bill Davenport’s soul, destined to echo forever in the music he makes, the life he chooses to live.

**NIKLAS** (whispering, with something like worship)

**BILL**

**BILL** (CONT’D)

He places it on the console, turns, and descends the stairs, leaving Niklas trembling, their heart pounding harder than any beat they’ve ever dropped.  The music roars on, but it’s Bill who carries the real sound in his pocket now.

Top floor, Montmartre. Skylights cracked open. Guitars leaning like drunkards against the wall. Papers everywhere. Vinyl spinning low in the corner—French jazz, ironically smooth.

**KATHY ESPICH** kneels on the rug, surrounded by lyrics. Hair in a messy knot. Cigarette dangling, forgotten. She’s got the energy of a storm waiting for a trigger.  **JEFF EVANS** paces slowly with a notebook, pen cap between his teeth. Barefoot. Shirtless. Eyes half-zoned, muttering about chorus placement and broken clock metaphors.  

Then—The door opens.

_BANG._

And _there_ he is.

**BILL** (low, casual)  
Knock knock, motherfuckers.

He steps inside, boots clomping, case in one hand, smirk already blooming.

**JEFF** (blinking)  
You’re early.

**KATHY** (surprised)  
You’re _glowing_. What did Amsterdam do to you?

Bill sets the Ghost Echo down, opens it like a priest unveiling relics.

**BILL**  
Brought you a gift. Got laid. Got loud. Then built a demon in a warehouse with some synth freaks. You’ll want to hear this.

He pulls out the tape, pops it into their makeshift deck.  Static. Then—That _sound_.  The warehouse recording. _That_ backwards-ringing apocalypse. Twisting, twirling, like sirens mating with cathedrals in reverse.  Kathy flinches—then leans forward, entranced.  Jeff stands dead still, jaw slowly dropping.  

Silence.

Then—

**JEFF** (deadpan)  
…The fuck is _that_?

**BILL** (grinning)  
It _rings backwards_. All analog. Subwoofer-fucked. Built it with a Soviet alarm clock, a soldering iron, and black coffee that could kill a horse.

Kathy stands, cigarette forgotten entirely.

**KATHY** (low, amazed)  
That’s subversive as _fuck_.

Bill’s grin widens.

**BILL**  
Thought of you when I made it.

Jeff rewinds, plays it again. Louder.

**JEFF** (low, thinking aloud)  
We’ve got that half-track—“Symmetries Collide”—with the open drum stem. This… could _carry_ it. Could shape the chorus. Maybe bend the bridge around it.

**BILL** (smiling)  
What’s it about?

Jeff shrugs, honest as sin.

**JEFF**  
Not a fucking clue. Haven’t figured that out yet. But I’ll write to this. Hell, I _have_ to.

Kathy nods, eyes locked on the spinning reels.

**KATHY**  
There’s damn fine promise in that mess, Bill. You wired a goddamn ghost.

Bill leans back on the arm of a couch, lighting a cigarette, pleased as hell.

**BILL**  
Happy to haunt.

**JEFF** (grinning)  
Keep it up.
