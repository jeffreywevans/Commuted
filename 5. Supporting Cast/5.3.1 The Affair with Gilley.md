# **Not Love. Not Hate. Just Utility + Echoes**

- **They didn’t need a future.**  
    They needed *a recurring outlet*.  A thing that cracked open the static of their respective lives.  And in the microcosm of those hotel rooms, those backstage corridors?  They were real. Fully. Brutally.
   
- **Sarah got intel.**  
    Real-time updates from the nucleus of the band, filtered through Gilley’s sweat and pillow talk.  But she wasn’t playing him — *they were both playing*.  He got proximity. She got perspective.
   
- **[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|Gilley]] got sex, power, validation.**  
    Not simply sex, but access. To someone who outranked him, outmaneuvered him, and, crucially, never pitied him.  That’s *crack cocaine* for a man like Gilley.
