## INT. DIVE BAR – NIGHT

Neon hum. Low ceiling. A looping sports highlight on mute. Behind the bar, two bottles of decent bourbon and a tragic jar of maraschino cherries.

**BILL** and **JIM** sit with **bourbon, neat**. Civilization in two short glasses.

**JEFF** arrives like a weather system: iPad, three pens, and the electric certainty of a man who has a metaphor to deploy.

**JEFF**  (doesn’t sit; already lecturing)  
Imagine ten million Slurpees hitting Earth at forty thousand miles per hour.

**BILL**  
No.

**JIM**  
Hard pass.

**JEFF**  
You can’t stop progress. Also, picture the blue raspberry ones, because their dye content is basically a spectrograph with a sweet tooth.

He slides onto the stool, flips open his iPad. The bartender, **MARLENE**, clocks the pens like they’re weapons.

**MARLENE**  
What are we drinking?

**BILL**  
Bourbon, neat.

**JIM**  
Bourbon, neat.

**JEFF**  
Water, and then… hypothetically, a bourbon Slurpee.

**BILL/JIM**  (in unison, scandalized)  
Neat.

**JEFF**  
It’s a thought experiment. Comets are Slurpees. Not philosophically, chemically! The old model said “dirty snowballs.” Cute. Then we got close: Halley showed jets like busted radiator hoses; Rosetta/Philae turned out to be a double-lobed, low-density, loosely aggregated rubble heap—porous as a stale donut. That’s a Slurpee with gravel. Cosmic convenience-store slurry from 4.6 billion years ago.

**BILL**  (to Marlene)  
Bourbon. Neat.

**JEFF**  
You heat the nucleus and it vents, those are brain-freeze jets. The coma? That’s the syrup fog. Two tails: dust tail, ion tail, one’s the corn-syrup mist, one’s the static that makes your hair stand up after you’ve been leaning on a CRT in 1996.

**JIM**  
I hate that I know exactly what that feels like.

**JEFF**  
Here’s the money shot. We used to think “rock.” But the tensile strength is pitiful. You can probably arm-wrestle a small comet to pieces with a determined Shop-Vac. They’re held together by ancient spit and vibes. You get close with sunlight and rotation, they shed like a cat on a black couch.

**BILL**  
Jeff, the thing in my glass is older than your metaphor and has fewer ingredients.

**JEFF**  
Respectfully incorrect; bourbon has a mash bill, comets have a mash bill: ices (water, CO₂, CO, ammonia), organics, silicates, bonus glycine if you’re lucky. The label reads: “Some assembly required, store in Kuiper Belt until needed.” Then a gravitational coupon drags it inner-ward, and boom, Slurpee time!

**JIM**  (staring at his glass)  
If he says “mouthfeel,” I’m leaving.

**JEFF**  
Mouthfeel is crucial. The regolith is crunchy. Thixotropic behavior, non-Newtonian slurry that stiffens under stress, like cornstarch and regret. Touch a comet with a lander at walking speed and you bounce because the surface says, “What if I were talc?” Then you harpoon it, and the harpoons don’t fire because of course they don’t; the machine is European.

**BILL**  
Our drummer’s from Ohio and the snare always fires.

**JIM**  (quiet pride)  
Every. Single. Time.

**JEFF**  
Acoustic analogy: those jets? Percussive transients. The nucleus spins. Outgassing shifts the moment of inertia and you get a tempo map no session player can follow. It’s a hi-hat made of nitrogen and shame.

**JIM**  (mind involuntarily engaged)  
So the jetting changes the click mid-take?

**JEFF**  
Exactly. Torque noise. Outgassing like a bad FOH engineer riding the fader with mittens. The tail’s a reverb trail; solar wind’s the slapback. And when that coma expands, it’s basically the fog machine from a 1989 Cure tour but with actual physics and fewer legal disclaimers.

**BILL**  
Bourbon. Neat.

**MARLENE**  
I got ears, honey.

**JEFF**  
We learned comets aren’t harbingers; they’re time capsules. Prebiotic organics hitching a ride. If life is a crime, comets are the getaway car. You crack one and it smells like the world’s first pantry: alcohols, aldehydes, a dash of hell.

**JIM**  
What flavor is “dash of hell”?

**JEFF**  
Blue raspberry. Always blue raspberry.

He sketches a lumpy hourglass on the napkin—labels it **“67P, do not eat.”**

**BILL**  
Jeff, somewhere a fifth-grade science teacher just started smoking again and doesn’t know why.

**JEFF**  
Pedagogy requires metaphor. You give a kid “rubble pile nucleus,” they glaze. You give them “ten million Slurpees at orbital velocity,” they look up from their phones and say, “Explain the brain freeze part.” Then you’ve got them. Hook set.

**MARLENE** (to Bill and Jim)  
He always like this?

**BILL**  
He’s… exact.

**JIM**  
It’s useful until it isn’t, then it’s still happening.

**JEFF**  
One more thing—

**BILL**  
Lord, deliver us.

**JEFF**  
When a comet outgasses, it preferentially vents along microfractures—like stress relief in human beings. You pressurize a system; it finds the path of least resistance. We call it self-care at scale. The coma is a boundary layer: inside is identity, outside is performance. The jet is the truth screaming through a crack.

**JIM**  
That’s uncomfortably accurate.

**BILL**  
Now he’s doing therapy with space slush.

**JEFF**  
We anthropomorphize to understand mechanisms. We de-mythologize to measure them. That’s progress: from omens to outgassing rates. From flaming swords in the sky to, “Hey, that’s a low-albedo rubble pile with a spin period of twelve hours and questionable cohesion.”

**JIM**  
What’s the BPM on twelve hours?

**JEFF**  
Point zero zero two eight. Not danceable.

**BILL**  
Negroni hour is danceable. This is bourbon hour. Neat.

**MARLENE** (sets two more)  
Neat, neat.

She looks at Jeff’s water, then at the Slurpee machine in the corner—dark, unplugged, weeping frost like a wounded robot.

**MARLENE**  
Machine’s busted, professor. No frozen sugar tonight.

**JEFF**  
Then the metaphor improves. Comets are unreliable; Slurpee machines fail constantly; the universe agrees with me.

**BILL**  
We’re drinking actual history while you argue with a broken appliance.

**JEFF**  
History argues back. Here’s your kicker: if one of those Slurpees, scaled, hits Earth, it’s not rocky like the movies; it’s a fragile snow-cone with delusions of grandeur. It still wrecks the neighborhood because kinetic energy doesn’t care if your mass arrives as granite or dessert. Same speed, same math, different flavor. Ten million Slurpees at forty thousand mph is just joules with a cherry on top.

**JIM**  
I hate that that’s elegant.

**BILL**  
I hate that I’m starting to taste blue raspberry in my bourbon.

**JEFF**  
That’s synesthesia. Or marketing. Either way, you’re welcome.

**MARLENE** (closing the loop)  
Professor, I got a metaphor for you: if you put ice in their bourbon, you’re the comet and I’m the dinosaur.

**JEFF**  
Understood. Strictly neat.

**BILL** (raises glass)  
To strictly neat.

**JIM**  
To not putting fruit in things that were never meant to have fruit.

They clink. Jeff smiles, finally, like a jet sputtering out after a long vent.

**JEFF**  
Final final thing—

**BILL/JIM**  
No.

**JEFF**  
—comets carry stories. We used to think they predicted doom. Now they predict lab results. That’s a better superstition. Knowing beats guessing. It’s still awe; it just has footnotes.

A beat. Even Bill has to nod at that.

**BILL**  
Marlene, keep them coming.

**JIM**  
And kill the TV. The universe is talking.

**MARLENE** (thumb on remote)  
Honey, the universe never shuts up.

She kills the screen. Neon and bourbon and three men listening to the hum of the Slurpee machine failing to be a comet.
