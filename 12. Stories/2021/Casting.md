# **EXCLUSIVE: The Cast of the Year—Breaking Down the Insane Ensemble of the Rock Drama Everyone Will Be Talking About**

**Variety Staff**

---

Hollywood loves a comeback, but what about a show that promises to break your brain before it even premieres? Enter the untitled rock drama that has assembled an all-star cast for what is already being dubbed “_Succession_ meets _This Is Spinal Tap_—on steroids.” With a cast stacked with Oscar nominees, Emmy winners, and a few curveball celebrity cameos, this is shaping up to be the show of the decade.

### **Paul Dano as Jeff Cremeans**

"I’ve never eaten a guitar string before," Dano reportedly told reporters on set, “but for Jeff, I figured it was time to commit.” Dano, known for his intense dedication, plays the band’s manager, described as the “Yoda of band politicking” by co-writer Quentin Tarantino. Expect Dano to deliver both the quiet gravitas and unhinged moments we’ve come to love him for.

### **Riley Keough as Kathy Espich**

Keough steps into the role of Kathy, the band’s muse, icon, and tragic centerpiece. "We wanted a performer who could make you cry just by drinking coffee," said Steven DeKnight, the show’s co-creator and showrunner. Keough reportedly insisted on singing live for all her scenes, claiming, “If I’m not bleeding out emotionally by the end of the take, it doesn’t count.”

### **Miles Teller as Bill Davenport**

Miles Teller, who plays the band’s brooding multi-instrumentalist, has already made waves with his character prep. “I’ve been learning how to play eight instruments simultaneously, blindfolded,” Teller said during a press junket. “Bill is a perfectionist, so I figured I needed to lose a few marbles in real life to get there.”

### **Joe Keery as Jeremy Evans**

Keery takes on the role of Jeremy, the band’s enigmatic guitarist. When asked about his inspiration, Keery said, “I’ve been shadowing a squirrel in Central Park for the last six weeks. Jeremy’s got that kind of frenetic energy.” Expect Keery to bring both charm and chaos to the screen.

### **Jesse Plemons as Jim Brown**

Plemons reportedly demanded a real drum kit be installed in his trailer for his role as Jim, the band’s drummer. “Drumming is 80% sweat and 20% blind faith,” Plemons said, “and I’ve got both.” He also teased a drum solo in episode six that “will change how you think about time itself.”

### **Tatiana Maslany as Avril Lavigne**

Maslany’s Avril is the wildcard. "I wanted to channel the energy of a raccoon who just found its way into a nightclub," Maslany said. Her character struggles with imposter syndrome and self-destruction. “There’s one scene where I throw a phone into the ocean and then jump in after it—it’s my favorite moment.”

### **Jennifer Connelly as Sarah Malcom**

Jennifer Connelly is Sarah, the band’s high-powered executive who is as brilliant as she is complicated. "Sarah’s a blend of Machiavelli and Cher,” Connelly joked. “She’s not afraid to make enemies—just afraid of not being remembered." Her final scenes are rumored to involve peaches, a gospel choir, and the ghost of Elvis.

### **Adam Driver as Jeff Evans**

Driver, cast as the band’s bassist, brought his signature intensity to the role. "I didn’t sleep for two weeks," Driver claimed. "Jeff is like a Rubik’s cube that’s been thrown into a blender—he’s messy but somehow still solves himself." Tarantino described Driver’s performance as “a masterclass in quiet implosion.”

### **Austin Butler as Gilley**

Butler’s portrayal of Gilley, the band’s frontman, is already drawing comparisons to Jim Morrison and Prince. “Gilley is the kind of guy who makes you want to punch a wall and then write a love song about it,” Butler said. Rumor has it Butler wrote 10 original songs for the show in one weekend, which he performed live at a cast party.

### **Conclusion:**

This cast is more than star-studded—it’s downright chaotic. With Tarantino and DeKnight at the helm, expect a mix of gut-punch drama, rock-and-roll absurdity, and moments that will leave you questioning your sanity. The untitled as yet series is set to premiere in 2025, and if the behind-the-scenes antics are any indication, it’s going to be a wild ride.

Stay tuned, and keep your banjo handy.
