# **The Jitters**

**INT. TOUR BUS – NIGHT

Jeff plucks a quiet tune on his guitar, his face lit by the soft glow of an overhead light. The bus is otherwise still, the band asleep or keeping to themselves. Avril steps in, hesitating for a beat before closing the door behind her.

**AVRIL** (softly)    
Couldn’t sleep?  

**JEFF** (shrugging, without looking up)    
Music helps.  

Avril moves closer, her fingers brushing the counter as she leans against it, watching him.  

**AVRIL**
Funny. I thought I was the only one who’d rather be up at night.  

Jeff chuckles faintly but keeps strumming. Avril moves to sit across from him.  

**AVRIL** (awkward)    
You know, Boston wasn’t just some… thing. It wasn’t nothing.  

Jeff’s strumming falters. He finally looks up, his expression unreadable.  

**JEFF
That was a long time ago, Avril.  

**AVRIL** (leaning forward)    
Doesn’t mean it didn’t mean something.  

She places her hand lightly on his forearm, testing the waters. Jeff looks at her hand, then back at her.  

**JEFF**(slowly)    
You’re part of the band now. It’s different.  

Avril pulls her hand back but doesn’t retreat. Instead, she takes a deep breath and suddenly lifts her hoodie, baring herself in a bold, almost reckless gesture.  

**AVRIL** (stubborn, almost playful)    
Tell me this is different.  

Jeff freezes, his eyes widening in surprise. For a long moment, the only sound is the hum of the bus engine. He gently sets his guitar down and stands, averting his gaze as he grabs a blanket draped over the back of a seat.  

**JEFF** (quietly, kindly)    
You don’t need to do this. You’re better than this.  

He hands her the blanket, his tone firm but not unkind. Avril quickly pulls her hoodie back down, her face burning with humiliation.  

**AVRIL** (biting)    
Guess I read that wrong.  

She storms out, but not before Jeff calls after her.  

**JEFF**
Avril—don’t let this ruin what you’ve got here.  

She hesitates for a moment, then disappears down the hall. Jeff exhales heavily, running a hand through his hair before returning to his guitar.

# The First Live Performance

---
**Setting**: Ohio Stadium, halftime at the Ohio State Spring Practice game. Over 102,000 fans fill the stands as Avril takes the stage with Commuted for their first public appearance. The tension is crushing, and Avril struggles to find her footing.  

---

**EXT. OHIO STADIUM TEMP CONCERT SET – DAY

The roaring crowd creates a deafening wall of sound as Avril steps onto the stage with Commuted. The spring sunshine bounces off her leather jacket and silver accessories, but her confidence falters as she takes in the sea of fans.  

**ANNOUNCER** (over stadium speakers)    
For the first time in six year, ladies and gentlemen, from Clark County ***O-H! 

**CROWD** (in unison)
***I-O!

**ANNOUNCER**
 …give it up for Commuted!  

The band launches into their opening song. Avril’s voice wavers on the first few lines, her nerves evident in her stiff posture and awkward movements.  

**CAMERA SHOTS:    

- A wide shot of the packed stadium, emphasizing the overwhelming scale.    
- Close-ups of Avril glancing nervously at the band, her hand gripping the microphone tightly.    
- Shots of the crowd, some engaged but others looking distracted or unimpressed.  

As the first song ends, Avril takes a deep breath, gripping the mic stand as the band transitions into the second track. Jeff leans over to her during the instrumental intro.  

**JEFF** (low, encouraging)    
*Loosen up. You’ve got this.  

Avril nods but doesn’t respond. Her voice cracks slightly on the first verse, and a flicker of frustration crosses her face.  

By the chorus, something clicks. She closes her eyes, losing herself in the music. Her movements grow freer, her voice stronger.  

**CAMERA SHOTS**

- A close-up of Avril’s face as she belts out a note, her confidence visibly growing.    
- Fans in the crowd start cheering louder, some holding up their phones to record.    
- A wide shot of the band playing under the massive stadium lights.  

By the end of the set, the crowd roars in approval. But as the band leaves the stage, Avril lags behind, her shoulders slumping.  

**INT. BACKSTAGE – DAY**

The band huddles backstage, congratulating each other. Avril, however, sits on a crate, staring at the floor.  

**BILL**
You sounded shitty out there.  

**AVRIL** (shaking her head)    
I botched it. First song was a mess.  

**JEFF** (sitting beside her)    
You kept going. That’s what matters.  

**AVRIL** (bitterly)    
Yeah, because I didn’t have a choice.  

Jeff places a hand on her shoulder, but Avril shrugs it off, standing abruptly.  

**AVRIL**
I’ll do better next time.  

She storms off, leaving Jeff watching her go.
