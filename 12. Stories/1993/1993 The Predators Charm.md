# **EXT. TCL CHINESE THEATRE – HOLLYWOOD BOULEVARD – NIGHT – MAY 19, 1993**

Hollywood pulses under halogen and hype. Flashbulbs pop like fireworks. A red carpet runs like a lifeline into the mouth of the mythic *Chinese Theatre*. Above it, a gaudy banner flaps in the breeze:  **“DARK VENGEANCE II: BLOOD RECKONING – WORLD PREMIERE.”**

The crowd surges against the barricades. Everyone’s screaming for someone.

**CAMERA FINDS: KATHY ESPICH, 22.**

She stands just outside the velvet rope — detached, mildly irritated, and inconveniently hot in an Army jacket and steel-toe Docs. She’s not here for the spectacle. She’s here because someone told her to show up. Maybe her manager. Maybe fate.

A laminated pass dangles from her lanyard:  **KATHY ESPICH – “COMMUTED” – 69 REASONS TOUR.**

The debut album sold ten million copies.  And still —  **To most people here, she’s “that chick from that band with five other guys.”**  The one whose lyrics cut deeper than they should.  The one whose stare lasts too long.  The one who didn’t smile in Rolling Stone.

**But if you know music — you know Kathy.**

**THE CROWD’S ENERGY SHIFTS.**

A limo door opens. Screams spike. A publicist hisses into a walkie. Another B-lister climbs out like a borrowed suit on legs. Behind her, someone whistles.  **MID-TIER HOLLYWOOD ACTOR (30s),** golden tan, reptile charisma.  
He sidles up like he owns gravity.

**ACTOR**  (Grinning)  
You’re either lost… or playing it way too cool.

Kathy doesn’t turn. Just takes the cigarette from behind her ear and lights it, watching the chaos play out in chrome reflections on a limousine door.

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|KATHY]]**  (Flat)  
You think you’re the first guy to say that to me this week?

He laughs. Offers a flask. She doesn’t take it. Not yet.

**WIDE SHOT:**

The theatre glows like a temple. The dragons grin.  Tonight’s premiere isn’t on the screen. It’s in the shadows.

# **EXT. TCL CHINESE THEATRE – NIGHT – CONTINUOUS**

**ENTER: DEAN RADLEY (35).**  
Tan, tousled, teeth too white. He was the bad boy love interest in *Pacific Beat* — that syndicated cop show your cousin used to tape off TV. He’s done three straight-to-video thrillers since, one cologne ad in Europe, and a full decade of charming his way into afterparties he shouldn’t be invited to.  His leather jacket hangs open over a sheer mesh shirt. He smells like clove cigarettes and money that isn’t his.

**DEAN**  (Half smile)  
So you’re the girl from “69 Reasons.”   Thought you’d be taller.

**KATHY**  (Without looking)  
Thought you’d be less… damp.

Dean wipes his forehead theatrically.

**DEAN**  
It’s the flashbulbs. They trigger a gland. Or maybe it’s you.  Honestly, I was hoping you’d show up solo.

Kathy exhales slowly, cigarette pinched tight between fingers. Her eyes finally meet his. They’re unimpressed. And curious. A dangerous mix.

**KATHY**  
You do this at every premiere, or just the ones with dragons?

**DEAN**  
Only the ones with legends.

He offers a flask, embossed with a silver scorpion.

**DEAN (CONT'D)**  
To mutual anonymity. For now.

She hesitates.  Not because she trusts him — but because she doesn’t.  She takes the flask. Takes a sip.  Returns it with surgical precision.

**KATHY**  
Tastes like ego and mouthwash.

**DEAN**  
Perfect. Just like me.

**ANGLE WIDENS:**

They’re swallowed by the chaos. Cameras flash. A dozen influencers-in-training shriek over some sitcom kid.  But in this corner, under the dragon’s stone claws —  two predators circle, just slow enough to look like flirting.

# **EXT. TCL CHINESE THEATRE – NIGHT – UNDER THE MARQUEE**

The noise blurs. Flashbulbs. Screams. The scrape of expensive heels on concrete.

**KATHY** and **DEAN** stand in a pocket of quiet carved from chaos. Just outside the red carpet. Just outside the safe zone.

Dean leans against the rope stanchion like he owns it. He’s not the type to ask permission.

**DEAN**  
You know, your voice is different in person.  Lower. Smokier.  Like you gargle with secrets.

**KATHY**  (flat)  
Must be the chlorine in the fame pool.

He laughs — easy, warm, too well-practiced. Then reaches into his jacket and pulls out the flask again. He doesn’t say anything this time. Just extends it. A casual offering. A dare.

**KATHY** watches it like it’s a loaded gun. Her eyes flick from the flask to his eyes — unreadable, reptilian, maybe kind. Maybe. She hesitates. A long moment. Then…She takes it.  One sip. Small. Controlled. She hands it back without a word. Dean raises his eyebrows, just a tick.

**DEAN**  
Well damn.  You *do* play guitar with your teeth.

# **EXT. TCL CHINESE THEATRE – RED CARPET EDGE – NIGHT**

Dean follows Kathy toward the mouth of the madness, always one step behind, like a shadow with perfect hair. The red carpet flashes alive — a swarm of cameras shifts. For a split second, all eyes swing toward *her*.

**A REPORTER** lunges forward — microphone out, smile plastic.

**REPORTER**  
Kathy Espich! *Kathy*!  One quick one — just one!

She freezes, more startled than flattered. A PA already motioning for her to move on.

**REPORTER (CONT’D)**  
Why didn’t you dress up?

**CAMERA PUSHES IN**  
Kathy in the army jacket, Docs, raw as hell. Hair barely brushed, cigarette barely out.

She smiles. Just a little.

**KATHY**  
I did. This is me dressed up.

The cameras *eat it up*. Flashbulbs detonate. A couple gasps. A producer somewhere takes notes. Behind her, **Dean** leans in close. Too close.  He brushes something off her shoulder — invisible lint, imagined dust.  His hand lingers an extra beat. Too smooth. Too casual. She doesn’t stop him. But she notices. So do we.

**DEAN**  (grinning low)  
Whole damn carpet just shifted when you stepped on it.  You always do that?

**KATHY**  (mutters)  
Not on purpose.

**CAMERA HOLDS**  
On her shoulder, now clean. On the place his hand just was.  On the part of her that knows it’s starting — whatever *it* is — and isn’t quite sure if she likes it.

But she’s not stopping it, either.

# **INT. SERVICE ELEVATOR – CHINESE THEATRE – LATER THAT NIGHT**

Metal walls. Muffled bass pulses from somewhere above. The light flickers like it's trying to escape.

**KATHY** leans against the back corner — arms crossed, chin down, uncomfortable in her own fame.

**DEAN RADLEY** stands across from her. Relaxed. Smug. Beautiful in a way that makes people lie to themselves.

**DEAN**  
You really don’t do this kind of thing, huh?

**KATHY**  
What gave it away — the combat boots or the existential dread?

He smirks. Checks his watch like he’s not checking her out. The elevator *dings*.

# **EXT. ROOFTOP – PRIVATE PARTY – MOMENTS LATER**

Hollywood spreads out in diamonds and fog. The rooftop is sleek, cruel, and curated. Lights strung above like star substitutes. Chillwave plays from hidden speakers. Waiters move like ghosts with trays of neon cocktails.

**CAMERA SWEEPS:**

Models. Agents. Musicians too famous to care. A guy in a mesh shirt is making out with a girl in a wedding dress. Nobody bats an eye. And in the middle of it all — **KATHY**, stunned silent. She wasn’t supposed to come. Didn’t RSVP. Didn’t plan this. She looks like she’s wandered into a dream someone else paid for.

**DEAN (O.S.)**  
You okay?

She doesn’t answer right away. Just watches a drone hover overhead like a tiny god.

**KATHY**  
This isn’t real.

**DEAN** (smiling)  
That’s the point.

# **EXT. ROOFTOP – PRIVATE PARTY – CONTINUOUS**

Kathy edges toward the staircase — the way back to the screening below. Her laminated pass swings against her chest like a compass.

**KATHY**  
I actually wanted to see the movie.

Dean steps into her path. Not blocking. Just… present.

**DEAN**  
Come on, Espich. This is the movie.  The schmoozing. The name-dropping. The half-sincere compliments with real consequences.  This *is* Hollywood.

**KATHY** (flat)  
I make records. I don’t do whatever this is.

Dean grins — goddamn perfect teeth, the kind that deflect guilt.

**DEAN**  
That’s why they love you. You’re real. Untouched. Pure indie girl energy.  But babe — you sold *ten million*. You’re one of them now. So go mingle.  You’ll have a ball. We’ll have a lot of fun tonight.

He says it like a promise and a threat rolled in silk. Kathy hesitates. She glances at the party — it’s a zoo of power. Beautiful predators in borrowed skin.

Dean presses a glass into her hand. It’s not hers. It’s not clear what it is. But it’s cold. It’s fizzy. It tastes like confidence. She sips. Just one.  Just enough to blur the edges of the noise.

**KATHY (softly)**  
I didn’t plan to be here.

**DEAN (smiling too much)**  
That’s how the best nights start.

# **EXT. ROOFTOP – LATER**

Time doesn’t pass here. It slurs. **KATHY** moves through the crowd like a tourist in her own dream. Every face she sees — she’s seen before. Sitcom dads. Oscar nominees. Women who used to be on *Baywatch*. Men who definitely were. Everyone’s skin glows like it’s been lit from within.

**RANDOM ACTRESS (smiling, too perfect)**  
Oh my *God* — are you that girl from Commuted?

**KATHY**  
Yeah. Uh, Kathy.

**ACTRESS**  
You guys are *sick*. We should do something. Like… collab. Or lunch. My team’ll call your team?

Before Kathy can respond, someone pinches her ass and shouts:

**SOME GUY (O.S.)**  
Rock 'n’ roll, baby!

**SLAP —** a high five from a vaguely familiar face, sunglasses at night, reeking of cologne and self-regard.

She’s laughing now. Not because it’s funny — because it’s all *too much*. Her brain’s running behind her body like a bad dub.

**FRAGMENTS OF DIALOGUE SWIRL AROUND HER:**

>*“Love your vibe—it’s so raw.”*  
>“*You should meet Lucas. He did sound on The Abyss.”*  
>“*You’re so small in person!”*  
>“*I’m serious — we’ll do something big.”*

She sips again. The drink’s almost gone. Or maybe it’s a new one.

**The camera floats. Focuses on her eyes — glassy, flickering, unsure.**

**DEAN REAPPEARS.**  
He’s close. Too close. His hand grazes her lower back — casually claiming her. He leans down.

**DEAN**  
You okay?

**KATHY**  (half-laugh)  
Everyone knows who I am and I have no idea who the hell *they* are.

**DEAN**  (smiling)  
You’re famous now. Get used to it.

He tucks a strand of hair behind her ear. She doesn’t flinch. But she doesn’t smile either.

**KATHY**  
Where’s the exit?

**DEAN**  (soothing)  
Come on. Let’s find someplace quieter.

He steers her — gently, expertly — toward a shadowed corner of the rooftop.  The lights get dimmer.  The music fades.  The crowd peels away.

# **INT. HOTEL SUITE – NIGHT – LATE**

The city glitters far below, an ocean of headlights and neon. Floor-to-ceiling glass casts ghost-reflections across the room.  Inside: shadows, soft jazz, room service leftovers. The minibar is open and clearly wounded.

**KATHY** is on the couch, sprawled sideways. Her boots are off. Her jacket’s draped over a lamp.  She’s laughing — not politely. Not performatively. *Honestly*.

**DEAN** is beside her, shirt open, perfectly rumpled. He’s got one hand around a tumbler and the other casually around her. They’ve been kissing. That much is clear. Now they’re kissing again. Sloppier this time. Hungrier.

**KATHY** pushes him back playfully. Her laugh gets tangled in his mouth.

**KATHY**  
You have *no* idea what I’m saying right now, do you?

**DEAN**  (smiling)  
Not a clue. Still want to hear it.

He kisses her neck. His hand slides over her shirt. Then under. Then full on her breast.

**KATHY** (half-laughs, half-protests)  
God, your hands are *everywhere*.

She doesn’t stop him. But her smile slips. Just a bit. She blinks hard. The room tilts. Her glass is empty, or maybe it was never full.

**KATHY (V.O.)**  
I knew where I was. Mostly.  I knew who I was with.  I just didn’t know what time it was.  Or when I stopped keeping track.

**FLASH —** her reflection in the glass door: smeared lipstick, hair wild, eyes too wide.

**DEAN**  
Bedroom’s better.

He stands. Offers his hand.  She takes it.  But slower now.  Like her body knows something her brain’s ignoring.

**CAMERA HOLDS ON THE GLASS DOOR.**

They pass out of frame.  The skyline flickers outside.  The lights dim.  The blur begins.

# **INT. HOTEL SUITE – DAWN**

A thick silence blankets the room. The city is pale and colorless beyond the windows, bleached by early morning.

**CAMERA MOVES SLOWLY — LIKE BREATH HELD TOO LONG.**

**Lipstick** is smeared across a pillow. A crimson smear like a wound.  **KATHY** lies on the bed.

**Still.**

**Stark naked.**

One leg half-curled. One arm draped over her stomach like a shield. Hair tangled across her face.  The sheets are twisted. The comforter is on the floor. A lamp has been knocked over. The minibar is a graveyard of little bottles. A heel sits on top of a room service tray like it belongs there.

**Her clutch is upside down** on the carpet, its contents scattered — a tampon, a crumpled receipt, her ID flipped face-down.  There are *no sounds*. No dialogue. Just the faint hum of the city seeping through the windows.

**KATHY’S EYES ARE OPEN.**

Unblinking. Red-rimmed. Empty.  Her hands are clenched.  Her lips are parted slightly — not breathing heavy. Just *being*.  Like her body hasn’t caught up to the fact that the night is over.

**CAMERA HOLDS. LONG.**

No music. No monologue. Just the wreckage. A beautiful room, beautifully ruined. And a woman, ruined with it.

# **INT. HOTEL SUITE – CONTINUOUS**

**CLOSE-UP: KATHY’S EYES.**

Open. Unfocused. Trying to swim up through syrup.  She blinks — once, twice — like she’s unsure what’s real. Her pupils don’t track right away. There’s no fear. No anger. Just… *dislocation.*

**CAMERA TRACKS DOWN —**

Her **hands** are clenched into fists. White-knuckle tight. Fingernails digging into palms.  
She doesn’t move. Not yet. She just breathes. Shallow. Measured. Like she’s checking if her lungs still work.

**A SMALL SHUDDER.**  
Not from cold. Not from fear. Just a reflex. A slow understanding, somewhere below consciousness, begins to take shape.  She shifts, just slightly. The sheet clings. Something inside her recoils. Not visibly. Internally. A recoil the body knows before the brain confirms it.

Her breath hitches. Eyes still open. Still not crying. Still not speaking.  The room is quiet. Too quiet.

**CAMERA STAYS CLOSE — HER FACE. HER HANDS. HER EYES.**  
The story is there.  And it’s saying everything. She swallows. Hard. Not from thirst. From *knowing*.

# **INT. HOTEL SUITE – MINUTES LATER**

**CAMERA WIDE. STILL.**  
Kathy shifts. Slowly. Carefully. Like her limbs don’t quite belong to her. She pushes herself upright with a grunt of effort. Not pain — *weight.* The sheet falls away.

**CAMERA FOLLOWS FROM BEHIND —** catching only her **back and bare buttocks** in soft silhouette as she stands. Bruises aren’t shown. They’re *assumed*. There’s no linger. No titillation. Just a naked back and a terrible quiet.

She stumbles. One foot, then the other. Unsteady. Her knee buckles slightly. She catches the wall. The **bathroom light** flickers on. Harsh. White. Surgical. 

She disappears inside.

# **INT. HOTEL BATHROOM – CONTINUOUS**

The light buzzes overhead.

**KATHY** is already halfway into her jeans — awkward, clumsy. She pulls her shirt over her head with trembling hands.

Her **face in the mirror** is barely recognizable.

Hair matted. Lipstick smeared to her chin. Eyes shot with red and heavy from too much something. Her mascara has migrated. Her eyeliner's gone. Her collarbone has a red mark she doesn’t remember earning. She just stares. She touches her cheek. Then her lips. Then grips the edge of the sink with both hands.

**SOUND: The water runs. Too loud. Too fast.**

She splashes her face, over and over — like it might erase whatever happened before the mirror. A few breaths. Deep. Shaky. Almost sobs — but not quite. She dries her hands on the hotel towel. Then takes out her **lipstick** — the same one from before — and throws it in the trash with sharp precision.

**CAMERA HOLDS ON THE MIRROR:**

A woman looking at a version of herself she didn’t choose. No tears. No rage. Just **silence**.
And what comes after.

# **EXT. RURAL CLARK COUNTY, OHIO – DAY**

It’s simple.

Wood floors. A twin bed with a faded quilt. Posters on the wall — REM, Patti Smith, and one curling Polaroid of the band taped crooked near the mirror.

**CAMERA MOVES SLOWLY.**

On the floor: an open **guitar case**. Empty. Velvet lining torn in places, strings curled like silver snakes in one corner.

**KATHY** walks through frame. Cigarette already lit. Bare feet. Wrinkled t-shirt. Jeans that have seen better days. She steps over the case without looking down. Doesn’t pause. Doesn’t comment. Just moves through the quiet like she belongs to it.

**CAMERA HOLDS — LONG.**

The fields outside shimmer with heat and distance. The wind carries the promise of October. Kathy exhales smoke. And walks out of frame.
