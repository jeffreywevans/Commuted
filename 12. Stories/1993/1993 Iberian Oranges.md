# **IBERIAN ORANGES**

---

# **CHAPTER ONE**

---


## **INT. RADIO MADRID – NIGHT SHIFT STUDIO – SPRING 1993**

A modest, smoky studio cluttered with stickers, coffee cups, and magnetic tape reels. The red ON AIR light glows above a glass window. A faint hum of synths plays under a commercial jingle just fading out.

**DJ MARIA** (late 30s, chain-smoking, chic in a chaotic way) leans into the microphone with a practiced grin. Across from her sits **KATHY** ESPICH**, 22, sleepless but radiant—leather jacket unzipped, heels kicked off under the console, legs curled under her. Her eyeliner is slightly smudged; she hasn’t slept in two days. She looks both over it and wide awake.

**DJ MARIA** (in Spanish-accented English)

And now—our most asked question of the week… yet again… Kathy, cariño, I am legally obligated to ask you: What does “*Come Inside Me*” really mean?

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|KATHY]]** (sighs, smiles without warmth)
God, is this number thirty-six?

**DJ MARIA**
Thirty-six? That’s just this tour. That’s not even counting the BBC or Holland.

**KATHY**
Should’ve named it Please Talk About This One Specific Line Instead of the Song as a Whole.

**DJ MARIA** (laughs)
Too long for a vinyl sleeve.

**KATHY**
So was the bridge solo. We still put it in.

A pause. Maria gestures gently, encouraging a “real” answer. Kathy leans into the mic, voice low and a little wicked.

**KATHY**
It’s about being ruined. Willingly. Physically, emotionally, all of it. Not just sex—it’s the decision to stop defending yourself. To say, “yeah, this might end me… and I’m gonna let it.”

She pauses.

**KATHY**
Also—it’s hot. Sometimes that’s enough.

**DJ MARIA**
Spain agrees. It’s currently \#1 in Barcelona and… Valencia and… well, everywhere.

**KATHY**
Tell Valencia I said hi and that I hope they used protection.

They both laugh.

**DJ MARIA**
One last question—how long did it actually take to record?

**KATHY**
Eleven minutes, thirty-six seconds.

**DJ MARIA**
Exactly?

**KATHY** (grinning wildly)
That’s how long he lasted.

Maria chokes on a laugh and slaps the desk. Kathy just grins, eyes gleaming under fluorescent light, already bracing for question thirty-seven.

---

## **INT. RADIO MADRID – CONTINUOUS**

The red ON AIR light dims. **DJ MARIA** slides a Mecano track onto the queue—something breezy and synthy, a tonal palette cleanser. She flips off her mic and leans back, exhaling dramatically.

**DJ MARIA**
Okay. You survived the thirty-sixth *Come Inside Me* inquisition. God bless you.

**KATHY**
They’ll ask again in Milan. They always ask in Milan.

**DJ MARIA**
Of course they do. They think they invented sex. So! Since you’re stuck here till the next train to Lisbon, I get to quiz you now.

Kathy raises an eyebrow, amused, playful.

**KATHY**
Is this like a pop quiz or a soul excavation?

**DJ MARIA**
Mm. Little of both. First question. When did you realize you could seduce an entire room just by whispering?

**KATHY**
Fourteen. Catholic lock-in. They brought acoustic guitars. I brought eyeliner and a grudge.

**DJ MARIA** (laughs)
Of course you did. Okay—next: Is Gilley as much of a disaster as he seems?

**KATHY**
Worse. But he’s our disaster. He plays like he’s being electrocuted by sex and jazz at the same time.

**DJ MARIA**
Perfect description. Third question: Which Commuted song do you actually think is the most dangerous?

**KATHY**
Not *Come Inside Me*. That one’s performance art. It’s “*Xmas Party*.” People don’t realize… that one’s true. Like, every word. You write something that dirty and real and suddenly people flinch.

**DJ MARIA**
Because they see themselves in it?

**KATHY**
Because they see what they did and didn’t own up to.

They pause. The song fades down in the background.

**DJ MARIA**
Okay, last one. Do you think you’re going to survive this ride you’re on?

Kathy doesn’t answer right away. She looks down at her chipped black nail polish, thumb tapping a steady rhythm against her boot.

**KATHY**
I’ll survive. But parts of me won’t.

She looks up, grinning like she just remembered it’s all a game anyway.

**KATHY**
That’s the price of being loud and soft. You get to burn twice as bright—and twice as fast.

The red ON AIR light flicks back on. Maria adjusts her headphones, a little softer now.

**DJ MARIA**
Ladies and gentlemen…That was Kathy Espich, blowing minds and burning down rules since 1992. Up next—La Unión, but don’t go anywhere. She’s not done yet.

Kathy leans toward the mic.

**KATHY**
Madrid, I hope you finish harder than your DJs do.

Maria facepalms. Fade to music.

---

## **EXT. RADIO MADRID – NIGHT – SPRING 1993**

Kathy steps out into the cooling Spanish air, the neon sign of Radio Madrid buzzing above her. She’s half in shadow, half under streetlight, her body language tight—like someone holding a scream between her teeth.

**JIM** BROWN** is already leaning against the rental car, chewing on a toothpick, drumstick in one hand like it never leaves him.

He opens the passenger door for her. She slides in without a word.

---

## **INT. RENTAL CAR – MOVING – NIGHT**

Madrid flickers past in streaks of yellow and orange. Kathy has her forehead pressed against the window, arms crossed so tight it looks like she’s bracing for impact.

**KATHY**
They asked about it again.

**JIM**
Yeah?

**KATHY**
Again.

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]]**
Well, it is kind of a—

**KATHY**
Thirty-sixth time, Jim. On this tour alone. We’ve got—what—twenty-one songs on *69 Reasons*, and every single fucking interview is “so what’s it like to get railed by your bandmate on an eleven-minute synth loop?”

Jim glances over. Calm, measured. Knows when to let her burn.

**KATHY**
You know what no one asks about? *“The Left Hand.”* Or “*Tangled Up*.” Or that track [[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill]] wrote about that girl in Bulgaria who made him cry on a church bench. No. It’s always me and [[4.3 **JEFF** EVANS CHARACTER BIBLE|Jeff]] fucking. Like I’m some walking climax with eyeliner.

**JIM**
Well…

(grins)
You kind of made it catchy.

Kathy whips around.

**KATHY**
You’re not helping.

**JIM**
I’m not trying to help. I’m trying to remind you. You wrote the damn spell, Kath. You said “*Come Inside Me*” like a prayer. People heard it, and they believed you. That’s power.

Kathy doesn’t speak for a moment. She bites her thumbnail, looking down at her lap.

**KATHY**
I just didn’t think power would be this… predictable.

**JIM**
It never is. But it still beats invisibility, right?

Beat. She nods. Barely.

**JIM**
Let ‘em talk. You’ve got more where that came from. Next album, you’ll bury ‘em in metaphors so deep they’ll need a priest and a pickaxe.

Kathy chuckles, finally. A tired, throaty thing. She leans her head back against the seat.

**KATHY**
Promise me something?

**JIM**
What’s that?

**KATHY**
If I ever get famous only for how I moan on tape…Shoot me in the leg and make me tour solo with a tambourine.

**JIM**
Fair. But I’m keeping the rights to the bootleg.

They both laugh. The car rolls quietly through the streets, the skyline catching the last glow of another loud, brilliant day in the life of Commuted.

---

## **INT. RENTAL CAR – NIGHT – MADRID**

They round another corner. The streets are narrower now, less neon, more cobblestone. The hotel is nowhere in sight.

**JIM**
I think I’m lost.

**KATHY** (groans)
Jim, we’ve been in this city for four days.

**JIM**
Yeah, and I’ve been high for three and a half of them.

**KATHY**
Don’t you have a paper map somewhere?

**JIM**
Yeah—

(rummaging in the glove box)
—but the signs are in Spanish, Kathy.

He pulls out a crumpled, half-folded street map of Madrid, held together by spite and gum wrappers. He slaps it open against the steering wheel, squinting.

**KATHY**
You’re holding it upside down.

**JIM**
No I’m not.

Madrid’s just that disorienting. I swear the streets move at night.

**KATHY**
Give me that.

She grabs the map, rotates it, traces a route with her finger.

**KATHY**
Okay, see? You missed the turn at Calle del Pez. Now we’re in some… I don’t know, haunted suburb of tapas and despair.

**JIM**
Sounds like the name of our next album.

**KATHY**
Only if I get to write about anything other than Jeff’s orgasm this time.

They pass a sign that says Calle del Desengaño.

**JIM**
What’s that mean?

**KATHY**
“Street of Disillusionment.”

**JIM**
…yeah. That feels about right.

**KATHY**
Pull over before we end up in Portugal.

Jim swings the car into a quiet alley beside a shuttered bakery.

**JIM**
If we die out here, tell the coroner my last meal was hamón ibérico and sexual frustration.

**KATHY**
That’s also going on your tombstone.

**JIM**
And yours?

**KATHY**
“She meant it.”

They both sit in the silence for a moment.

Then Jim:

**JIM**
Alright. You drive.

Kathy grins, already climbing over the center console.

---

## **INT. RENTAL CAR – NIGHT – MADRID – CONTINUOUS**

Kathy slides into the driver’s seat with a smooth confidence that’s half instinct, half vengeance. She adjusts the mirror with a theatrical flick.

**KATHY**
Buckle up, Ringo. I’m about to make this city my bitch.

Jim, mildly offended, clicks his seatbelt.

**JIM**
Hey, I got us close.

**KATHY**
You got us within whispering distance of a monastery and a laundromat. That’s not close, that’s the scenic route to shame.

She floors it in reverse for just a second, swings the car around, and makes two tight, deliberate turns like she’s been driving Madrid for years.

Suddenly, the street opens up.

**KATHY**
Calle de Arenal. Boom.

**JIM**
…that was a guess.

**KATHY**
That was divine intervention, sweetheart. And also, I can read a goddamn map.

**JIM**
Oh yeah? Fluent in Spanish now?

**KATHY**
No, but I am fluent in Not Being a Lost Man Who Refuses to Ask for Directions.

Jim grins despite himself.

**JIM**
You’re never gonna let me live this down, are you?

**KATHY**
I’m gonna write a bridge section about this.

**JIM**
Please don’t.

**KATHY**
It’ll rhyme “plaza” with “dumbass-a.”

**JIM**
Jesus.

She takes one hand off the wheel, flicks her hair back like a villain in a noir film, eyes on the road and smug as sin.

**KATHY**
Face it, Brown. I drive the band, I drive the van, I drive the verse…and now I’ve driven your sorry ass home.

They cruise onto the hotel street, headlights cutting through the early-morning haze. Kathy slows to a purr and eases into the loading zone like she owns it.

Jim exhales.

**JIM**
…okay. That was kind of hot.

**KATHY**
Everything I do is kind of hot.

She kills the engine. Silence. Then:

**KATHY**
That’s forty bucks. Navigation fee.

**JIM**
Charge it to the label.

---

# **CHAPTER TWO**

---

## **EXT. HOTEL RITZ MADRID – NIGHT – 1:43 A.M.**

The rental car screeches softly into the valet lane, headlights off, engine still ticking as the heat settles.

Standing just outside the glass doors, arms crossed like a statue of judgment in a blazer and combat boots: SARAH MALCOLM, 25, Island Records publicist, PR coordinator, and professional herder of musical chaos.

She looks like she’s been up for three days. She has been up for three days.

**[[5.3 Sarah Malcolm|SARAH]]** (deadpan)
Well, well, look what the fuckin’ moonlight dragged in.

Kathy and Jim climb out of the car like high schoolers caught sneaking in at dawn. Kathy tosses her hair; Jim shrugs like this is all very normal.

**KATHY**
We got lost.  Technically, he got lost.  I just—survived it.

Sarah looks Kathy up and down. Then at Jim. Then back to Kathy.

**SARAH**
I have three morning shows, two magazine shoots, and an FM radio segment that wants to film your face while you pretend to like churros. And all I ask—all I ask—is that the two public faces of Commuted not disappear into the Spanish underworld for four hours.

**KATHY**
You said I could disappear for two.

**SARAH**
It’s not a sexcapade, Kathy. It’s a press tour.

**KATHY**
Well then you shouldn’t have put me in a band with someone who can’t find a hotel and someone else who makes the press think I’m the second coming of Venus in fishnets.

Sarah sighs, pinches the bridge of her nose.

**SARAH**
You are hot-looking. Just own it. Spain loves a hot woman. I could get you on a stamp right now.

**KATHY**
I’m not hot. I’m haunted-looking. There’s a difference.

**JIM**
She’s got a haunted hotness. Like a sexy ghost who died in a music video.

**KATHY**
Thank you, Jim.

**SARAH**
I’m not doing this with you. Tomorrow—and I mean tomorrow—you are sitting down with TVE, El País, Radio Nacional, and some morning show that plays synthwave over weather maps. You’re wearing eyeliner, not irony, and you’re going to smile like you like being famous.

**KATHY**
I don’t like being famous.

**SARAH**
Too late. Spain does.

Kathy groans, leans against the car like she’s about to slide down into asphalt.

**KATHY**
Can’t you send Gilley? Gilley likes attention.

**SARAH**
Gilley is currently passed out in a bathtub full of oranges and wearing someone else’s passport. So no.

Jim snorts.

**SARAH**
Both of you—go upstairs. Hydrate. Pretend you didn’t spend the last four hours quoting Springsteen lyrics and missing exits. I’ll see you in the lobby at 8:00. Sharp. With clothes. Preferably ones that don’t smell like a drum riser.

Kathy starts to protest.

**SARAH**
Don’t.

Kathy salutes her with a wink. Jim follows, dragging the crumpled map behind him like a dead pet.

Sarah stands in the quiet, watching them go.

**SARAH** (muttering)
Fucking artists. Fucking Spain. I need a raise. Or a lobotomy.

She lights a cigarette with the flame of resignation and exhales toward the moon.

---
## **INT. HOTEL RITZ MADRID – SARAH’S SUITE – NIGHT**

The door clicks open.

SARAH MALCOLM steps in like a woman entering her own funeral—briefcase swinging, heels in hand, hair frazzled, and soul fully combusted. She dumps her bag on the desk with enough force to knock over a lamp.

**SARAH**
Jesus Christ, it’s like babysitting a sex cult.

She kicks the door shut behind her.

From the other room comes the unmistakable splash of water. Then—

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]** (offscreen, cheerfully)
Hey, babe! You’re late.

**SARAH**
No. You’re early. Or unconscious. I haven’t decided yet.

She crosses into the suite’s dimly lit bathroom—and stops cold.

---

## **INT. BATHROOM – CONTINUOUS**

**GILLEY**, naked as sin and twice as smug, is lounging in the bathtub like a Roman senator on sabbatical. The tub is littered with orange peels. Whole oranges bob in the water like lost souls. There’s a small radio on the edge of the sink playing Spanish bolero.

**GILLEY**
You know, the Spanish do citrus better than the Californians. These are tight. Acidic. Sexy. Like you when you’re yelling.

Sarah just stares. No words.

**GILLEY**
Want one?

He lifts an orange slice toward her mouth with theatrical flair, water dripping from his wrist like a renaissance painting gone horribly wrong.

**SARAH**
You’re eating oranges in my bathtub.

**GILLEY**
Mm-hmm. Saved you the best one. It’s heart-shaped. Kind of.

He holds it out. It is not heart-shaped. It’s been chewed.

**SARAH**
Gilley, I swear to God, if you flooded the minibar again I’m billing your father.

**GILLEY**
You’re so tense. Sit on the edge. Tell me about the children. Did Kathy seduce another radio station?

**SARAH**
They got lost. She’s homicidal. Jim’s directionally dyslexic. You—you are a sentient liability soaking in citrus.

**GILLEY**
I’m an artist in recovery.

**SARAH**
Recovery from what?

**GILLEY**
Publicity.

He slurps another orange slice, grinning like he invented temptation. Sarah exhales hard, dragging her hands down her face.

**SARAH**
I hate this band. I love this band. I need wine. I need—Jesus—I need rules.

**GILLEY**
No you don’t.  You need a hot bath and someone who knows how to peel fruit with his teeth.

He winks.

Sarah turns. Walks back out. Slams the door behind her.

**GILLEY** (calling after her)
I’ll save you a slice!

Beat.

From inside the bathroom:

**GILLEY**
Or… at least a peel.

---

## **INT. HOTEL RITZ MADRID – SARAH’S SUITE – NIGHT – 25 MINUTES LATER**

The door opens again with a soft click.

SARAH MALCOLM enters—this time slower. Quieter. She’s barefoot. In her hands: an unopened bottle of Rioja and two mismatched wine glasses stolen from the lounge downstairs. Her blazer is gone. Her hair’s been tied up—sloppily, rebelliously.

She closes the door behind her. Locks it.

---

## **INT. BATHROOM – CONTINUOUS**

The oranges have dwindled. The water’s gone lukewarm. **GILLEY** still floats, looking more aquatic than human, one knee breaching the surface, a slice of orange perched on his forehead like a lazy crown.

He hears her before he sees her.

**GILLEY**
You came back.

**SARAH**
Don’t make it a thing.

**GILLEY**
Everything’s a thing with you.

She sets the bottle on the vanity with a deliberate clink. Places the glasses beside it. Her hands move methodically now—unbuttoning her blouse. Sliding it off. Tossing it over the back of a chair. She peels away the day like a layer of armor.

Next, her slacks. Folded neatly. Placed on the counter.

She is left in her workplace undergarments—practical black bra, mid-rise panties. Cotton. No lace. No seduction. Just function. The kind of underwear chosen by someone who didn’t think tonight would involve decisions.

She doesn’t blush. She doesn’t stall.

**SARAH**
Pour it.

**GILLEY**
You sure?

**SARAH**
I’m not here to be sure. I’m here to shut my brain off.

**GILLEY**
Cheers to that.

He leans up, dripping water onto the tile as he grabs the bottle and uncorks it with surprising grace. He fills both glasses—one slightly more than the other. She takes the fuller one without comment.

**SARAH**
This is not romance.

**GILLEY**
This is Madrid.

They sip. Silence for a beat.

**GILLEY**
You’re still wearing more clothes than me.

**SARAH**
I’m still wearing enough.

**GILLEY**
I wasn’t complaining.

He leans back in the water, watching her like she’s the most fascinating threat he’s ever met.

**GILLEY**
You’re good at this.

**SARAH**
What?

**GILLEY**
Being in control while pretending you’re not.

She doesn’t respond.  She sips her wine.  Then sets it down.  And climbs onto the side of the tub, legs crossed at the ankles, her back against the tile. The steam curls around her shoulders like smoke.

**SARAH**
I keep this whole band alive.

**GILLEY**
I know.

**SARAH**
And tonight, I don’t want to answer to any of you.

**GILLEY**
Then don’t.

He reaches for another orange. Offers it to her.  She takes it.  They sit there, in the low light, in the middle of Madrid, in silence—the manager and the frontman, wrapped in citrus and exhaustion and unspoken things that don’t need music to be felt.

---

## **INT. HOTEL BATHROOM – CONTINUOUS**

The room is quiet except for the soft drip of water from Gilley’s elbow and the hum of the building’s old pipes. **SARAH** leans against the tiled wall, glass in hand, eyes half-lidded but restless. Gilley reclines in the tub like he was born in porcelain, content, unrushed.

**SARAH** (breaking the silence)
We can’t keep doing this.

**GILLEY**
Why not?

**SARAH**
Because it’s a mess.  Because I manage you.  Because one night turns into two, turns into… I don’t know what the hell this is anymore.

**GILLEY**
Then maybe stop trying to name it.

**SARAH**
I have to name it. That’s literally my job—label it, file it, keep it from blowing up in our faces.

**GILLEY**
Yeah, well…  I’m quite comfortable not defining it.

He shifts slightly, water lapping the rim. He looks at her—not smug this time. Just honest, for once. It hangs in the air like the scent of citrus and steam.

**GILLEY**
You keep talking about “this” like it’s a problem.  I think it’s just a moment. A really nice, hot, very unstable moment.  But it’s ours. And we’ve never pretended otherwise.

**SARAH**
You never pretend anything. You just float through it until someone gets burned.

**GILLEY**
And you? You build walls so fast you don’t even realize you’re the only one stuck inside.

He sips his wine. She watches him. Annoyed. Affectionate. Tired. All at once.

**SARAH**
You’re impossible.

**GILLEY**
And yet… here you are. Again.

**SARAH**
Because I like oranges. And apparently, regret.

He laughs softly. The kind of laugh that says, yeah, maybe this is doomed, but God, it feels good right now.

**GILLEY**
So let’s not call it anything.  Let’s just sit in it.

**SARAH**
Until when?

**GILLEY**
Until the water goes cold.  Or the wine runs out.  Whichever comes first.

Sarah doesn’t answer. She picks up her glass, drains it slowly.  Then she says nothing.  And stays exactly where she is.

---

## **INT. HOTEL BATHROOM – LATER THAT NIGHT**

The bottle of Rioja is half-empty. The oranges are gone. **SARAH** now sits cross-legged on a towel beside the tub, robe wrapped loose around her, her skin flushed with wine and steam. **GILLEY** leans forward in the water, arms folded along the edge, watching her with something halfway between amusement and concern.

She’s been quiet for too long.

**GILLEY**
You paranoid someone’s gonna find out?

She doesn’t answer right away. Just rolls the stem of her wine glass between her fingers.

**GILLEY**
It’s okay if you are. I’m not gonna laugh at you.

**SARAH**
I’m not paranoid.

**GILLEY**
You are. Just a little.  You flinch every time someone says “Island” like it’s a curse word and not your damn paycheck.

**SARAH**
Because if the label finds out I’m fucking the singer of the most scandal-happy band on the planet, I’m not just fired. I’m publicly crucified. I’d get demoted to babysitting interns at A&R showcase nights in Schenectady.

**GILLEY**
First off, I’m not just the front man. I’m also a cautionary tale and a fashion icon.

Second—

(leans closer, voice low, gentle)
—I’ve been exercising the greatest degree of discretion possible to protect you. For months.

**SARAH**
You call discretion taking a bath in my hotel room?

**GILLEY**
You call not locking the door discretion?

She frowns. Touché.

**GILLEY**
Look, I know what I am. And I know what this is.  But I swear to you—I’m good at this shit.  The sneaking. The shadows. The art of not existing in anyone else’s version of the story.  I’ve done this dance before. But I’ve never done it to protect someone who actually mattered.

Sarah looks at him. Really looks at him.  No jokes in his eyes. No smirk.  Just a man soaking in tepid water, being more serious than anyone expects of him.

**SARAH**
And what if I screw it up?

**GILLEY**
Then I take the hit.  I’m already the one they expect to fuck up.  You? You’re the spine of this whole operation. They need you clean.  So let me be the shadow in the hallway. I don’t mind.

Beat.

**SARAH**
I hate how good you are at this.

**GILLEY**
Yeah. Me too.

He lifts what’s left of his wine. A quiet toast.

**GILLEY**
To shadows and steam.

She clinks her glass to his. And drinks.  No more flinching. At least not tonight.

---

## **INT. HOTEL BATHROOM – MOMENTS LATER**

The steam has settled into the tile, curling around the baseboards like lazy ghosts. The Rioja bottle is dangerously low. **SARAH** shifts slightly on her towel, legs tucked under her, robe hanging off one shoulder. She’s staring at Gilley—not with frustration anymore, but something far more dangerous: contemplation.

**SARAH**
I didn’t intend to have sex tonight.

**GILLEY** (grinning)
Oh?

**SARAH**
Didn’t even pack the good underwear.

**GILLEY**
Lucky for you, I’m not much of a textile snob.

She rolls her eyes, but her smile threatens to break the surface.

**SARAH**
I’m just saying—this wasn’t the plan.  I was gonna yell at Kathy, slap a press schedule on Jim’s forehead, and maybe sleep for four hours.  Instead… I’m half-naked, tipsy, and dangerously close to compromising my morals.  Again.

**GILLEY** (laughs, deep and real)
Morals? You?  Come on, darling—you work for a record company. That beast doesn’t even exist.

**SARAH**
There’s still a line.

**GILLEY**
Sure. But in this business, that line’s made of dry ice and plausible deniability.

**SARAH**
God, you’re infuriating.

**GILLEY**
And yet…

He gestures between them, water rippling.

**GILLEY**
Here we are. Third city this week. Same bathtub. Same tension.

**SARAH**
Don’t forget the oranges.

**GILLEY**
Never. I’m sentimental like that.

She takes a long sip of her wine. Then, with a dry smile:

**SARAH**
If I cave again, it’s your fault.

**GILLEY**
