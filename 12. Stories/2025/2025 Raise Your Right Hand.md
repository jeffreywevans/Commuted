# **RAISE YOUR RIGHT HAND**

---

# **CHAPTER: ONE**

---
## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – TUESDAY AFTERNOON – LATE SUMMER 2025**

Golden light slants through the cracked blinds, dust motes hanging like cigarette ghosts. The air is a swamp of old coffee, tangled cables, and lamb grease from the kebabs Kendall dragged in from some hole-in-the-wall joint across the street. The studio is cluttered but holy: racks of synths like Cold War control panels, a Rhodes piano sagging under stacks of marked-up sheet music, a couch with stuffing peeking through cigarette burns.

Kendall sits on that couch, kebab stick in hand, chewing slow like an executioner. Sauce stains her wrist, glistening. She doesn’t wipe it away. She savors it.

The door *slams*.

**AVRIL** storms in. She doesn’t glide or enter. She *erupts*. Leather jacket like armor, black boots hammering the floorboards, sunglasses still on as though she’s hiding from a sun only she can see.

**AVRIL**  (furious, breathless)  
They fucking sued me. Again.

Kendall doesn’t move. Just tilts her head, still chewing, eyes calm. A scientist watching a volcano form.

**KENDALL**  
How awful is it this time?

Avril rips the sunglasses off, hurls them onto the Rhodes. They skid, nearly topple, saved by the grit of dried dust. She’s pacing like a caged wolf, voice cracking with rage.

**AVRIL**  
All I did—*all I did*—was transfer the goddamn domain name. My old legacy site. Dead thing. Nobody used it. I moved it to the one Tim Wright likes, made it cleaner, cheaper, smarter. His LLC handles the backend, saves me money. I thought—cut costs, honor a small request, *be efficient*. And now? Federal fucking case.

She stops, jabs a finger at the ceiling like God Himself is the plaintiff.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
They’re treating it like I broke into Fort Knox and pissed on the Constitution.

She throws her arms wide, jacket creaking like old leather armor.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
For a *domain name*!

The word hangs in the air, ridiculous and devastating at once.

Kendall licks tzatziki off her thumb, smirks.

**KENDALL**  
You sleeping with one of these lawyers at least? Get the friends-and-family discount?

Avril freezes mid-stride, whip-cracks her glare onto Kendall. Sunglasses off, her eyes burn—green, venomous, exhausted. She doesn’t rise to the joke. She *leaps over it*.

**AVRIL**  
No. No, no, no. You know what? Shakespeare was right. *The first thing we do, let’s kill all the lawyers.*

Her voice booms in the studio, echo ricocheting off steel and wood. It’s half-bellow, half-incantation. She spits the line like scripture she finally understands.

Bill’s synth wall hums faintly, as if the circuits are vibrating with her fury.

Kendall sets down the kebab stick, finally meeting her at eye level. Calm, steady.

**KENDALL**  
Welcome to the family, Av. You wanted Commuted? This is what it costs. Blood. Sweat. And apparently subpoenas.

Avril collapses onto the couch beside her, leather creaking, shoulders trembling under fury and fatigue. She buries her face in her hands. The kebab wrapper crinkles under her thigh, greasy and loud.

**AVRIL (muffled, raw)**  
Goddamn leech fuckers.

Silence. Only the hum of the amps, the faint hiss of dust settling in sunlight.

Then Avril lifts her head. Her mascara smudged, her hair sticking in damp strands. She looks less like a superstar and more like a soldier left bleeding in the mud after a battle she didn’t start.

**AVRIL**  
I didn’t come here for this. I came here for fucking *music*.

Her voice cracks on *music*. The word is a plea, a scream, a prayer.

Kendall watches her with the steady patience of someone who’s seen too many wars—political, musical, legal—and knows the battlefield is always papered with contracts. She picks at the last shred of meat on the skewer, sucks the salt and fat.

**KENDALL**  
Music doesn’t save you from the machinery, Av. It just makes the machinery hungrier.

**AVRIL**  (snarling)  
Then fuck the machine. Let it starve.

The door opens again, creaking like it hasn’t been oiled in years. **BILL DAVENPORT** steps in, sleeves rolled, carrying a half-drained Gatorade. He takes in the sight: Kendall with her kebabs, Avril half-collapsed on the couch, rage crackling in the air like live wire.

**BILL**  
…what did I miss?

**KENDALL**  
Av got sued for existing. Again.

Bill doesn’t laugh. He just mutters something under his breath about the inevitability of entropy, sets the Gatorade down, and starts fiddling with the synth rack.

**AVRIL** (erupts)  
Don’t *shrug* at me, Bill! Do you know what it feels like to be sued for doing the *right* thing? To try to make something easier, cheaper, cleaner—just to be hammered into the dirt because some asshole wants to prove they still own me?

Bill doesn’t look up.

**BILL**  
Yeah. I know exactly what it feels like.

That shuts her up. For a second.

Avril presses her fists into her thighs, hard enough to leave marks.

**AVRIL**  
I swear to God, this world doesn’t want me free. They don’t want me in Commuted. They want me branded, barcoded, shoved back into some pop princess box I outgrew fifteen years ago.

Kendall leans forward, low, voice sharp enough to cut.

**KENDALL**  
Then burn the box.

Avril’s eyes flash. She looks at Kendall like she’s been handed a weapon.

**AVRIL**  
I could. I fucking could.

Her grin is savage now. The lawsuit hasn’t vanished, but the anger has transmuted—alchemy from despair to violence, from fatigue to fight.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
If they want war, they picked the wrong bitch to corner.

---

The kebab grease stains the console. The lawsuit papers wait in some New York office, fat with signatures and threats. But in that golden, dust-thick studio, something else lives: a girl from Napanee, furious and small and unstoppable, ready to scream the lawyers into silence with the only thing that has ever been hers—her voice.

This is how Commuted absorbs people: not gently, not politely, but by initiation through fire. Avril wanted *music.* Instead she found herself baptized in paperwork and lawsuits, kebabs and Shakespeare, learning the first rule of survival in this world—if you can’t beat the machine, you scream louder than it.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The kebab wrappers are wilted now, grease soaking into console paper. Avril still vibrates with rage, perched on the couch like a coiled spring. Kendall leans back, arms crossed, skewers like cigarette ends between her fingers. Bill has retreated into his modular rack, patch cables slung like veins.

The door bangs open again.

**JEREMY EVANS** lopes in, wiry, bald, dressed in his perpetual flannel-with-sleeves-rolled. He’s got a sweat sheen—he’s been running, as always. He catches the tail end of Avril’s threat.

**JEREMY**  (flat, dry)  
You’re Canadian. You can’t burn your Constitution. You don’t have a written one.

Behind him, **JEFF EVANS** shuffles in, carrying a bass case like it’s an anvil. He doesn’t look up from the floor, just drops the rebuttal as if compelled.

**JEFF**  (muttering, professorial)  
That’s a misconception. Canada does have a written constitution. The Constitution Act, 1867, and the Charter of Rights and Freedoms, 1982, among other statutes and conventions—

He pauses, sees the faces around the room glazing over, realizes his mistake. His glasses slide a little down his nose.

**JEFF (cont’d)**  
Never mind. If I explain, it’ll bore everyone. I’ll stop.

There’s a beat.

Then, in perfect unison, *all three—Avril, Kendall, Bill*—say it:

**ALL**  
Thank you!

Jeff nods once. Accepted. He sets down his bass and goes back to checking cables, as though silence is the natural end of any sentence.

Avril takes a breath. Her anger twists into a new shape: mischief. Her lips curl, and suddenly she detonates her Tsar Bomba.

**AVRIL**  
Actually… I’m studying for American citizenship.

The room *erupts*.

Kendall chokes on air, coughing into her hand like she swallowed a chili pepper. Bill’s head jerks up from the synth rack, eyes wide like a cat hearing thunder. Jeremy freezes mid-step, then barks out a disbelieving laugh. Even Jeff looks up, which for Jeff is basically a scream.

**JEREMY**  
Say *what*?

**BILL**  (half-laugh, half-groan)  
Oh, Jesus Christ.

**KENDALL**  (leaning forward, incredulous)  
You? An American? You hate half the states and mock the other half in interviews!

**AVRIL**  (grinning, defiant)  
Yeah. And I’ve got the study guide. Civics questions. Preamble. Federalist Papers. All of it. I’ve been sneaking flashcards between rehearsals.

**JEREMY**  
That’s like a dog deciding it wants to be a cat.

**JEFF**  (murmuring, sharp)  
Or maybe it’s like a wolf deciding it wants to live in the city.

Everyone turns to look at Jeff.

**JEFF**  (quiet, matter-of-fact)  
You want the passport, Av, you’re saying you want to belong here. In this mess. With us. With the lawsuits, the contracts, the—noise.

**AVRIL**  
Exactly.

Silence swells. The dust motes hang in amber light. It’s not just a joke anymore—it’s a vow.

Avril sits straighter. The rage that dragged her in has cracked open, and underneath it burns something harder, cleaner: resolve.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
I’ve been Canadian my whole damn life. But Commuted isn’t Canadian. It’s American. It’s Ohio. It’s lawsuits and sweat and stadiums and all-night diners. If I’m gonna bleed for this band, I might as well take the oath.

Kendall whistles low, half-impressed, half-terrified.

**KENDALL**  
You’re gonna have to swear to defend the Constitution you just threatened to burn.

**AVRIL**  (smiling wolfishly)  
Yeah. And I’ll mean every word.

---

The kebabs are gone. The lawsuits still circle like vultures. But in this moment, Avril Lavigne doesn’t look like the pop-punk princess dragged into another band’s mythology. She looks like a soldier crossing the border by choice, ready to plant her flag in Commuted’s war-torn soil.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The bomb still hangs in the room: Avril, studying for American citizenship. Everyone’s eyes are on her, the air charged, kebab grease and disbelief mingling into something combustible.

Kendall leans forward, skewers dangling between her fingers like cigarettes, eyes narrowed—not buying it.

**KENDALL**  
What’s the *real* reason, Av?

Avril stiffens.

**KENDALL (cont’d)**  
I saw your testimony on Parliament Hill. The hearings. Your whole song and dance about therapeutic use. This isn’t about stars and stripes. It’s about MDMA access.

Bill’s head jerks up, caught between shock and a smirk. Jeremy raises his brows, impressed. Jeff just exhales through his nose like he’s been expecting this reveal.

Avril glares, defensive, a flush creeping up her neck.

**AVRIL**  (flat, sharp)  
It’s not about taxes.

**KENDALL**  
I didn’t say it was.

**AVRIL**  
Yeah, well, Ohio’s taxes, the IRS—they hammer me just like they hammer anyone stupid enough to get famous here. I can live with that. But don’t pretend this is about me running from a tax bill.

(beat—her voice drops, lower, raw)  
It’s about medicine. About my right to not have to fly across the goddamn border every time I want treatment. Canada drags its feet. The U.S.—*your* U.S.—is moving faster.

She stops, realizing she just admitted it out loud.

Silence.  Even the amps seem to hum softer.  Kendall watches her carefully, like she’s peeling away the last protective layer.

**KENDALL**  
You didn’t address the elephant in the room.

Avril clenches her fists, jaw tight. Then she finally exhales.

**AVRIL**  
I want to belong to the place that doesn’t make me beg for my own sanity. If that means pledging allegiance, swearing the oath, paying more taxes than I should—fine. I’ll do it. I’m not fucking ashamed.

Her eyes glitter with something fiercer than rage now: clarity.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
This isn’t about a green card or a goddamn passport. It’s about *survival.*

---

Jeremy lets out a low whistle, muttering something about how Avril just declared war on both countries in one sentence. Bill mutters, *“Well, shit,”* and starts twisting knobs like he can process her confession through sound. Jeff, for once, doesn’t correct a single fact.

Avril just sits there, small and volcanic, daring anyone to argue.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The studio feels smaller now, like the walls are inching inward with every word Avril spits. She’s perched on the couch, hair falling in messy strands around her face, cheeks blotched with fury. Kendall leans back with arms crossed, the kebab skewers finally abandoned. Bill sits rigid by the synth rack, pretending to adjust knobs but really just trying to breathe through the tension. Jeff leans against his amp stack, inscrutable. Jeremy lurks by the door, arms folded, watching like he’s at a prize fight.

Avril’s voice cracks open again, sharper, darker.

**AVRIL**  (angry, gutted)  
You want the truth? Canada *disdains* me. All those smug fuckers on Parliament Hill with their speeches about our “superior health care system.” Superior my ass. I begged. I testified. I laid my goddamn soul bare about what happened to me in New York. 2024 chewed me alive, and I stood there asking for help. And what did I get? Shrugs. Delays. A pat on the head and a “we’ll study it further.”

Her voice breaks, but she pushes through, fists pressing into her thighs.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
After what happened to me in New York, it’s betrayal. *Betrayal of my core identity.* They looked at my scars, my wreckage, and told me to wait my turn in line like I was asking for a fucking nose job.

She pulls a crumpled pack of gum from her jacket pocket, tosses it onto the console like evidence. One little foil square half-slid out.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
But one thing stays the nightmares. One thing keeps me from waking up screaming. A tab of MDMA. That’s it. That’s the medicine. Not their antidepressants that make me feel embalmed. Not their platitudes about counseling waitlists. Just that. One pressed pill. And my own country won’t fucking give it to me.

The words shiver through the air like electricity. Kendall doesn’t move. Bill stops fidgeting. Even Jeff looks up.

The door bangs again, and **JIM BROWN** barrels in, hoodie halfway unzipped, drumsticks jammed in his back pocket like spare weapons. He senses the heat instantly, grins crooked.

**JIM**  (trying to cut through)  
Hey, hey. Don’t sweat it. Being an American’s fun. Free refills, loud parades, fireworks every goddamn week if you know the right county. You’ll get used to it.

Avril turns on him, eyes wet, voice breaking into a jagged edge.

**AVRIL**  
Fun? FUN? Jim, I’m *heartbroken*. You think I wanted this? You think I wanted to torch the one thing that shaped me—my very Canadian sense of fairness—just to get a doctor to look me in the eye?

Her voice drops to a whisper, ragged.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
I grew up believing the system was supposed to catch you when you fall. That fairness wasn’t a myth. And now? Now I’m chasing pills across borders like some goddamn criminal.

Jim’s grin falters. He rubs the back of his neck, uneasy.

Avril looks around, her whole body shaking with exhaustion.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
And with these new goddamn lawsuits—it’s every *fucking* thing against me again. Always again. My label. My country. The law. Even my own goddamn body.

Silence falls. Heavy. The amps hum like distant thunder.

For once, nobody cracks a joke. Not Kendall, not Jim, not even Jeremy.

Avril sits there, hollowed out and furious, clutching the ghost of her country with one hand and the promise of another with the other—neither offering her mercy.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The kebab wrappers are dead soldiers. The room is soaked in Avril’s fury, the smell of grease and sweat fusing with her confession about MDMA, about betrayal, about her homeland’s cruel delay. Nobody laughs now.

Jim shifts uncomfortably, scratching at his jaw. He’s still got drumsticks poking out of his hoodie pocket, like he’s armed but useless. His voice drops low, gentler than anyone expects.

**JIM**  
Av… look, I don’t want to play dad here. But you’re spiraling again. We’ve seen this. You know when we tour, Cremeans always has a psychologist traveling with us. That’s not a coincidence. He keeps one in his back pocket like extra cymbals. Do you—

(beat)  
Do you need help?

The room holds its breath. Bill’s hand hovers over the synth knobs. Jeff pauses in mid-adjustment of his strap. Kendall doesn’t even blink.

Avril wipes her eyes roughly with the heel of her palm. The mascara smears, but she doesn’t care. Her voice comes out ragged, defiant.

**AVRIL**  
No. I don’t need a shrink. I don’t need another clipboard telling me I’m “processing trauma.”

(beat—suddenly sharp, almost comic in her fury)  
I just need to figure out the difference between Madison and Monroe.

Everyone stares.

**JEREMY**  
…what?

Avril slumps back into the couch, tugging her jacket tighter, half-laughing through the tears.

**AVRIL**  
The civics exam. The goddamn citizenship test. They’re gonna ask me about dead presidents and I can’t keep them straight. Madison. Monroe. Who’s who? One’s short, one’s tall? One wrote papers, one just looked like a paperweight? Fuck if I know.

She lets out a manic laugh, then jabs a finger at the ceiling.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
And Jesus hell, if you’re gonna have two political parties, why name one the *Federalist* and the other the *Anti-Federalist*? What kind of masochist branding is that? You couldn’t think of a different word? You had to just tack *“anti”* on and call it a day? No wonder the whole system’s broken.

Her laugh skids into something harsher, a snarl.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
I just want the bitch, okay? The passport. The papers. I’ll memorize their founding fathers, their wigs, their stupid compromises. Just give me the bitch.

The silence that follows is thick, but not empty. It’s the silence of four Americans watching a Canadian tear herself apart to belong—half admiring, half horrified.

Jim finally sighs, shaking his head.

**JIM**  
Only you, Av. Only you could make U.S. civics sound like a bar fight.

Avril smirks through the wreckage of her face, eyes glassy, jaw stubborn.

**AVRIL**  
Then let’s fucking brawl!

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The air has shifted. The kebab grease, the lawsuits, the jokes about Federalists—they’re shadows now. What takes over is Jim’s voice, stripped of sarcasm, stripped of rhythm, bare like a drum with the skin peeled off.

**JIM**  (flat, deadly serious)  
No. I’m not laughing this off. Not this time.

He steps forward, plants himself in the center of the room like he’s about to call a set change. His eyes pin Avril to the couch.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
Because we never got Kathy the help she needed. Not really. Not until it was already too late. Half a day into rehab and she was gone. That was our failure.

Avril blinks. Her smirk falters.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
And Gilley? We didn’t watch him close enough. We let him grind himself down, night after night, until his body just quit. He worked himself to death under our noses.

Bill looks away. Jeff shifts his weight. The ghosts are here now.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
And Jeff—hell, we spent twenty goddamn years calling it “eccentricity” and “temperament.” We didn’t know he was on the spectrum. Didn’t try to understand. Just put up with it. He lived in hell while we looked the other way.

Jeff’s jaw clenches. He doesn’t deny it.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
And Jeremy’s drinking? Don’t talk to me about luck. That was dumb luck. Dumb fucking luck he didn’t drink himself into the grave before somebody pulled him out.

Jeremy looks at the floor. Silence.

Jim leans forward, voice cracking but hard as stone.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
So no, Avril. We’re not gonna “wait and see” with you. We’re not gonna laugh about civics flashcards while you bleed out in the margins. You can either get the help you need, get the medication you need, or—

(beat, savage)  
—or you can take a long walk off a short pier.

The words hit like a brick through glass. Nobody breathes.

Avril stares at him. Tears hover but don’t fall. She’s small in the corner of the couch, leather jacket swallowing her, but her eyes flare back with something volcanic.

**AVRIL**  (low, trembling)  
You’d throw me out like that? After everything?

Jim doesn’t blink.

**JIM**  
I’d rather throw you out alive than bury you with a song on your lips.

The amps hum. Bill sets his hand on the Rhodes to steady himself. Jeff’s face is stone, unreadable, but his hands are shaking at his sides. Kendall doesn’t speak—her whole job is to chronicle, and she knows a moment when the band’s survival is on the knife’s edge.

Avril finally looks down, her knuckles white where they grip her thighs.

**AVRIL**  (quiet, broken)  
I just wanted the bitch. The passport. The belonging. The fight.

**JIM**  
Then fight smart. Not suicidal.

The silence after is unbearable. A silence so heavy it could crush the room.

# **CHAPTER TWO: HELP ME**

---

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The silence holds, heavy as wet cement. Avril’s chest heaves. Jim’s ultimatum still hangs in the air like smoke from a blown speaker. Everyone waits for her to spit fire back, to snap, to deny. But instead—

She breaks.

Her voice cracks in half.

**AVRIL**  (whispered, raw)  
I can’t.

The word stuns the room.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
(louder, shaking)  
I *can’t*. I’m broke. I’ve cracked. I’ve pushed my body as far as it can go, and past it. I’m at the line. Beyond the line is death and dismemberment.

Her face twists. She looks less like the Av who walked into the studio—leather, fire, fury—and more like someone stripped to the bone. The truth, naked and dangerous.

The four men—Jeff, Jim, Bill, Jeremy—stand frozen. Commuted has seen chaos, has seen overdoses, has seen one another bleed and burn, but *never this*. Never someone in their circle flat-out admitting the breaking point. Kathy never said it before she collapsed. Gilley laughed instead of confessing. Jeff buried it under intellect. Jeremy drowned it in alcohol. Jim spun it into rhythm.

But Avril lays it down like a weapon on the table: *I can’t.*

It shocks them.

Jeremy blinks like he’s been slapped. Bill stares at the floor, hands trembling, cables forgotten. Jeff’s jaw works, but no words come. Jim just breathes hard, shoulders rigid, eyes wide.

They *want* to help. Every cell in their bodies says: pull her back, keep her safe, fix it. But the truth is brutal—none of them can unless she lets them. Unless she actively chooses.

Kendall watches from the couch, skewers abandoned, pen in hand, knowing she is watching the most honest thing Commuted has ever witnessed.

**JEFF**  (soft, almost ashamed)  
We’ll help. But you’ve gotta ask.

**JEREMY**  (quiet, nodding)  
Yeah. You have to say it.

**BILL**  
We can’t carry you unless you let us.

Finally, Jim again. Voice low, raw, without armor.

**JIM**  
We’ll do whatever it takes, Av. But you’ve gotta choose it for sure.

The room is a prayer, the amps humming like a choir, the light turning gold as if it knows the weight of what’s been said.

Avril sits on the couch, head in her hands, breath hitching, the weight of every lawsuit, every nightmare, every pill denied pressing down. She’s cracked open. For once, Commuted doesn’t mock or dodge or disguise. They just wait.

Because the band can’t drag her over the line. She has to *walk back herself.*

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CINCINNATI – LATE SUMMER 2025 – TUESDAY AFTERNOON**

The air is so still you can hear the faint buzz of a cable not quite plugged in all the way. Avril sits hunched forward on the couch, small in her leather jacket, knees knocking, mascara streaking in clumsy smudges. The fire is gone from her voice. What’s left is something nobody has ever heard from her—not on stage, not in interviews, not even in the late-night chaos of tour buses.

**AVRIL**  (quiet, trembling)  
I need help.

The words fall like bricks, heavy and undeniable.

Nobody moves. Jeff’s fingers twitch on his strap but he doesn’t look up. Bill swallows, his Adam’s apple bobbing like he’s choking on dust. Jeremy leans against the doorframe, his eyes wide, arms folded like he’s holding himself together. Jim, still in the center of the room, exhales slow, the tension in his jaw loosening just a hair.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  (voice breaking)  
It’s been three years. Three years into this… whatever the hell you wanna call it. My reinvention. My second life. And I love it. I fucking love it. Being here, in this band, in this madness. It’s the first time since I was seventeen that music feels like mine again.

She presses her fists against her thighs, digging in like she wants to bruise.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
But my body doesn’t work half the time. My brain flickers out like a bad light bulb. Some days I can’t get out of bed. Some nights I can’t sleep without the pill. And I keep telling myself it’s fine, it’s manageable, that if I just… push harder, scream louder, it’ll sort itself out.

She shakes her head, finally looking up. Her eyes are wet, furious at herself, pleading at them.

**AVRIL (cont’d)**  
But it doesn’t. And I can’t push anymore. I’ve cracked. And I don’t want to end up like—

Her voice cuts off. She doesn’t say Kathy’s name, doesn’t say Gilley’s. She doesn’t have to. The ghosts are already in the room.

Silence.

Then Jeff speaks, voice low, the closest he’s ever come to tender.

**JEFF**  
You’re asking now. That’s the difference.

**BILL**  
And we’ll back you. Whatever it takes.

Jeremy pushes off the doorframe, steps closer. His voice isn’t soft—it’s blunt, but steady.

**JEREMY**  
You don’t have to carry it alone anymore.

Finally, Jim kneels in front of her, lowering himself so he’s eye to eye. No jokes. No bravado. Just the drummer, stripped raw.

**JIM**  
We’ve lost too many. You’re not gonna be one of them. Not on my fucking watch.

Avril exhales a jagged breath, and for the first time since she stormed in about lawsuits and constitutions and medicine, her shoulders drop. She’s not cured, not whole, not fixed. But she’s *asking*.

And that is everything.

**JEREMY** (to Avril)
Anthems are questions. You asked one.

**AVRIL**
And?

He nods toward the shoreline, toward the tower shrinking, toward a farm miles inland where a lawyer-pastor is putting down his phone. 

**JEREMY**
The answer came back.

She nods. It’s not triumph, exactly. It’s steadiness. A durable yes.

## **HOTEL PARKING LOT, SANDUSKY – DAWN**

The trucks wheeze into silence, engines ticking in the humid Ohio morning. The band stumbles out, dragging bags, eyes swollen from no sleep. Cedar Point roller coasters loom black against the rising sun.

Jim, ever the organizer, squints at the hotel façade, jangling the keys in his hand.

**JIM**  
All right. Rooms. How many we need? Four? Five?

For a heartbeat, nobody answers. The birds in the lot are shrill.

Then, overlapping, sharp as a slap:

**AVRIL and JEFF (simultaneous)**  
Five.

Too quick. Too final.

The others glance sideways. Bill raises his brows. Jeremy stifles a laugh. Jim blinks, because he had expected four.  The word hangs there, heavy with something unsaid. Not just logistics. Not just beds. It’s the weight of a promise made weeks ago, still fresh enough to sting, still raw enough to be declared out loud in a parking lot at dawn.

Nobody explains. Nobody asks. They shoulder their bags and head toward the lobby, shadows long across the asphalt.

And the answer is five. Today forward?  Always five.
