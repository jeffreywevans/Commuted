# **ROLLING STONE – MARCH 2014 ISSUE**  

## **"The Ones Who Lit the Match"**  
_Greg Dulli Talks Afghan Whigs, the 2014 Grammys, and Why Commuted Still Makes Everyone Nervous_

---

## **INT. HOLLYWOOD ROOSEVELT – SUNSET SUITE – 2:11 A.M. – MORNING AFTER THE GRAMMYS**

Greg Dulli is nursing a bourbon in a glass that used to hold Diet Coke. He’s not sleeping tonight. Not because of nerves. Because of noise. Because _Commuted_ played the Grammys like they were crashing the building, and it reminded him what rage in perfect pitch still looks like.

He lights a cigarette.

**GREG DULLI:**  
You remember where you were when _69 Reasons_ dropped, right?

He doesn't wait for the answer. Of course you do.

**ROLLING STONE (RS):**  
Summer ’92. You guys were grinding _Congregation_. They were on _SNL_ before they had a label photo.

**GREG:**  
Exactly. We’re hauling gear up two flights in Cincinnati, and those kids are burning churches on late night. Every band on Sub Pop was pissed. We all said the same thing: _Who the fuck are these kids?_

**RS:**  
And what was the answer?

**GREG:**  
They were the kids who’d kill you and write the eulogy in blood. You had Kathy Espich howling like she’d just pulled your spine out of your back. Jeff Evans writing basslines like hate mail. Jim Brown playing drums like he was summoning the Four Horsemen. Gilley already half-naked and halfway gone. And Bill? Jesus. That guy could sample your heartbeat and make it sound like a war crime.

**RS:**  
Do you remember the jealousy?

**GREG:**  
That’s not a memory. That’s muscle. _69 Reasons_ wasn’t just a record—it was a challenge. We were all playing clubs, and they _exploded_ from a fucking garage. No crawl. No climb. Just skyrocket.

And the thing is—they didn’t fake it. That record was feral. It _blew up because it was right_. But when you’ve been busting your ass, and someone gets there first with a scream and a synthesizer? You start asking if you’re doing it wrong.

**RS:**  
Did you listen to it?

**GREG:**  
I memorized it. Every drum fill. Every breath Kathy took. That album didn’t play songs—it _accused_ the listener. Track four still gives me vertigo.

**RS:**  
So what made them different?

**GREG:**  
They _felt_ unmarketed. But they were savvier than we gave them credit for. Ric Ocasek didn’t just produce it—he _understood_ it. He let the chaos breathe. And Sarah Malcom at Island? She didn’t push it through radio. She let the girls with bruises and boys with fury _find_ it. Word-of-mouth fury. That’s currency. That’s eternal.

**RS:**  
You think the rock community resented them more than Sub Pop did?

**GREG:**  
Rock guys _despised_ them. Because they weren’t trying to be men. They were trying to be _demons_. Or prophets. Gilley looked like a hitchhiker who’d steal your car and fuck your girlfriend. Kathy _was_ the girlfriend. Then she fucked your dad. That band didn’t just sing songs. They showed up, stole the mic, and made your pain look boring.

**RS:**  
And then they kept going.

**GREG:**  
That’s the worst part. They didn’t flame out. They got _weirder_. Bolder. By the time _That Look of Release_ dropped in ’95? Half the country hated them. The other half tattooed the lyrics on their ribs.

**RS:**  
And yet… they never “belonged.”

**GREG:**  
No one _wanted_ them to belong. You put them next to Soundgarden or REM and they make everyone look like they’re playing dress-up. Commuted was raw _on purpose._ And they had a woman at the front who wasn’t there to seduce you—she was there to _gut you._ That terrified everyone.

**RS:**  
What’s changed in 2014?

**GREG:**  
Nothing. And that’s what’s wild. Tonight at the Grammys? They played “Victory Monument” like it still bled. Kathy’s dead, and they summoned her anyway. It wasn’t a performance. It was a fucking _séance._

**RS:**  
You looked emotional during the set.

**GREG:**  
Man. I was crying like a teenage girl on prom night. You can’t fake that kind of truth. You can fake rage, fake cool, fake heartbreak. But what they did on that stage? That was an _exorcism._

**RS:**  
So what do you say to bands who still resent them?

**GREG:**  
Grow up. Or better yet—_get better_. They earned every second of it. Their debut wasn’t luck. It was lightning striking a gas leak. You mad it wasn’t you? Then go light your own fucking match.

**RS:**  
And you? You respect them now?

**GREG:**  
I respected them in ‘92. I feared them by ’94. And in 2014?

He leans forward. Ashes flick to the table. His voice drops to something sacred.

**GREG:**  
I _worship_ them.

---

## **POSTSCRIPT – MARCH 2014**  

_Commuted_ walked offstage at the 2014 Grammys without giving interviews. Kathy’s absence was never addressed. No tribute video. No montage. Just the music—played loud enough to shatter ribs. The camera didn’t cut away once.

Greg Dulli watched from the wings, and he wasn’t the only one crying.
