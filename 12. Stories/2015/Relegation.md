**Jim finally stands. Face tight.**

**JIM (cont’d)**  
So what, she gets to keep the job and I get to eat it?

**GILLEY**  
No one’s asking you to swallow it.  Just to not let it swallow the band.

Jim paces once. Breath short. Like he’s walking off the impulse to hit something. Hit **Gilley.**

Then—

**JIM**  
So she disappears from the room, but still writes the lyrics down in her head?

**GILLEY**  
She’s good, man.  Better than any of the interns you’ve threatened to set on fire.

**JIM (dry)**  
That’s a low bar.

**GILLEY**  
But she’s family.

**JIM**  
I head Cremeans—You don’t fuck family.

Gilley winces. Deserved.

**GILLEY**  
Look, I know I stepped in it.  I’m not asking you to trust me.  Just asking if you can live with her being *off-site* and *out of your bed.*

**Jim pauses.**

**JIM**  
And out of *yours?*

Gilley meets his eyes.

**GILLEY**  
Yes.

Beat. Jim looks at the floor. Then the ceiling. Then back at Gilley.

**JIM (quiet)**  
Fine.  But the first time I see her trying to document from a distance like she still belongs in the story, I’m calling time of death.

**GILLEY**  
Understood.

**JIM (flat)**  
Now get the fuck out of this hallway before I remember how good it felt to punch you back in ‘07.

Gilley walks. Doesn’t run. Doesn’t thank him. Just leaves.  Jim stays behind.  And for the first time since the morgue, he sits on the drum case and actually lets himself cry.  Quiet. Honest. Alone.

---

## INT. CREMEANS’S OFFICE – SOUNDSTAGE BACKROOM – EARLY AFTERNOON – 1:02 P.M.

The frankincense is out.  The lamp is off.  The coffee is colder than most apologies.  **Kendall stands just inside the partition.** She’s cleaned up—barely. Lip balm instead of lipstick. Jeans with a belt, not drawstring shame. But her expression’s the same as it was when Gilley took her badge: **waiting to be sentenced.**

**Cremeans is already seated.**  He doesn’t stand.  Doesn’t greet her.  Just gestures toward the chair across from him like this is any other logistics meeting.

She doesn’t sit.  She stands with her hands in her pockets like she’s still hoping this is a misunderstanding. It isn’t.

**CREMEANS**  
Okay, you’re not being fired.

Beat.

**KENDALL**  
Okay.

**CREMEANS**  
But you’re also not coming on tour.

**KENDALL**  
Right.

**CREMEANS**  
You’ll be reassigned.  Digital ops. Website. Remote content strategy. Interview filtering. Release copy.  You’ll write.  But you’ll write from *home.*

Silence.

**KENDALL**  
Is that… permanent?

**CREMEANS**  
Long as it needs to be.

She swallows hard.

**KENDALL**  
I can do more.

**CREMEANS**  
Yeah.  But can you do less?

He leans back. Crosses his arms. Looks up at her with the kind of pragmatism that’s never personal—but always brutal.

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
Let me explain it in terms you understand.

**Kendall stiffens.**

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
One of the problem people in this equation can walk onstage and make **sixty thousand people** scream in sync.  Can move tickets.  Move merch.  
Move *feeling.*  The other one?

**He stares her down.**

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
*You* don’t sell out the top deck.  You don’t get the standing ovation.  You don’t get the encore.

Kendall looks down. Red floods her face—anger or shame or both.

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
You’re a beautiful writer.  But this? This is a business.  And in the business, you don’t fuck the frontman.  And you definitely don’t try to stay in the van when your husband can’t look you in the eyes.

She says nothing.

He lets the silence breathe.  Then—softens, slightly.

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
But maybe this is a gift.  You’ve been chasing ghosts and deadlines for ten years.  Maybe now you go home.  Put your head on the same pillow as your kids.  
Make school lunches.  Watch them become people.  Spend every doggone night under the same roof until they leave it.

Beat.

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
You can still write.  Still shape the narrative.  Just not from the inside anymore.

**Kendall (quietly)**  
So that’s it.

**CREMEANS**  
That’s it.

**Kendall (hoarse)**  
You really think I’m expendable?

**CREMEANS**  
I think everybody is.  That’s why we try so hard to matter while we’re here.

He reaches into the drawer. Pulls out her badge.  Doesn’t hand it to her.  Just places it gently in the bottom of a manila envelope.

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
You’ll get your logins tonight.  Calendar updates by morning.  We’ll start with the press release for the tour.

He slides the envelope across the table.

**CREMEANS (cont’d)**  
Leave your keys and go.

**Kendall doesn’t move.**  Not right away.  When she does, it’s slow.  Measured.  A woman with a new wound. A quieter role. And a goddamn draft to finish.

## INT. BROOKLYN VRBO – EARLY EVENING – 6:13 P.M.

There’s a smell of fried onions and something once-frozen heating on the stove.  Jim’s boots are by the door, lined up with an aggression she recognizes.  
The lights are on. TV is off.  **He’s home.**

**Kendall unlocks the door. Steps inside.**  She’s holding the envelope from Cremeans like it contains radioactive shame. She doesn’t take off her shoes. Doesn’t speak right away.

Jim is in the kitchen.  No music. No drink.  He doesn’t look up when she enters.

He’s washing a single dish. Carefully. Like it’s meditative.

**KENDALL**  
They’re sending me home.

Jim doesn’t flinch.  Still scrubbing.

**KENDALL (cont’d)**  
Not fired.  Just off the road. Off the record.  Off the inside.

He places the dish on the drying rack. Wipes his hands. Turns.

**KENDALL (cont’d)**  
Five years of exile, give or take.

**JIM**  
That’s not exile.

She tilts her head. Cautious.

**KENDALL**  
No?

**JIM**  
It’s relegation.

Beat.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
Learn the word.  Isn’t that what you’re supposed to be good at?

The sarcasm is thin, but sharp.  Like the first ice over a lake you used to trust.

**KENDALL**  
I deserved that.

**JIM**  
You deserve better.  You just didn’t act like it.

Silence.

**KENDALL (soft)**  
You’re not leaving me?

He looks at her like she just asked if the earth was flat.

**JIM**  
I’ve got a tour to run.  A band to resurrect.  Two kids who think we’re functional.

He walks past her. Opens the fridge. Pulls out a Coke.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
I’ll see you in ten weeks.

**KENDALL**  
What happens then?

**JIM**  
We decide if we’re still married.  If I can touch you without remembering you came home tasting like someone else.  If you want to come back not because the tour’s over but because you *missed me.*

He drinks.  Eyes never leave hers.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
You go to Springboro.  You write the site.  You make lunches.  You take your penance like a grown-up.

She nods. The envelope crinkles in her hand.

**KENDALL**  
Okay.

**JIM**  
And if you ever want back in?  Not just the job.  *Me.*

He walks past her again.

**JIM (cont’d)**  
You’re gonna have to learn how to say “sorry” like it isn’t just another story you’re writing for someone else.

She doesn’t cry.  She stands in the middle of the room like she’s been shot through the spine.  And he walks into the bedroom.

The door doesn’t slam.  It just closes.
