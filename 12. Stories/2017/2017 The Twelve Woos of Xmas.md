# **THE TWELVE WOOS OF XMAS

---

## **INT. JEFF’S BASEMENT STUDIO – FORT ANCIENT, OHIO – MIDNIGHT – STORM RAGING OUTSIDE – 2017**

The rain is horizontal. The gutters are choking. The whole house shakes with the low thunder roll of a Midwest storm that doesn’t quit. Downstairs, the basement is a bunker: cables like vines across the floor, racks of synths blinking like alien eyes, beer cans sweating, an ashtray overflowing.

Jeff is slouched on the rolling chair, hunched forward, glasses sliding down his nose, one hand nursing a bottle, the other already at the keys. He’s been layering pads for hours, long, cathedral tones, swelling like the storm overhead. He’s not speaking. He’s not smiling. He’s painting with sound, his face unreadable except for the twitch of his jaw when something hits right.

Gilley stumbles in, damp from the rain, eyes lit up like he’s riding the lightning itself. He kicks the door shut, shakes his shaggy hair, and laughs too loud for the room.

**GILLEY**  
You hear that storm? It’s begging us, man. It’s begging for a hymn.

Jeff doesn’t look up. He just plays a chord so big it swallows the room.

**JEFF**  
Then shut up and listen.

The pads bloom. Low, then higher. Empty space filled with echo. Gilley grabs a mic, tests it, the squeal of feedback like another bolt of lightning cracking through the house. He starts riffing—half words, half drunken sermon:

*“Women on the loose / Get more ammo / Need more beer / And I got twelve dollars.”*

Jeff nods once, keeps building. He dials in electronic percussion—precise, beat-driven thuds that march under the synth swells. Not rock drums yet. Not live. Something cleaner. Machine heartbeats in the storm.

Gilley’s pacing now, slamming the mic stand into the cement like he’s nailing down the lyrics by force.

**GILLEY**  
*And they gone / And I’m gone / And it’s gone / And I’m gone.*

The song builds like a wall. Melodic lines start sneaking through, Jeff coaxing them from his rig, simple motifs at first, then rising, harmonizing, climbing like sirens. The air in the room thickens. Both men are sweating.

The storm outside cracks open the power lines, lights flicker, but Jeff doesn’t stop. He’s got soaring synths now, layered over layered, like a cathedral being born in real time.

Then, without warning, Jeff slams his palm down, **TRIGGERING THE DRUMS**.

Thunderous. Cinematic. The kind of percussion that doesn’t just fill the room, it *claims* it. Floor tom samples that sound like the sky splitting, snares like rifle shots.

Gilley throws his head back and screams into the mic:

**GILLEY**  
*WOO WOO WOO WOO WOO—*

Twelve times, each one louder, each one more deranged, the storm outside practically answering back.

By the middle of the song, it’s no longer just music. It’s weather. It’s ritual. Jeff’s bass synth growls underneath, the pads are skyscrapers, the melodic lines are sirens, and the drums… the drums are war.  The two of them lock eyes. No words. Just the shared knowledge that this isn’t a sketch, it isn’t a demo. It’s a weapon.

They ride it to climax—Jeff pounding keys, Gilley screaming like he’s testifying, the storm outside rattling the foundation. And when it finally crashes into silence, both men are gasping, drenched in sweat, beers knocked over and foaming on the cement.

**GILLEY**  (breathless, laughing, hoarse)  
That’s it. That’s the fuckin’ finale.

Jeff just nods. One slow nod. He already knows the song isn’t just written, It’s alive.

That’s how *“Twelve Dollars Left”* entered the canon: not as a tidy track, but as a drunk dare, a thunderstorm, and a basement sermon that built itself into an anthem fit for stadium finales.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – NEXT WEEK – LATE NIGHT – 2017**

Cables snake everywhere. Modular rigs humming. The room smells like solder and Red Bull. Bill’s on the floor cross-legged with a soldering iron, working on some godforsaken box of knobs and wires. Jeremy Evans is perched on a stool, guitar in lap, frowning like he’s grading everyone’s sanity. Jim’s sprawled on the couch, hoodie up, tapping his sticks on an empty pizza box.

Jeff and Gilley have dragged in their stormborn monster. A laptop, a hard drive, and the manic gleam of men who haven’t slept since the storm.

Jeff hits play.

The room fills with **swelling pads** and **machine-beat percussion**. It sounds… cinematic. Not Commuted. Not the Commuted they know. Jeff’s face doesn’t change; he’s locked into the sound like a preacher waiting for the gospel to land.

Gilley, already pacing, swigs from a bottle and jumps on the downbeat.

**GILLEY**  (snarling, almost gleeful)  
*“Women on the loose / Get more ammo / Need more beer / And I got twelve dollars—”*

Jeremy Evans squints. Bill stops soldering. Jim raises an eyebrow.

**JEREMY EVANS** (flat)  
This is… EDM with a head injury.

**BILL** (slow, dry)  
Feels less like a song and more like a hostage situation.

Jeff pushes his glasses up, finally looks at them.

**JEFF**  
Good. That means it’s working.

The track keeps building, melodic lines climbing, synths soaring. Then the **drums hit**, Jeff’s programmed cinematic cannons, thunder in stereo.

Jim sits up straighter. His hands twitch like he’s already hearing how he’d tear into a kit alongside it.

**JIM**  
Okay… okay, that’s actually sick. That’s… big. Like… finale big.

Gilley slams the mic stand down, screams the chorus: twelve deranged **“WOO”s**, each one echoing off the studio walls. Bill actually laughs—once, short, surprised out of him.

**BILL**  
You’re serious. You want us to play this live.

**GILLEY**  
Not play it. Ride it. Like a fuckin’ storm.

The music drops to silence. Jeff kills the playback, lets the ringing in everyone’s ears fill the gap.

**JEFF**  
It’s not about hooks. It’s not about charts. It’s ritual. It’s finale. It’s the point where the show becomes a riot.

Jeremy Evans leans back, skeptical but intrigued despite himself.

**JEREMY EVANS**  
Alright. Maybe. But you’re not dragging me out there just to play three notes and scream “woo.”

**GILLEY**  
That’s where you come in, brother. You and Bill.

(beat)

**JEREMY EVANS**  
Oh, fuck no.

**BILL**  
Wait—hold up—what do you mean “come in”?

Jeff stands, paces once, then turns like a general delivering orders.

**JEFF**  
Harmonies. Layered. Thick. The crowd won’t expect it. They’ll think it’s just Gilley’s circus act—then BOOM. Vocals stack. Voices collide. It becomes a wall.

Bill shakes his head, half-laughing, half-terrified.

**BILL**  
Jeff, my voice is for counting time signatures. Not for public consumption.

**GILLEY**  
Bullshit. You got pipes. You’ve been hiding them under synth racks.

**JEREMY EVANS**  
And me? I don’t harmonize. I shred.

Jeff steps closer, staring at his brother.

**JEFF**  
Not tonight. Tonight you sing. You *scream* until the blood vessels in your neck threaten mutiny. We need your bite, Jer. And Bill’s range to glue it.

The room goes quiet. Bill stares at the soldering iron in his hand like it might save him. Jeremy looks between his brother and Gilley like he’s calculating the odds of mutiny.

Jim, from the couch, grins wide.

**JIM**  
Do it. Fucking do it. I want to watch Jeremy sing backup like a church kid and Bill cry into his vocoder.

**BILL**  (flat)  
I hate you, Jim.

**JIM**  
Love you too.

Beat.

Jeremy finally exhales, rubs his bald head.

**JEREMY EVANS**  
If I do this… and I mean if… this song better blow the goddamn roof off.

**JEFF**  
It will.

Bill groans, sets down the soldering iron.

**BILL**  
Fine. But if I sound like shit, I’m quitting mid-verse and hiding behind the synths.

**GILLEY**  
You won’t.

Jeff starts the track again. The pads swell. The percussion kicks in. Jeff nods toward the mic stands, voice calm, certain:

**JEFF**  
Let’s build the wall.

That’s how the persuasion goes: not reasoned, not rational—just the inevitability of Jeff’s gravity and Gilley’s manic faith.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE – 2:13 A.M. – SUMMER 2017**

Empty beer bottles litter the floor. Rain from earlier has steamed off the windows, leaving the glass fogged. The room is a pulsing shrine of LEDs and analog glow—synth towers stacked like nuclear reactors. The storm has passed, but inside it’s still chaos.

They’ve run *“Twelve Dollars Left”* a half-dozen times tonight, drunker and louder each pass. Jeff’s 808 programming has given the song a machine pulse, precise but clinical. Jim’s been side-eyeing it from the couch all night, tapping his sticks impatiently on a pizza box.

Finally, he stands.

**JIM**  (half-slurred, half-grinning)  
Move. I’m driving.

Jeff starts to protest but Jim’s already rolling his kit closer, dragging cymbals, kicking cables aside like weeds. He slams down a beat in perfect sync with the 808—then hammers fills over it, triplets rattling the walls.

The room explodes. Suddenly there are two drum sounds in the air—Jim’s human thunder crashing against Jeff’s programmed cannons. It’s chaotic, glorious, drunk as hell. Everyone’s screaming over the racket.

**GILLEY**  (yelling over the noise)  
**THAT’S IT. BOTH. KEEP BOTH. LET IT FUCKING COLLIDE.**

Jim grins like he’s caught a lightning bolt. For once, no resistance. He’s in.

Bill, though? Bill is perched at his synth tower, stiff as a man asked to strip naked in church. He’s fiddling with knobs, muttering to himself.

**BILL** (flat)  
This is insane. My shit doesn’t belong here. You don’t want arpeggios over caveman woo-ing.

Gilley drops to one knee like he’s swearing fealty to a king, eyes wild, sweat dripping down his nose.

**GILLEY**  
No, Billy boy. I want the biggest thing you’ve ever built. I want you to crack the sky. You remember that ride? Blue Angels cockpit, banking over the goddamn ocean? That. Give me *that*. Sweeping. Terrifying. The sound of the world turning upside down five times a second.

Bill stares at him. That flight had scared him shitless and thrilled him in equal measure. The memory isn’t just visual—it’s physical. His stomach dropping, the horizon flipping.

**BILL** (cautious, still skeptical)  
How EDM are we talking here?

Jeff, without hesitation, adjusting his glasses:

**JEFF**  
Straight out of Prague.

The room goes still for half a beat. Bill blinks.

**BILL** (quiet)  
You want me to make a Czech rave out of twelve bucks and a pack of beer?

**JEFF**  
Exactly.

Gilley howls with laughter, nearly falling over.

**GILLEY**  
**YES. YES. FUCKING YES.**

Bill sighs, mutters something about lunatics, then spins to his rig. His fingers start pulling pads into the mix, slow at first, hesitant, then bigger, bolder. He layers arpeggios that rise like jet contrails, sweeping chords that spread like wings.

The room transforms again: Jeff’s synth bass snarling underneath, Jim pounding his kit into the 808s, Gilley screaming into the mic, Jeremy scratching out jagged guitar lines, and Bill, suddenly, reluctantly, painting the whole thing with panoramic sound.

The air vibrates. The song is ridiculous, deranged, but monumental. It feels like the room might levitate.

Jim slams a cymbal crash so hard it topples a half-empty beer. Gilley’s on the floor screaming *WOO WOO WOO*. Jeff is stone-faced, nodding in time, glasses fogged with sweat.

Bill closes his eyes. For a second, he’s back in that cockpit, seeing the world tilt sideways. And he keeps playing.

---

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE – 3:04 A.M. – 2017**

The room is drowning in sound. Layers upon layers, too much of everything: 808s still pulsing in one channel, Jim’s kit detonating like artillery in the other, Bill weaving synth trails wide as the sky, Jeff’s bass snarling like it wants to collapse the drywall. Gilley is bent over the mic stand, shirt stuck to him with sweat, howling another round of *WOO WOO WOO*.

Jeremy Evans hasn’t said a word for ten minutes. He’s in the corner, Strat strapped on, fingers sliding across the fretboard with the offhand authority of someone filling space he knows doesn’t belong to him. Chords here, jagged accents there. He knows Jeff and Bill don’t want riffage—they want vibration, sub-bass earthquakes, bodies bouncing. He’s just painting cracks in the wall.

Jim finishes a brutal fill that leaves the snare wobbling on its stand. He tosses his sticks onto the couch and laughs, breathless.

**JIM**  
Jesus Christ. That’s the most violent shit I’ve done since album one. Remember that? Had to haul my kit out into the goddamn hallway because I kept blowing lights.

**JEFF** (flat, calm)  
These subs are going to hit thermal limits. We’ll burn through cabinets by the second chorus.

**BILL** (dry, still tweaking knobs)  
I’ll rework the synth voicings, try to duck out of your big hits. Otherwise? We’re voiding warranties on a nightly basis.

**GILLEY** (grinning like a devil)  
Perfect. Rock and roll is nothing but voided warranties.

The room falls into a tense quiet, the storm of noise still ringing in their ears. Jeremy sets his guitar down on the amp.

**JEREMY EVANS** (flat, skeptical)  
Alright. You’ve got volume. You’ve got chaos. But you’re missing a center. Right now it’s just… weather.

Jeff pushes his glasses up, turns to him.

**JEFF**  
That’s why you’re here.

Jeremy stares at him like he’s speaking a foreign language.

**JEREMY EVANS**  
No. No fucking way. I shred. I don’t scream “woo” like a drunk frat boy!

Gilley stumbles over, throws an arm around Jeremy’s shoulders, beer breath and sweat thick as glue.

**GILLEY**  
Brother, we don’t want shred. We want you on the mic. We want your bite stacked on mine, and Bill’s ghost tones under it. A wall of sound, man. A *riot choir*.

**JEREMY EVANS** (half-laugh, half-snarl)  
You’re out of your goddamn mind.

Jim leans back in his chair, smirking.

**JIM**  
He’s right. You’re terrified. Which means you should do it.

Jeremy glares at him.

**JEREMY EVANS**  
Terrified? Of this circus? I’m not terrified. I’m insulted.

Jeff doesn’t blink.

**JEFF**  
Then prove it. Sing.

The silence hangs, thick. Bill looks up from his synth wall, actually watching Jeremy for once.

Jeremy exhales, grabs the mic like it owes him money, and snarls into it—ragged, harsh, nothing like Gilley’s howling. It cuts through the mix like barbed wire. Bill instinctively layers a harmony under it, low and spectral, and the sound becomes something new: not just chaos, but structure, menace with depth. 

The room vibrates like a live wire. Everyone freezes.

Then Gilley erupts, pounding Jeremy’s back like he’s just scored a touchdown.

**GILLEY**  
**YES. FUCK YES. THAT’S THE WALL.**

Jeremy drops the mic, shaking his head, muttering—

**JEREMY EVANS**  
You’re insane. You’re *all* insane.

But he doesn’t leave.  Jeff just nods, satisfied.

**JEFF**  
Good. Then it’s ready.

That’s the turning point: the moment *Twelve Dollars Left* stops being an experiment and becomes a **Commuted totem**.
