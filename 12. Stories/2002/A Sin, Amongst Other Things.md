## **INT. MGM GRAND GARDEN ARENA – MAIN STAGE – NIGHT – 2002**

*17,000 people. Las Vegas desert night. Steam, sweat, and thunder. The lights haven’t even gone down yet, and they’re already chanting. The stage smells like cordite and testosterone. The monitors hiss like vipers in heat.*

**Kathy is offstage—snorting something brutal off a laminated pass. Jeff is already at his bass, head down, knuckles tight. Bill and Jim share a look like they’re about to witness a public execution. Jeremy hasn’t said a word in 36 hours. Cremeans is watching from side stage with a Bible in one hand and a radio in the other. Gilley steps forward.**

He takes the mic. Doesn’t smile. Doesn’t blink.

**GILLEY (snarling):**
*“We’re not here for your fucking healing.”*

(beat)
*“We’re here to punish the idea that anything gets better.”*

Smoke rolls low across the stage like a brushfire looking for someone to blame. Lights still up. No house dim, no intro music. Just an arena full of people vibrating with anticipation and *not even remotely ready*. **Jeff is already on stage**, pacing like a caged animal, his bass slung low, amp growling in standby. He hasn’t looked anyone in the eye in twenty minutes. His fingers twitch on the fretboard—**he’s furious**, and nobody knows why. Probably doesn’t even matter anymore.

**Kathy is late** on purpose. Not fashionably, not dramatically—**spitefully**. When she finally emerges from side-stage, she’s barefoot, eyes pinwheeling, mascara halfway to her collarbones. She's wearing a see-through wrap she probably stole, and there’s still **cocaine in her smile**.

**Cremeans** watches from the wings, stone-faced, Mountain Dew in hand. His radio is on, but he’s not speaking into it.

**CREMEANS (quietly, to no one):**
“She’s gonna do the B-side. God help us.”

**Kathy saunters to the mic.** Grabs it like it owes her money.  She doesn’t greet the crowd.  She doesn’t check her monitor.  She just leans in and *pours venom*.

**KATHY (low, slurred but surgical):**
“You want the hit?”

*(beat)*
“Go fuck yourself. That was for the radio.”

*(beat)*
“This is for the ones who bled in the cheap seats.”

**Wham.**  A tremolo-drenched **Spaghetti Western guitar riff** cuts through the fog—**“Cinematic Desert Glow”**.  The *B-side.* Obscure. Introspective. Wrong for an arena.  But not tonight.  **Jim starts a stompbeat**, slow and predatory. **Bill layers in haunting synth swells**, like the wind echoing through a ghost town.  **Jeremy** slashes into the chord progression like he’s carving a name into stone.

And then Kathy sings:

> *The sun’s sinking low, it’s a fire in the sky…*
> *Feel the rhythm rise… can’t deny…*

**The lights drop to blood-red.** The crowd is *absolutely paralyzed*.  **Jeff hasn’t moved.** He’s glaring across the stage at Kathy, hands on the bass, daring her to miss a note.  She doesn’t.  She *slays* it.  Coked-out, off-tempo, perfect in the wrongest way.  By the **bridge**, people are crying in the pit.  By the **last chorus**, someone throws a bra, someone else throws a punch.

*Raise your voice in the desert night…*
*Fallout settling, bathed in light…*

**Kathy lets the final line hang in silence.**

*Underneath the fire’s final show…*

Then she drops the mic.  And walks off.  **Jeff doesn't even watch her go.** He just hammers one final **bass note**—*so deep it feels like an aftershock*—then turns his back on the crowd. No applause line.  No smile.  Just **wreckage**.
