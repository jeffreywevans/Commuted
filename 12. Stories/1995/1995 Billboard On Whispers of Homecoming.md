**📰 Billboard Early Review – June 27, 1995**

**"Commuted’s 'Whispers of Homecoming' Has \#1 Written All Over It"**
**Byline:**
Vanessa McKay, Senior Music Correspondent, *Billboard Magazine*

---

**Opening Paragraph:**

"\*While controversy still swirls around Commuted’s upcoming full-length *That Look of Release*, one thing is already abundantly clear:

Their lead leak — *Whispers of Homecoming* — isn't just a song.  It’s an *emotional demolition team* dressed up in slow-dancing nostalgia."

---

**Soft Power, Hard Impact**

"From the opening chords shimmering like autumn leaves,  to Kathy Espich’s impossibly tender vocal performance,  *Whispers of Homecoming* feels less like a recording and more like a shared memory you forgot you were still carrying around.*"

"The lyrics tread that delicate line between innocent affection and barely-contained longing — a balancing act so deft it feels almost dangerous.*"

"By the time Espich murmurs the now-legendary chorus, *'You won’t need a room,'*  
the listener isn’t just hearing the story — they’re inside it, heart pounding, remembering the one person they never quite stopped loving."

---

**Commercial Outlook:**

"*Radio programmers are already buzzing:*  a soft launch at one Midwestern station reportedly triggered over 200 listener calls within the first two hours.*"

"\*This track doesn’t just have staying power —  it has gravitational pull. Expect *Whispers of Homecoming* to debut high and linger long."
