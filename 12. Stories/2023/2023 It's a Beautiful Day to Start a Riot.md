# ROLLING STONE

## _December 24, 2023 | 3:13 PM EST_

### “It’s a Beautiful Day to Start a Riot”:

## **Commuted Breaks the Internet With the Most Chaotic Lineup Change in Rock History**

_By Casey Maxwell | Senior Writer (Holiday Overtime, Don’t Ask)_

---

At 7:00 AM this morning, most of us were contemplating a third cinnamon roll and muting our parents’ group chat. Not _Commuted_ fans. While the rest of the country was blearily opening gifts, the most infamous band of the last quarter-century was handing the world the nuclear code for a fandom meltdown.

> [!warning] **HOLY SHIT**
> **Avril Lavigne. Lead vocals. Commuted 3.0. This is not a drill.**

Welcome to Christmas Eve, 2023—a day that will live in infamy, euphoria, and probably at least three therapist bills.

### The HBO Era Meets Reality

To understand why Twitter, Reddit, and at least one Toronto Tim Hortons are still melting down twelve hours after the news, let’s recap: HBOMax’s Emmy-dominating _Commuted: American Ruin_ dropped two seasons of prestige chaos.

- _Season One_ took us from Bethel Township garages (so much flannel, so much self-loathing) to the first taste of Midwest mythmaking.
- _Season Two_ was pure gasoline: the making of _69 Reasons_, the iconic cover that got three chain stores banned in Alabama, and enough behind-the-music carnage to make Fleetwood Mac look like The Wiggles.  

The show broke streaming records. So did the band—just, you know, for going on five-year hiatus after Gilley’s final on-stage meltdown and that 2018 “temporary” farewell tour.

**Nobody, and I mean nobody, expected this band to resurrect.** Certainly not on Christmas Eve. And certainly not with Avril “Sk8er Boi” Lavigne carrying the torch that Kathy Espich lit (and, let’s be honest, doused with whiskey and set on fire).

### Chaos Unwrapped: How It Happened

Let’s review the hour-by-hour trainwreck, as only Rolling Stone’s group chat can assemble:

- **12:01 AM:** The AP drops a “sources say” bomb: Avril is the new Commuted vocalist. The subreddit r/commuted instantly transforms from a graveyard to a war zone. Discord mods call in sick.
- **2:01 AM:** CBC confirms. Canadian Twitter achieves national self-actualization. Poutine memes surge 2000%.
- **3:17 AM:** Commuted’s X account posts the Canadian national anthem. No caption. “O Canada” becomes the soundtrack to a million panic attacks.
- **7:00 AM:** The band’s website banner swaps out the classic “Ohio Four” silhouette for a five-piece, the new middle shadow unmistakably female, with a flash of platinum hair and what the internet swears is a tartan skirt.
- **1:01 PM:** A casual bomb: “Commuted @ Ohio Stadium, April 13th. Might be fun.” The included photo—Core Four, plus a woman with a maple leaf patch, entering The Shoe. No face, all attitude.

### Fandom: DEFCON 1

Imagine the Beatles announcing Yoko as their new frontwoman—on _Christmas Eve_, during a royal wedding, while the World Cup is tied at 0-0 in stoppage time.  Now double it.

“Mods are running trauma support hotlines,” posted one Discord user. “If you don’t see me at halftime, assume I died on the queue.”

By 9 AM, every corner of the internet had picked sides:

- **Never Surrender:** Kathy loyalists, quoting “Left Hand” and threatening to riot if Avril so much as _hums_ “Come Inside Me.”
- **Avril’s Army:** The pop-punk faithful, meme-lording their way through every Canadian joke in the arsenal.
- **The Trolls:** Every band in America, from The 1975 to Jack White, dropping “O Canada” covers and trying to get in on the chaos.
- **Industry Vultures:** MLB, Universal, even Joey Votto tweeting, “Nice hat, eh?” like he’s in on a heist.

Meanwhile, Commuted themselves stayed almost totally silent. Jeff posted a selfie with a sticky note—“Hi, Reddit!”—just to poke the bear. Jim Brown posted a Mountie hat facepalm. Bill went full thirst trap in a Team Canada soccer jersey. Jeremy? He’s asking for Maple Leafs tickets while wearing a Blue Jackets sweater and grinning like he just set the penalty box on fire.

### The Stakes: More Than a Comeback

Here’s what makes this more than a “just another lineup change” headline.  **Commuted didn’t just lose a singer. They lost an axis.**

Kathy Espich wasn’t replaceable—and for five years, nobody tried. Every time the “who should front the band?” debate surfaced, it ended in scorched earth. If HBO’s dramatization is even half true, nobody’s ever _wanted_ the job, least of all anyone with a Top 40 hit and a platinum hair straightener.

And yet…AvriI Lavigne was there when it mattered. Two tracks on _Be First, Be Smarter, or Cheat_ (yes, _that_ “36 Degrees Fahrenheit”—Grammy winner, viral earworm, code for heartbreak and betrayal). A whispered affair with Jeff Evans—publicly confirmed on a BBC2 interview in a moment that instantly entered rock’s Mount Rushmore of “oh shit” TV.

Now, with Commuted 3.0, the calculus is different. The past is chaos. The future is open.  
Will Avril “Canadian Kathy” Lavigne conquer The Shoe? Or will she become just another ghost in Commuted’s haunted machine?

### “Might Be Fun”: What Comes Next

April 13th, 2024. Ohio Stadium. First show in five years.  Avril at the mic, four men daring the world to say it’s not real.  A fandom divided but wide awake.

The setlist will be dissected for weeks before the show. Will they dare open with “The Hilton”? Will “Left Hand” be holy ground, or will it be torched anew? Is this a one-off, or is it the start of a new era—a Midwestern resurrection with a Canadian twist?

**One thing is certain: No one is sleeping tonight. Not the fans. Not the band. And definitely not your humble Rolling Stone reporter.**

Merry Christmas. Let the games begin.
