# **BAND DEMOCRACY**

---

# **CHAPTER ONE: OF THE PEOPLE**

---

## **INT. BBC STUDIO – LONDON – DECEMBER 4, 2023 – 11:11 A.M.**

The set is too clean. It smells like static and starch. White floors. Black curtains. A wall of LEDs standing in for atmosphere — sterile blues, tasteful monochrome, the kind of austerity the British mistake for dignity. Stagehands whisper behind clipboards like nurses preparing end-of-life care.

There are four director’s chairs, perfectly spaced, arranged like a sentencing panel.

In them: the four remaining members of Commuted.

**Jeff Evans**, bassist and architect of the band’s spine, sits stage left. Stocky, grey at the temples, his once-luxurious hair now a hesitant tide pulled back behind his ears. The wire-rimmed glasses are new — or maybe just less defiant. He’s wearing boots that still look gig-ready and a jacket that smells like tour buses and resignation.

Next to him: **Jeremy Evans**, younger brother, lead guitarist, and the band’s onetime pyromaniac. Bald now — deliberately so, clean as vengeance — with the kind of marathoner’s build that only comes from divorce and too many audiobooks. He holds himself like a man who knows how cameras lie, back straight, legs crossed, fingers fidgeting with his wedding ring.

Center-right: **Jim Brown**, drummer, chaos engine, grudgeholder emeritus. He slouches with theatrical ease, as if leaning backward could somehow turn back time. His black hoodie is unzipped over a shirt from the 2002 tour, as if daring someone to point it out. His hair, once wild, is buzzed tight now, the widow’s peak as unapologetic as the scowl it brackets.

Far right: **Bill Davenport**, synthesizer surgeon and accidental monk. Built like a squat statue, chin tucked, gaze sharp. His arms are crossed like a father at a school play. He’s wearing the kind of black-on-black ensemble that rich men wear to look poor. His watch is antique. His patience, thinner.

The five-piece is now four.

The missing weight is everywhere.

**Kathy Espich**, frontwoman, hurricane, matriarch, *dead* — 2013. Aneurysm in Boston. They don’t say her name yet.

**Jeremy Gilley**, rhythm guitar, co-founder, charming bastard, *dead* — 2020. Stroke in bed at his crash pad outside LA. They *might* say his name. Once.

Behind the chairs: a single line of white light cuts the stage like a scar. A silhouette of the band’s old logo flickers faintly above them — half erased, like a ghost refusing to haunt properly.

This isn’t a reunion.  It’s a post-mortem.  And they know it.

They are global rock royalty now — survivors, yes, but not unscathed. The aura has calcified. The hair is thinner, the shoulders stiffer, the jokes slower. But the *charge* is still there. A battery with just enough juice to cause sparks, if someone’s dumb enough to start touching wires.

The BBC host has a notecard and a nervous smile. The cameras are already rolling.  No one breathes quite right.  
Then — the first question.  The one that opens the wound.

Cut to: Jeff. Lips twitching. Eyes narrowing. And something old, something beautifully undemocratic, beginning to stir.

---

Excellent bones — now let’s strip them, scar them up, and rebuild the stage in full bandwidth. You want to feel like **you’re there**, under those buzzing studio lights, watching these legends try not to flinch under the weight of their own myth. This is no longer just a Q&A. It’s a **seance disguised as a special**. Here’s the rewritten, extended, and fully maximalist version:

## **INT. BBC2 ALEXANDRA PALACE STUDIO – NORTH LONDON – DECEMBER 4, 2023 – NIGHT**

It’s too bright in here.

The studio is a gleaming white vault, the kind of post-industrial austerity that makes even legends look like suspects. Nothing soft. No backdrop. No props. No sanctuary. Just floor-to-ceiling light panels and four men arranged like museum pieces — living artifacts lit from above, aging rock gods caught in the cold geometry of live television.

The cameras are rolling.

The clock says 8:47 p.m., though no one’s keeping time except the floor manager, who taps her headset like she’s trying to restart her brain.

Stage left, a ***BBC logo glows in matte white*** across a massive vertical screen. Center stage: **four black director’s chairs**, set in a hard semicircle. No table. No coffee mugs. No fucking nostalgia candles. Just the glare of four thousand watts and the ambient hum of a soundstage that smells like bleach and old plywood.

The men look… weathered. That’s the polite word.

**JEFF EVANS**, bassist, sits with one foot hooked around a chair leg, his spine curved into a shape that says “don’t ask me to believe in this.” His hands — big, square, slow — drum restlessly on his knees. Hair thinned to the point of a polite retreat, he still carries himself like a man who could lift an amp cabinet one-handed. But there’s a tightness around the eyes. A fatigue that didn’t used to be there. His tour jacket is vintage. The **authentic kind**, the kind with actual sweat in the lining.

Beside him, **JEREMY EVANS**, younger brother, lead guitar, coiled and lean. Bald by choice and genetics, marathon-trim and aggressively alert. There’s a twitch in his jaw like he’s biting back opinions or panic or both. His shirt is black, simple, fitted. His boots are clean. His hands haven’t stopped moving since they sat down.

**JIM BROWN**, the drummer — and everyone’s favorite controlled detonation — lounges next. Hoodie. Beard. The same stare he gave MTV producers in ’95 when they asked him not to swear. There’s a flask peeking out of his back pocket. It’s not subtle. He’s smiling, but it’s the kind that warns you: ***Don’t. Push. Me.***

**BILL DAVENPORT**, synth god and secret moral compass, sits last. Thick arms folded. Wrists heavy with silver. A black Henley, sleeves rolled. He’s the only one not fidgeting. Not blinking. The only one who looks like he might be thinking twelve steps ahead, but still wouldn’t save you if you choked on your own question.

All of them have aged — obviously, brutally, *correctly*. Fifty-something in rock years is like dog years plus war trauma. They are scarred, slouched, sober where it counts. Royalty. But the ***kind*** that made their kingdom from **basements**, **busted amps**, and **fistfights in radio stations**. Commuted was never built for legacy. It was built to survive. And they did.

Barely.

Missing: **KATHY ESPICH** — vocalist, matriarch, icon — gone ten years this coming spring. Brain hemorrhage in a hotel room in Berlin. No warning. No encore.

Missing: **JEREMY GILLEY** — rhythm guitar, wit incarnate, dead three years from a stroke in Portugal. He died with a cigarette in his mouth and a song on a USB stick. They found him mid-mix.

The absence of these two is a fifth and sixth presence onstage. You can feel it in how they sit — **wider apart**, **less certain**, like they’re still saving room.

Across from them, **JOOLS HOLLAND**, the last man on Earth who can pronounce “post-punk” without flinching, holds a notecard and a pen like they might protect him. Polished shoes. Navy suit. Hair swept back with British elegance and funeral energy.

He nods solemnly, voice crisp.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
Gentlemen, welcome. It’s… good to see you together again. Even if the occasion isn’t easy.

**JEFF**  (gruff, brushing something off his knee)  
Better to be seen than embalmed.

**JEREMY**  (still staring at the floor)  
That’s optimistic.

**JIM**  (sotto, to no one)  
First mistake.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (smiling lightly, careful)  
The last time we sat down like this was… what, 2008? The *Tethered Tour*?

**BILL**  (still hasn’t blinked)  
The night before Gilley punched a lighting tech in Helsinki.

**JEFF**  
He deserved it.

**JIM**  
The tech or Gilley?

**JEREMY**  
Both.

Laughter. Small. Brief. Then it fades.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (quiet now)  
Of course, since then… you’ve lost two of your own.

(A silence drops like glass.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  Kathy in 2013. Gilley in 2020.  
Their absence is… felt.

**JIM**  (leans forward, deadpan)  
Like a missing lung.

**JEFF**  
Or a bass string tuned too tight. You don’t notice until it snaps.

**JEREMY**  
Or you just keep playing wrong, and it starts to sound right.

**BILL**  
That’s how you get post-rock.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And how has that… loss, changed you?

**JEFF**  (shrugs)  
Less noise.  More ghosts.

**JEREMY**  
It’s harder to lie in the studio now.

**JIM**  
Which sucks, because lying was the fun part.

**BILL**  (grim smile)  
Now we just work. Quietly. Until we don't.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And creatively? Has anything been brewing?

**JEREMY**  (stiffens)  
You mean are we still writing?

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
Yes.

**JIM**  (barks a dry laugh)  
Writing, sure. Recording? Not so much. Touring? We’ll see.  Surviving? So far.

**JEFF**  
The new stuff doesn’t sound like us. Or it sounds too much like us. Either way, it’s a problem.

**BILL**  
Or it’s not. Maybe we just stop pretending the band’s a democracy.

(That lands.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND** (eyebrow raised)  
Interesting phrase. Was it ever?

**JIM**  (frowning)  
Depends on who’s sober and who’s horny.

**JEFF**  
Depends on the year.

**JEREMY**  
Depends on whether Kathy was in the room.

(Another silence. This one hurts more.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And now?

**JEFF**  
Now it’s just math.  Four chords. Four members. Four funerals pending.

**BILL**  
So we keep playing. Until one of us can’t.

(Beat.)

The light above them buzzes. A camera light goes red. The floor manager waves a five-minute warning. Nobody blinks.

They don’t know it yet, but the clip of this moment will go viral within 24 hours. Fans will call it haunting. Critics will call it proof. The band will call it ***Tuesday.***

## **INT. ALEXANDRA PALACE STUDIO – NIGHT – CONTINUED**

The air’s gone thick.

The heat from the lights feels cruel now, interrogative. Sweat beads on Jim’s neck. Jeff rubs the side of his nose like it might buy him time. Bill adjusts the cuff of his sleeve, once, then stops himself. Jeremy doesn’t move.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (casual, but sharp)  
Kathy’s absence is still… palpable.  Her voice — so distinct, so *inhabited* — was foundational.  How have you coped with that kind of loss?

**JIM**  (sits up, voice clipped)  
Look, man. We’re here. Ain’t that enough?

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (mild)  
Of course. But for many, the album *Be First, Be Smarter, or Cheat* was your public reckoning. It felt like a eulogy, like a raw open wound…

**JEREMY**  (cutting in, sudden)  
It was a fucking *accident*.

(Beat.)

**JEREMY**  
That album should never have happened.

**BILL**  (low, almost inaudible)  
It didn’t happen. It escaped.

**JEFF**  (still calm)  
We were grieving. I was grieving. It was a way through.

**JIM**  (grimacing)  
Through what, Jeff? The sound of your own fucking heartbeat?

(Jeff stiffens.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (apologetic, but drawn in)  
So it wasn’t a… collaboration?

**JEREMY**  
Oh, *technically* we all signed off. But don’t mistake *survival* for *consent*.

**JEFF**  
It was about honoring her.

**JIM**  
It was about controlling the story.

(The room freezes for a beat. Even the floor manager stops moving.)

**JEFF**  (quiet)  
That’s not fair.

**JEREMY**  
Neither was burying her and cutting the vocals three months later.

**BILL**  
We told you. She wasn’t *gone* yet. She was just… offstage.

**JEFF**  
You’re rewriting history.

**JIM**  
You *wrote* the history. In Logic Pro. Alone.

(Beat. Jeff breathes out slowly, hands folded between his knees. Then—)

**JEFF**  (smiling, suddenly)  
Remember when John Legend came by the studio?

(Everyone turns.)

**JEREMY**  (low)  
Oh, fuck me.

**JIM**  
You’re not doing this.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (blinking)  
Wait—John Legend?

**JEFF**  
Yeah. April 2013. He was in Boston for something — Harvard talk, I think. Big fan. Reached out through some assistant. We got him in the room for a weekend.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (leaning in)  
That’s…extraordinary. So he played on the record?

**JEFF**  
Oh yeah. Two tracks, actually. Nothing flashy. Just some beautiful, haunted piano lines. Understated, but *surgical*. You can hear him if you know where to listen.

**JEREMY**  (flatly)  
You layered him in at 3 a.m., Jeff. Nobody else signed off.

**JIM**  
Didn’t even *know* until the mastering session.

**BILL**  
We don’t talk about that record.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (still pressing)  
But that kind of collaboration — the merging of grief, legend, and legacy — surely that’s—

**JEREMY**  
(interrupting, sharp)  
No.

**JIM**  
We played the funeral. That was the album.

**BILL**  
Anything after that was…  

(a beat)  
…Jeff’s love letter to himself.

(The silence that follows is volcanic.)

Jeff doesn’t respond. His hands are clasped tight. His knee bounces, once, then stops.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (softly)  
Do you regret making it?

(Jeff looks up. His eyes are glass.)

**JEFF**  
No.  But I regret… being alone with it.

**JEREMY**  
And now so do we.

(Another silence. Brittle. Hot.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (sitting back)  
Understood.  Let’s take a short break.

A red light clicks off. The studio falls into that strange half-light that comes between takes. Someone offscreen coughs. Jim reaches for the flask. Jeremy pulls his sleeves down. Bill stares into space like he’s back in that mixing room, hearing Kathy’s voice echo into oblivion.

Jeff closes his eyes.

Cue: Black screen. Fade to archival footage.

# **CHAPTER TWO: BY THE PEOPLE**

## **INT. ALEXANDRA PALACE STUDIO – NIGHT – CONTINUED**

The tension doesn’t break so much as shift — like weather moving sideways.

**BILL**  (sits up slightly, voice dry)  
Yeah, John was there a couple days.  That opening track — the one with the ghost-piano and bowed strings?  He wrote the first two chords. I wrote the rest.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (eyes going wide)  
Wait — that was a *John Legend* original?

**BILL**  (chuckling)  
Two chords. But yeah. The rest of it?  Viola was mine. And the synth bed under it. That whole intro… that’s me playing like I forgot what key grief was in.

**JIM**  (grinning)  
And forgot to tune the viola.

**BILL** (ignores him)  
Two takes. Second one was perfect. I still have the bleed on cassette.

**JEREMY**  (muttering)  
Of course you do.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (still stunned)  
That’s—  

(truly impressed)  
That’s actually incredible.

**BILL**  (flat)  
I know.

(Beat. Jeff shifts in his chair.)

**JEFF**  (soft, thoughtful)  
It was a special time. A time of healing.  We were… all lost.

(The phrasing hangs in the air like steam.)

**JEFF** (CONT’D)  
Music was how we stayed alive.  That album—*Be First, Be Smarter, Or Cheat*—it saved us.

**JEREMY**  (low)  
Here we fucking go.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (leaning in)  
So it really was about *coping*.  That’s never been said so directly before.

**JEFF** (nods slowly)  
No, it hasn’t.  But it was our way through. We were bleeding.  The studio was the only place we weren’t falling apart.

**JIM**  (staring at the floor, dry)  
Speak for yourself.

**BILL**  (sotto)  
He always does.

(A long pause.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
It’s a powerful testament, truly. And such a unique sound.  The strings, the empty space in the mix.  You even used viola.

**BILL**  (grinning now)  
Yeah. Weird day. John was noodling these falling thirds, something gospel-adjacent. I grabbed this busted old viola from the back closet. The thing had *duct tape* on the bridge. I don’t even know why it was in the building.

**JEREMY**  
You said it was haunted.

**BILL**  
And it *was.*  It tuned itself like it wanted to be played. First pass was trash. Second pass?  

(clicks his fingers)  
Felt like summoning.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And you kept it?

**BILL**  
I *insisted.*  Jeff wanted to cut it.

**JEFF**  (raising hands, smiling)  
I was wrong! That’s why we collaborate.

(The look Jeremy gives him could peel paint.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
So what other… strange collaborations happened in that window?

(Beat. You can *feel* Jeff prepping the pivot.)

**JEFF**  (grinning slightly)  
Well—I mean, there *was* that one night—

**JIM**  (eyes narrowing)  
Oh Jesus.

**JEREMY**  
Jeff—

**JEFF**  (ignores them)  
We were pulling a twenty-hour session. Everyone’s fried. And I had this moment— Just this *clarity*—

**JIM**  
Don’t say it.

**JEFF**  
Avril Lavigne.

(A full-body *groan* from the rest of the band.)

**BILL**  
God *damn* it.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (eyes lighting up like Christmas)  
Wait—Avril *Lavigne*?

**JEFF**  
Dead serious. I heard her voice on top of Track Six. I could *hear* it. That scratchy, glassy scream-into-the-phone thing she does— It would’ve *made* the bridge.

**JEREMY**  
We had *Kathy’s stems*, Jeff.

**JEFF**  
Exactly. It would’ve been a *duet*. A passing of the torch.

**JIM**  
It would’ve been a fucking *seance*.

**BILL**  
She was still married to the guy from Nickelback. You think *that* was the right time?

**JEFF**  
She *called me back,* okay?

(Silence.)

**JEFF**  
She listened. She liked the track. She wanted to come in. It just didn’t happen.

**JEREMY**  
Because *we stopped it*, Jeff.  Because that record was already bleeding out. And you wanted to pump it full of *collabs* like it was an awards show.

**JIM**  
We needed closure.  Not fucking Canadian pop-punk.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (to camera)  
We’re going to take a short break.

(The red light clicks off. The floor manager steps in. Nobody moves.)

Jeremy rubs his eyes. Jim mutters something obscene and pulls the flask. Bill leans back, unreadable. Jeff stares at his shoes like they owe him an answer.

---

## **INT. ALEXANDRA PALACE STUDIO – NIGHT – CONTINUED**

The red light is back on.  And Jeff?

Jeff is glowing. Or sweating. Or both. His hands are loose now. His mouth unzipped. He’s passed the point of moderation and entered the gilded chapel of over-share. He’s not answering questions. He’s steering the ship.

**JEFF**  (sipping water he wishes was wine)  
So yeah, I had her flown in.  Avril. We… *worked* together.

(He does the air quotes.)

Everyone freezes.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (blinks slowly)  
I’m sorry — just to clarify — You’re saying Avril Lavigne was flown in to *collaborate* with the band?

**JEREMY**  (cutting in, venomous)  
No. He’s saying *he* flew her in. Solo. No warning. No context.

**JEFF** (smoothly)  
She wanted to be part of it. We vibed. She heard the roughs, loved the material. Stayed for a week.

**JIM**  (eyebrows raised)  
Week and a half, if I remember. You were *radiating*.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (stunned)  
And you played together?

**JEFF**  
Yeah. That one track — “Perfect Weapon.”  The solo you thought was Jeremy? That was Avril.  

(beat)  
She nailed it in two takes.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (turning to Jeremy)  
That… wasn’t you?

**JEREMY**  (eyes hard, nostrils flaring)  
Nope.  I had the flu. Came back. The part was done.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (quietly)  
That’s… a revelation.

**JEFF**  (grinning now)  
She was magic. Truly. We needed a new frequency. She had it.

**BILL**  
You mean *you* needed it.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And was this purely creative? Or was there… something else?

(Jeff exhales. A beat of performative shame.)

**JEFF**  
It was complicated.

**JEREMY**  (muttering)  
You absolute piece of shit.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (to Jeff)  
Care to elaborate?

**JEFF**  (taking a long pause)  
We were both unraveling.  She was out of her label contract, I was… in freefall.  It was never supposed to be more than a few nights. But it…  

(beat)  
…turned into something.

**JIM**  (sotto, chuckling darkly)  
What he mean to say was that he fucked her sideways for a year.

**JEFF**  
It was brief. Messy.  But it pulled something out of me that I thought was dead.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And you wrote through it?

**JEFF**  
Every fucking night.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
So this wasn’t just a collaboration. It was… inspiration?

**JEFF**  
It was like bleeding through a filter made of silk and gasoline.  We burned together. We made something *real*.

(The rest of the band is dead silent.)

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
And she knew today's revelation would be made public?

**JEFF**  
No.  

(Shrugs.)  
But it’s time.

(The cameras roll. Somewhere in the control room, an engineer mutters, “Jesus fuck.”)

**PRODUCER** (O.S., into comms)  
We’re already live. Legal can get stuffed.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (softly, with reverence and a hint of hunger)  
Well.  That’s quite a scoop.

**JEFF** (smiles, wet-eyed)  
It happened.  It mattered.  It’s on the record now.

**JEREMY**  (snarling)  
So was Kathy’s fucking *ghost track*. You gonna spill that too?

**JEFF**  
Maybe.

**BILL**  
Then we’re done.

(The red light blinks. The floor manager waves. They cut to commercial.)

Jeff sits there, blinking, thrilled and destroyed. He doesn’t know which yet.  The others are already halfway out of their chairs.  Backstage, a BBC social media intern is texting the word **“BREAKING”** in all caps.

# **CHAPTER THREE: FOR THE PEOPLE**

## **INT. ALEXANDRA PALACE STUDIO – NIGHT – CONTINUED**

The air is molten. No one moves. The stage lights hum. A bead of sweat slips down the back of Jim’s neck. Bill exhales, finally. Jeremy’s fists are clenched so tight his knuckles gleam under the LEDs.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (tentative, polite)  
Well… thank you for your honesty.  This has been a… *revealing* conversation.

**JEFF**  (grinning like a man who just burned his house down)  
Yeah. No problem.

(He leans back, satisfied. His knee bounces. He thinks he’s liberated something.)

From the **control booth**, a voice cuts in — unheard by those on set, but bleeding into our scene like static on tape.

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT (O.S., intercom)**  
Uhh, guys… are we sure about this? That last bit’s nuclear.

**DIRECTOR (O.S., intercom)**  
Yeah. But it’s *excellent* television. Keep rolling.

Back on set, the silence is breaking — crooked and awkward.

**JIM**  (smiling bitterly)  
Well. That escalated quickly.

**JEREMY**  (low, to himself)  
Un-fucking-believable.

**JEFF**  (still high off his own transparency)  
Felt good, though.  Been carrying that around for a decade.

(That’s when **Jeremy turns.** Not just in his chair — he *faces* him now. Head-on. Eyes dark.)

**JEREMY**  
Yeah?  You mean ***she*** felt good, right?

(Beat.)

**JEREMY**  
Her ***cunt*** felt good.  You couldn’t stop talking about it. Not even in the *fucking* studio.  We were recording *grief,* Jeff.  
And you were hard the whole goddamn time.

(Jeff stiffens. The smile disappears.)

**BILL** (low)  
Jesus Christ.

**JIM**  (to no one)  
…here we go.

**JEFF**  (flat, defensive)  
It wasn’t like that.

**JEREMY**  
Bullshit.  It was *exactly* like that.  You wanted to fuck your way into a new sound.  You used her voice like it was yours to bend. Just like you did with Kathy.

**JEFF**  (voice rising)  
*Don’t you fucking bring Kathy into this.*

**JEREMY**  
You did that already.

(Beat.)

**JEFF**  
I *loved* her.

**JEREMY**  
No.  You ***possessed*** her.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (quietly)  
We may need to—

**BILL**  (sharply)  
Don’t you *dare* cut.  Let it happen.

**JIM**  
Let him burn.

Jeff sits there, chest rising, face flushed. He knows what’s coming next. So do we.  This isn’t the moment they fall apart.  This is the moment they finally admit they already have.

## **INT. ALEXANDRA PALACE STUDIO – NIGHT – CONTINUED**

The light has dimmed somehow. Not in brightness — but in spirit. Even Jools feels it. He shifts, recalibrates. Then, he tries a softer tack.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  
Let’s talk about “Thirty-Six Degrees Fahrenheit.”  A classic, yes — but an enigma.  How did that one… emerge?

**JEFF**  (pauses, playing casual)  
That one was a bit of an accident. I was just— messing around, and the melody fell out.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (grinning)  
A happy accident, then?

**JEFF**  (stretching the lie)  
Yeah. Cold day in the studio.  Everyone was bundled up.  And out of nowhere — this riff, this… little haunted thing.

**JOOLS HOLLAND**  (nods)  
And the title?

**JEFF**
