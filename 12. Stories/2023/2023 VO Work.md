## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – LATE AFTERNOON – AUGUST 2023**

The studio is as much shrine as workspace. Warm, heavy Ohio air. Dust swirling golden through the window, catching on motes of skin and old secrets. Shelves stacked with outmoded gear, cables looped like snake skeletons. A Rhodes piano and a modular synth wall that hums when no one’s looking. Four men, forty-something, splayed across mismatched furniture: Jeff in his faded flannel, Bill behind the battered console, Jeremy with a Marathon t-shirt tight on a runner’s frame, Jim in his classic black hoodie, a coffee cup that once saw war.

A silence that means respect, or exhaustion, or both.

A paper sits on the table, Quentin Tarantino’s chicken-scratch and the thick scent of magic marker. It reads:  
_"This story’s too wild to trust to a camera alone. Maybe you boys let a voice lead the way."_

BILL  
(leans back, hands laced, skeptical as always)  
So… Tarantino wants a narrator. Like _goodfellas_, but with less cocaine and more restraining orders.

JEFF  
(snorts)  
We already gave him everything—thirty years of tapes, diaries, legal threats, my fucking bar mitzvah video. Now he wants to _explain_ us?

JIM  
(quiet, low)  
He wants a spine. Something that ties it all together. Makes it myth, not just montage.

JEREMY  
(bouncing a drumstick on his knee, eyes sharp)  
What, our lives aren’t enough? Needs a voice to say “In that moment, Commuted realized the blow job lyric would destroy Cincinnati forever…”?

BILL  
(grinning, biting)  
Or worse. “As Bill Davenport turned up the arpeggiator, he realized his last remaining testicle was about to vaporize.” I can write this shit myself, Quentin.

JEFF  
(running a hand through his hair, voice clipped)  
It’s about control, isn’t it? Tarantino doesn’t trust the images, he doesn’t trust us. He wants to script the mess, steer the ship through voiceover.

JIM  
Or maybe he gets that we’re impossible to follow without a tour guide.  
(murmurs, mostly to himself)  
I mean, half our fans think Gilley died in 2006.

JEREMY  
(sharp, defensive)  
He didn’t die. He moved to Palm Springs and started fucking Pilates instructors.

BILL  
(laughing, then suddenly serious)  
Who narrates? That’s the only question that matters. You get some prissy British actor, they’ll make us sound like Masterpiece Theatre.  
(smiles at Jim)  
Get someone like Lyonne, you might actually smell the sweat.

JIM  
(nods, reflective)  
Or get someone who hates us. Make it raw. Make it mean.

JEFF  
What about—  
(pauses, frowning)  
What about the music? If you overlay narration, you’re stepping on Kathy’s lines. On _my_ lines.

JEREMY  
You want fans to get it, or you want them to feel it? There’s a difference. Some things can’t be said out loud.

BILL  
And yet, Tarantino wants it said. Hell, maybe he’s right. Maybe myth needs a fucking narrator.

JIM  
Myth _is_ the narrator.

Long silence. Outside, the church bells ring, a little off key. None of them flinch.

JEFF  
Let’s just be honest: a voiceover is only as good as the truth it’s willing to tell. If we get final cut, I say yes.  
(soft, almost kind)  
But only if they’re willing to say everything. Even the shit that hurts.

BILL  
And fuck the price, right? We’ll just sell another story to HBO.

JEREMY  
Raises his coffee cup.  
To the voice in our heads, then. May it never sound like our dads.

JIM  
(soft, prayerful, to the ceiling)  
Let it be someone who knows the difference between sin and salvation.

They all sit, the August heat settling on their skin. The note from Tarantino glows in the sunbeam, the ink threatening to fade. No decision made. But a pulse of possibility. Out in the street, the city keeps time, waiting for someone to speak first.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – LATE AFTERNOON, CONTINUOUS**

A sound. Not laughter exactly, but that shared exhale you only get with people who know your wounds. Jeremy leans forward, elbows on his knees, eyes sharp beneath that three-day stubble, half-amused, half-protective.

**JEREMY**  
(lighting a fuse)  
Jeff. Dude. You were raised a Jehovah’s Witness. _I_ was in the same goddamn household. Now you’re out here talking about “myth” and “salvation” like you’ve got a sideline in televangelism.

He shoots Jeff a look, equal parts challenge and care.

**JEREMY (CONT’D)**  
Are you feeling okay? Is this… is this your “I want a narrator” phase, or do you just need to be excommunicated again?

**JEFF**  
(doesn’t flinch, just grins, a little feral)  
I was also raised on Rush. And Talking Heads. I contain multitudes, little brother.

**JIM**  
(grinning, dry)  
Multitudes, sure. And unresolved trauma.

**BILL**  
(to Jeff, teasing but with warmth)  
You want myth, Jeff? Or you just want a way to rewrite the parts where we look like assholes?

**JEFF**  
(turning, deadpan)  
Aren’t those the best parts?

He glances at Jeremy, not angry, not defensive—just exposed for a second, that old sibling ache.

**JEFF (CONT’D)**  
Maybe I want the narrator to tell it like it was. Maybe I want someone else to admit I’m not the villain in every story.

**JEREMY**  
(smiling, gentler)  
You were never the villain. Just the guy with the best words for regret.

Bill makes a show of scribbling something in his notebook. Jim drums out a funeral march on the table.

**BILL**  
Okay, so: Tarantino gets his narrator. But only if Jeff signs off on the gospel, Jeremy provides the heresy, and Jim writes the footnotes.

**JIM**  
Deal. But I get final say on the soundtrack.

Jeremy claps Jeff on the back, harder than necessary, then doesn’t let go.

**JEREMY**  
Seriously, man. We all survived the same damn dogma. Maybe letting someone else tell the story is the only way we get to hear it.

A quiet. Not peace, but something close—a truce, hard-won, banded together in the late August light.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – LATE AFTERNOON – STILL AUGUST 2023**

There’s a lull, sunlight crawling down the synth rack, as Jeff leans forward, glasses low on his nose, suddenly conspiratorial.

**JEFF**  
You know what always fucked with me about the original _Dune_ movie? The voiceover narration. That whole “let’s have the characters say their thoughts out loud” thing. Just—(waves a hand)—kinda weird, right?

He looks over at Jeremy, a pointed sibling dig.

**JEFF (CONT’D)**  
Like, “I must not fear. Fear is the mind-killer…” out loud, but not out loud? Broke my brain.

**BILL**  
(shrugging, almost reverent)  
It was a David Lynch film. It was _supposed_ to break your brain. Lynch makes everything sound like a fever dream after an Adderall binge.

**JEFF**  
(smiling)  
I _like_ Lynch films. My favorite’s _Twin Peaks._ That’s the right kind of weird.

**JEREMY**  
(groans, runs a hand through his hair)  
Don’t remind me. Gilley saw the Red Room and the football and just about lost it. Afterward, he told everyone, “I just saw a monkey fuck a football.”

Bill nearly chokes on his Gatorade. Jim, not missing a beat, raises an eyebrow, deadpan as gospel.

**JIM**  
You sure that wasn’t Jeff? That metaphor’s got… layers. Real bent layers.

**JEREMY**  
(stubborn, but laughing)  
Nope. That was Gilley. That was not my brother who said that. I remember because he repeated it for, like, a _year_. He’d say, “Fire Walk With Me? I just saw a monkey fuck a football.” He even said it in interviews. _Our_ interviews.

A short, ragged burst of laughter. Bill wipes his eyes, puts his hand over his heart in mock solemnity.

**BILL**  
Gilley, poet laureate of the Midwest.

**JIM**  
Maybe _he_ should narrate. Just random football-monkey wisdom over the entire docu-series.

**JEFF**  
(smiling, eyes softening)  
The only way you could make it truer. Or weirder.

**JEREMY**  
Or more us.

And for a second, the studio holds the echo—comradeship, mockery, memory, all tangled together like cables on the floor. The August dusk turns the dust gold.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – EVENING, LIGHT SOFTENING – CONTINUOUS**

A pause, the kind that lets you hear the room: hum of old amps, creak of Bill’s chair, one last car horn outside. Jeff’s thinking, lips twisting, something working itself loose.

**JEFF**  
Hey—actually, that’s not a bad idea. Who _is_ going to play Gilley? Let him be the narrator. I mean, sure, a good thirty percent of the series takes place after he dies, but—

He glances at Jeremy, waiting for the snark.

**JEREMY**  
(nodding, a little too quick)  
Yeah, but—no. Think about it. Kathy, we _know_ died in 2012. Here we are, thirteen years later, still dragging her ghost around. Whoever they hire for her part—hell, maybe Riley Keough, maybe someone nobody’s met yet—maybe _she_ narrates. Keeps Kathy involved, keeps her voice alive.

He’s picking at the label on his water bottle, the old nervous tic.

**BILL**  
(grins, then gets serious, voice cutting through)  
That’ll be really fucking pungent. A female narrator, all the way through, commenting on the inherent sexism of the system. Every time the boys club closes a door, she’s the one kicking it off the hinges. Imagine—her voice, calm as napalm, just _calling out_ every industry cliche while the cameras ogle.

**JEFF**  
(quick, defensive)  
There was no sexism. Kathy _transcended_ sex. She burned through gender and landed somewhere above it.

Bill just laughs, low, throat full of old arguments and affection.

**BILL**  
Yeah, that’s your dick talking, Jeff.

Jeff’s jaw sets, but he’s not angry—just sharper, suddenly sure.

**JEFF**  
No. No, fuck that. Kathy would be _appalled_ that we’re even having this discussion. She’d say, “Let the work speak. Let the blood stain the floor. Don’t put me in a pink box just because it sells better.” She hated being anyone’s symbol. She wanted to be a fucking _storm_.

**JEREMY**  
(soft, almost respectful)  
She _was_ a storm. Doesn’t mean the sky was fair.

**JIM**  
(drumming fingers on his knee, dry as ever)  
Or that we didn’t all get wet.

A silence. Bill’s eyes find the window. Jeff stares at his shoes. Jeremy leans back, thinking about Riley Keough, about Kathy, about how nothing in this room is ever just about memory—it’s about what still hurts.

**BILL**  
All I’m saying—whoever narrates, they better not flinch. Not for Kathy. Not for any of us.

**JEFF**  
(nods, voice cracking just a bit)  
Yeah. No flinching. Not now, not ever.

And the dusk folds in around them, not closing the conversation, just holding it open—like the wound that made the band.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – EVENING – CONTINUOUS**

A shift—Jeff straightens, more animated now, a ripple of the old “ideas man” coming out. The studio smells like solder and sweat, the day leaking into night.

**JEFF**  
Here’s the elephant, guys. Nobody’s saying it, so I will: I’ve been working side gigs in the video game world the past five years. And let me tell you, “name brand” actors—famous faces, screen stars—they charge more per minute than our entire first album budget. You get a celeb in here, suddenly HBO’s wondering why their catering budget is cold pizza and bad hummus.

Bill rolls his eyes, flicks a switch on the Rhodes just to feel the click.

**BILL**  
So what? You want the same guy who does detergent commercials to do backup vocals for us now?

Jeff leans in, sharp, focused, a rare note of fervor.

**JEFF**  
You don’t get it—voice actors are _dangerously_ great now. Seriously. The talent pool? It’s like a submarine full of killer whales. These people can break your heart, make you hard, make you laugh—all in a three-minute audition. They do “unhinged parent,” “traumatized android,” “orgasmic librarian” and they don’t even break a sweat.

Jeremy’s grinning, catching the edge of Jeff’s conviction.

**JEREMY**  
He’s right. Ever listen to a video game trailer with a nobody and just _feel_ it in your guts? Sometimes it’s better than a big star. The work’s hungrier.

**JEFF**  
They know how to serve story. No ego. No “I need my close-up.” Just pure, perfect performance. For a tenth the price.

Bill, stubborn, taps a cable against the desk.

**BILL**  
I just don’t want a soap seller narrating Kathy’s death. Or selling my fucking first divorce to the fans. There’s gotta be a line.

Jeff’s eyes flash, the conviction hard as bone.

**JEFF**  
You want raw, you want myth, you want us at our most goddamn _human_? Sometimes you need to let the wolves in. Forget “name brand.” Hire the voice that can cut glass with three syllables.

**JIM**  
(dry, nodding)  
And if they can sell soap and heartbreak, hell—maybe they can sell a little truth, too.

The room hangs on that—fame, money, honesty. All the lines you draw, all the ones you break. Out in the hall, the stairwell echoes: someone else’s voice, but it could be yours.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – EVENING – CONTINUOUS**

Jim stretches his legs, cracks his knuckles, and fixes Jeff with a look—half-joking, half-dare, fully alive.

**JIM**  
Okay, _computer_. Since you’re our resident game developer and voice actor evangelist—_who da fuck_, then? Name some names. Or at least a couple pipes that don’t sound like they’re fresh outta Marvel contracts.

Jeremy jumps on it first, brow furrowed, lips twisting around memory and mischief.

**JEREMY**  
Off the top?  
— _Elias Toufexis_. He’s the guy who did Adam Jensen in _Deus Ex_. Gravel in his throat, sounds like he’s been up all night on bourbon and regret. He could narrate a hangover and make it sound like scripture. Plus, he’s done a million games—cheap enough to still pick up the phone.

— _Courtenay Taylor_. Voice of _Jack_ in _Mass Effect 2_, the _female Sole Survivor_ in _Fallout 4_. Her voice is pure gasoline, but she can drop down and do haunted as fuck, too. Grit with velvet. She’d kill the narrator gig, especially if we want the story rough around the edges.

**JEFF**  
(nodding, warming up)  
Exactly. There’s _Ashly Burch_—she does _Aloy_ in _Horizon Zero Dawn_, _Chloe_ in _Life Is Strange_. She can do hope, ruin, seduction, all in a single breath. And she won an Emmy for a goddamn cartoon. These people aren’t amateurs, they’re assassins.

Bill raises an eyebrow, actually listening now.

**BILL**  
Anyone who sounds like they didn’t go to drama school with Emma Watson?

Jeremy grins, keeping the rhythm:

**JEREMY**  
— _Dave Fennoy_. He’s the _Lee_ in _The Walking Dead_. His voice could make a eulogy sound like a love song. Or _Kimberly Brooks_, who’s done everything from _Mass Effect_ to _Psychonauts_. If you want a little gravitas, a little sex, a little grief—she’s it.

**JEFF**  
(pointing, charged up)  
We bring in a pro, nobody outside the credits will even see it coming. And they’ll never flinch at the mess. That’s the win.

**JIM**  
(smiling, impressed)  
All right. That’s a fucking list. And none of them sell soap, right?

**JEREMY**  
Only if you want them to. But they could sell sin to a nun.

Laughter—loud, honest, relieved. The band knows, for a second, they could actually pull this off.

## **INT. BILL’S STUDIO – EVENING – CONTINUOUS**

Jeff snaps his fingers, half-standing, caught up in a wave of pure, embarrassing fandom.

**JEFF**  
You know who I actually want? The guy who voices _Geralt_ in _The Witcher_ games. Doug Cockle. That’s the sound of a man who’s been stabbed, fucked, haunted, and still keeps going. That’s what I want for Commuted. Someone who sounds like cigarettes and hard-earned wisdom.

Bill raises a hand like he’s slapping a stop sign.

**BILL**  
No, no, no—FemShep. Jennifer Hale. She’s the _real_ north star of game narrators. Command presence, but can still break your heart. We get Hale, you get brains, grit, and enough warmth to melt Jeremy’s cynicism. That’s a voice you _trust_.

Jeff grins, shaking his head.

**JEFF**  
Not bad, not bad—but definitely not Paragon. If we do FemShep, it’s got to be _Renegade_ all the way. No blue options. Only red. Break the rules, burn the house down, walk out with the cat.

Jeremy just stares, brow crumpled, not even pretending.

**JEREMY**  
I have absolutely no fucking clue what you’re talking about. Are we naming vodka flavors? Is this like, some weird Zodiac thing?

Bill laughs, big and open, shaking his head.

**BILL**  
It’s called _culture_, Jeremy. Get with it.

Jeff just points, half-apologetic, half-triumphant.

**JEFF**  
Look, the point is: get the right voice, the story tells itself. Just don’t make it sound like a cartoon. Or a vodka ad.

**JIM**  
Or a fucking soap seller.

A beat, then Jeremy shrugs, surrendering.

**JEREMY**  
Just make sure whoever it is can pronounce “Commuted” and “fuck.” That’s all I care about.

And the room dissolves in laughter, relief, and a little hope, the band feeling like maybe, just maybe, their story will get told right.
