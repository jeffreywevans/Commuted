## **INT. Waffle House – EARLY EVENING – DECEMBER 2, 2018

Cremeans sits in a booth, black notebook open, pen in hand, sipping what might be coffee. A laminated menu blocks the bottom third of his field of vision. The waitress has stopped asking if he’s ready to order.

**[[5.2. JEFF CREMEANS MODERN REVIEW|CREMEANS]]** (V.O.)
Three days until the show. I’ve already agreed to import yak milk, source a goat, and build a steam-powered riser like I’m working on a David Blaine special. And then… here comes Jeff.

Cue the Waffle House doorbell.

DING-A-LING.

Jeff walks in, wild-eyed, dressed like he’s just emerged from a bunker with bad lighting and great acoustics. He doesn’t sit. He simply stands across the booth, palms flat on the table.

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|JEFF]]**
I need six live raccoons and a priest.

**CREMEANS**
…come again?

**JEFF**
Raccoons. Not metaphorical. Not CGI. Alive. Preferably untrained. And a priest. Catholic or Eastern Orthodox, I’m flexible.

**CREMEANS**
Jeff, it’s a stadium show. Not a reboot of Homeward Bound with ecclesiastical tension.

**JEFF**
They’re for the encore. Symbolism. Chaos. Rebirth. Also, they’ll wear hats.

Cremeans closes his notebook. Slowly.

**CREMEANS**
Why a priest?

**JEFF**
Well, who else is gonna bless the raccoons?

**CREMEANS**
…What do the raccoons do?

**JEFF** (dead serious)
They choose the setlist.

Cremeans stares at him like he’s doing calculus on a pop tart wrapper.

**CREMEANS**
Jeff, the setlist is already—

**JEFF** (interrupting)
Pre-determined music is fascism.

**CREMEANS** (flat)
You wrote twenty-three of the songs!

**JEFF**
And I regret every moment of creative tyranny.

**CREMEANS**
What happens if they don’t choose anything?

**JEFF** (without hesitation)
Then we open with "In the Air Tonight" but slower.

Beat.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
I miss the goat.

## **INT. Waffle House – EVENING – SAME SCENE CONTINUES

Cremeans is staring at Jeff like he’s waiting for the punchline to arrive by Greyhound.  Jeff doesn’t blink. He’s doing the dead serious face—the one that usually precedes either genius or a small explosion.

**CREMEANS**
Okay. Let’s rewind. Are you high?

**JEFF** (calmly)
No. Well, not chemically. Maybe spiritually.

Beat.

Jeff leans in, lowers his voice like he’s handing over nuclear launch codes.

**JEFF**
I’m just messing with you, man. The raccoons? The priest? Pure theater. A little absurdism for the road.

Cremeans exhales sharply, collapsing against the booth like he just survived a hostage negotiation.

**CREMEANS**
You had me about to call the Diocese of Dayton and a wildlife rescue hotline in the same breath.

**JEFF** (grinning slightly)
But you're not Catholic!  See? You’re alive now.

He finally sits. Orders nothing. Just folds his hands like he’s at a board meeting.

**JEFF** (CONT’D)
But I do have one actual request.

**CREMEANS** (cautious)
If it involves any species that hisses or plays dead, I’m walking into traffic.

Jeff shakes his head solemnly.

**JEFF**
I need you to leave eight will-call tickets at the box office under the name… Ron Swanson.

**CREMEANS**
The… Parks and Rec guy?

**JEFF** (nodding)
Yes. Ron Swanson. He may be fictional, but the energy is real. The man is a metaphysical lodestar.

**CREMEANS**
Jeff, he’s a sitcom character.

**JEFF**
Exactly. But what if he’s real? What if… on some plane of reality, he shows up?

**CREMEANS**
And what if I have to explain to security why a man with a mustache and a woodshop punch card is being waved through VIP?

**JEFF**
That’s between you and destiny.

**CREMEANS** (scribbling it down anyway)
Eight tickets. Ron Swanson. What section?

**JEFF** (without hesitation)
Whichever section has the best view of Jeremy’s fretboard. He’ll appreciate the craftsmanship.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
Some days I’m not their manager. I’m their babysitter, confessor, and curator of performance art.

**CREMEANS** (aloud)
Anything else? Any requests for the ghost of Julia Child or a Cabbage Patch Kid?

**JEFF** (seriously)
No. But I have a hunch the ghost of Julia Child already has front row.

## **INT. LUCAS OIL STADIUM – LOADING BAY – NIGHT BEFORE THE SHOW

The place is chaos: forklifts, roadies, stacks of flight cases, and a fog machine someone accidentally activated during lunch. Bill stands center-stage, hair tied back, wearing a headset that isn’t connected to anything. He's holding an iPad and yelling at a subwoofer.

A **STAGEHAND** approaches, timid.

**STAGEHAND**
Hey uh… Mr. Davenport? There’s a note from venue ops about will-call. Someone flagged it. Something about… Ron Swanson?

Bill freezes mid-gesture. His eyebrow twitches.

**[[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|BILL]]**
Ron. Swanson?

**STAGEHAND**
Yeah. Eight tickets. All-access. Name’s been posted on the door.

Bill looks around like he’s being punked by sound waves.

**BILL**
Is this a prank? Did Gilley do this? Is this his new thing instead of farting into the vocal mic?

**STAGEHAND** (shrinking)
I think Jeff put them in. He told the box office.

Bill doesn’t move for a full two seconds.

**BILL** (to himself)
Of course he did.

## **CUT TO: INT. HALLWAY – MOMENTS LATER**

Bill is marching through the bowels of the stadium like an angry drumline in skinny jeans. He finds Jeff in a side room sitting cross-legged on a yoga mat, writing lyrics with a quill pen and drinking sparkling water out of a mason jar.

**BILL**
Jeff.

**JEFF** (not looking up)
That’s me.

**BILL**
Why the fuck is Ron Swanson on our guest list?

**JEFF** (serenely)
He’s from Pawnee.

**BILL** (exploding)
HE’S NOT REAL!

**JEFF** (finally looking up)
Neither is the concept of genre, but here we are.

Bill blinks. Jeff continues calmly.

**JEFF** (CONT’D)
Think about it. Swanson transcends medium. He’s a distillation of masculinity, woodworking, and controlled rage. He belongs at a Commuted show. Especially ours.

**BILL** (genuinely stunned)
You gave him eight tickets.

**JEFF**
He’s got a lot of friends. Possibly a jazz quartet.

**BILL** (deadpan)
Did you also invite Batman? Gandalf? The fucking Babadook?

**JEFF** (sincerely)
Gandalf doesn’t do stadiums. The Babadook, maybe, but I only had eight tickets.

Bill pinches the bridge of his nose like he’s trying to kill a migraine with willpower alone.

**BILL**
You’re unbelievable.

**JEFF** (shrugging)
I believe in many things. Including Pawnee, Indiana.

**BILL**
That is not a real town.

**JEFF** (softly)
It is… in here.

Jeff points to his heart.

**BILL**
I’m going to staple this guest list to your forehead.

**JEFF** (smiling)
That’s a boundary violation, Bill.

**BILL** (to himself, walking away)
I hope Leslie Knope shows up and punches you in the dick.

**JEFF** (calling after him)
She’s got seats too!

## **INT. LUCAS OIL STADIUM – CATERING TENT – LATE NIGHT, DEC 4

Industrial lighting hums. The smell is stale hummus, strong coffee, and despair. Jeremy is chewing through a protein bar like it insulted his family. Bill storms in, holding a copy of the guest list like it’s evidence in a felony trial.

**BILL**
I need to talk to you about Jeff.

**[[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|JEREMY]]** (without looking up)
Is this about the fish thing or the sleep-talking?

**BILL**
Worse. He left eight tickets for Ron Swanson.

Jeremy stops mid-bite. Slowly turns his head.

**JEREMY**
The guy from Parks and Rec?

**BILL**
Yes. The one who lives in fictional Pawnee, Indiana. The one portrayed by Nick Offerman. That guy. He thinks he’s coming to our show.

**JEREMY**
Is… Jeff okay?

**BILL**
That’s the problem. Yes. That is Jeff okay. This is his idea of normal.

**JEREMY**
Why?

**BILL**
He said… he wanted to “honor masculine stillness.”

**JEREMY**
What does that even mean?

**BILL**
***I DON’T KNOW, JEREMY. I HAVE A MASTER’S DEGREE AND I DON’T KNOW.***

Beat.

Jeremy leans back in his folding chair. He takes a long, grim sip of lukewarm Gatorade.

**JEREMY** (quietly)
Okay. So what do we do?

**BILL**
What do we do? There’s nothing to do. I’m just here so someone else can witness this behavior. I need documentation. If he tries to give one of the raccoons a backstage laminate again, I want someone to back me up in court.

**JEREMY**
Jesus.

**BILL**
No.  It's Jeff. Way worse.

**JEREMY**
Alright. I’ll… talk to him?

**BILL**
And say what?

**JEREMY** (blank)
…that Ron Swanson isn’t real?

**BILL**
You think that’s gonna work on the guy who once argued that the moon is "just Earth’s unpaid intern"?

**JEREMY** (quietly, traumatized)
He made a spreadsheet about that.

**BILL**
Exactly. So good luck. But if someone shows up tomorrow with a mustache and a canoe paddle, we’re just letting them in.

**JEREMY**
Okay. Okay. I’ll handle it. I’ll… do a sanity pass.

**BILL** (heading for the coffee)

Get in line. That ship sailed in 2006 and crashed into a glacier made of Kathy’s unspoken contempt.

**JEREMY** (softly)
She’d know how to talk him down.

Beat. They both look at the floor. Shared silence. Reverent. Then—

**BILL** (sighing)
You ever think we’re the weird ones?

**JEREMY**
Every goddamn day.

They sip coffee and stare at the guest list like it’s haunted.

## **INT. LUCAS OIL STADIUM – BACKSTAGE HALLWAY – EARLY MORNING, DEC 5

A steel door swings open with a creak straight out of a horror movie. Jim steps into the corridor, wearing aviator sunglasses, a bathrobe over sweatpants, and flip-flops with socks. He’s holding a breakfast burrito like it’s a microphone.  He sees Bill and Jeremy, dead-eyed on a couch, sipping coffee like it’s morphine. The tension is a thick fog of existential dread and expired Red Bull.

**JIM**
Did you hear what Jeff did this time?

**JEREMY** (deadpan)
You're gonna have to narrow that down.

**BILL** (not looking up)
Is it about the tickets or the metaphysical reckoning of meat?

**JIM**
…What?

**JEREMY**
Never mind. What’s he done now?

**JIM** (grinning)
He tried to Venmo Nick Offerman.

**BILL** (freezes mid-sip)
You’re lying.

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]]** (dead serious)
Fifteen bucks. Memo line said, “Gas money to Pawnee. Bring the sax.”

**JEREMY**
Oh my God.

**BILL** (hissing through his teeth)
He’s escalating.

**JIM**
Then he made a second payment with the memo, “Or use this to buy raw lumber and paternal disappointment.”

**JEREMY** (quietly)
He’s just… gone full Jeff.

**JIM** (nodding, solemn)
Full. Fucking. Jeff.

**BILL** (standing up, pacing)
Okay, okay, we still have time. The show's tonight. If we can contain him, steer him away from digital platforms, and maybe distract him with a rehearsal—

**JIM**
Too late. He’s on Instagram Live doing a dramatic reading of the Pawnee City Charter.

**JEREMY**
Is he at least clothed?

**JIM** (shrugging)
From the waist up. Unclear beyond that. There's fog.

**BILL** (to no one in particular)
How is it always fog with him?

**JIM** (grinning)
It’s the Ron Swanson aesthetic. He called it “log-based masculinity.”

**JEREMY** (utterly defeated)
I don’t even know what that means.

**JIM** (biting burrito)
It means he needs to be supervised.

**BILL**
Great. So who's gonna go get him?

(Long pause.)

ALL THREE (in unison)
Cremeans!

## **INT. FORD EDGE – DRIVING NORTH ON I-465 – LATE MORNING, DEC 5

The SUV interior is a mix of fast food receipts, legal pads, and a travel mug that has seen things. Cremeans is behind the wheel, deadpan, jaw tight, chewing antacids like they’re communion wafers. WZPL is playing Christmas music too earnestly for his mood.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
Sometimes in life, you get called to great missions. Rescue. Reconciliation. Redemption.  Other times, you’re sent to the goddamn airport to pick up a man who might be… Ron Swanson.

### **EXT. INDIANAPOLIS INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT – ARRIVALS LANE – MINUTES LATER

Cremeans pulls up to the curb, hazard lights on, squinting at every man in a flannel shirt and mustache. The tension is real.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
I could’ve been checking pyro logistics. I could’ve been making sure Gilley’s mic stand wasn’t zip-tied to a bottle of Jäger.  Instead, I’m playing a very specific game of facial hair roulette.

A MAN approaches. Tall. Broad-shouldered. Tan Carhartt jacket. Impeccable mustache. Zero expression. Holding a small leather bag. No rolling luggage. No visible phone. Just… stillness.  Cremeans leans out the window.

**CREMEANS**
You… uh… Swanson?

MAN (gravelly)
Name’s Mike. Jeff said I’d be under a different name.

Cremeans stares. Man stares back. Mutual suspicion. Ancient silence.

**CREMEANS**
So you’re not Ron Swanson. But you're the guy pretending to be Ron Swanson… because Jeff told you to?

**MIKE**
I’m a performance artist. Jeff said it was “conceptually necessary.”

**CREMEANS** (beat)
…Get in.

## **INT. FORD EDGE – DRIVING BACK TO STADIUM

Cremeans drives. Mike sits silently. No music. No words. Just vibes. Deep, masculine, woodworking-in-the-void vibes.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
You ever stare into the abyss and find a man who smells like cedar and doesn’t blink for thirty straight minutes?  I have. Today. And I’m probably gonna have to get him a VIP badge.

Mike finally speaks.

**MIKE**
Do you have a band saw?

**CREMEANS**
No.

**MIKE**
You should.

Cremeans contemplates opening the door at 65 mph.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
I don’t know what Jeff’s planning. I don’t know what this man is.  But I do know I’m gonna need a bonus. And probably a sedative.

---

## **INT. FORD EDGE – MOVING – I-70 EAST TOWARD LUCAS OIL STADIUM – LATE MORNING, DEC 5

Cremeans taps his fingers on the wheel. Windshield smeared with salt dust. Wipers going half-heartedly. The man in the backseat—Mike, a.k.a. Ron Swanson—hasn’t said a word in ten minutes. Just sat there. Breathing slow. Calm. Unfazed by Indiana traffic or mortal consequence.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
Something’s off. Something’s too… still.

Cremeans glances up at the rearview mirror. Takes a good look this time.  The mustache. The jawline. That same quiet intensity you get from men who can both forge a horseshoe and dismantle your worldview in a single sentence.

**CREMEANS** (V.O., nervous)
Wait a minute.

He flips the mirror slightly. Adjusts the angle. Squints.

**CREMEANS** (aloud, cautious)
Hey… uh… you ever been on TV?

**MIKE** (gruffly, still deadpan)
Sometimes.

**CREMEANS**
Like… NBC?

**MIKE** (beat)
A few seasons.

**CREMEANS**
With… Amy Poehler?

The man looks up and meets his eyes in the mirror. Slowly. Like a bear deciding whether or not you’re edible.

**MIKE**
I am [Nick Offerman](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pP4acHxygqk).


**CREMEANS**
…Jesus.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
It’s not a guy pretending to be Ron Swanson.  It’s not a Ron Swanson cosplayer.  It’s not even a delusional fan.  It’s Nick Offerman.

Cremeans’ mouth is suddenly dry. He reaches blindly for his travel mug and spills coffee on his lap. Doesn’t flinch.

**CREMEANS** (tight)
Jeff invited you?

**NICK** (with unbothered power)
He sent me a Venmo request. Said the show was absurd and sacred. I respected that.

**CREMEANS** (quietly)
He summoned you.

**NICK**
I drove from Chicago. Took the scenic route. Listened to John Prine. Bought maple syrup at a Mennonite stand.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
I’m driving the actual Nick Offerman to a Commuted concert because Jeff invited him using digital pocket change and the power of chaotic intention.

Cremeans’ hands shake slightly. He drops the mirror back down. He cannot look anymore.

**NICK** (gently)
You okay?

**CREMEANS**
No, sir. No, I am not okay.

**NICK** (kindly)
That’s understandable.

Beat.

**NICK** (CONT’D)
Do you want me to whittle something calming?

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
This man is going to show up backstage and Jeff is going to treat him like a pope. Or a druid. Or both.

**CREMEANS** (sighs aloud)
Yeah. Yeah. Whittle something. I need… I need a totem.

Nick Offerman reaches into his satchel. Pulls out a small block of cedar. Produces a pen knife with ceremonial reverence.  Cremeans nods slowly, trying to accept his new reality.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
My life is a fever dream directed by Wes Anderson’s evil twin.

## **INT. FORD EDGE – EXITING I-70 – LATE MORNING, DEC 5

The skyline of Indianapolis looms ahead. Cremeans white-knuckles the steering wheel, eyes forward, processing the cosmic joke that is his day. In the backseat, Nick Offerman—alias “Mike,” formerly “Ron Swanson,” now fully revealed—is calmly sharpening a small block of cedar. The scent of clean wood fills the SUV like an artisanal anxiety diffuser.

**NICK** (softly, contemplative)
Mind making a detour?

Cremeans flinches like the man just asked for blood.

**CREMEANS** (guarded)
Uh… where to?

**NICK** (dead serious)
There’s an 84 Lumber off South Emerson. Only place in this city that stocks the good stuff. I want to whittle something appropriate. A… peace offering.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
Oh no.

**CREMEANS** (aloud)

A peace offering for…?

**NICK**
Whoever’s the most freaked out when I show up. Could be your lighting director. Could be your rhythm guitarist. Could be someone’s emotional support roadie. Doesn’t matter. I want to offer a talisman. Something carved. Personal. Powerful.

**CREMEANS**
You… want to carve a custom emotional trauma gift?

**NICK** (nods once)
It’s the gentleman’s way.

Beat.

**NICK** (CONT’D)
But you’ll need to pay for the wood. I don’t work for free.

**CREMEANS** (flat)
Of course you don’t.

**NICK** (firmly)
Even performance mysticism needs boundaries.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
I am buying lumber for Nick Offerman so he can whittle an apology for being himself in proximity to Jeff Evans.

Cremeans signals for the off-ramp, already mourning his per diem.

**CREMEANS**
Do you need anything specific? Oak? Maple? Teak?

**NICK** (shaking head)
Walnut. Spalted, if they have it. If not… ash. With character.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
This man doesn’t ask. He makes existential requests.

**NICK** (smiling faintly)
We’ll be quick. I have cash. But I don’t do checkout lines. You’ll handle that.

**CREMEANS**
Yes, your holiness.

**NICK**
No. That’s Jeff. I’m just the craftsman.

## **EXT. 84 LUMBER PARKING LOT – TEN MINUTES LATER

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
This is either the greatest prank Jeff has ever pulled…Or the origin story of a legend.  And somehow?  It’s probably both.

### **INT. 84 LUMBER – INTERIOR – LATE MORNING, DEC 5

Industrial halogens buzz overhead. Rows of pristine planks stretch into lumber infinity. The air smells like sawdust and unspoken masculinity. **NICK** (murmuring reverently)
Ah… here she is.

**NICK** (CONT’D)
She’s seen storms. Probably a fungus problem at the cellular level. Beautiful. Complicated.  Perfect for a man having a professional meltdown.

**CREMEANS** (wary)
Who’s the gift for, exactly?

**NICK**
That’s up to the vibes. But if I had to guess?  Probably Bill. The kind of guy who doesn’t yell unless he’s been emotionally poisoned by abstract behavior. Might need something he can rub like a worry stone. Or chew.

**CREMEANS** (sighing, nods)
Yeah. That tracks.

Nick passes the board to Cremeans like he’s transferring sacred relics. Cremeans checks the price tag. $138.00. For a single piece of lumber.

---

**CREMEANS** (deadpan)
Do you want a receipt?

**NICK** (without hesitation)
Burn it.

### **CUT TO: INT. 84 LUMBER CHECKOUT – MOMENTS LATER

Cremeans stands at the counter, face blank, holding the board like it’s radioactive. The cashier—a teen with a vape pen and a forehead tattoo of a semicolon—scans the wood slowly.

**CASHIER**
Gift?

**CREMEANS** (distantly)
Performance art.

**CASHIER**
Cool, cool.

**CASHIER** (CONT’D)
You want to join our loyalty—

**CREMEANS**
Absolutely not.

### **EXT. PARKING LOT – MOMENTS LATER

Cremeans opens the trunk. Nick places the board inside with ritual care, then shuts the trunk like he’s sealing a sarcophagus.

**NICK** (quietly)
There. That’s going to make someone feel… held.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
Held.  With wood.  Paid for by a stadium tour budget.

**CREMEANS** (aloud)
Back to the venue?

**NICK** (grinning)
Let’s go spook some musicians.

## **INT. FORD EDGE – RETURNING TO LUCAS OIL STADIUM – NOON, DEC 5

Cremeans is back behind the wheel, staring straight ahead like he’s processing alien contact. In the passenger seat, Nick Offerman sits with serene purpose, the freshly purchased spalted walnut plank resting across his lap like a holy artifact.

Silence.

Then—

**NICK** (calmly)
I’ll need privacy.

Cremeans doesn’t turn his head.

**CREMEANS**
Of course you will.

**NICK**
Two hours. Maybe less if the spirit moves quickly. But I wouldn’t rush the wood. It’s expressive.

**CREMEANS** (tight-lipped)
Right. We wouldn’t want to rush the… expression.

**NICK**
Also.

(Pause. This part is serious.)

**NICK** (CONT’D)
I’ll need some Lagavulin.

Cremeans slams the brakes at a red light a little harder than necessary.

**CREMEANS**
You need Scotch?

**NICK**
I prefer to call it… creative lube. It clarifies the grain. Opens the blade. Loosens the memory.

You’re going to get drunk and whittle in our venue?

No, no. Not drunk. Aligned.

Cremeans stares out the windshield.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
He’s going to disappear into a service corridor with a knife, a board, and $100 single malt, and he’s going to emerge with a totem of healing for a man who only drinks cold brew and knows four words in Latin.

**CREMEANS** (aloud, resigned)
Do you need anything else? Smoke machine? Falconer?

**NICK** (tilting head)
If someone has a badger skull, I won’t say no.

**CREMEANS**
I hate this job.

**NICK** (pleasantly)
That means you’re doing it right.

## **INT. STADIUM BACKSTAGE – SERVICE CORRIDOR OUTSIDE STORAGE C – 2:15PM, DEC 5

The fluorescent lights overhead flicker like they’re questioning their own existence. Cremeans stands outside Storage C, slouched against the wall, holding a clipboard and a paper Mt. Dew cup that’s been empty for an hour. From behind the door, faint scraping sounds—like controlled, sacred violence—continue.

…

Cremeans sits at a folding table backstage, surrounded by cords, laminate badges, and three open boxes of beef jerky no one claims responsibility for. The door creaks open.

Enter: Nick Offerman.

He steps in with the gravitas of a man who has just stared into the grain of the universe and come back with a thesis. In his arms: a hand-carved object. It looks like an alien reliquary. Equal parts bowl, fist, maze, and topographical regret.  He gently sets it down on the desk, directly on top of a schedule marked “DO NOT LOSE.”

**NICK** (sincerely)
This… is for the one most destabilized by my presence.

Cremeans stares. The object seems to emit a hum of wood-based contemplation.

**CREMEANS**
…It has levels.

**NICK** (nodding)
So does grief.

Cremeans circles it once like it might explode. It’s smooth and splintered, symbolic and accidentally erotic. It looks like something stolen from a dream sequence in a German film about divorce.

**CREMEANS** (carefully)
So… this is for Bill?

**NICK**
Or Jeremy. Depending on who screams first.

Beat.

Nick pats the totem once, tenderly. Then, like this is completely unrelated, he clears his throat and adjusts his flannel sleeves.

**NICK** (calmly)
By the way.  What time is sound check?

Cremeans looks up. The fear returns.

**CREMEANS**
…Why?

Nick reaches into the duffel bag slung over his shoulder and pulls out…

A gleaming, vintage Selmer Mark VI saxophone.

Cremeans leans back in his chair like he’s being mugged by destiny.

**CREMEANS** (whispering)

Sound check is in an hour. Wear in-ears. Don’t point the bell at the LED wall. And stay away from the pyros. Gilley has a thing about sparks and moustaches.

Get out.

**CREMEANS** (V.O.)
