# **PENGUINS ON PARADE**

---

## **INT. INTERVIEW STUDIO – NYC – AFTERNOON – NOV 2007**

A polished set. Velvet chairs. Cameras humming. KATHY ESPICH sits poised, legs crossed, hair pulled back like a loaded gun. She’s dressed sharp—black blazer, dark lipstick, zero intention of playing nice.

The INTERVIEWER is young. Polite. Smiling with the nervous energy of someone who knows they’re about to ask something *they shouldn’t*—but has been told to *try anyway*.

**INTERVIEWER**
So… there’s a lot of speculation around “*Rock Bottom*.” You’ve never really addressed it directly. Is it about Gilley?

**KATHY** (sits still for a long beat)
You know what I hate about that question?

(leans forward, voice low, eyes locked)
It’s not that it’s personal. It’s not that it’s obvious. It’s that it’s *lazy*.

**INTERVIEWER** (small laugh)
Well, I mean, it’s one of the most intimate songs on—

**KATHY**
You want to know if I fucked him. That’s what you’re asking.

(audience gasps—off-camera)

So I’ll save you the polite detour. Yes. I fucked him. Repeatedly. Enthusiastically. He was 20. I was 20. He had the charisma of a god and the emotional maturity of a salad.  We had sex in green rooms, on floors, in bathtubs  so rusted they looked like crime scenes. He liked to choke me while quoting Bukowski. I liked when he said my name like it was a lyric he hadn’t earned.

It was hot. It was stupid. It was ***fucking real***.

(leans back, arms crossed)
And then I wrote about it.  I took the smell of his sweat and the sound of him begging and the ache of realizing I was just another notch he’d autograph and forget—And I ***made it immortal***.  *Rock Bottom* is not a love song. It’s not a diss track. It’s a ***fossil.*** Of what happens when two kids with too much ego and no brakes crash into each other for six months and call it romance.

**INTERVIEWER** (tiny voice)
Wow.

**KATHY**
You wanted simple? Buy a poster. But if you want *truth*? Here it is: Gilley was the worst decision I ever came to climax on. But without that mistake? You wouldn’t know my name.  You wouldn’t know *his* either.

(a beat of silence)
So yeah. It’s about him. And it’s also about me. And it’s about anyone who ever begged someone to stay just five minutes longer when they knew they were being used.

(interviewer blinks, stunned)
Next question.

**FADE OUT.**

## **INT. SERVICE HALLWAY – BACKSTAGE – NIGHT AFTER THE INTERVIEW AIRS**

Cinderblock walls. Buzzing fluorescents. The thrum of a stadium still cooling down after another riot of a show. Techs pass quietly with gear. Distant laughter echoes. But here?

It’s **dead quiet**.

**GILLEY** leans against a wall, a towel around his neck, still glistening with stage sweat. Hair perfect, of course. He’s got a half-smile and a phone in hand—probably reading the internet *explode* over Kathy’s interview.

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]** appears like a storm with a center of gravity. Hoodie up. Eyes locked. No entourage. No hesitation.

**JEFF** (quiet, lethal)
You lied to me.

**GILLEY** (doesn’t look up)
You’ll have to be more specific. I lie a lot.

**JEFF**
About Kathy. About when it ended.

*That* gets Gilley’s attention. He lowers the phone. The smile dims.

**GILLEY** (soft)
What’d she say?

**JEFF**
Doesn’t matter what she said. Matters what *you* did. Did you fuck her after we signed?

**GILLEY** (beat)
Jeff—

**JEFF**
Don’t ***Jeff*** me. You let me believe it ended before the deal. Before we had a name. You let me carry that story like a ***shield***.

(steps closer)
And now she’s out there talking about green rooms and ***bathtubs***. You said it ended before we signed. You ***said*** it.

**GILLEY** (quiet, choosing words)
It ended before the tour. Mostly. We slipped a few times. We were still figuring shit out.

**JEFF**
Bullshit. That wasn’t a ***slip***. That was ***half a year*** of lying to my face while I cleaned your ***fucking ashtrays***.

**GILLEY**
Jeff, we were ***kids***. We were ***dumb*** and ***hot*** and ***terrified*** of everything, including each other. You wanted a clean narrative. We gave you one. Congratulations. Now we’re older and the truth’s got ***jagged edges***.

**JEFF**
You ***let me defend her***. I shut down ***label gossip***, fans, reporters. Told them it was  ancient history. Because ***you both told me it was over***. Because I ***trusted*** you.

**GILLEY** (leans off the wall, serious now)
I didn’t ask you to do that.

**JEFF**
No. You just ***let me.*** Because it was ***easier***.

**GILLEY** (slow breath)
Yeah. It was.

**JEFF**
So what now, huh? You gonna tell me you ***loved*** her?

**GILLEY**
No. I plowed my dick in her. That’s not love. That’s ***vanity***.

(beat)
And she’s been pulling herself out of the crater ever since.

**JEFF**
So what—you just get to walk around with your charm and your ***mic*** and your ***wink*** while she gets stuck singing ***“Rock Bottom”*** every night like it’s not ***a tombstone you wrote together***?

**GILLEY**
She made that song hers. She ***made*** it. I just… ***happened*** in it.

**JEFF**
Yeah. Well, congratulations. You’re officially not the myth. You’re the ***mistake***.

(turns to go)
And I don’t think she’s the only one who ***regrets you*** now.

**GILLEY** (quietly, to himself)
Yeah. I figured that out about three verses in.

### **(CONTINUATION – SAME HALLWAY – A MINUTE LATER)**

Gilley’s back against the wall. Quiet. Alone with the aftermath. The towel around his neck might as well be a noose of memory. His phone screen dims, forgotten in his hand.

**JEFF** (returns, sharp footsteps, voice flint-hard)
And one more thing, Gilley.

(Gilley looks up. Doesn’t flinch. Just waits.)

**JEFF**
You’re an ***asshole***.

(beat. No rage. Just the clean snap of truth.)

**JEFF (CONT’D)**
That is all.

He turns. Walks away for good this time.  Gilley stays frozen. Doesn't nod. Doesn't blink. Just stands there. Alone with a truth that doesn't echo. It *rings*.

## **INT. DRESSING ROOM – POST-SHOW – NIGHT**

The room smells like sweat, setlist paper, and old amps. Half-empty water bottles everywhere. A vibe of aftermath. **KATHY** is toweling off, removing rings, stripping stage armor like it’s war gear. Her eyes are tired, but sharp. Always sharp. **JEFF** enters casually—too casually. Leans in the doorway like he’s just here to chat. But Kathy knows Jeff doesn’t *chat*.

**JEFF** (pleasant)
Hey. Quick one for the archives.

Kathy looks at him. That subtle flicker. The one that says, *what are you circling?*

**KATHY**
Shoot.

**JEFF**
When’d you stop sleeping with Gilley?

(beat)

No context. No warning. Just that.

**KATHY** (still, a breath)
That’s what this is?

**JEFF**
Just curious. Inquiring minds.

She looks at him, weighing him. Not panicked—calculating. But this isn’t the stage. There’s no harmony to hide in.

**KATHY**
Spring. '96. Right before we signed the paper in that rat-ass office with the guy who called me "kiddo."

**JEFF**
You sure?

**KATHY**
Yeah. We were still rehearsing in that warehouse in North Jersey. The one with no heat and one light bulb over the drum kit.

(beat)

Why?

**JEFF**
No reason. Just wanted to make sure the record matches the liner notes.

(beat)

Good memory, by the way. Real vivid.

**KATHY**
You don’t believe me.

**JEFF**
I believe the truth has a way of bleeding under the door.

She stands straighter. Defensive? No. Just *ready*.

**KATHY**
I told you what I remember.

**JEFF**
So you didn’t sleep with him in any hotel. No green room. No van. No champagne, no tour buses. No signed contracts. Just—what—cheap carpet and your mom’s couch?

**KATHY**
Exactly.

(quiet)
That was the worst part.

(beat)
It didn’t even take luxury to make me stupid.

**JEFF**
Huh.

(half-turns to leave)
Funny how that’s not the version you gave the press.

**KATHY** (interrupts, low)
I was performing. You think Gilley’s the only one who can lie beautifully?

**JEFF**
I think only one of you still confuses poetry with memory.

(beat)

Good night, Kathy.

He leaves.  Kathy stands there. Alone now. Not shaken. But she *knows*. The damage is done.

## **INT. EMPTY STAGE – SOUND CHECK ENDED – GIANTS STADIUM HALF DARK**

Cables coiled like snakes. Guitars lined up like soldiers awaiting orders. The afterglow of volume still humming in the air.  **KATHY** is sitting at her keys, fiddling with something meaningless, just to have her hands moving. **JEFF** walks in—not stage casual, not bandmate neutral. He walks in like a man whose compass just broke in the middle of a map he thought he memorized.

**JEFF**
We signed with the head of Island Records *at the airport* in Dayton, Ohio. *1991.* Terminal C. Rain on the tarmac. Remember?

**KATHY** (turns slowly, wary)
Of course.

**JEFF**
No you don’t. Not the way you should. Because apparently you were ***still fucking Gilley*** through that whole period.

(silence. He steps closer.)
Three CD releases. Three ***world tours***. Five U.S. circuits. Five ***goddamn tours.*** You ***told me***—with your face blank and your voice cold—that it ended before the Persian Gulf War was over. You said he was in the rearview before *Desert Storm even got rolling*. You ***promised*** me it was over before we had even ***printed merch***.

(quiet now. terrifying)
You ***lied***.

**KATHY**
Jeff—

**JEFF**
No. No ***spin***. No ***“versions”***. You lied. You ***let me stand between you and the rumor mill like a fucking human firewall***—while you kept slipping into his hotel room three floors up. You let me ***protect*** you.

(beat, voice cracking just once)
Why doesn't anybody ever keep their story ***straight*** around me?

**KATHY** (softly, but not weak)
Because you’re the only one who expects the truth to stay clean.

(beat)

You think I *wanted* it to last that long? You think I was proud? I was ***addicted***. To the chaos. The chemistry. The ***attention.*** And he knew it. He ***fed*** it. And I told myself it was over ***so many times*** that eventually I believed it. Even if my body said otherwise.

**JEFF** (voice shaking)
You were ***my firewall***, too. You were my ***constant.*** I built the ***band’s ethics*** on ***your word.*** And now? Now it’s all ***revisionist press junket poetry***.

**KATHY**
Jeff, I lied because I was ***ashamed.***  And you don’t build truth around shame. You ***bury*** it.

(quiet)
You were the only one I wanted to believe in me.

**JEFF**
Then you shouldn’t have made me the ***custodian of your cover story.***

(beat. shorter. broken)
You ***used*** me.

(turns. leaves)

### **(CONTINUATION – STAGE LEFT – SECONDS LATER)**

Kathy’s still at the keys. Eyes fixed on nothing. The last confession still hovering in the air like a spent note.  Footsteps again. **JEFF**, backlit in the stage doorway. He doesn’t walk far. Doesn’t need to.

**JEFF**
Kathy?

(she turns, slowly)
You’re an ***asshole.***

(beat)
That is all.

He exits again.  Kathy doesn't flinch. She just stares after him. Mouth tight. Chin up. But behind the eyes? A ***landslide*** of things she’ll never say.

**FADE OUT.**

## **INT. HOTEL BALCONY – NIGHT – HIGH FLOOR – CITY BENEATH THEM**

Wind whispers. The skyline flickers. Two cocktails untouched on a table between them. KATHY leans on the railing, arms folded like armor. GILLEY sits back in a chair, legs crossed, head tilted like he’s listening for God to cough.

**KATHY**
We need to get our story straight. Or Jeff is going to ***decapitate*** us. With words. With silence. With that **biblical disappointment** look he gets when someone rearranges a song without telling him.

**GILLEY** (nods solemnly)
I don’t want to die by Jeff. Too poetic. Too slow. He’d start with the ankles and make it a three-act tragedy.

**KATHY**
He doesn’t yell. He *commits autopsies* with his eyes.

(beat)

We’ve already done the sin. Now we need the ***liturgy***.

**GILLEY** (grinning)
So what’s the gospel? We ended it before the ink dried on the Island contract?

**KATHY**
Before the ***rumble of jet engines and lawyer cologne***. Before champagne. Before the first hotel keycard. Back when we thought ***fame was the goal*** and not the curse.

**GILLEY**
Right. Back when your mom’s couch was our ***bridal suite*** and I lived in a condo so humid mushrooms grew on my amp.

**KATHY** (quietly)
We were so ***stupid***.

**GILLEY** (sincerely)
God, we were ***beautiful*** in our stupidity.

**KATHY**
Don’t get romantic. We didn’t love each other. We ***unlocked*** each other. Like a virus unlocks fever.

**GILLEY**
Still the best sex I’ve had in a room with a space heater and no curtain.

**KATHY** (grins despite herself)
Still the worst decisions I made while ***stone-cold sober***.

(beat)

Summer of ’92. Montreal. The dryer. That’s the last time. That’s the story. No deviations.

**GILLEY**
No remixes. No deluxe editions. No ***behind the music*** footnotes.

**KATHY**
And if Jeff asks again?

**GILLEY**
We ***chant it***.

**KATHY**
We ***tattoo it.***

**GILLEY**
We ***swear it on whatever God still returns our calls.***

(beat)

You ever think he knew the whole time?

**KATHY**
Jeff? Jeff knows ***everything***. He just waits until you lie to his face to remind you how disappointed he was ***preemptively***.

**GILLEY** (long pause)
Do you think he ever loved us a little less for being…us?

**KATHY**
Only on the days we ***made him right.***

They sit. The wind moves. Below them, the city forgets. But they don’t. They drink—not to toast. To bury.

## **INT. REHEARSAL SPACE – DIM, EMPTY – MIDDAY**

Dust floats in the beam of a single overhead light. Old amps hum faintly from the walls like ghosts refusing to fade. The band is gone, the room is still, but three figures remain:

**JEFF**, standing. **KATHY**, sitting on a cracked piano bench. **GILLEY**, lounging in a chair that’s too comfortable for the tension in the air.

The silence has weight. Truth has come to collect.

**JEFF** (slow, measured)
Why don’t you just try the ***truth***? Why don’t you both just ***try the truth***?  

(beat, no rage—just a tired, holy ache)

Because, I mean… ***why lie***? You’re not twenty anymore. No one’s asking for your virginity or your virtue. Just your ***honesty***.

**KATHY** (quiet)
You think we haven’t tried?

**JEFF**
I think you’ve ***performed***. You turned memory into ***myth***. And then you ***believed*** it.

(beat)

I’ve listened to you both rewrite the script so many times I could ***teach a class*** on revisionist romance. And I still don’t know ***who*** you were to each other. Or ***what***.

**GILLEY** (softly, uncharacteristically unguarded)
We were… ***stupid***. And ***lonely***. And ***hot***. And sometimes ***happy***.

**JEFF**
But you weren’t ***honest***.

**KATHY**
Because the truth ***hurts***.

**JEFF**
It ***hurts anyway.*** Lying doesn’t ***soften*** it. It ***rottens*** it.

(pause)

You both ***owed me.*** I built this ***band*** around ***your word***. And now I find out your ****timeline’s bullshit***, your ***memories are curated***, and your ***shame is still steering the goddamn ship.***

(beat)

So I ask again. Why lie?

**KATHY**
Because we thought we could ***outrun it.***

**GILLEY**
Because ***truth never made anyone a legend.***

**JEFF**
No. But ***it makes you human.***  And I’d ***rather be in a band with humans*** than ***ghosts playing greatest hits from a lie.***  Try the truth. ***Once***. See what happens.

He walks out. Kathy exhales like she’s been holding her breath for a decade. Gilley leans forward, elbows on knees, staring into nothing.

**GILLEY** (quiet)
He just decapitated us with a ***whisper***.

**KATHY**
Yeah. He does that.

## **INT. WRITING ROOM – MORNING – SUNLIGHT SLITTING THROUGH BLINDS**

The room hums with creative tension. Not hostility—*potential energy*. Whiteboards scribbled with chords, half-drunk coffees, cigarette butts, acoustic guitars leaned against sofas like tired dogs.

**KATHY** has a legal pad. **GILLEY** tunes an old Fender, absentmindedly. **JIM**, **BILL**, **JEREMY** toss phrases back and forth in low rhythm. Then **JEFF** enters, cool, calm, the storm *already underway* behind his eyes.

He pulls a folded sheet of lyrics from his jacket. No ceremony. No intro.

**JEFF**
I wrote something. Could be new. Could be old.

(passes it to Kathy)

She reads. Her face freezes.

**KATHY**
You ***nuked us from orbit.***

**GILLEY** (casual, like tasting a live wire)
It’s the only way to be sure.

**JIM** (reading over her shoulder)
Wait… this is about that night at the Fairmont?

**JEFF**
Room 1107. I was next door.

**KATHY**
Jesus Christ.

**GILLEY**
That was ***sixteen years ago.***

**JEFF**
Correct. But the walls weren’t sixteen years thick.

**BILL** (laughing, stunned)
You’ve been *sitting* on this?

**JEFF**
No. I’ve been ***haunted*** by it. Every ***moan***. Every ***“UHH, UHH.”*** It was like living inside a ***live feed from a porn shoot*** starring my ***bandmates***.

**KATHY**
You wrote ***a song*** about it?

**JEFF**
I wrote ***a reckoning***.

**GILLEY** (reads, chuckles)
“*I’m ready for sex, to kindle my dreams*.” That’s beautiful, man. Honest. Pathetic. ***You’re welcome.***

**KATHY** (slow, measured)
You waited 16 years to weaponize that moment?

**JEFF**
No. I waited 16 years to ***make art out of the war***.

(pause)
We said we were gonna write new material. So here it is: ***truth***. Raw. Loud. Pornographic. ***Yours. Mine. Ours.***

**GILLEY** (leans back, eyes shining)
I’d sing that chorus.

**KATHY**
I’d ***strangle*** you during the bridge.

**JEFF** (as he sits)
Perfect. Sounds like ***Commuted.***

## **INT. WRITING ROOM – SILENCE AFTER DETONATION**

Everyone’s staring at Jeff. No one blinks. The lyric sheet lies in the center of the table like a confession scrawled in blood.

**JEFF** stands, arms crossed, not angry—*resigned*. **GILLEY** leans back in his chair, legs out, arms wide, waiting for the joke to land. **KATHY** doesn’t move, but she’s steel behind her eyes. The others—Bill, Jim, Jeremy—stay silent. It’s all gone too far.

**JEFF** (flat)
That’s the last song I’ll ever write for Commuted.

(beat)
I’m ***done.***

**GILLEY** (scoffs)
Oh come on, don’t ***pull a Bono***.

**JEFF**
I’m not pulling ***anything***. I’m walking away from ***dishonest bastards***, ***assholes***, ***ne’er-do-wells***, ***cunts***, and ***junkies***.

**JEREMY** (grinning)
Throw in ***booze hounds*** and you’ve got the ***whole set, Jeff***.

(leans forward)
So what ***gives***?

(beat)
What ***finally*** cracked that moral dome of yours?

**JEFF**
What cracked it? Living in a ***touring psych ward*** for almost two decades.  Writing ***truth*** in a band where ***everyone lies for sport***.

(beat)
I kept waiting for the ***reckoning***. For ***you*** to finally own it. For ***her*** to stop editing the past like a Rolling Stone feature. But this? This ***room***? It’s ***too quiet when you lie***. And ***too loud when you perform***.

**KATHY**
Jeff…

**JEFF**
No.

(beat)
You want another chorus? Here’s one:

*“I gave them my blood, they gave me backspin.
I played them my heart, they remixed the sin.
They smiled while drowning, they sang through the burn—
I wrote them a future, they ***fucked it in turn.”***

(beat)
Put ***that*** on vinyl.

**GILLEY** (sits up, no more jokes)
Jeff, man. Come on.  We’re ***filth***, we know. But we’re ***your*** filth.

**JEFF**
Not anymore.

(quiet)
I want to ***make things*** that don’t come with ***apologies attached.***

**KATHY**
So this is it?

**JEFF**
Yeah. This is the ***encore you don’t get to plan***.

(turns to go)
I’m not ***mad*** anymore. I’m just ***empty***.

**GILLEY** (shouting after him)
You’re gonna ***miss us***!

**JEFF** (already at the door)
That’s the ***worst part***.

**DOOR CLOSES.**

Silence. The lyric sheet still on the table. No one touches it.

We’re gonna burn this band to the ***fucking ground.***

## **INT. BAND MANAGER’S OFFICE – CREMEANS HEADQUARTERS – MIDDAY**

The room smells of lemon polish, ink, and quiet Southern judgment. **CREMEANS**, in his always-pressed khakis and a starched button-down, sits behind a desk covered in manila folders, USB drives, receipts in rubber bands, and a single, perfect bottle of unopened Mountain Dew.

Across from him, the six members of ***Commuted*** look like a hungover criminal lineup at a talent show.

**CREMEANS** (calm, flipping a page)
This? This ain’t the darkest chapter. This is ***Tuesday***.

(beat)

I have ***spreadsheets*** of darker chapters.

**KATHY**
Cremeans, Jeff ***quit.***

**CREMEANS** (sipping Mountain Dew like it's communion wine)
Jeff’s ***quit before.*** In ’97, in Montreal, in the blizzard. In ’02, after the Grammys, because someone—I won’t name names—threw up ***on*** a French ambassador.

**GILLEY** (a rictus grin)
Technically ***next*** to him.

**CREMEANS** (ignores him, flips a tabbed folder)
Now, as for ***you two***…

I ***knew*** you were ***screwing***. You weren’t ***subtle***. And do you know how I knew? Not the glances. Not the tension. Not even the ***lyrics***. It was ***hotel damage***.

(he taps a list)

1993, Cleveland: ***Bathtub filled with Perrier.***
2001, Sydney: ***Feather pillow explosion, literal cloud in the hallway.***
2006, Rio de Janeiro: ***Closet respackled because someone’s sequins were ***embedded*** in the drywall… in the ***exact shape*** of a ***female ass***.

**BILL** (laughing despite himself)
Was it ***textured***?

**CREMEANS**
It was ***haunting***.

You ***left your butt in Brazil***.

**KATHY** (to Cremeans)
And you never said anything?

I ***manage***. Not ***moralize***. You two were ***fornicating*** and ***wrecking furniture***. I paid the bills. Logged the receipts. Filed them under ***Recurrences of Chaos, Unrepentant.***

You think Jeff’s departure is a ***surprise*** to me? I’ve been prepping ***contingency plans*** since he ***refused to get on the helicopter in '04*** because Gilley was ***singing Meat Loaf in falsetto*** with ***no pants.***

**GILLEY**
It was ***art.***

**CREMEANS**
It was ***alarming.***

Now. You want to implode? Fine. Just ***do it on time, on beat, and under budget.*** Otherwise? You’re ***off the tour***.

Then:

**GILLEY**
Can I at least keep the ***sequins***?

**CREMEANS**
Only if you sign the ***dang album.***
