**INT. BILL DAVENPORT’S STUDIO – BREAK ROOM & DRUM ROOM – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – 1:54 P.M.**

(SOUND: Muffled metal cutlery scrapes ceramic. A plastic fork cracks in two. Jeff Evans sits still, red flannel buttoned too high for summer, eyes scanning the rim of his can, ignoring the sandwiches the way a monk ignores lust—outwardly. Inside: calculus, weather, the screaming geometry of rhythm.)

On the far wall: **double doors**—shuddering. The break room is quieter than a funeral, but beneath the silence: **BOOM, SNAP, SHUDDER**—the sound of drums too primal to be music. The others chew, pretending not to listen.

Cremeans lingers, restless. His eyes flick from sandwich to door. He hears it like a call to prayer. Or a warning.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**  
That drumming… it sounded… primal.

A decision. The doors, wide as a confession booth, yield. He steps through.

**INT. DRUM ROOM – CONTINUOUS**

Light: bruised yellow from overhead bulbs. Air: sweat, cymbal-dust, last night’s tequila evaporating in the carpet.

The kit: not just a drum kit, but a fortress—cymbals on bent stands, pads like shields, sticks splintered at the tip. **Jim** is at the heart of it all: wild hair stuck to his temples, white t-shirt pasted to his chest, arms pumping in some frantic semaphore.

The camera lingers on his hands—callused, bitten, absolutely alive. **He’s not playing for anyone. He’s beating something out of himself.**

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|Jim]] (V.O.)**  
If you want the truth, you have to bruise it out of the bones.

He crashes through a mess of time signatures, a drummer in a fistfight with God. Then, abruptly—*a shift*. He lands, perfectly, impossibly, on the beat to “When the Levee Breaks.” Bonham from memory, but with more violence, less mercy.

**SOUND:** The room fills with Zeppelin—structured chaos. Jeff Evans appears behind Cremeans, unnoticed, arms folded. His eyes: blank? Or calculating?

Cremeans lets the rhythm crawl up his spine, then snaps out of it, waving. Nothing. Jeff steps forward, deliberate, taps Jim.

**JIM** (panting, pulling off headphones)  
Jesus—what—

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]**
To what do we attribute this current state of… exigency?

**[[5.2. Jeff Cremeans character bible|Cremeans]] (V.O.)**  
He always had to make things sound so… formal.

**Jim** leans back, chest heaving, sweat slick on his throat.

**JIM**  (breathless)  
It’s… it’s Jeff. He’s… he’s acting really weird.

He looks at Cremeans, as if hoping for absolution.

**JIM (V.O.)**  
I didn’t know how else to explain it.

The camera pulls back: three men, sweat, silence, energy.

---

**INT. DRUM ROOM – CONTINUOUS**

Cremeans sighs, a sound big enough to fill the room.

**CREMEANS**  (mock despair)  
I bet he’s playing another one of his sick, complicated jokes.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**  
He always had to be so…extra.

A challenge.

**CREMEANS (CONT’D)**  (firm)  
Alright, Jeff. Here’s the deal. Either you start speaking with a Flesch score like the rest of us…or you go to the ER and get your head examined.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**  
It was time to put an end to this charade.

The camera catches Jeff’s face—a blank so deep it’s almost erotic. He blinks, once, twice.

**JEFF EVANS**  (suddenly plainspoken)  
I’ll try not to be so…dramatic…in the near future.

**JEFF EVANS (V.O.)**  
I can turn it on and off like a switch.

Jim stands, shaky.

**JIM**  
Okay, so…Kathy’s been trying to…you know…get with Jeff. Jeff’s not interested in…getting with anyone. Jeff fat-shamed the entire band.

**JIM (V.O.)**  
It was a bizarre situation.

Jeff, ever the not-quite-apologetic, nods.

**JIM (CONT’D)**  
And…it took us like two or three minutes to even realize he was insulting us.

**JIM (V.O.)**  
It was that subtle.

Jeff allows himself a crooked half-smile. Jim, relentless, keeps going.

**JIM (CONT’D)**  
Oh, and…[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy]], Jeff, and [[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill]] have been hammering away on lyrics for a…concept…double album.

**JIM (V.O.)**  
That was the biggest shock of all.

Cremeans laughs—once. Then he’s all steel.

**CREMEANS**  
A concept double album? Did Paul ever have to deal with this kind of nonsense?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**  
This was getting out of hand.

**CREMEANS (CONT’D)**  (emphatic)  
We don’t do concept albums! And we definitely don’t do double albums! If you have that much material, you release two different albums!

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**  
It was simple logic. No freebies. Two albums? Two SKU's.

Jeff is unmoved, almost smirking.

**JEFF EVANS**  (polite but firm)  
I would respectfully submit that the interconnected thematic elements and narrative arc of the aforementioned musical compositions necessitate their presentation as a cohesive and unified artistic entity, thereby precluding the fragmentation thereof into discrete and independent releases.

**JEFF EVANS (V.O.)**  
I'm back in the saddle again!

---

**INT. DRUM ROOM – CONTINUOUS**

The tension explodes—*not* with words, but with action.

**SOUND:** The shake and fizz of a Mountain Dew bottle, an explosion waiting to happen.

Cremeans grabs the bottle, shakes it harder, pops the cap—points it at Jeff.

**CREMEANS**  
Snap out of it, Jeff! Talk like a regular human being again!

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**  
Enough was enough.

**SOUND:** The bottle erupts, spraying Jeff in green sugar, catching in his hair, dripping down the red flannel like the weirdest stigmata.

The camera holds on Jeff’s face, sticky, stunned.  Jim’s laughter detonates—raw, unselfconscious, real. He’s doubled over, tears mixing with sweat.

**JIM (CONT’D)** (laughing)  
Oh my god…

**JIM (V.O.)**  
One of my best friends had just one-upped the other… and now he was paying the price!

Jeff, dignity mostly intact, deadpans:

**JEFF EVANS (V.O.)**  
I have been… assaulted… with a sugary beverage.

The camera pulls wide: three men, one drum kit, Mountain Dew stains, and a tension finally—blessedly—broken.
