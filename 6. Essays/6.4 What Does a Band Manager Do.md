# What Does a Band Manager Do?



A high-profile rock band’s manager isn’t just a glorified secretary — they’re the glue that keeps the chaos from collapsing in on itself, the ringmaster in a circus where the lions are hungover and the trapeze artists are fighting over royalties. The role spans **both creative and business spheres**, but the balance depends on the personalities in the band and the manager’s own clout.

Here’s the breakdown:

---

### **1. Business Management & Strategy**

- **Deal Negotiation:** Record contracts, tour deals, merch licensing — making sure the band doesn’t sign away their souls (or future royalties) in fine print.
- **Financial Oversight:** Budgeting tours, tracking income streams (album sales, streaming, sync licensing), and making sure the money actually makes it to the band’s accounts instead of evaporating into “industry expenses.”
- **Tour Planning & Logistics:** Booking venues, routing tours to maximize profit and minimize burnout, coordinating travel, visas, and gear shipping.
- **Brand & Career Direction:** Positioning the band in the public eye — choosing which festivals to play, which late-night shows to hit, and which questionable brand sponsorships to reject.

---

### **2. Creative Influence**

- **Sounding Board:** Even if they’re not writing songs, a good manager can give blunt, market-savvy feedback on material — “This chorus slaps” vs. “This will tank in a live set.”
- **Shaping Image:** From album art to stage costumes, they can weigh in to ensure the aesthetic matches the market positioning (and doesn’t kill ticket sales).
- **Collaboration Vetting:** Deciding whether a duet with a pop star is a career-boosting crossover or a sellout disaster.
- **Filtering Opportunities:** Shielding the band from projects that could derail their credibility — or nudging them toward bold moves that expand their audience.

---

### **3. Psychological & Political Role**

- **Conflict Management:** Mediating band feuds before they go nuclear — or knowing when to let members stew for the sake of better songwriting.
- **Industry Relationships:** Leveraging their network with labels, promoters, press, and streaming platforms to get the band into the right rooms.
- **Damage Control:** Handling PR crises — whether it’s a canceled show, a viral onstage meltdown, or a tabloid scandal.
- **Keeping the Train Moving:** Making sure the creative work doesn’t get buried under personal drama, bad business, or the “creative coma” that comes after a hit record.

---

### Influence on Creative vs. Business Decisions

A strong, respected manager can influence both sides heavily — for example, insisting the band record a radio-friendly single to help finance a riskier concept album.  

A weaker or less trusted manager may find the band keeps them strictly on the business side, with little say in the art.  

The sweet spot is when the manager understands the band’s ethos and can **protect the music while growing the empire**. Think Peter Grant with Led Zeppelin — ruthless in business, but never at the expense of the band’s mystique.

# Jeff Cremeans as Commuted's Band Manager

---

### **1. Moral Imperative in Business**

- **Scriptural backbone** – He likely sees a contract not just as a legal instrument, but as a moral covenant. Once you agree, it’s not just about compliance; it’s about integrity before God and others.
- **Aversion to rashness** – Proverbs 19:2 (“It is not good to have zeal without knowledge, nor to be hasty and miss the way”) could practically be his negotiating mantra.

---

### **2. Procedural Discipline**

- **Read → Pray → Counsel → Decide** – That loop mirrors a Southern Baptist decision-making process in other life areas (church voting, major personal choices).
- **Multiple passes** – It’s not procrastination; it’s due diligence with a moral safety net. Every re-read is a way to confirm he’s not compromising ethics, reputation, or the band’s future.

---

### **3. Reputation Effects**

- **Few mistakes** – The industry learns that you can’t rush him, which frustrates some dealmakers but earns grudging respect from others.
- **“Their word is bond”** – That’s gold in the music business. Promoters, labels, and partners learn that when Cremeans signs, there’s no wiggle-out clause coming six months later.
- **Negotiation deterrent** – Opportunistic operators often avoid even trying to lowball or bury him in bad terms because they know he’ll catch it.

---

### **4. Narrative Uses**

- It’s a slow-burn character trait you can show *in action*:
    
    - A tour almost collapses because he takes three weeks to sign the routing contract, but the delay saves the band from being locked into an overextended schedule.
        
    - A streaming deal gets pushed to the last minute, but his insistence on a moral clause (no ad tie-ins with certain industries) preserves the band’s image years later.
        
- Creates **organic conflict** with band members who are more impulsive — especially in high-stakes, high-emotion periods.

### **3. Fits His Faith-Driven Approach**
