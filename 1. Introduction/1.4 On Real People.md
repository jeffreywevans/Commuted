# **On Real People**

Injecting real-world people into the narrative of the Commuted universe serves several interconnected purposes:

1. **Grounding the Fiction in Reality**: Including figures like John Legend or Joe Rogan helps anchor the fictional band Commuted in a believable cultural landscape. These references act as signposts, allowing the audience to place Commuted within a broader musical and media ecosystem. It bridges the gap between fictional mythos and actual cultural memory.

2. **Enhancing Authenticity**: By incorporating known personalities in believable scenarios (e.g., John Legend jamming with the band in a Boston studio), the narrative deepens its verisimilitude. It allows the fictional events to feel more plausible, as if they could have happened just out of public view.
   
3. **Cultural Commentary and Parody**: The use of public figures often doubles as commentary. For instance, the band’s interactions with the BBC journalist or the emotional chaos during the Grammy Awards parody real media behavior and the exploitative nature of celebrity coverage. It’s not just name-dropping—it’s cultural interrogation.
   
4. **Emotional Weight and Juxtaposition**: When [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]] mentions John Legend during an interview, it marks a rare moment of emotional vulnerability. The band, normally guarded, inadvertently reveals depth and grief. Using a respected real-life musician in that moment elevates the stakes and underscores the sincerity of Jeff's memory.
   
5. **Juxtaposition of Personas**: The characters of Commuted are deeply flawed, emotionally volatile, and often resistant to the norms of fame. Dropping them into interactions with hyper-professional, media-trained real-world celebrities highlights their dysfunction and eccentricity. It’s a way of emphasizing their outsider status.
   
6. **Creating Legacy through Association**: The suggestion that [[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy Espich]] influenced or collaborated with real-world icons elevates her posthumous legacy within the story. It makes her absence felt more acutely. It implies she mattered not just within the band's internal mythology but potentially in the broader musical canon.
   
7. **Foreshadowing and Irony**: Real-world references often serve as ironic counterpoints. Jeff's nostalgic invocation of John Legend contrasts sharply with the band's present-day dysfunction. It hints at a lost golden age and a future that didn’t pan out as desired.
   
8. **Narrative Momentum and Shock Value**: When real-world names get casually dropped in a surly, media-unfriendly interview, it subverts audience expectations and jolts the plot forward. These moments often become narrative hinges, as with the BBC interview that spirals into unprecedented emotional territory after the Legend reference.
