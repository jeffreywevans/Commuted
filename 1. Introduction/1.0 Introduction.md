## **Series Overview: Commuted**

### **Logline**
Six Midwestern teenagers form a rock band in 1989. By 2032, they are music industry legends. What lies between is a volatile, sex-fueled, emotionally scorched odyssey of identity, betrayal, trauma, and raw artistic ambition. This is not a redemption arc. This is a war story.

### **Series Premise**
*[[2.2 Commuted|Commuted]]* is a serialized, character-driven drama tracking the rise, collapse, reinvention, and mythologizing of a fictional rock band over four decades. The show starts in the winter of 1989 in rural Ohio and expands into a nonlinear, multi-timeline narrative that spans from teenage garages to global arenas, from awkward first kisses to public overdoses, from cheap flannel to couture leather. We follow the band not just as artists, but as fractured individuals: neurodivergent outcasts, angry introverts, manipulative charmers, and emotionally neglected kids who grow into morally compromised adults.

The story is centered on [[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy Espich]], the band's lone female member, whose evolution from overlooked backup guitarist to defiant creative force rewrites every internal power dynamic. The men in the band shift around her: some resisting, some following, all exposed. Music is not an escape. It is the crucible.

### **Genre & Tone**
Adult prestige drama. Coming-of-age meets band biopic meets psychological warfare. Think *The Social Network* meets *Almost Famous* meets *Showgirls* (directed by Paul Verhoeven and written by Quentin Tarantino in his most emotionally sincere, sexually unflinching mode). It's caustic, lyrical, raw, funny, tragic, and deeply nostalgic — but with teeth.

### **Structure**
The show unfolds over eight seasons:
#### **Season 1**: The origin — 1989–1991. Forming the band, discovering power, and defining the internal battlefield.
#### **Season 2**: Ascension and fracture — 1992-1995. First shows, first betrayals.
#### **Season 3**: Industry meets identity — 1995-1998. Record deals, image-making, collapse.
#### **Season 4**: Reinvention — 1999-2008. New sounds, new wounds, new frontlines.
#### **Season 5**: The End Of the Beginning — 2009-2012.  Who gets remembered, who becomes immortal, and why do we care? 
#### **Season 6**: Boston — 2012-2013, intercut with all timelines. A comeuppance for Commuted. Starts with Kathy's stroke in rehab, then gets far worse.
#### **Season 7**: The Fall — 2014-2023, intercut with all timelines. Failure, flailing, hiatus, and hell. Is Commuted redeemable?
#### **Season 8**: Legends and Their Lies — 2024-2032, intercut with all timelines. Who gets remembered, and who gets erased, through the lens of the most unreliable narrators.

Each episode is anchored by a "Touchstone Moment", a character or band-defining decision, often in quiet contrast to the chaos around it. Episodes blend present-day scenes with jump cuts to future consequences and past wounds.
