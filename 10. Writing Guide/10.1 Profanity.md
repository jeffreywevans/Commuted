**Dialogue Tone: Adult Language & Verbal Realism –** ***Commuted***

**Mandate:**  

Characters in *Commuted* speak the way real people speak when they’re unguarded, fucked up, trying to impress someone, trying to bury someone, or trying to stop themselves from falling apart. This is not network TV dialogue. This is *scorched-earth, Midwestern truth-speak.*  This is an excerpt from the [[10.0 Writing Stylebook|Writing Stylebook]].

---

**1. Profanity as Grammar:** Profanity in *Commuted* is not decoration — it’s punctuation, emotional scoring, and at times, a primal scream. It’s used:

- As rhythm: “Fuck, no. Fuck. No.”
- As indictment: “You selfish, performative, backstabbing *asshole.*”
- As communion: “You’re my best fucking friend, man.”
- As silence filler: “Shit, I don’t know, fuckin’… maybe.”

Characters don’t “drop F-bombs.” They *breathe* them.

---

**2. Voice Specificity:** Every character uses adult language differently — profanity is a signature.

- **[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]:** Uses cursing as both weapon and armor. Sharp, staccato.
- **[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy]]:** Rarely curses, but when she does, it detonates. Her “fuck” is nuclear.
- **[[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill]]:** Precision swearing. Philosophical profanity. “You’re not wrong, but you’re still full of shit.”
- **[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|Jim]]:** Uses vulgarity to assert dominance, usually loud and unnecessary.
- **[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|Gilley]]:** Oddly poetic. Biblical swearing. “You’ve invited Satan into rehearsal, boys.”
- **[[5.2. Jeff Cremeans character bible|Cremeans]]**: **NEVER** swears.  If Cremeans uses profanity, someone is dying.  Jeff Cremeans has a strong moral aversion to traditional profanity and will use euphemism even when virulently angry.
    
---

**3. Layered Meaning:** Cursing can be:

- **Intimate** – “Fuck you” can mean “please don’t leave.”
- **Ritualized** – The band swears *together*, like shared slang or inside jokes.
- **Exhausted** – Some swears are just sighs with teeth.

---

**4. Rules of Use:**

- If a curse doesn’t reveal character or emotion, keep it.
- Never censor reality. If someone’s spiraling, let the language spiral with them.
- Language should reflect era — Gen X teens swore differently than 2020s adults. No anachronisms.   
- Don’t neutralize rage. Let the ugly words sit on the table.

---

**5. Sample Excerpts:**

- “You think this is about ego? You smug fuck, this is about *survival.*”
- “Jesus wept, Cremeans, you really are the devil’s overdressed nephew.”   
- “I’m gonna go jerk off to a chord progression and cry, okay? Leave me the fuck alone.”

---

**6. Tone Note for Writers:** The dialogue in *Commuted* should feel like a demo tape: raw, imperfect, unforgettable. Swearing is part of the music — don’t mute it. Let it distort. Let it howl. Let it land like a goddamn cymbal crash.
