**The “No Fade to Black” Checklist** is the **ritual safeguard** against emotional omission and sanitized writing. It’s not just about keeping a sex scene explicit or describing sweat—it’s about ensuring that every **moment that wants to be felt** is **fully inhabited.** This checklist is designed for use **during revision** or even **during drafting when you feel the instinct to cut away.**  This is part of the [[10.0 Writing Stylebook|Writing Stylebook]].

---

## 🕯️ **“No Fade to Black” Checklist**

*A sacred realism tool for scenes that demand truth, mess, and presence.*

---

### 1. **Where Does the Emotion Peak?**

- 🔲 Have you **stayed in the moment** long enough for the emotion to crest?
    
- 🔲 Have you captured the **exact physical response** to that emotion? (E.g. breath hitch, jaw tension, fingertip tremble, sweat drip)
    
> *If you stop at “she gasped,” you’ve faded. What came next? Where did her hand go? Did she flinch, moan, freeze, weep, clench?*

---

### 2. **Have You Documented the Body?**

- 🔲 Have you shown what the body **does** in this scene—without shame or euphemism?
    
- 🔲 Do you include the **textures**, **fluids**, **smells**, **aches**, and **awkwardness** that live inside the moment?
    
> *Did he come? How did it land? Was she wet? Where? What did it taste like? Did the bedsheet stick to her thigh?*

---

### 3. **What Would a Cinematic Cut Avoid?**

- 🔲 Have you kept the **gum under the table**?
    
- 🔲 Did you describe the **glare off the instrument**, the **damp in the underwear**, the **bandage she’s hiding**, the **teeth grinding** through the joke?
    
> *Include what a PG film would remove. That's your honesty check.*

---

### 4. **Where Does the Sound Live?**

- 🔲 Is there **ambient noise**? Breathing? City outside? The sound of shoes kicked off? The *tick* of a fingernail on glass?
    
- 🔲 Do **pauses have weight** or sound? Silence is never empty. It rings.
    

> *Sound isn't just dialogue. It's interruption. It's emphasis. It's memory surfacing in the room.*

---

### 5. **Is There Shame? Is There Power?**

- 🔲 Have you allowed for **emotional contradiction**? Does someone feel triumphant *and* cheap? Loved *and* filthy? Holy *and* humiliated?
    
- 🔲 Have you given space for characters to **judge themselves**, or hide that they are?
    
> *If she regrets it halfway through, show the exact moment. If he stares at the ceiling and lies about loving it, catch that lie in his chest.*

---

### 6. **What Does the Room Remember?**

- 🔲 Have you described the **space** like a witness?
    
- 🔲 Does the room reflect or contradict the action happening in it?
    
> *That chair saw this before. That stain on the wall? That’s from last year’s breakdown. Don’t ignore it. Let it whisper.*

---

### 7. **What Happens *After* the Moment?**

- 🔲 Did you stay long enough to show **the aftermath**? (The limp dick, the clean-up of her vagina, the laugh, the silence, the recoil, the craving.)
    
- 🔲 Did you allow for **dissonance**? (Characters lying, out loud, or by omission? Faking camaraderie, faking orgasms, delivering their truth, not the absolute truth.)
    
> *He kisses her shoulder, but she’s already looking at the door. She zips up her jeans without meeting his eyes. She prays under her breath.*

---

## ✍️ Usage Tip:

Use this checklist **when you feel the impulse to skip** or write a placeholder like:

> “And then they made love.”  
> “She left it unsaid.”  
> “They all sat in silence.”
