# **The World of *Commuted***

*Commuted* takes place in a heightened version of America from 1989 through the 2030s, a deeply textured, emotionally resonant parallel timeline that mirrors our world but tunes it to a different frequency. The characters don’t just live through history, they change its rhythm.

## **1. Setting Anchors – Time and Place**

1989–1994: Rural and suburban Ohio. Rust Belt detritus. Unheated garages. Small-town oppressiveness contrasted with bursts of teenage audacity. Local malls, school dances, VHS rentals, the back rows of used record stores — all sacraments.

1995–2005: The road. Tour buses, motel parking lots, dingy green rooms. Industry cities, like L.A., Chicago, London, Berlin, Tokyo, but never home. Fame is isolating. Sex is currency. The sound is the only constant.

2006-2012: A rebirth, as four of the six members shake drugs.

2012-2014: Götterdämmerung

2023–2032: A half-glamorous, half-ghostly future. Commuted are icons now. Their history is part of the culture, but their truth is not. The world has remembered them wrong. The final episodes slowly unravel the mythology to show the mess underneath.

---
## **2. The Industry as Battleground**  

Labels, managers, radio stations, awards shows are the arenas where power is brokered and souls are bartered. The music industry is portrayed with both archival realism and narrative exaggeration.  Contracts are cursed scrolls; studio execs are smiling vampires. Fame is never just given, it's sold and the currency is your soul.

---
## **3. Cultural Context and Parallels**  

Every era within *Commuted* is written with deliberate echoes of the real world:

#### The ‘90s media obsession with “girl power” weaponized against Kathy’s creative control.

#### The post-9/11 cultural tone shift mirrors the band’s descent into paranoia and exploitation.

#### The 2020s nostalgia cycle commodifies their past and distorts their image for corporate gain.

---

## **4. Fictional but Plausible World-Building**

#### **Venues, cities, record labels, magazines, podcasts, and producers** are invented but intricately connected to real-world equivalents. Every fictional location has a texture, a history, a smell.

#### **Commuted’s discography** has complete liner notes, tours, and feuds are woven into the broader fictional music landscape.

#### **Famous cameos and crossovers** are plausible but always just off-screen. A bitter SNL monologue. A Rolling Stone cover. A thrown drink at a VH1 awards show.

## **5. Media Presence**  

The band’s cultural saturation grows season by season:
#### Bootleg tape culture → Zine press → Late night TV → MTV → Tabloid scandals → Reddit conspiracies → Spotify algorithm resurrection → AI hologram tours.

## **6. Technological Integration**  

Technology is both savior and betrayer. [[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill]] champions digital tools. [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]] resents them. [[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy]] adapts and subverts. The show explores:
#### Analog vs. digital recording
#### Napster’s impact
#### AI voice-cloning lawsuits in 2031
#### The savage nature of connectivity

## **7. Rules of the World**

#### Memory is unreliable. Flashbacks contradict.
#### Nobody tells the full truth, especially on camera.
#### Fame doesn’t absolve — it disfigures.
#### The music is always real, even when everything else is fake.
#### You can break the law; you cannot escape the law of consequences.
