EXT. TOKYO DOME – PARKING LOT – 3:15 P.M. – AUGUST 2028 – SUMMER

The heat is a living thing. Not sun-on-your-skin heat — inside-your-bones heat. The kind that curls up in your marrow and decides to stay awhile. Asphalt shimmering. Air so wet it feels like chewing warm cloth.

The Commuted tour bus idles, diesel breath mingling with the faint tang of curry from a nearby food stand. Inside, the band has retreated to the upholstered cave, blinds down, AC working like a drowning man. Soundcheck in forty-five minutes.

INT. TOUR BUS – CONTINUOUS

Jeff’s hunched over a laptop, headphones clamped on. Bill is sprawled sideways on the couch, phone in hand, scrolling with the vague menace of someone ready to pick a fight just to feel alive. Jeremy is shirtless, damp with sweat, drinking Pocari Sweat like it’s bourbon. Jim is absently tapping out paradiddles on his thigh, eyes half-shut.

The bus door swings open.

AVRIL climbs up the steps, hair sticking to her neck, lanyard and photo ID swinging against her chest. She’s wearing ripped black shorts, a vintage Clash tank, eyeliner melting just enough to look intentional.

Jeremy doesn’t even say hello — just reaches out, snatches her badge off her chest, and squints at the photo like he’s holding evidence in a murder trial.

JEREMY (flat, accusing)
I know what the real Avril looks like. You’re that imposter.

A beat. No one breathes.

Bill bolts upright, eyes wide in theatrical horror.

BILL (shrieking like he’s announcing a death in the family)
Avril’s dead! They replaced her!

Jeff sighs, pulls one earcup off.

JEFF
Here we fucking go.

Avril plants both hands on her hips.

AVRIL
Really. We’re doing this now? Forty-five minutes to soundcheck and you two are playing Reddit conspiracy theorist?

JEREMY (leaning in, mock-serious)
Your jawline’s different. In this photo, you’ve got the ‘04 jaw. Now? You’ve got… post-’16 jaw.

BILL
And your eyes. Those are Clone Eyes. The dead giveaway.

JIM (not looking up)
If you guys start measuring her skull, I’m getting off this bus.

AVRIL (smiling, dangerous)
Measure my skull and I’ll put yours through the fucking window.

Jeremy bursts out laughing, the kind that fills the bus and makes the AC sound like a whisper.

KENDALL 
She’s fine. She’s real. And even if she’s not — this one can sing, so who cares?

Avril snatches her badge back from Jeremy.

AVRIL (tight smile)
Thanks, Dad. I’ll remember this next time you forget your lyrics and need me to bail you out.

BILL (aside to Jeremy)
You know if she was a clone, they’d upgrade her pitch. And she’s still flat in “Victory Monument.”

Avril flips him off without turning around, drops her backpack on the couch, and cranks the mini-fridge open for a cold bottle of water.

JEFF (deadpan)
Forty-three minutes.

Nobody moves. The bus hums with heat, with electricity, with the kind of tension that’s half-play, half-something else.

Bill leans back again, grinning.

BILL
If she is the fake Avril… she’s the best fake we’ve ever had.

Avril smirks, takes a long drink, and doesn’t answer. Which is answer enough.

INT. HOTEL SPA – TOKYO – 3:02 A.M. – SUMMER 2028

The hotel’s rooftop spa is a glass box in the sky, steam ghosting against floor-to-ceiling windows. The city sprawls below, neon still buzzing, trains still clattering far past last call. The moon’s just a white smudge in the heat haze.

The hot tub gurgles lazily. I’m in it, shoulders bare above the froth, hair pinned up and damp from the steam. Across from me, Avril stretches out, head tilted back, neck gleaming. Her bikini’s hanging on the teak rack with mine — we’ve reduced ourselves to the kind of easy, naked indifference that only comes when the world outside is too loud and the water’s too hot to care.

We’re flushed from the night — not just from the show, though the ringing in our bones is still the crowd’s doing. But from the afterparties. The corridors. The knowing looks.

Avril cracks an ice cube between her teeth from the glass she’s holding. Smirks.

AVRIL
It’s about time these fuckers are starting to really tease me.

I blink, because there’s a satisfaction in her voice that’s not stage-ready — it’s personal, raw, a little vicious.

AVRIL (cont’d)
This is starting to feel a lot more like home, isn’t it?

She says “home” like it’s both an embrace and a warning. Like the smell of motor oil in a garage, like the band’s arguments in August heat back in Ohio — or the late nights in New York when you can feel the whole city leaning in to hear your secret.

I swirl my fingers in the water, the heat licking my wrists.

KENDALL
You mean our kind of home. The kind with knives in the silverware drawer and everybody pretending they’re just here for the champagne.

She laughs — a low, warm ripple.

AVRIL
Exactly.

Outside, Tokyo keeps burning its lights for no one. Inside, the steam closes the space until it’s just us, two women with the stage still in our muscles, knowing the teasing isn’t just banter. It’s blood in the water. And we’ve both learned to swim in it.

INT. HOTEL SPA – TOKYO – 3:06 A.M. – SUMMER 2028

The water’s at that perfect, slow-boil burn where your skin goes pink and your brain starts plotting. Avril leans back against the tile lip of the tub, ice clinking in her glass. I watch the steam curl around her collarbones and think of the way the guys were so pleased with themselves earlier, like middle school boys who’d just learned how to work a lighter.

I let the pause stretch — then grin.

KENDALL
Retaliation.

Avril cracks one eye open.

KENDALL (cont’d)
In kind.

A slow smirk spreads over her mouth, the kind that says you have my attention.

KENDALL (cont’d)
A long time ago I had to learn what “mutual assured destruction” was — because Jeff wouldn’t shut up about it. Every argument, every tour schedule fight, every contract dispute: “If they nuke us, we nuke back.”

Avril snorts, tips her head toward me.

AVRIL
And you’re saying… we prank the guys?

KENDALL (leaning forward, elbows on knees)
Not just prank. Humiliate. The kind of thing that lives in their heads rent-free for years.

AVRIL (pretending to think)
Public? Or private?

KENDALL
Private enough to keep Cremeans from having a stroke, public enough to sting.

She chews on her ice cube like it’s an idea, then tosses it into the water.

AVRIL
Okay. Options.

KENDALL
We start with Jim — he’s the easiest. Switch his in-ear monitor feed so every count-in is replaced with my voice reading his old MySpace poetry.

Avril bursts out laughing so hard she slaps the water, sending a wave against my shoulder.

AVRIL
And Bill —

KENDALL
— we reprogram his synth presets so the first note of “Victory Monument” plays the Mortal Kombat theme.

AVRIL
Jeremy’s trickier. He expects sabotage.

KENDALL
Which is why we do it slow. Replace one pedal at a time until the whole chain just makes duck noises.

Her head goes back again, that laugh — half-feral, half-exhausted.

AVRIL
What about Jeff?

I raise my brows.

KENDALL
Jeff gets the full doctrine. Mutual assured destruction.

AVRIL
Meaning?

KENDALL
Meaning I make sure he thinks I’ve found something… unspeakable. And I leave just enough evidence for him to sweat bullets for a week before I reveal it’s nothing.

Avril’s eyes gleam like stage lights on wet vinyl.

AVRIL
You’re evil.

KENDALL
I’m home.

Outside, Tokyo glitters like it’s in on the joke. Inside, the two of us lean in, already stitching together the kind of plot that makes a band like ours worth surviving.

INT. HOTEL SPA – TOKYO – 3:11 A.M. – SUMMER 2028

Avril’s laughter is still dripping off the tiles from the Jeff bit. She sits forward now, forearms on her knees, chin shiny with steam.

AVRIL (still catching her breath)
Okay, but — what the fuck with that man?

I tilt my head.

AVRIL (cont’d) (louder)
What the fuck with that man, fine?

She laughs again, the kind of laugh that has no oxygen in it, just sound and disbelief.

AVRIL (cont’d)
Unspeakable!

It echoes in the glass box, the word unspeakable bouncing between us like she just named a new genre of music.

I can’t help it — I grin.

KENDALL
You have no idea.

She arches a brow, playful, predatory.

AVRIL
Try me.

KENDALL
I won’t. Not because I’m protecting him. Because I’m protecting you.

Her eyes narrow like she’s just been challenged.

AVRIL
Protecting me from what?

KENDALL
From the fact that once you know, you can’t un-know. And then you’ll wake up three years from now in a hotel bathtub in Stuttgart, thinking about it, and you’ll wish to God I’d kept my mouth shut.

Avril’s jaw drops — and then she laughs again, loud, delighted, kicking at the water so it splashes over my shoulder.

AVRIL
Oh my God, now I have to know.

KENDALL
Nope.

She points at me, mock-accusing.

AVRIL
You’re a sadist.

KENDALL (raising my glass)
Mutual assured destruction, darling.

And she laughs again, helpless, shaking her head — because she knows exactly how far down the rabbit hole I’ve already gone with that man, and how much deeper I’m willing to dig if provoked.

INT. HOTEL SPA – TOKYO – 3:18 A.M. – SUMMER 2028

The water’s stopped feeling like silk and started feeling like soup. My skin’s flushed from neck to ankles, that uniform pink that says you stayed in too long. Even my hair’s gone the shade of dark red it gets when it’s wet, and I’m pretty sure if anyone looked down between my thighs right now, the carpet would match the drapes — and the blush.

I push up from the ledge, water sluicing off me in sheets, steam rolling up my spine. My legs feel loose, boneless, like I could just fold onto the tile and sleep there.

Avril tilts her head, watching.

AVRIL (mock-suspicious)
You’re getting out this soon?

I grab a towel from the rack, start blotting the steam-slick heat off my chest.

KENDALL
Yep. I’m a married woman. Sue me.

She grins at that, the kind of grin that says married is a technicality, not a deterrent.

I wrap the towel high under my arms, hair dripping down my back, and glance over my shoulder.

KENDALL (cont’d)
But tomorrow — we ride at dawn.

Avril’s smirk widens.

KENDALL (cont’d)
Victory or death.
