# **GOD’S OWN BASEMENT**

---

## **INT. GILLEY’S PARENTS’ GARAGE – NIGHT – DECEMBER 1989**

First rehearsal. Not first jam, not first hangout, not first “hey, let’s play something” – first real, on-purpose, fuck-the-future rehearsal. The garage is 41 degrees and falling. They haven’t figured out layering yet, so they’re all in dumb combinations: fingerless gloves and sleeveless flannels, wool socks and holey jeans. Every kid in this room believes with absolute conviction that they will be famous in four months. Maybe five, if winter’s a bitch.

Gilley sets up the mic stand like he’s defusing a bomb. He’s the tallest, so he gets to front. Also because he yells the loudest and his parents pay the utility bill. Jeremy Evans, lithe and twitchy, has already broken two sticks on the lawn mower. Jeff keeps silently rearranging the pedals on his bass board in an order that only makes sense to him. Bill’s synth is balanced on an old ironing board. Kathy is tuned and ready, but saying nothing, because her nerves come out quiet.

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]**
Okay. We start with “Bleed American,” then do the one Jeff wrote, then the Pixies cover if we don’t suck.

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|JEFF]]** (muttering)
It’s not called “the one Jeff wrote.” It’s called “Regicide.”

**GILLEY**
Whatever. Don’t make it a history lesson, man.

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|KATHY]]**
Pixies last. We’ll blow our voices on it otherwise.

**[[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|JEREMY]]**
I won’t. I’ve got throat chakra dominance.

**[[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|BILL]]**
You have bronchitis.

Jeff grins into his collar.

The drum kit is borrowed from a cousin. The guitar strap is duct-taped. The mic cord is too short. The amp gives off a high whine that only Kathy and dogs can hear. But when they start—when the count hits four—they’re electric. Not good, not yet. But charged. Like goddamn copper wire run under skin.

By the second chorus, Jeff and Bill are instinctively syncing bass to synth like they’ve been playing together for years. Kathy’s guitar is tentative, but she’s got the best sense of dynamics. Gilley forgets a lyric and howls a new one that’s better. Jeremy breaks another stick but doesn’t stop. The lawn mower gets dented.

They stop. They breathe. They stare at each other.

**JEFF**
Do it again.

And they do.

The heater never comes on. The synth drops a key halfway through the second run. Gilley bleeds from the knuckle because he punched the mic by accident. Nobody cares. Time skips like a scratched CD.

Outside, the wind shifts. A jet flies low. The garage groans.

Inside, six teenagers make noise and call it church.

## **INT. GILLEY’S PARENTS’ GARAGE – NIGHT TWO – DECEMBER 1989**

**Same setup. Same cold. Same egos. Slightly worse moods.**

The band’s halfway through their second run of “Regicide.” It’s too fast. Kathy’s strings are slipping. Jeremy keeps jumping time. Bill’s synth cuts out on the chorus and Jeff’s jaw has been clenched since verse two.

**GILLEY** (shouting)
Jesus, can we play the song and not try to *defeat* it?

**JEREMY**
I *am* playing it. You’re the one screaming like you’re getting exorcised.

**GILLEY**
Because I *can’t hear myself*! Kathy, is your amp even on?

**KATHY** (checking)
Yes.

**GILLEY**
Well then *point it somewhere*, ‘cause it sounds like it’s aimed at Jesus.

**KATHY** (flat)
Maybe he needs it more than you do.

**BILL**
Can we just stop and fix the fucking chord progression on the bridge?

**JEFF**
The chord progression is *fine*. You’re the one who came in half a measure late.

**BILL**
I came in late because Jeremy missed his cue!

**JEREMY**
I didn’t *miss* it. I *recontextualized* it.

**KATHY**
Oh my god.

**JEREMY**
I’m serious! I shifted the beat to counterpoint the synth. It’s called *dissonance.*

**JEFF**
It’s called a *fuckup*, Jeremy.

**JEREMY** (smug as fuck)
You mean a *recontextualized fuckup.*

**GILLEY**
You keep using that word like it’s an apology in a trenchcoat.

**BILL**
Can we all agree the problem is Jeremy’s obsession with the word *recontextualize*?

**JEREMY** (loudly)
I just think we’re too obsessed with *precision*!  Punk isn’t about playing it right. It’s about *meaning it*.
**KATHY**
You mean like when you forgot the entire second verse?

**JEREMY**
That wasn’t forgetting. That was *recontextualizing silence.*

**GILLEY**
Oh fuck *you*, Jeremy.

**JEFF**
Every rehearsal is like a lecture from a kid who eats glue.

**JEREMY**
Yeah? And every bassline you write sounds like a panic attack in lowercase.

**JEFF**
You think you're clever, but you're just an upper-middle-class narcissist with a distortion pedal and a fear of melody.

**BILL**
Says the guy who composes his bass parts like he’s scoring a funeral in Morse code.

**KATHY**
Can we *please* just—

**GILLEY** (cutting her off)
No, no, let it burn. Jeremy thinks “recontextualizing” means “flailing like a wet rat.”

**JEREMY**
It *does* if the rat has a fucking *vision.*

**JEFF**
What vision? A flaming trash can and an unpaid therapy bill?

**KATHY** (finally snapping)
You want to talk about vision? *I* wrote the only song we’ve finished without screaming and you all *ignored* it for two weeks because it didn’t have a penis metaphor.

**BILL**
That’s not true!

**KATHY**
Really? What’s it called then?

**BILL**
Uh… “Track Two”?

**GILLEY**
We’ll name it later! Jesus!

**JEREMY**
Let’s name it *Recontextualize Me, Daddy.*

**JEFF**
I *swear to God* I will beat you with that lawn chair.

**BILL**
This is why we need a click track.

**JIM (O.S.)** (yelling from the bathroom)
**You need a fucking *therapist*!**

**(Beat. Everyone turns.)**

**GILLEY**
Jesus Christ, were you taking a *shit*?

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]]** (entering, wiping hands on jeans)
No. I was listening to you *children* argue like it was a debate club for people with undiagnosed conditions and cheap gear.

**JEFF**
That’s rich coming from the guy who thinks tempo is a *suggestion.*

**JIM**
No, tempo is a *reality,* and yours is warped by *ego and weed.*

**JEREMY**
Tempo is *socially constructed*.

**EVERYONE**
SHUT THE FUCK UP, JEREMY.

**(Beat.)**

**JEREMY** (quietly)
Still a good word.

**(Beat. Everyone breathes. Even the fan sounds exhausted.)**

**KATHY** (deadpan)
So. Same time tomorrow?

**JEFF**
Yeah.

**GILLEY**
Yeah.

**BILL**
Sure.

**JIM**
I’ll bring Advil.

**JEREMY**
I’ll bring synonyms.

**JEFF**
Bring duct tape.

**(They all start to pack up. Nobody apologizes. Nobody needs to.)**

## **INT. GILLEY’S PARENTS’ GARAGE – POST-BLOWOUT – NIGHT – DECEMBER 1989**

The amp is still humming even though no one's touched it in ten minutes. The fan’s off. The room is thick with the smell of old sweat, hot cables, and secondhand adrenaline. Empty soda cans litter the workbench like shrapnel from a nerd war. Someone cracked a cymbal. No one admits who. The fight is over but the air is still crowded with ghosts.

Bill sits cross-legged by the door, back against the drywall, silently re-soldering the cable Jeremy yanked too hard. Gilley’s leaning over the lyrics sheet like it insulted his mother. Jim is off somewhere in the house getting aspirin or bourbon or both. Jeff’s coiling cords again with the precision of a man trying to make his spine uncoil. Kathy is nowhere.

Which is why it takes them a second to realize Jeremy is gone too.

**CUT TO:**

## **INT. GARAGE – REAR CORNER – BEHIND THE MARGINALIST’S STACK – CONTINUOUS**

It's not even a full Marshall stack. It's the *Marginalist* — a cursed flea market half-stack Jeff bought for $40 and three weed brownies. It growls like a chainsaw and smells like radiator fluid. Behind it is just enough room for a desperate person to hide.

Jeremy is curled there, knees pulled up, hoodie hood over his face like he’s auditioning for an early Depeche Mode video. The margins of his notebook — *“Setlist Doodles (DO NOT READ)”* — are full of alternate lyrics, diagrams of imaginary pedals, and a sketch of Gilley mid-shout labeled *Vocal God??* with six question marks and a tiny heart.

His nose is red. His eyes are red. He’s not sobbing exactly. Just leaking with purpose.

**JEREMY (muttering)**
Fuckin’ *recontextualize* that, you bastards…

**He punches the floor once, lightly, like it's complicit. Then curls tighter.**

Because here’s the thing: Jeremy *knows* he's the weakest link. Not in skill — he's *good*. But in standing. Gilley’s got height and cheekbones and *frontman privileges*. Jeff is the serious one, the one adults talk to like he’s already got a mortgage. Kathy plays like she’s on fire and doesn’t *need* permission. Jim is a chaos engine but nobody dares challenge him. Bill is quiet, weird, and technically savvier than everyone, so he gets away with it.

Jeremy? Jeremy is seventeen, sleeps in a room with Star Wars sheets, and *still has to get a ride* to practice. His bandmates are *technically adults.* They can vote. They can buy spray paint without getting carded. Kathy once bought a beer at a punk show and nobody blinked. Meanwhile, Jeremy still has to pass *American Government* to graduate next year.

And now they’re all yelling at him because he wants to *shift the beat* and “recontextualize” and yes, he knows he said it too much. But when you’re the youngest, every idea has to fight for air. And when they land, they land *hard*.

And what for?

**The fucking Tecumseh High School Christmas Talent Show.**

A glorified church basement stage with a sagging velvet curtain and a plastic Christmas tree that smells like closet. A show judged by *Mrs. Larkin*, the substitute librarian who hates drums. A venue with exactly three working microphones and an AV guy who once accidentally played “*Hot for Teacher*” during a student choir recital.

They are risking their dignity for a chance to *maybe* not get laughed off stage in front of sophomores and ex-girlfriends.

And yet?

It *matters*.

Because Jeff is bored out of his mind at a state university after just two months and has no plan B. Because Kathy got a job shelving books at the Tipp City library and it’s killing her soul one Dewey decimal at a time. Because Gilley is flunking Econ but still believes, deep in his dumb, hot chest, that rock can save him. Because Bill's parents keep muttering about trade school. Because Jim is probably going to get someone pregnant before New Year’s and has exactly zero coping skills.

And because Jeremy? Jeremy still believes in *magic*. He thinks music can fix things. Make people see him. Make them *shut up and listen*.

He wipes his nose with the sleeve of his hoodie, takes a shaky breath, and says aloud:

**JEREMY**
It’s a *good* word.

And then—because he’s seventeen and everything is a dare—he stands up, shoulders back, face blotchy, voice steady, and marches back around the Marginalist.

Everyone turns.

**JEREMY (clear, sharp)**
I wrote a new bridge. It's in 5/4. You can yell at me *after* you hear it.

**(Beat. Gilley raises one eyebrow. Jeff shrugs. Bill unplugs the soldering iron. Jim walks back in with a bag of Doritos and a bottle of aspirin.)**

**KATHY (appearing suddenly, somehow holding her guitar and chewing gum)**
Let him play it.

**JIM**
Wait, are we—? Are we *rehearsing again*?

**JEFF**
Yeah. Because if we’re gonna get humiliated at Tecumseh High, we might as well do it in *time.*

**GILLEY**
Fuck it. One more run.

**They plug in. Jeremy nods once, cracks his knuckles, and counts them in.**

## **INT. TECUMSEH HIGH SCHOOL – MULTIPURPOSE AUDITORIUM – 6:50 P.M. – DECEMBER 14, 1989**

Everything is red and green and full of fucking *tinsel*. Tinsel like a threat. Tinsel like it’s trying to choke the very air out of the space. A dying Christmas exploded in here and nobody called an ambulance. Paper snowflakes dangle from the rafters. A homemade sign hangs crooked from the basketball hoop: *HOLIDAY TALENT EXTRAVAGANZA 1989!* The word *extravaganza* is underlined three times and glittered into oblivion.

The stage isn’t really a stage. Just a raised platform—barely a foot up—floored with plywood and masking tape marks. Folding chairs flank the edges like it’s a funeral for art. A plastic manger scene has been shoved off to stage left, half-covered in a blanket. Baby Jesus is upside down in a Tupperware bin marked *COSTUMES*.

**ENTER: the band.**

Unnamed. Unlisted. Uninvited, according to some committee members. They walk in like outlaws at the saloon—late, cold, pissed off, and carrying gear.

**KATHY** is holding her guitar case like a weapon.  **JEFF** has a milk crate full of pedals.  **BILL** has his synth under one arm and a bag of beef jerky in his mouth.  **JIM** is dragging the cracked cymbal in one hand like a goddamn executioner.  **GILLEY** is grinning with too much teeth.  **JEREMY** is pacing.  

**JEREMY**
I have an idea.

**JEFF**
I’m afraid not.

**JEREMY** (incensed)
I *have an idea.*

**BILL**
Martin Luther King had a dream. Look where that got him, dumb fuck.

**GILLEY**
Yeah, it ended up getting him into a YouTube video.

**KATHY**
There are better ways to end up memorialized than getting your head blown off in Memphis.

**JEREMY**
*Shut up shut up shut up shut up.*
Look, we’ll play “Regicide.” But give me two minutes—just *120 seconds*—to knock them dead. I’ll open solo. No vocals, no intro. Just me. Fill in behind me. Then hammer it. *Trust me.* Recontextualize what I do.

**(Beat. Jeff glances at his pedalboard like it just coughed.)**

**JEFF**
I don’t know if my flanger works properly.

**JIM**
Yeah, my cymbal? Somebody cracked it the other day. It’s gonna sound a little weird.

**KATHY**
It sounds a little weird *anyways.*

**(Gilley chuckles, low and wicked.)**

**GILLEY**
Let’s just rip.

**KATHY**
Fine.

**GILLEY**
Amen to that.

## **INT. BACKSTAGE – MOMENTS BEFORE SET – NIGHT**

They huddle in a corner behind a faux fireplace built from cardboard boxes. A choir of freshman girls is butchering “Carol of the Bells” onstage. Someone dropped a xylophone mallet. It echoes like regret.

**STAGEHAND (senior in a Santa hat)**
You guys’re up next. You’re not the, uh, *Magical Elf Harmony*, are you?

**JEFF**
No.

**STAGEHAND**
Cool. Just… don’t cuss, okay? Mr. Hutchinson’s mom is in the front row and she faints easy.

**JIM**
So do I.

**STAGEHAND**
Cool cool cool.

**(He leaves. A sleigh bell jingles somewhere in the distance like a death knell.)**

## **INT. STAGE – TECUMSEH HIGH – MOMENTS LATER**

The lights dim slightly, like they’re too tired to commit. The plastic poinsettias shiver in the HVAC current.

**ANNOUNCER (voice cracking, off-mic)**
Uh, next up… um… we have… “a musical performance.”

**(Polite clapping.)**

**GILLEY**
(to Jeremy)
You sure about this?

**JEREMY (grinning like he’s already dead)**
Not even a little.

**(He steps forward. Alone. Cable in hand. Guitar already plugged in. Amp humming.)**

**JEREMY (quiet, to himself)**
Two minutes. Fuck ‘em up.

**(And then—)**

He plays.

Not a song. A *wound*. A freakshow riff dragged through reverb and minor key distortion, built from the skeletons of *Bauhaus* and *Dead Kennedys* and the soundtrack to every fever dream he’s ever had. It howls. It *bleeds*. It climbs.

The crowd doesn’t know what to do. Middle schoolers gape. Moms clutch their programs. Someone giggles. Someone else gasps.

Thirty seconds in, Bill joins—ghost notes, ambient synth that sounds like a haunted church organ being strangled in a cave.
Then Jim, light on the snare, building tension with rimshots.
Then Jeff, all low end and threat.
Then Kathy.
Then Gilley—shouting into the void with a grin like a car crash.

**And suddenly—**

It’s *Regicide*.

But it’s *feral.*
It *roars*.
It *lurches*.
It *eats the room alive.*

**AND THEN—**

**BOOM.**

The amp shorts. The synth fuzzes out. Jim cracks the cracked cymbal clean in two.

**The power flickers. Half the tinsel detaches and *rains down* like silver blood.**

**A snowflake catches on Kathy’s tuning peg and just *spins*.**

The whole crowd is *frozen*.

Then—*

One person claps.
Then another.
Then a scream from the back: “Y’ALL FUCKING ROCKED.”

Then applause. *Real* applause.

**(Beat. The band blinks like they’ve just survived a crash.)**

**KATHY**
We just committed treason in front of Santa.

**GILLEY**
Felt kinda holy.

**BILL**
I think the Virgin Mary just flipped us off.

**JIM**
Worth it.

**JEFF**
Flanger still didn’t work.

**JEREMY** (smiling like a motherfucker)
But *I* did.

**(They stomp offstage trailing smoke, glitter, and the first goddamn taste of belief.)**

## **INT. FACULTY ROOM – TECUMSEH HIGH SCHOOL – POST-SHOW – NIGHT**

The *judging committee* sits in a loose triangle of stress: a card table, three folding chairs, and a half-eaten cookie tray. Coffee’s gone lukewarm. The air smells like peppermint disappointment and cheap aftershave.

**MS. POWERS** — mid-forties, tired but sharper than anyone expects — is leafing through crumpled program sheets with a Bic pen tucked behind her ear. Her glasses are halfway down her nose. She's the only one here who actually *cares*.

**COACH RENNER** — football guy. Crew cut. Windbreaker. Perpetually aggrieved. Crossed arms, thighs like tree trunks, chewing on a toothpick like it’s his last defense against an aneurysm.

**VICE PRINCIPAL DOHERTY** — balding, tweedy, clutching a clipboard like it might grant him authority. The kind of man who once called an assembly because of hallway PDA and used the phrase *inappropriate touching* three times.

**COACH RENNER**
I mean, we *can’t* give first place to those punks. They played, what, *two-thirds* of a song? And broke half the equipment doing it?

**MS. POWERS**
They *performed*. There's a difference.

**VP DOHERTY**
(to no one)
The program clearly stated a maximum of six minutes per act.

**MS. POWERS**
They came in at four, so unless we’re penalizing *efficiency*, I think they qualify.

**COACH RENNER**
They were *loud.* My wife said her seat was vibrating.

**MS. POWERS**
That’s called “bass,” Coach. It’s a musical element. Look it up.

**VP DOHERTY**
(half-heartedly)
They were also… aggressive. One of them screamed “burn the crown” in front of Mrs. Hutchinson. She looked *quite alarmed.*

**MS. POWERS**
That’s Jeremy. He’s an Aries.

**COACH RENNER**
And what about Bill? I still say he could’ve made all-county as a fullback. Or linebacker. Hell, we needed a kicker. Kid had a center of gravity like a beer keg.

**MS. POWERS**
He liked marching band.

**COACH RENNER**
Marching band doesn’t win games.

**MS. POWERS**
No, but it taught him how to keep time, which is more than I can say for your offensive line.

**(Beat. The coach glares. She doesn’t blink.)**

**VP DOHERTY**
Well… they weren’t exactly festive. I mean, it *was* a holiday concert. Where were the sleigh bells?

**MS. POWERS**
They brought their own snowstorm—*in the form of tinsel debris, feedback, and raw existentialism.* That’s Christmas if you ask me.

**COACH RENNER**
They didn’t even *have a name.*

**MS. POWERS**
That’s because they *haven’t earned one yet.* That’s what makes it exciting.

**(Beat. Powers leans forward, glasses finally sliding off her nose.)**

**MS. POWERS**
Look. Gilley, Bill, and Jeff? All from the gifted program. Honor grads. Kathy? Did a senior thesis on the parallels between Ovid and Joy Division. Jim’s practically feral but he drums like he’s trying to talk to God with his feet.

They’re *good*. And worse, they’re *real*.

This district has spent twenty years trying to prove our academic acceleration track actually works. Those six just gave us proof.

**(Silence. Coach shifts in his chair. The vice principal sighs.)**

**COACH RENNER**
…Ten bucks is all they get?

**MS. POWERS**
Ten bucks and a paper certificate.

**VP DOHERTY**
I’ll print it out. Add clip art.

**COACH RENNER**
(grumbling)
Fine. But I still think “Deck the Halls” with ukulele solos deserved honorable mention.

**MS. POWERS**
Only if the halls are on fire.

**(They all nod, resigned. Ten dollars. First prize. Done.)**

## **INT. HALLWAY OUTSIDE – MOMENTS LATER**

Ms. Powers slips out of the room with the ten-dollar bill in one hand and a certificate that says *“1ST PLACE – HOLIDAY TALENT EXTRAVAGANZA”* in Comic Sans.

She pauses at the drinking fountain. Listens. Faintly, from the parking lot, she can hear the *clatter* of laughter, the *clang* of gear being dumped in the trunk of someone’s dad’s car, and *Jeremy shouting “RECONTEXTUALIZE THIS, YOU SONS OF BITCHES!”* into the cold night air.

Ms. Powers smiles.

Then mutters:

**MS. POWERS**
Go burn the fucking crown, kids.

## **INT. TECUMSEH HIGH SCHOOL – CAFETERIA-STAGE HYBRID – 8:45 P.M. – DECEMBER 14, 1989**

The *awards ceremony* is held where the drama program stores folding chairs and dignity. The stage is still covered in glitter fallout and one upside-down wise man from the nativity tableau. A dying poinsettia sits center-stage like an omen. The winners’ names are being read off a *handwritten list* on looseleaf notebook paper.

The crowd consists of twelve parents, a pair of unclaimed siblings, and Coach Renner picking at a Rice Krispies treat. Ms. Powers steps up to the mic, holding a five, three ones, and a crumpled one-dollar bill folded like a tiny hat. The certificate is already bent. The ink is smudged.

**MS. POWERS**
And now… First Place in the 1989 Holiday Talent Extravaganza goes to…

**(a pause, as if there’s suspense. There isn’t.)**

…the unnamed band composed of Jeffrey Evans, James Brown, Katherine Espich, Jeremy Evans, William Davenport, and Jeremy “Gilley” Gilley.

**Polite claps. Someone sneezes. A child drops a thermos.  The six trudge up like suspects in a lineup.**

**MS. POWERS** (handing Gilley the prize)
Congratulations. Here’s ten dollars. Cash.

**GILLEY** (weighing the bill in his hand, nodding with ironic reverence)
Now hey…

(patting his jeans)

I got two in my pocket, and ten here…That means I got twelve left.  And I’m gone.

**(He spins on his heel and walks off stage without waiting for applause.)**

**(Beat. The others stand awkwardly in his wake like confused ducks.)**

**JEFF** (deadpan, staring into the void)
I see no point to be here anymore.

**(He turns to leave.)**

**JIM** (calling after him)
Say thank you, you dumb *shit*!

**MS. POWERS** (sharply)
Language.

**JIM** (recalculating mid-air)
Say thank you, you dumb *bully*.

(beat)

Sorry about the profanity, Ms. Powers.

**(She gives him a long, squint-eyed look. Not approval. Not disapproval. Just “I’ve taught middle school for fifteen years and nothing surprises me anymore.”)**

**MS. POWERS**
I see what you did there.

**(Kathy shrugs. Bill pockets a cookie. Jeremy does finger-guns in the air like he’s accepting an Oscar.)**

**OFFSTAGE – GILLEY (yelling back through the cafeteria doors)**
Meet me at the motherfucking Taco Bell!

## **INT. TECUMSEH HIGH SCHOOL PARKING LOT – MOMENTS LATER**

Snow flurries begin. The sky hangs low and orange from the streetlights. Tinsel clings to Jeremy’s boot like a cursed bookmark. Their silhouettes fan out across the pavement like a band of exhausted pirates escaping a sinking Christmas ship.

Six kids. No name. No plan. Twelve bucks. And an open Taco Bell.

It’s the beginning of *everything*.
