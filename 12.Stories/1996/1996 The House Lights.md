## **INT. GRAMMY AWARDS – BACKSTAGE HALLWAY – NIGHT (EARLY 1996)**

Bill Davenport stands half in shadow, tie already loosened, clutching a glass of something he isn’t drinking. He’s wired, jittering from the ceremony, all the industry noise rattling in his skull. Down the hall comes **Eddie Van Halen**, in tuxedo pants but Converse sneakers, hair wild, a man who looks like he just ducked out of a garage rehearsal instead of walking off the broadcast stage.

They collide at the craft services table, two scavengers eyeing the last shrimp cocktail.

**EDDIE** (grinning, voice gravelly)
You’re Bill, right? Commuted.

**BILL** (a little starstruck, nodding)
Yeah. Uh…guilty.

Eddie leans in close, conspiratorial.

**EDDIE**
I gotta tell you. Love the energy, love the band. But…why the hell you walking onstage to *Jump* every night?

Bill blinks, caught off guard.

**BILL**
It works. Crowd knows it. Big hook, big lights—

**EDDIE** (cuts him off, playful but pointed)
Yeah, yeah, I know. It works. That’s the problem. It’s my firework show, not yours. You got a whole catalog of your own. Light the fuse with your stuff, kid. Or you’re just…what, a cover band with better shoes?

There’s no malice, just that big-brother grin. Eddie claps Bill on the shoulder, shrimp cocktail in hand, and drifts off down the hallway, whistling his own riff.

Bill stands there, stunned, chewing on the words like an electrical current. For once, he feels seen, and called out.

## **INT. ARENA – NIGHT – LATE  SPRING 1996**

House lights go dark. The old script says *Jump* should come blasting through the P.A., neon and fun, but tonight it’s silence—just the hum of a seventeen thousand restless bodies.

Then it starts.

A low synth drone. Industrial, metallic. Bill’s fingers move in the dark like a machinist cranking a lathe. Layer by layer, guitars slip in—not riffs, not yet, but dissonant harmonics, swelling like sirens in a concrete canyon.

Jim’s kick drum lands next: steady, deliberate, like boots on asphalt. Jeff’s bass crawls in underneath, melodic but uneasy, like a heartbeat fighting to keep time. Jeremy’s guitar is the first to break free—bright chords slashing through the murk, hopeful but jagged, like light cutting through smoke.

The crowd roars—not because they know the tune, but because they know something *different* is happening.

Behind the set wall, the band huddles one last time. They’re hearing their own future announce itself in real time.

Bill glances up, half-grin, remembering Eddie’s words: *“Light the fuse with your stuff, kid.”*

## **INT. BACKSTAGE – ARENA – NIGHT**

The house is black. The crowd is a low thunder rolling above their heads. Normally this is when Jim steps into the circle, eyes blazing, preacher’s fire in his chest. But tonight? Bill raises his hand.

**BILL**
No. I’m taking this one.

The others blink. Jeff smirks like he’s misheard. Jeremy laughs nervously. Even Jim just stares, baffled. But Bill’s already locked in, veins bright with adrenaline.

**BILL** (voice low, measured at first)
We spent five years borrowing another man’s lightning bolt. Eight years letting somebody else’s riff drag us out of the dark. And Eddie looked me in the eye and asked *why not your own fire?*

He scans them, daring anyone to look away.

**BILL** (louder now, words clipped, Stoic steel edged with fury)
This? This noise out there? It’s not a party. It’s a clash of arms. Every time we walk onstage, the world is asking us if we still matter. If we can still burn.

He pounds his fist into his open palm.

**BILL**
The answer is yes. We light our own fuse tonight. Not Eddie’s. Not anyone’s. Ours. *Now.*

His voice breaks—not from weakness, but sheer voltage.

**BILL** (almost shouting)
We walk out there, and we *remind them*—we don’t play to be liked. We don’t play to be safe. We play because this—
(he thumps his chest hard)
—*this* is the only thing that keeps us ***alive***!

The band is frozen a beat too long, processing. Then Jeff lets out a bark of laughter. Jeremy slams his fist against the wall. Gilley’s eyes are wide. Even Jim, robbed of his preacher’s pulpit, is nodding slowly, thumping his sticks together like a drumroll.

**JIM** (shouting)
Fuckin-A.  On four. 1-2-3-4!

**ALL** (bellowing)
**COMMITTED!**

The circle breaks in a roar.

## **BACKSTAGE – SAME MOMENT**

The roar of the crowd above them is peaking, waiting for the fuse to catch. Bill’s speech hangs in the air, raw and shocking. The others are still reeling—until **Gilley** lets out a loud, swaggering laugh that cuts through the tension.

**GILLEY**
Hell yes, Davenport! That’s what I’m talking about. Took you long enough to grow fangs.

He slaps Bill on the back hard enough to almost knock him forward, grinning like a man who’s just been handed the keys to a stolen car.

**GILLEY** (to the others, swaggering)
You hear that? That’s our boy right there. Stoic lil' Bill, running hot. Means tonight’s gonna be *filthy.*

Jeremy hoots. Jeff grins despite himself. Even Jim, stick-tapping, gives a little nod. The circle is buzzing now, but it’s not the preacher’s fire, it’s a dangerous, sharp-edged voltage, and Gilley loves every watt of it.

**GILLEY** (leaning close to Bill, mock-serious)
Careful, man. You keep talking like that, they’ll start mistaking you for a frontman.

Bill doesn’t reply.  He just exhales sharp through his nose, eyes fixed on the stage door. But there’s a glint in him the others haven’t seen before.

The stage manager throws the signal. The house drops to blackout. The band surges forward. And this time, when the first mood piece hits, it’s not just music,  it’s the echo of Bill’s words, vibrating in every amp.

The guitars crescendo, synths screaming, crowd on edge and then, without warning, the lights slam on and the band bursts onto the stage, already in motion, already inside the storm.

From that night forward, they never used *Jump* a
