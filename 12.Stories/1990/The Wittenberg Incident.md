## **THE WITTENBERG INCIDENT**

**SPRING 1991**  
**EXT. WITTENBERG UNIVERSITY – CLARK COUNTY, OHIO – 1:12 A.M. – DAMP NIGHT**

The Volvo hisses as it cools. They’re parked under a floodlight that buzzes like it resents being awake. Around them: manicured campus quiet, lecture halls asleep, and chapel spires stabbing moonlight through a gauze of early mist.

She’s curled up in the passenger seat, legs bare and tangled in a concert hoodie he keeps in the trunk. Gilley is smoking with the windows cracked and the stereo off. No music tonight. Just the sound of insects resurrecting themselves for spring.

But Stacey, breathing softly beside him, is the kind of girl who will *always* know his favorite verse — the one that sounds like blasphemy when you moan it.
