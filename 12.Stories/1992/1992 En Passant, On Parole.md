She wonders if this is a new high for Jeff—turning sex into art and making her the vessel for both—or a new low for her, scraping together a performance from another woman’s body and his fevered genius.

Kathy exhales, flicks ash into a paper cup, and tries again—muttering the chorus under her breath, half to herself, half to the woman in the photograph whose body is now a map for her own voice.

## **INT. VANDALIA WAREHOUSE – CORNER BY THE COFFEE POT – MIDMORNING**

Kathy corners Jeff by the battered coffee maker, her voice pitched low and tight, every word laced with fatigue and hard-won authority.

**KATHY**  
I can’t work with this chaos, Jeff. You handed me a pack of Polaroids like I’m supposed to reconstruct the Rosetta Stone from a centerfold.  

(Shaking her head, frustrated.)  
If you were thinking straight, you’d have copied the lyrics to paper. Given me a clean sheet. Cut out the—  

(gestures, exasperated)  
—the whole naked Polaroid circus. In hindsight, you gotta admit it’s a little…immoderate.

Jeff rubs the back of his neck, sheepish, honest for once.

**JEFF**  
You’re not wrong. I should’ve written them down clean. But when you’re in the moment, you just…you don’t see the mess until after.  If I could do it over, I’d have saved everyone the awkward slideshow.

Kathy holds up a Polaroid, thumb over Ha-yoon’s nipple, eyes narrow.

**KATHY**  
It’s not just messy. Sharing these—  

(voice softens, honest, almost hurt)  
—this feels like it violates Ha-yoon, doesn’t it?

Jeff shakes his head, steady.

**JEFF**  
Not really, no. I asked her. Flat out. “Can I photograph you? Can I share this work?” She said yes. She knew the deal. Hell, Bill was in the same room with her at one point—Koreans have different boundaries sometimes, and yeah, she grew up in Riverside, near the Korean Baptist church, so she’s not exactly an exhibitionist, but she’s not precious either.

Kathy snorts, rolling her eyes.

**KATHY**  
That doesn’t exactly help. She’s Baptist?  We already got one Baptist and Cremeans is two steps from bonkers.

Jeff laughs, genuinely, shaking his head.

**JEFF**  
You oughta sit down with Cremeans, Kath.  Seriously. Not a band meeting, not a strategy call. Just you and him, head to head. He’s not half as crazy as you think, and I trust him. Implicitly.

Kathy crosses her arms, uncertain, not quite meeting his eyes.

**KATHY**  
You’re asking as a favor?

**JEFF**  
Yeah. For me, Kath.  Sit with him. Really listen.  If you come out of it thinking he’s a lost cause, I’ll drop it. But I bet you won’t.

She stares at him for a long moment, then pockets the lyrics, the Polaroids, the question still burning in her chest.

**KATHY (quiet, grudging)**  
I’ll think about it.

Jeff nods, respect and relief mingling on his face.

**JEFF**  
That’s all I’m asking.

The band begins tuning up behind them, new song forming in the background—a little more order in the chaos, maybe, if only for a few bars.

## **INT. VANDALIA WAREHOUSE – LOBBY AREA – POST-REHEARSAL, MIDDAY**

Jeff is slinging his gear into a battered duffel, the Polaroids tucked safely out of sight. Kathy’s rubbing her temples, spent but still humming with the leftover static of rehearsal. The sun is high, baking the empty parking lot in harsh white stripes.

Jeff tosses his bag over his shoulder, keys jangling.

**JEFF**  
I’m heading up Dixie Highway, crashing at my place in Tipp City.  You’re welcome to come with—guest bedroom’s clean, probably quieter and safer than the drive all the way out to your place.  

No drama, no noise, just you and whatever’s on cable.  Kathy gives a tired half-smile, flopping back into the battered recliner by the door.

**KATHY**  
I think I’ll pass, Jeff. I need to crash right here. This old chair’s got my back better than most people.

She watches him for a moment, eyes narrowed, searching his face.

**KATHY (cont’d)**  
Why are you being so nice to me, anyway?  You don’t usually volunteer a bed unless there’s something in it for you.

Jeff pauses, looks genuinely wounded—then amused, then something else, unreadable.

**JEFF**  
I’m nice to you all the time.  You just never see it unless you want something, or if I’m doing exactly what you told me to do. Your perspective’s flawed, Kath.

She shrugs, unapologetic.

**KATHY**  
It’s the only one I’ve got. I can’t swap out my brain for yours, much as you’d love it.

Jeff grins, rolling his eyes.

**JEFF**  
My mom would always lecture us about walking a mile in someone else’s shoes.  I never understood that.  Hell, I’ve got size thirteen feet—no one in this band could fill ‘em.

Kathy snorts, shaking her head.

**KATHY**  
Every time I think I hear you take a step forward, Jeff, you hop backwards immediately after.

He gives her a little salute, lingering just long enough that the silence is honest—not awkward.

**JEFF**  
You ever need a new pair of boots, you know where to find me.

He steps out into the glare, leaving the door swinging behind him, and Kathy, still stubborn, settles deeper into the chair, watching the dust motes dance. The ache is old, the comfort familiar. The band is a mess, but it’s *their* mess.
