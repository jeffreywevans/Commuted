**🎤 Rolling Stone | May 2025**

**\*“She Died, They Burned, We Listened:”**

The Album That Shattered Commuted—And Changed Everything\*

**By Lena Jerichow**

---

*"They didn’t write a tribute. They wrote a suicide pact they somehow survived."*  
—Markus Velle, co-producer on *Be First Be Smarter or Cheat*

It’s been twelve years since Kathy Espich’s heart stopped in a Boston ICU. Seven minutes. No DNR. A nurse collapsed. A chaplain saw something that drove him from the priesthood. And somewhere in Ohio, four men who called her bandmate, lover, rival, sister, enemy, and salvation wrote an album that still makes audiences cry, scream, and walk out halfway through.

*Be First Be Smarter or Cheat* isn’t a record. It’s a **funeral pyre** set to orchestral metal, artillery samples, and unrelenting grief. And the band that made it—Commuted—**has never recovered**.

---

**“No one was okay.”**

The album’s now-legendary ninth track, “What They Say About Payback (Batt A, 1/134 FA),” includes a real field sample of 155mm howitzer fire. The band never explained why. They never had to.

*“That track is about rage. About impotence. About screaming at a god who sends you voicemail,”* says one former engineer, who asked not to be named. *“We didn’t mix that song. We survived it.”*

Each track is a **document of collapse**: “Terrified” rewires liturgy into dread; “Time of Death” mirrors an EKG readout in its meter; “Thirty-six Degrees Fahrenheit” is as cold and slow as the autopsy it implies.

They never toured it. They never played a single song from it again. They refuse to even mention it by name in interviews.

*“That album didn’t save us,”* bassist [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff Evans]] muttered on a now-pulled podcast appearance. *“It preserved the wreck.”*

---

**Postmortem Silence**

In interviews, surviving members admit they still speak to Kathy—*in songs*, in *ritual*, in the **ritual of never talking about her**.

*“We buried a piece of that record in every show since,”* said one roadie. *“But they don’t want the fans to grieve with them. They want to grieve around them.”*

Their recent reunion tour *omitted* every track from the 2013 album. When asked, the band’s rep responded with a single sentence:

*“That material is off-limits. Period.”*

**Legacy, Without Permission**

Today, *Be First Be Smarter or Cheat* is a required listen in several music theory and trauma studies programs. It's hailed as the **Requiem of the Post-Punk Age**. And it's the only album in the last 25 years that **no one who made it will discuss**.

When I reached out to Island Records for comment on this article, I received an email from Commuted’s partner.

It was blank. Except for the subject line:*“This is why we don’t talk.”*

And they won’t.  But the music already did.

When I reached out to Commuted to comment on this article, I received an email from their management. It contained no message. Just a subject line:

**“You. Will. Sleep. With. The. Gosh. Darn. Fishes.”**  
—xJLC

They don’t forget.  And they don’t forgive. But the music remembers everything.
