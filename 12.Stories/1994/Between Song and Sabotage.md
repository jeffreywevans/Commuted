## **EXT. BACK PARKING LOT – TOWER RECORDS – CLEVELAND, OHIO – FALL 1994 – 10:37 P.M.**

It started in that bastard strip of mercury vapor night between the tour van and the loading dock, where half the road crew was still arguing about who packed the wrong DI box and someone else had opened a warm bottle of Cutty Sark with a cymbal key. There was asphalt beneath her Doc Martens, but Kathy didn’t walk. She prowled. Between set and after-party. Between song and sabotage.

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy]] (V.O.)**
They think I’m a songbird in combat boots. But I’m just a girl who learned to scream louder than her body. And tonight? I want blood. Or applause. Or both.

He was leaning against the dumpster. **Adam**. Local scene guy, part-time poet, full-time misfire. The kind with ink on his knuckles and a philosophy minor that hadn’t paid off. She spotted him during soundcheck mouthing the second verse to “Last Call” and biting his lip like it meant something.

Now he looked like he wanted to confess or combust.

**KATHY**
He’s mine.

It wasn’t a flirtation. It was possession. She said it loud, unafraid of echoes. Someone near the door howled approval. Another clapped like he’d just won a bet. Gilley, already halfway through his third cigarette, just raised an eyebrow and leaned against the van like he was watching a sacrament.

**KATHY (V.O.)**
I don’t need the band to back me tonight. I need them to *watch*. I want witnesses.

Adam came toward her like gravity wasn’t a law but a dare. He grabbed her shirt in both hands. Not yanking, not ripping — just lifting. A ceremony. One cotton inch at a time until it cleared her head and arms and left her bare-chested in the Cleveland air. No stage lights. Just the cool, smug fingers of an October breeze brushing her nipples to attention.

She didn’t shiver because she was cold. She shivered because every eye was *still there*. Not leaving. Not saving her. Not looking away.

**KATHY (V.O.)**
I’m not ashamed. I’m *present*. I’m live. You get that, Adam?

He dropped her shirt to the ground like it was evidence and kissed her with the messy awe of someone who’d just been upgraded from fan to co-conspirator. His tongue tasted like cinnamon gum and insecurity. His fingers trembled like he thought this might be his last chance at godhood.

The crowd tightened, but stayed silent now. Like they’d wandered into church by accident.

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]] (O.S.)** (to no one, or maybe everyone)
If she makes him cry, I’m buying her breakfast.

She pushed Adam back against the concrete wall and whispered something nobody could hear — but everyone could feel. His knees buckled. Her belt came undone with a whisper and a snap. His did too. Her boot scuffed the metal of a milk crate and that sound, that *clang*, was the only percussion they needed.

**KATHY (V.O.)**
Let them see. Let them know. Let them fucking *understand*. I’m not the girl in the back row. I’m the chorus you remember after the roof caves in.

And then — she gripped the waistband of his jeans, leaned forward, and turned the act into a goddamn aria. No hesitation. No shame. Just motion. Just power. A show in a shadow between light and rumor. And when he gasped — when he gave in — she stood up, grabbed her shirt off the pavement, and wiped her mouth with the hem.

Then tossed it in the trash.

**KATHY**
You’re welcome, Cleveland.

And walked back to the van bare-chested, grinning, unbothered, immortal.

## **INT. WCNX RADIO STATION – CLEVELAND – THE NEXT MORNING – 6:02 A.M.**

Crackling neon. Burnt coffee. A producer with a ponytail and a hangover tries to cue up *Sixty Nine Reasons* for the third time but keeps skipping to Track 4. Static hiccups through the monitors.

The phone lines? Lit.

Local legend **“Imperturbable Moe in the Morning”**, a man built like a truck stop sausage with a headset mic and a framed Limp Bizkit promo shot on the wall, is already sweating through his faux-leather chair.

**IMPERTURBABLE MOE** (laughing)
So. If you were *at Tower Records* last night, you already know what I’m talking about. But if you weren’t? Oh baby — do I have a story for you.

Calls flood in. One guy swears he saw Kathy pull a condom from her boot. A woman says her cousin took a Polaroid — and wants $40 for it. Another caller just screams, “She *blessed* us!”

And **Cremeans**? Oh, he caught her. Not live. But on the replay.

## **INT. HOTEL ROOM – HOLIDAY INN – CLEVELAND – LATER THAT DAY**

Cremeans’ laptop is open. Kathy, still damp-haired from the world’s fastest shower, towel around her neck, peeks in and sees the paused video thumbnail. Crowd. Wall. Shirtless. Her.

**[[5.2. JEFF CREMEANS MODERN REVIEW|CREMEANS]]** (calmly, like a lawyer before sentencing)
So.

**KATHY** (casual, peeling a banana)
So?

**CREMEANS**
Cleveland PD says they’re “monitoring the situation.” Tower Records says we’re banned for life. And Imperturbable Moe has offered you a job.

**KATHY**
Was I good?

**CREMEANS**
Jesus *Christ*, Kath.

**KATHY**
I mean it. Did I *look* good? It matters. If you're gonna go semi-viral for a parking lot blowjob, you want your angles right.

Cremeans pinches his eyes. Breathes in Mountain Dew. Doesn't scream.

**CREMEANS**
Do you know how hard I’ve worked to keep your image dangerous but *legally* defensible?

**KATHY**
Then call it performance art. Or call it merch. Sell shirts that say "HE'S MINE."

## **INT. KENDALL’S OFFICE – D.C. – TWO DAYS LATER**

I saw it on a grainy VHS the morning it hit the distributor. I paused the tape. Rewound it. Paused again.

I’d already written half an essay before she zipped his pants.

**[[5.5 Kendall Weatherby|KENDALL]] (V.O.)**
She wasn’t trying to shock. She was trying to claim. Not just him. *Us.* The audience. The cameras. The critics. The chorus.

I didn’t catch her. I *witnessed* her. Big difference.

## **INT. TOUR VAN – HEADING WEST – 3 DAYS LATER**

**GILLEY**, finally, broke the silence.

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]**
You know you’re gonna be on a thousand bootlegs by next month.

**KATHY**
Cool. I want the one with the crowd mic turned up.

**GILLEY**
You should’ve warned me. I’d have joined in.

She turned to him. That look — hot knife in soft butter.

**KATHY**
Next time, bring your own wall.

Did she get caught?  Yes.  By **rumor**, **replay**, **radio**, **retrospect**.  But punished?  Never.  She walked through fire and sold the ashes as souvenirs.

## **INT. CREMEANS’ OFFICE – DAYTON, OHIO – NOVEMBER 1994 – TWO DAYS AFTER THE INCIDENT**

The blinds are drawn. The inbox is full. The phone won’t stop.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
They think I’m a warden. A glorified babysitter in khakis. But someone’s gotta keep the wolves from eating each other. And someone’s gotta file the bleedin' taxes.

The tower of paperwork includes:
- A cease-and-desist from Tower Records corporate.
- A note from Legal about Kathy’s “involuntary exposure.”
- A fan letter in glitter pen addressed: “To the Queen of Cleveland Blowjobs.”

He hasn't opened that one yet. He leans back. Cracks his knuckles. Eyes the small devotional calendar on his desk.

**Psalm 46:1 – God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble.**

He sighs.

**CREMEANS**
More like Ruth the intern.

Just then, the phone rings *again*. He answers it without checking the ID.

**CREMEANS**
Cremeans.

**BILL (O.S.)**
She’s asking if we can sell the video.

**CREMEANS**
Sell the—Jesus. No. Tell her to eat a sandwich and hydrate. *And* to wear a shirt in front of press, please. For once.

**BILL (O.S.)**
…So no merch, then?

Cremeans hangs up. He looks at his hands. They’re shaking slightly. He’s not mad.

He’s just so *tired*.

## **INT. BUSY DINER – LATER THAT NIGHT**

Cremeans eats alone. Meatloaf. Unsweet tea. A small Bible open to Romans beside the napkin dispenser. The waitress brings pie. He didn’t order pie.

She winks.

**WAITRESS**
It’s from the girl in the booth over. Said you looked… overworked.

He turns. The booth is empty.  A napkin folded on the seat reads: *“Thanks for babysitting the monsters. <3 —K”*

He stares at it.  Then smiles. Small. Sad. Sincere.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Even hell has HR. And sometimes? It brings you pie.
