# **KATHY QUITS THE GUITAR**

---

# **CHAPTER ONE: PONDERING THE TRUTH**

---

## **LIVING ROOM, DEMME RESIDENCE, BETHEL TOWNSHIP, CLARK COUNTY, OHIO, CHRISTMAS NIGHT, 1994**

The living room is warm, lit by the glow of the Christmas tree and the flickering television screen. **JO ANN DEMME** sits on the couch, knitting. **KATHY ESPICH** sprawls in an armchair, picking at the frayed edges of the cushion. Annie Lennox is performing on a music channel.

**JO ANN:** (Pointing her knitting needle at the TV) 
Now, ain’t that the woman from that video? The one with the… what was it…“*Sweet Dreams*”? The one with the, uh…the shaved head?

Kath rolls her eyes, a slow, deliberate movement.

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|KATHY]]:** 
Mom, it’s *Annie Lennox*. And yes, she *was* in Eurythmics, and yes, “*Sweet Dreams Are Made of This*” is one of their biggest hits. But this is her *solo* stuff. Completely different vibe.

**JO ANN:** (Shrugs)
Well, they all sound the same to me. Loud.

**KATH:**  (Scoffs) 
Oh, come *on*. This is… soulful. This is *powerful*. This is… 

(She trails off, searching for the right word) 
This is *perfect*.

**JO ANN:** (Sets down her knitting, giving Kath a concerned look) 
You seem…agitated, honey. More than usual.

**KATH:** (Sighs, running a hand through her hair) 
Mom, it’s… it’s the band. It’s… everything.

**JO ANN:**  
The band? What about the band? Weren’t you just on tour? Seemed like you were having a grand ol’ time, jet-setting all over the damn world.

**KATH:** (A bitter laugh escapes her lips) 
“Grand ol’ time.” Right. It’s…Look, we put out this new record, right? “*Shall I Beg?*” Critics loved it, gave us all these glowing reviews. But nobody *bought* it. Nobody gives a flying fuck.

**JO ANN:** (Frowning) 
Kathy, language.

**KATH:** (Waves a dismissive hand) 
Sorry. But it’s true! It’s like…we poured our hearts and souls into this thing, and it just… flopped. Well, not *flopped* exactly, but it didn’t do *shit* compared to “*69 Reasons*.”

**JO ANN:**  
Well, honey, not every album can be a…a…

**KATH:** (Interrupting) 
A goddamn multi-platinum-selling phenomenon? I know, Mom. I *know*. But it’s more than that. It’s…I feel like I’m just… fading into the background.

**JO ANN:**  
What do you mean? You’re the only girl in the band! You’re…you’re Kathy Espich!

**KATH:** (Her voice drops to a near whisper) 
But am I, Mom? *Really*? Jeremy’s shredding like a motherfucker. Jeff’s right behind him, getting better every damn day. And Gilley? That son of a bitch could stand there and fart into a microphone and the crowd would go wild. And me? I'm just…*there*.

**JO ANN:** (Reaches out and takes Kath's hand) 
Honey, you’re talented. You know that.

**KATH:** (Pulls her hand away, pacing the room) 
Talent ain’t worth a damn dime if nobody notices it! I’m playing my heart out, but it’s like… it’s like nobody even sees me. I’m just another guitar player in a band full of guitar players. One’s a virtuoso, one’s got the looks, and one’s got the goddamn *it* factor and I'm just…fucking *there*.

**JO ANN:** (Her voice firm) 
Kathy.

**KATH:**  (Stops pacing, looking at her mother with a mix of anger and despair) 
I’m telling you, Mom. I’m bottling this shit up inside, and it’s about to explode.

Kathy takes a deep breath, recognizing the warning in her mother’s tone. She knows when to push and when to pull back. This is a pull back moment, but she's still going to tell her mother what's what.

**KATHY:**
Okay, okay. Sorry. But it’s… it’s like being a woman in rock, Mom. It’s a goddamn…a *hell* of a thing. You’re always fighting for attention. You gotta be louder, faster, sexier, *something* to even get noticed. And even then…

She pauses, a flicker of genuine sadness crossing her face.

**KATH:**  
Even then, you’re still just…the girl. The chick guitarist. The token female. And the worst part? The absolute *worst* part? In this case, they’re ignoring me for the *right* reasons.

**JO ANN:** (Knitting needles clicking softly) 
What do you mean by that?

**KATH:** (Sits back down heavily in the armchair) 
I mean…Jeremy’s a goddamn *virtuoso*. He’s got this…this raw talent, this…this *fire* in his playing. He can make that guitar sing, scream, whisper…do just about anything he wants. And [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]? He’s right on his heels. He’s been practicing his ass off, and he’s getting damn good, damn fast.

**JO ANN:**  
And you’re not?

**KATH:** (Shakes her head slowly)
I’m…I’m *good*. I’m a solid rhythm guitarist. I can hold my own. But I’m not…I’m not *special*. I’m not… 
(She struggles for the right word again, then spits it out with a mix of resignation and anger)
I’m *average*.

**JO ANN:** (Stops knitting, looking intently at her daughter)
Average? Kathy, that’s…that’s ridiculous. You’re far from average.

**KATH:** (A harsh laugh escapes her)
No, Mom. It’s the truth. And it’s not even jealousy, not exactly. It’s… it’s frustration. It’s knowing that I’m putting in the work, but I’m just not…I’m not cutting through. I’m getting drowned out by Jeremy’s flash, Jeff’s energy, and Gilley’s…well, Gilley’s just Gilley. The guy could read the phone book on stage and get a standing ovation.

**JO ANN:**  (A thoughtful pause) 
So, what are you going to do about it?

**KATH:** (Looks at the TV, where Annie Lennox is now seated at a piano, her voice filling the room) 
I don't know yet. But I'm thinking…maybe it's time for a change. A big one.

---

## **LIVING ROOM, DEMME RESIDENCE, BETHEL TOWNSHIP, CLARK COUNTY, OHIO, CHRISTMAS NIGHT, 1994**

**KATH:** (Looks back at the television, where Annie Lennox is now seated at a piano, her voice filling the room)
Look at her, Mom. Just…look.

**JO ANN:** (Turns her attention to the TV, a slight smile on her face)
She’s got a powerful voice, that’s for sure.

**KATH:**  
It’s more than that. It’s…she’s not hiding anything. She’s not trying to be something she’s not. She’s just…*being*. And she’s commanding the whole damn room doing it. Sitting at a piano no less.

**JO ANN:** (Raises an eyebrow)
You seem to be enjoying this quite a bit.

**KATH:** (A small, genuine smile touches her lips)
I am. I really am. It’s…inspiring. It’s like… she’s showing me that there’s other ways to do this. Other ways to… to make your voice heard.

**JO ANN:** (A warm look in her eyes)
You mean the show? Or…us?

**KATH:** (Looks at her mother, her smile widening slightly)
Little bit of both, I guess. It’s…I miss this, Mom. I miss being here. Being…normal.

**JO ANN:** (Reaches over and gently squeezes Kath’s hand) 
I miss you too, honey. It gets awfully quiet around here when you’re gone.

**KATH:** (Looks back at the TV, her expression thoughtful)
It’s… it’s weird. Being on the road, it’s like you’re living in this bubble. This crazy, chaotic, loud bubble. And then you come back here, and it’s like… 

(She snaps her fingers)
…nothing. Just…quiet. And it makes you think. It makes you…re-evaluate things.

**JO ANN:** (Nods slowly)
I can imagine.

**KATH:** (Her voice softens)
Like…maybe I don’t have to be the loudest guitar player in the loudest band to make a difference. Maybe…maybe I can find my own way. My own…voice.

She glances back at the television, her eyes fixed on Annie Lennox at the piano. The camera zooms in on Lennox's hands as they move across the keys, creating a cascade of notes.

**KATH:** (Almost to herself)
Maybe…maybe it’s time for a change of instruments.

---

## **KITCHEN, DEMME RESIDENCE, BETHEL TOWNSHIP, CLARK COUNTY, OHIO, CHRISTMAS NIGHT, 1994**

**JO ANN:** (Looks at Kath, a mischievous glint in her eye) 
You know what? I think we need a break from all this…deep thinking. How about we press pause on Annie and bake a batch of Christmas cookies?

**KATH:** (A surprised but genuine smile spreads across her face) 
Cookies? You mean…like, real cookies? With frosting and sprinkles and all that?

**JO ANN:** (Chuckles)
The very same. What do you say?

**KATH:** (Gets up from the armchair, stretching)
I say…hell yeah.

Jo Ann gets up and heads to the kitchen, Kath following close behind. Jo Ann pulls out bowls, measuring cups, flour, sugar, and all the other necessary ingredients.

**JO ANN:** (As she starts measuring ingredients)
So, you said something about finding your own…voice. You mean with the…what was it… the piano?

**KATH:** (Leans against the counter, watching her mother)
Yeah. I was thinking…

**JO ANN:**  
But…honey, you don’t even *play* the piano.

**KATH:** (Shrugs)
I know. Not really.

**JO ANN:**  
Well, what exactly *do* you play besides that electric guitar of yours?

**KATH:** (A slight smile plays on her lips) 
Pretty much just the guitar. I mean, I messed around with a few other things when I was a kid – recorder, a little bit of drums – but nothing stuck. The guitar…that was it.

**JO ANN:**  
How’d you even learn? You never took lessons, did you?

**KATH:** (Starts helping her mother by cracking eggs into a bowl) 
Nope. Self-taught. Picked it up, figured it out. Started with some old blues riffs, then got into punk, then… well, you know the rest.

**JO ANN:** (Stirs the batter, a thoughtful expression on her face) 
So, you’re just going to…pick up the piano the same way?

**KATH:** (Starts carefully placing cookie dough onto baking sheets) 
I don’t know. Maybe. Maybe I’ll take some lessons. Maybe I’ll just…mess around with it until it clicks. But I gotta try something different. I gotta do *something*.

They work together in comfortable silence for a while, the rhythmic clinking of bowls and utensils filling the kitchen. The smell of baking cookies begins to permeate the air.

**KATH:** (After a pause, her voice softer) 
It’s just…I’m tired of being just…okay. I want to be… more.

# **CHAPTER TWO: DEAL WITH THE TRUTH**

---

## **PRIVATE ROOM, THE COUNTRY CONNECTION RESTAURANT, NEW CARLISLE, OHIO, AFTERNOON, DECEMBER 26, 1994**

The private room at The Country Connection is buzzing with the low hum of conversation and the clinking of glasses. The air smells of burgers, fries, and that distinct small-town diner aroma. The band, Bill, Kath, Jeremy, Jeff, Gilley, and Jim, are gathered around a large table, littered with empty soda glasses and half-eaten plates. The atmosphere is festive, but there's an undercurrent of tension, a lingering reminder of the "*Shall I Beg*?" fallout.

Gone are the days of cheap beer and dollar-store gifts. Now, there are expensive bottles of whiskey, leather-bound books, and other high-end presents scattered around the table.

Kath sits quietly, observing the scene. She watches Jeremy excitedly showing off a new guitar pedal, Jeff laughing at something Gilley said, Jim deep in conversation with Bill about some new keyboard technology. A wave of nostalgia washes over her. These guys… they’ve been through a lot together.

**KATH:** (A small smile plays on her lips)
You guys remember when our Christmas parties consisted of a case of PBR and some gas station snacks?

**[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|GILLEY]]:**  (Raises his glass of Dr. Pepper)
Ah, those were the days. Simpler times. Before we were rolling in…well, not *rolling* exactly, but before we could afford decent booze.

Everyone laughs, the tension in the room easing slightly.

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|JEFF]]:**  
Remember that time we tried to deep-fry Twinkies in Jim’s dad's garage?

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]]:**  (Shakes his head, grinning) 
Don’t remind me. He's still finding grease stains.

The conversation flows, bouncing from one memory to another. Kath listens, enjoying the camaraderie, but a sense of melancholy lingers. She knows this is likely the last time she’ll be part of this dynamic in the same way.

**KATH:** (After a lull in the conversation, she clears her throat. Everyone turns to look at her.) 
Hey, guys…there’s something I wanted to talk to you about.

The atmosphere shifts. The lightheartedness fades, replaced by a sense of anticipation.

**[[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|Jeremy]]:**  
What’s up, Kath?

 Kath takes a deep breath. She knows there’s no turning back now.

**KATH:** 
I’ve been doing a lot of thinking lately…especially since being home for Christmas. And… well, I’ve decided that I’m going to be making some changes.

She pauses, letting her words sink in. The room is silent.

**KATH:**  
I’m going to be…I’m going to be stepping back from playing guitar with the band.

The silence hangs heavy in the air.

---

## **PRIVATE ROOM, THE COUNTRY CONNECTION RESTAURANT, FADING AFTERNOON, (CONT'D)**

The silence hangs heavy. Then, a cacophony of reactions erupts.

**GILLEY:** (Laughs, a nervous edge to his voice) 
What? You’re shitting me, right? This is some kind of…Christmas joke?

**JEREMY:** (Frowning) 
Kath, what are you talking about?

**JEFF:** (Stares at her, his expression a mix of confusion and hurt) 
You’re…leaving?

**JIM:** (Looks at Kath, his brow furrowed with concern) 
What’s going on, Kath?

**[[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|BILL]]:** (Quietly) 
Why?

Kath looks at each of them, her expression unreadable.

**KATH:**  
I’m not leaving the band. I’m just… not playing guitar anymore. I’m going to focus on vocals…and piano.

**GILLEY:**  
Piano? You don’t even…

**KATH:** (Cuts him off sharply) 
I know what I fucking play, Gilley. And I know what I’m *going* to play.

**JEREMY:**  
But…why? We’re about to start working on the new album. We need you.

**KATH:** (Her voice hardens) 
You’ll manage.

**JEFF:** (His voice laced with hurt)
Did I do something? Is this about… us?

**KATH:** (Turns her gaze on Jeff, her expression softening slightly, but still firm) 
This isn’t about you, Jeff. This is about me.

**JIM:** (Leans forward, his voice gentle)
Kath, you can talk to us. We’re your friends. What’s really going on?

**KATH:**  (Her eyes flash with anger) 
What’s going on is that I’ve made a goddamn decision, and that’s all you need to know.

**BILL:** (Quietly) 
But it doesn’t make any sense.

**KATH:**  (Slams her hand on the table, making everyone jump) 
It doesn’t have to make sense to *you*! It makes sense to *me*. That’s all that fucking matters!

The room falls silent again. The festive atmosphere has completely evaporated, replaced by a thick tension.

**GILLEY:** (After a long pause, his voice low and dangerous)
You’re being a real bitch about this, Kath.

**KATH:** (Stares him down, her voice ice cold) 
And you’re being a nosy son of a bitch, Gilley. It’s my life. My decision. And I’m not going to fucking explain myself to any of you.

She stands up abruptly, pushing her chair back.

**KATH:**  
I’m done with this conversation.

She turns and walks out of the private room, leaving the band in stunned silence. The bottles of whiskey and leather-bound books suddenly seem out of place, a stark contrast to the raw emotion that just filled the room.

---

## **PARKING LOT, COUNTRY CONNECTION RESTAURANT, MOMENTS LATER**

The cold winter air bites at Kath’s face as she stands outside The Country Connection, a cigarette burning between her fingers. The parking lot is mostly empty; the only sounds are the distant hum of traffic and the crunch of gravel underfoot. Jim emerges from the restaurant, pulling his coat tighter around him. He spots Kath and walks over.

**JIM:** (His voice gentle) 
You okay?

Kath takes a long drag of her cigarette, exhaling a plume of smoke into the cold air.

**KATH:**  
Peachy.

**JIM:** (Sighs) 
You know you didn’t have to do that in there.

**KATH:** (Shrugs) 
Had to be done.

**JIM:** 
But…why so secretive? We’re your friends, Kath. You can tell us anything.

Kath flicks ash onto the ground.

**KATH:**  
There’s nothing to tell.

**JIM:**  
Come on, Kath. I know you. You only smoke tobacco like this when something’s really eating at you.

**KATH:** (Looks at him, a flicker of something unreadable in her eyes)
Maybe I just felt like a cigarette.

**JIM:**  (A small, knowing smile plays on his lips) 
Right. And I’m the Easter Bunny.

Kath doesn’t respond, taking another drag of her cigarette.

**JIM:**  
Look, I’m not trying to pry. But… this is a big deal. You’re changing your entire role in the band. We just… we want to understand.

**KATH:** (Her voice low and firm)
There’s nothing to understand, Jim. I’m just… moving on.

**JIM:**  
Moving on to what? Why the piano? Why now?

**KATH:** (Turns away, staring out at the empty parking lot)
It just feels… right.

**JIM:** (Puts a hand on her shoulder) 
Kath…

Kath stiffens slightly under his touch.

**KATH:** (Without turning around) 
Don’t.

Jim removes his hand.

**JIM:** (After a long pause)
Is this… is this something you’re ever going to tell us? Ever going to tell *me*?

Kath takes a final drag of her cigarette, then drops it to the ground and crushes it under her boot. She finally turns to face Jim, her expression resolute.

**KATH:**  
Not ever, Jim.

She looks at him directly in the eyes, her gaze unwavering. There’s a finality in her voice that leaves no room for argument. Jim looks back at her, a mixture of sadness and confusion in his eyes. He knows that she means it. He knows that this is a closed door.

---

## **PARKING LOT, COUNTRY CONNECTION RESTAURANT, OLDER AND COLDER**

Jim watches as Kath walks towards a sleek, dark Acura parked a short distance away. It’s the nicest thing she’s ever owned, a tangible symbol of the band’s success. She unlocks the door, slides into the driver’s seat, and slams the door shut.

Jim stands there for a moment longer, watching the car. He sees the brake lights flash on, then the headlights. The engine turns over, and the car idles quietly.

Inside the car, Kath grips the steering wheel tightly. She closes her eyes, takes a deep breath, and then…she bangs her forehead against the steering wheel, a sharp, frustrated thud. She does it again, a little harder this time.

**KATH:** (Muttering to herself) 
Goddamn it. Goddamn it all to hell.

She sits back in the seat, breathing ragged. She stares straight ahead; her eyes fixed on the empty road in front of her.

**KATH:** (Speaking more firmly now, as if trying to convince herself)
This was the right thing to do. It was. It *is*.

She starts to repeat the reasons in her head, the carefully constructed justifications she’s been rehearsing.

**KATH:** (Whispering)
I wasn’t…I wasn’t shining. I wasn’t…me. I need to…I need to find my own voice. My own way.

She repeats the phrases again, each time with more conviction, as if by sheer repetition she can erase any doubt that lingers.

**KATH:**  
I wasn’t shining. I wasn’t me. I need to find my own voice. My own way.

She puts the car in gear and pulls out of the parking lot, the taillights disappearing into the winter darkness.
