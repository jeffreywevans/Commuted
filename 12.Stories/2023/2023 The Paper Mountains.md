## **INT. GOTHENBURG LANDVETTER AIRPORT - GENERAL AVIATION TERMINAL - LATE AFTERNOON -DECEMBER 17, 2023**

The crisp Scandinavian air bites at Jeff and Jeremy Evans as they descend the steps of the Gulfstream NetJet. The sky is a bruised twilight blue, the winter sun already dipping below the horizon. Gothenburg Landvetter Airport’s general aviation terminal is sleek and modern, a stark contrast to the utilitarian feel of larger commercial hubs.

Jeff stretches, a long, drawn-out movement that betrays his transatlantic slumber. He rubs the sleep from his eyes, trying to orient himself. Jeremy, however, is a picture of focused energy. He snaps shut his iPad, a subtle click the only indication that he’s ceased his relentless work. His eyes, sharp and alert, scan the small crowd gathered near the terminal exit.

There, amidst the business travelers and private jet crews, stands Tim Wright. He’s a vision of futuristic cool, a cyberpunk dandy. A tailored dark grey overcoat drapes over a black turtleneck, and polished boots gleam under the terminal’s bright lights. But the most striking feature is the pair of sleek, almost invisible VR glasses perched on his nose. They glow with a faint inner light, hinting at the digital world he’s just stepped out of.

Jeff and Jeremy approach, their footsteps echoing slightly in the quiet terminal. As they get closer, Tim seems to materialize from his digital reverie. He smoothly removes the VR glasses, a wide grin spreading across his face.

**TIM**
Brothers\! My brothers in arms\! You made it\!

He strides forward, embracing Jeff first in a firm, brotherly hug.

**TIM**
Jeff, you look like you just woke up from a cryogenic freeze. Good flight?

**JEFF** (Yawning) 
Slept the whole way. Best way to beat jet lag.

Tim claps Jeremy on the shoulder, a knowing look in his eyes.

**TIM**
Jeremy, always working. You never stop, do you?

Jeremy offers a tight, professional smile.

**JEREMY**
Deadlines, Tim. You know how it is.

Tim winks, a sly grin playing on his lips.

**TIM**
Oh, I know more than you think. Especially about *certain* deadlines.

Jeremy’s eyes narrow slightly, a flicker of suspicion crossing his face. He glances quickly at Jeff, who is oblivious, still shaking off the last vestiges of sleep.

**TIM** (Ignoring Jeremy’s look, his voice bright and enthusiastic) 
Anyway, enough chit-chat\! I’m thrilled you could both make it on such short notice. I know how demanding your schedules are. It means the world to me. Especially after our little Norwegian adventure a few months back.

He gestures towards the exit.

**TIM**
We have a very important meeting. A very… *special* meeting. And trust me, you don’t want to be late.

He leads them towards a waiting car, the glow of Gothenburg’s city lights beckoning in the distance. The scene ends on a close-up of Jeremy's face, a mixture of curiosity and apprehension lingering in his eyes, completely unaware of the surprise that awaits him.

The doors of the airport slide open, revealing a sleek, matte black Cybertruck, parked with a confident disregard for designated parking zones. Jeff stops dead in his tracks, a mixture of awe and envy washing over his face.

**JEFF**
You’ve got to be kidding me.

Jeremy raises an eyebrow, observing his brother’s sudden fascination.

**JEREMY**
What is it? Another one of your midlife crisis vehicles?

**JEFF** (Eyes glued to the Cybertruck) 
This isn’t a crisis, Jeremy. This is… *art*. This is the future of transportation. This is…

**TIM** (Grinning, gesturing towards the truck)
Hop in, gentlemen. Let’s roll.

Jeff reluctantly tears his gaze away and climbs into the passenger seat, running a hand over the angular dashboard. Jeremy follows, settling into the back.

**JEFF**
I’ve been wanting one of these for ages. But my daughters… they think it’s an abomination. “*Dad, you’ll look like a space dork*.” That’s a direct quote.

**TIM**
So? What’s stopping you? You’re *Jeff Evans. You can do whatever you want.

**JEFF**
Yeah, but… I’m also a dad. I have to consider their feelings. Imagine dropping them off at college in this thing. They’d never forgive me.

**TIM**
But think of the utility\! The durability\! The *presence*\! This thing can survive a zombie apocalypse\!

Tim pulls the Cybertruck smoothly into the Gothenburg traffic, navigating the city’s streets with practiced ease.

**JEFF**
I know, right? It’s practically bulletproof. I was even talking to Elon the other day about maybe redesigning the windshield wipers, they’re kind of a weak point right now.

Tim momentarily forgets the level of Jeff’s connections.

**TIM**
You talk to *Elon*? Like, Elon Musk Elon?

**JEFF**
Yeah, well, he’s a busy guy these days. But we chatted briefly.

Tim shakes his head, impressed.

**TIM**
Right. Of course. You’re practically neighbors on Mars, aren't you? Or is it just the nearest red-headed pussy?

Jeff and Jeremy burst into laughter, the tension from the flight dissipating. They all know Jeff’s reputation for appreciating the company of red-haired women, a source of both amusement and not so gentle ribbing amongst his friends.

**JEFF** (Laughing) 
You know me too well, Tim. You know me too well.

As the laughter subsides, Tim’s expression turns slightly more serious.

---

**TIM**
So, Jeremy… how long has it been now?

**JEREMY**
January 4th, 2021. Best decision I ever made. I haven’t felt this good in over a decade.

Jeremy pats his stomach, now lean and toned. His once full head of hair is now mostly gone, replaced by a distinguished salt-and-pepper beard that adds to his newfound air of rugged health.

**JEREMY**
I’m running five miles a day now. One hundred and thirty-nine days straight, to be exact.

He pulls out his iPad, displaying a fitness app with detailed logs of his runs.

**TIM**
One hundred and thirty-nine days? Damn.

**JEREMY**
I might even run my third marathon next year.

Tim raises an eyebrow, impressed.

**TIM**
Running marathons now, huh? That’s… dedication.

**JEREMY**
It’s… a substitute, I guess.

**TIM**
For the booze?

**JEREMY** (Nods) 
Yeah. Pretty much.

Tim, a former Navy corpsman who underwent gastric bypass surgery in his forties, shudders slightly.

**TIM**
I wouldn’t run unless I was being chased by a reindeer.

He says this with a completely straight face, leaving Jeff and Jeremy unsure if he’s joking or not. He mostly isn't. The Cybertruck continues its journey through the city, carrying the three men towards their mysterious destination.

Tim expertly maneuvers the Cybertruck into a parking spot in a lot clearly marked for emergency services. The lot is surprisingly full, a mix of police cruisers, ambulances, and other official vehicles. A low hum of activity pervades the air, and snippets of Swedish conversations drift on the cold air.

**JEFF**
What is this place? Some kind of emergency services convention?

**JEREMY**
I don’t recognize any of the uniforms. And I definitely don’t speak any Swedish.

**TIM** (Grinning) 
You don’t even know how to say “Do you want to fuck?” in Swedish, Jeff? Shame on you.

**JEFF**
Only Gilley would have bothered to learn that.

Tim’s expression shifts, the playful banter fading into a more serious demeanor. Jeff and Jeremy immediately pick up on the change, their postures straightening, their expressions becoming more businesslike.

**TIM**
Right. Let’s get down to it.

He turns to face them, his tone now crisp and professional.

**TIM**
I’ve been talking to Bill and Jim. I’ve also spoken to each of you separately over the past few weeks. I think I have a pretty good handle on the situation.

**JEFF**
You usually do. You’re like a human search engine.

**TIM** (A slight smile) 
I’ve come to the conclusion that you guys are hitting a wall when it comes to finding a replacement for Gilley.

**JEFF**
That’s an understatement. Every time we sit down to discuss it, we end up going in circles.

**JEREMY**
It’s been… rigorous. Thoughtful. I’m actually quite proud of how well we’ve all handled it. It could have been a real bloodbath, but everyone’s been remarkably… comradely.

**TIM**
That’s good to hear. It’s a sign of how much you’ve all matured.

**JEFF**
I just wish it was 2004 again. Things were simpler then. But, you know… water under the bridge.

He sighs, then continues.

**JEFF**
A lot of the old… acrimony… has cleared up over the past six weeks. Maybe some of these changes are going to stick after all.

Tim’s eyes narrow slightly, a hint of a challenge in his voice.

**TIM**
Like what kind of changes? Have you finally given up on your… extracurricular activities?

Jeremy interjects quickly, a note of defensiveness in his voice.

**JEREMY**
He’s… he’s being more *strategic* about it. He’s not chasing after every woman who gives him the time of day anymore. Especially not any of our friends.

Jeremy glances nervously at Jeff, a hint of worry in his eyes.

**JEFF** (Shrugging) 
I still… you know… I’m not celibate. But it’s not like it was before.

Tim looks at Jeff, a polite but pointed question in his eyes.

**TIM**
So… zipper control is… improving?

The question hangs in the air, a delicate balance between friendly ribbing and genuine concern. Tim presses a button on his key fob, and a nondescript door set into the side of the emergency services building clicks open. As Jeremy looks around, confused, a middle-aged man in a crisp uniform approaches the Cybertruck. He gives Tim a curt nod.

**TIM** (To Jeremy, gesturing towards the door)
Alright, slide on over, Slick. We got company.

Tim then adds, with a sideways glance at Jeff:

**TIM**
I got a buddy, same problem as you. Solved it… twice. But that’s another story for another time. A story best told over a bottle of aged tequila and a… well, let's just say a *very* understanding senorita.

Jeremy, still slightly bewildered by the sudden change of location, shuffles across the backseat. The massive rear door of the Cybertruck smoothly ascends, revealing…

**DAVE GROHL.**

He’s sitting in the back, looking every bit the rock icon, but with a surprisingly warm and welcoming smile. It’s a dramatic reveal, the kind Tarantino would frame with a lingering close-up and a sudden burst of classic rock on the soundtrack (which, of course, we can only imagine here).

**JEFF** (A genuine smile spreading across his face)
Dave! Holy shit!

**JEREMY** (Equally surprised and pleased) 
Wow! Dave…what the hell are you doing in Gothenburg?

**DAVE** (Grinning, extending a hand to Jeff) 
Jeff! Jeremy! Good to see you guys. It’s been… what, a few years now? Last time was that MTV thing, right? The one where Kanye tried to… well, you know.

**JEFF** (Shaking Dave's hand) 
Yeah, that’s right. Good to see you too, man.

Jeremy shakes Dave's hand next.

**JEREMY**
Yeah, man, it's been way too long.

**TIM** (Stepping back slightly, a satisfied look on his face)
Gentlemen, I believe you all know each other.

**DAVE** (Nodding towards Tim) 
This guy here… he’s a man of mystery. A real… *fixer*. He told me some old friends of his were coming to town, and I figured, what the hell? It’s been too long.

**JEFF** (Looking at Tim, then back at Dave) 
You brought *Dave Grohl* to Gothenburg? What’s going on, Tim?

**DAVE** (His expression turning slightly more serious) 
I’m here for a good cause, actually. Been working with a local foundation to raise some funds for the families of some fallen emergency service workers. Tragic thing happened back in November… big fire. Lost four brave souls.

He pauses, a somber look in his eyes.

**DAVE**
These guys, these firefighters, paramedics… they’re heroes. Real heroes. They put their lives on the line every single day. And sometimes… sometimes they don’t come home. So, I figured, if I could lend a hand, lend my name, my face… whatever I can do to help… well, I’m gonna do it. I have some personal history here in Gothenburg. It just felt right.

He looks around the parking lot, then back at the Evans brothers.

**DAVE**
It’s been a real honor to be a part of this. These people… they deserve all the support we can give them.

The tone is now serious, respectful, a stark contrast to the earlier banter. The scene ends with the three musicians looking at each other, a shared sense of respect and understanding passing between them.

Dave leans back in the plush leather seat of the Cybertruck, his expression thoughtful. The hum of the nearby emergency vehicles provides a low, constant backdrop to the conversation.

**DAVE**
So, Tim tells me you guys are… well, you’re trying to fill Gilley’s shoes.

He pauses, a slight frown creasing his brow.

**DAVE**
Look, I get it. You’re a band. You want to keep making music. But let me tell you something, from someone who’s been there… you don’t *replace* Gilley. You just don’t.

He looks directly at Jeff and Jeremy, his gaze intense.

**DAVE**
You don’t replace anyone. Especially not an icon. Especially not someone who was… well, who was *that*. You know? What you do is, you hire a talented man, or a talented woman. That’s the key difference. You find someone who brings their own fire, their own voice, their own… *thing* to the table. You don’t try to recreate what was lost. You create something new.

He shifts in his seat, his expression becoming more introspective.

**DAVE**
After… after Kurt… it was… well, it was the end of the world. At least, it felt like it. Nirvana… that was… that was everything. It was our lives, our hearts, our souls poured out into music. And then… it was gone. Just like that.

He snaps his fingers, the sound echoing in the confined space of the truck.

**DAVE**
There was no way to replace Kurt. There just wasn’t. I mean, how could you? He was… he was a force of nature. A lightning strike. You don’t replace lightning. You just… you try to find shelter when the storm hits.

He looks out the window for a moment, his gaze lost in the twilight.

**DAVE**
I spent years… years just… adrift. I didn’t know what to do. I didn’t even know if I *wanted* to do music anymore. It felt… wrong. Like I was… I was betraying something.

He turns back to Jeff and Jeremy, his voice becoming stronger, more resolute.

**DAVE**
But then… eventually… I realized… I had to do *something*. I had to keep going. Not to replace what was lost, but to… to find something new. Something that was *mine*. Something that came from me, from what I had left.

He pauses, taking a deep breath.

**DAVE**
It wasn’t easy. It was… terrifying. Starting over. Putting myself out there again. But I knew I had to. I owed it to myself. I owed it to the music. And… I think… I owed it to Kurt, too. To not let what we created just… disappear.

His voice softens slightly.

**DAVE**
So, I started Foo Fighters. It wasn’t Nirvana. It was never meant to be. It was something different. Something… *else*. It was my way of… of dealing with the loss, of finding my own voice again.

He looks at Jeff and Jeremy, his eyes filled with understanding.

**DAVE**
You guys loved Gilley. I get that. He was a part of your sound, your history. But trying to find someone to *be* him… that’s a dead end. It’s a disservice to his memory, and it’s a disservice to yourselves.

He pauses again, letting his words sink in.

**DAVE**
Find someone who can bring something *new* to Commuted. Someone who can help you write the next chapter. Don’t try to rewrite the past.

Jeff nods slowly, his expression thoughtful. He’s known for his often harsh criticism of Cobain, a stance that has caused controversy in the past. But he’s trying to be respectful here, understanding the weight of Dave’s words.

**JEFF**
I… I hear what you’re saying, Dave.

**JEREMY** (Nodding in agreement) 
Yeah. That makes a lot of sense.

The atmosphere in the Cybertruck is now heavy with the weight of shared experience, the unspoken understanding of loss and the need to move forward. Dave observes Jeff’s carefully neutral expression, the subtle effort he’s making to avoid his usual criticisms. A small, appreciative smile touches his lips.

**DAVE**
You know what, Jeff? I appreciate you keeping your goddamn mouth shut. Seriously. That’s… that’s actually pretty big of you.

Jeff, surprised by the direct praise, offers a small, almost sheepish smile in return.

**JEFF**
Thanks, Dave.

Dave takes a deep breath, the light in the Cybertruck reflecting in his eyes. The conversation shifts, becoming more personal, more raw.

**DAVE**
Look… it’s not just about losing a bandmate. It’s about losing a… a brother. Someone you’ve shared everything with. The highs, the lows, the… the absolute absurdity of it all.

His voice cracks slightly, and he looks out the window for a moment, gathering himself.

**DAVE**
Losing Taylor… it was… it was like getting hit by a truck. Again. You think you’ve been through the worst of it, you think you know what loss feels like… and then… bam. It hits you again, even harder.

He turns back to Jeff and Jeremy, his eyes filled with a deep sadness, but also a steely resolve.

**DAVE**
It’s not just the music, you know? It’s the… the everyday stuff. The inside jokes, the late-night conversations, the… the shared silences. It’s the knowing look you give each other on stage when something goes wrong, or when something goes unbelievably right. It’s the… the shared history. The years of sweat and tears and laughter.

He pauses, his voice becoming quieter, more introspective.

**DAVE**
You spend so much time with these people… they become more than just bandmates. They become your family. Your chosen family. And when you lose one of them… it’s like losing a limb. You’re just… incomplete.

He rubs his hand across his forehead, struggling to keep his emotions in check.

**DAVE**
And then… then you have to figure out how to keep going. You have to figure out how to… to honor their memory without… without trying to recreate them. Because you can’t. You just can’t.

He looks directly at Jeff, his voice firm.

**DAVE**
You can’t replace the irreplaceable. You can’t replicate the magic that was there. You can only… you can only find a new kind of magic. A new kind of energy. A new kind of… connection.

He takes another deep breath, steeling himself. He’s got a point to make, and he’s not going to let his emotions get in the way.

**DAVE**
And that’s what I’m trying to tell you guys. Don’t try to find another Gilley. Find someone who can bring something *different* to Commuted. Someone who can help you evolve, who can help you grow. Someone who can help you find that new magic.

He looks at Jeff, then at Jeremy, his gaze filled with sincerity.

**DAVE**
Because that’s what Gilley would have wanted. He wouldn’t want you to be stuck in the past. He’d want you to keep moving forward, to keep making music, to keep… *living*.

He pauses, his voice softening slightly.

**DAVE**
And that’s what Taylor would have wanted for us too. He wouldn’t want us to stop. He’d want us to keep playing, to keep making noise, to keep… celebrating the power of music.

He looks out the window again, his gaze lost in the distance.

**DAVE**
Because that’s what it’s all about, isn’t it? The music. The connection. The shared experience. That’s what matters. That’s what endures.

He turns back to the Evans brothers, his expression now calm and resolute.

**DAVE**
And that’s what you guys need to focus on. Not on finding a replacement. But on finding a new way to keep that music alive.

Tim leans forward, a mischievous glint in his eyes.

**TIM**
Oh, he’s just getting warmed up. Trust me.

Dave grins, a familiar twinkle in his eye. He leans forward conspiratorially, lowering his voice slightly.

**DAVE**
Now, as Gilley would say…“Now we shall touch penises together, and be brothers forever!”

The effect is instantaneous. Jeff and Jeremy erupt in uncontrollable laughter, tears welling in their eyes. It’s a deeply personal joke, a piece of Gilley’s unique, often crude, humor that few outside their inner circle would know.

**JEFF** (Gasping for air) 
Oh my god… how… how did you…

**JEREMY** (Wiping tears from his eyes) 
That’s… that’s ancient. Justified and ancient.

**DAVE** (Chuckling) 
Let’s just say… word gets around. Especially when it’s that… *memorable*.

He sobers slightly, his expression becoming more thoughtful.

**DAVE**
Look, the solution to your problem… it’s right in front of you. You’re just too close to it to see it.

He gestures between Jeff and Jeremy, then points towards the front of the vehicle, where the driver's seat would be if this wasn't a self-driving car.

**DAVE**
Your new frontman… it should be Jim.

Jeff and Jeremy exchange a stunned look.

**JEFF**
Jim?

**JEREMY**
*Jim Brown?*

**DAVE**
The one and only. Think about it. Jim’s got a fantastic voice. A deep, rich baritone. I’m telling you, the only guy I can think of with pipes like that is Mark Lanegan, may he rest in peace. And Bill, he’s hilarious, but he keeps it under wraps most of the time. You need a familiar face, a familiar *voice* at the head of the band. Especially since…

He pauses, glancing at Tim, then back at the Evans brothers.

**DAVE**
…especially since I have a strong feeling you’re going to be bringing on a new female vocalist as well.

Jeff and Jeremy exchange a silent, pointed glare at Tim. They clearly hadn’t shared this particular detail with Dave.

**DAVE** (Ignoring the tension) 
Now, hear me out on Jim. It makes perfect sense. He’s been with you guys from the start. He knows the songs, he knows the fans, he knows the *vibe*. He’s got the stage presence, the charisma… he just needs a little push. And trust me, with the right material, with the right direction… he’ll absolutely kill it.

He leans forward, becoming more animated.

**DAVE**
Think about the dynamic. You’ve got Jim, solid as a rock, anchoring the band with that incredible voice. Then you bring in a fresh, new female energy. It’s the perfect combination. It’s… it’s a reinvention without losing your roots. It’s evolution, not revolution.

He continues, building his argument with the energy and passion of a seasoned performer.

**DAVE**
You've been struggling to find someone external, someone who fits the “Gilley mold.” But that mold is broken. It’s shattered. You can’t recreate it. So, stop trying. Look inward. Look at what you already have. You have a diamond in the rough sitting right there in your band. You just need to polish it up a bit.

He gestures emphatically.

**DAVE**
Jim’s not trying to *be* Gilley. He’s Jim Brown. He’s got his own style, his own personality. And that’s what’s going to make this work. It’s not about replacing a legend. It’s about finding a new legend within your own ranks.

He pauses, taking a breath before continuing his persuasive argument.

**DAVE**
This isn’t some random guy off the street. This is *Jim*. He’s family. He’s been through thick and thin with you guys. He understands the music, he understands the history, he understands the *heart* of Commuted. That’s something you can’t just find in an audition.

He leans back again, his voice softening slightly.

**DAVE**
Look, I know this is a big decision. It’s not something you can just decide overnight. But I’m telling you… give it some serious thought. Give Jim a chance. You might just surprise yourselves. You might just find that the answer you’ve been searching for has been there all along.

The weight of Dave’s words hangs in the air. Jeff and Jeremy exchange another look, this time with a new sense of consideration. The idea of Jim as the frontman… it’s definitely something to think about.

The weight of Dave’s previous words hung heavy in the air for a moment. Then, Tim, ever the instigator, nudged the conversation forward.

**TIM**
See? Told you he was just getting started.

Dave, as if a switch had been flipped, became instantly animated, energized. The somber mood lifted, replaced by a vibrant enthusiasm. He seemed genuinely thrilled that he had the Evans brothers’ undivided attention.

**DAVE**
Right\! So, you want to talk about *survival* in the music industry? You want to know how to navigate the minefield of broken promises, shady managers, and the constant pressure to reinvent yourself?

He leans forward, his eyes wide, his voice filled with passion.

**DAVE**
Well, let me tell you something. It ain’t for the faint of heart. It’s a goddamn jungle out there. A concrete jungle, filled with hungry wolves and… and… well, let’s just say a lot of people who want a piece of your pie.

He gestures emphatically with his hands.

**DAVE**
The first thing you gotta understand is that it’s not about talent alone. Talent is important, sure. But it’s not enough. You gotta have the… the *grit*. The *determination*. The absolute refusal to give up, even when everyone else is telling you to quit.

He pauses, taking a deep breath.

**DAVE**
You gotta be willing to work your ass off. Day in and day out. You gotta be willing to play every dive bar, every dingy club, every godforsaken festival in the middle of nowhere. You gotta be willing to sleep in vans, eat gas station sushi, and deal with sound guys who don’t know their ass from a hole in the ground.

He snaps his fingers for emphasis.

**DAVE**
And you gotta be smart. You gotta learn the business side of things. You gotta understand contracts, publishing, royalties… all that boring stuff. Because if you don’t, someone else will take advantage of you. Trust me, I’ve seen it happen way too many times.

He leans back, his voice softening slightly.

**DAVE**
But most importantly… most importantly, you gotta stay true to yourself. You gotta stay true to your music. You gotta remember why you started doing this in the first place.

He looks directly at Jeff and Jeremy, his gaze intense.

**DAVE**
Because at the end of the day, that’s all that matters. The music. The connection you make with your audience. That’s what’s going to keep you going when things get tough. And trust me, things *will* get tough.

He pauses, taking another deep breath.

**DAVE**
You’re gonna face rejection. You’re gonna face criticism. You’re gonna face setbacks. You’re gonna have moments where you want to throw in the towel and say, “Fuck it, I’m done.”

He looks at them intently.

**DAVE**
But you can’t. You just can’t. Because if you give up, you’ll never know what could have been. You’ll never know what you’re truly capable of.

He leans forward again, his voice becoming more urgent.

**DAVE**
You gotta keep pushing. You gotta keep creating. You gotta keep evolving. You gotta keep finding new ways to connect with your audience. You gotta keep reinventing yourself, without losing sight of who you are.

He pauses, a slight smile playing on his lips.

**DAVE**
And that’s where Jim comes in. He’s a part of your history. He’s a part of your DNA. He’s the perfect bridge between your past and your future. And with a new voice alongside him… that’s not just evolution. That’s a goddamn explosion.

He sits back, his energy slightly subsiding, but his eyes still shining with enthusiasm. He looks at Jeff and Jeremy, a look of quiet satisfaction on his face. He senses he’s finally broken through their defenses, that his message has finally landed.

**DAVE**
So… what do you think?

The silence in the Cybertruck is now charged with a new kind of energy, a sense of possibility and excitement. Jeff and Jeremy exchange a look, the gears turning in their minds. Dave’s words have resonated with them, and they know they have a lot to think about.

Dave abruptly shifts gears, the intensity of his previous speech fading into a more casual, conspiratorial tone.

**DAVE**
So, changing the subject entirely… I heard whispers… rumors on the wind… that you guys are finally getting inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame this year. Congratulations, by the way. Long overdue.

He looks at the Evans brothers with genuine curiosity.

**DAVE**
Have you… you know… picked someone to present you yet? That’s always a big deal.

Jeremy waves his hand dismissively.

**JEREMY**
We’re still working on the setlist, Dave. That’s the real priority.

Jeff, however, seems more engaged by the question.

**JEFF**
Actually, yeah. I’ve got a couple of ideas floating around.

**DAVE**
Oh, really? Spill it. Who are you thinking? Come on, don’t be shy.

**JEFF** (Hesitantly) 
I don’t know… it’s not really…

**DAVE** (Interrupting, with a playful but firm tone)
