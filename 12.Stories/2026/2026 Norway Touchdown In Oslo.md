### Touchdown in Oslo: Champagne, Tourist Plans, and the Forbidden Name

The Gulfstream landed smoothly in Oslo, the fighter jets peeling away into the night like they had actual business to attend to.

As soon as the seatbelt sign clicked off, Tara stretched luxuriously, looking around their obscenely lavish cabin. “I can’t believe Jeff just… casually pays for this.”

“Yeah, well,” Jodi said, unbuckling her belt. “This is the same man who once spent eight grand on artisanal honey because, quote, ‘the bees deserved it.’”

Naomi, still adjusting to this new tier of wealth, whispered, “How much does honey usually cost?”

Tara patted her shoulder. “Oh, sweetie.”

As they disembarked and stepped into the freezing Norwegian air, Jodi pulled her coat tighter around herself. It was March, it was dark, and it was colder than Satan’s personal deep freezer.

Naomi took one step onto the tarmac, inhaled, and immediately started coughing.

“Oh my God,” she choked, pulling her scarf up. “Why is the air so sharp?!”

“It’s called winter, baby duck,” Tara said, completely unfazed. “You get used to it. Or you die. One of the two.”

Jodi ignored them, pulling out her phone to text Jeff.

Jodi: Landed. Freezing. Currently herding idiots toward the airport.

Jeff: Sounds about right. Hotel’s ready for you. Call me when you’re settled in.

Jodi: Not if, but WHEN I get frostbite, you’re obligated to nurse me back to health.

Jeff: Fine. But if I do, that counts toward the “twice” rule.

Jodi: Does not.

### Airport Lobby: Planning Tromsø & Avoiding the Forbidden Topic

By the time they made it through passport control (Naomi got flustered, Tara nearly told a border agent to go fuck himself, Jodi had to intervene), they found themselves in the airport lobby, waiting for their connecting flight to Tromsø.

Naomi, still clutching her herring tin like a talisman against the unknown, flipped open her notebook. “Okay! We have four days in Tromsø while Jeff’s in the studio. What are we doing?”

Tara draped herself across a row of chairs. “Step one: Not dying.”

“Great start,” Naomi muttered, writing it down like an actual plan.

Jodi scrolled through her phone. “We should do the Northern Lights chase one night. Even if it’s cloudy, it’s worth it just for the vibe.”

Naomi gasped. “Oh my God, yes! That’s, like, a bucket list thing for me!”

Tara nodded. “Alright. So that’s one night where we get frostbite on purpose. What else?”

Naomi flipped through a printed guidebook (because of course she had one). “Ooooh, we could do dog sledding!”

Jodi perked up. “That actually sounds kind of amazing.”

Tara raised an eyebrow. “You wanna get yeeted through the snow by a pack of feral wolf-dogs?”

Jodi grinned. “Yes.”

Naomi jotted it down. “Okay, that’s another half-day. We should also try some traditional food! Maybe a nice seafood dinner?”

Tara yawned. “Sure. As long as I get a drink that makes me forget I have a job.”

Jodi was about to reply when she caught a certain name in Naomi’s notes.

Avril.

Her entire demeanor shifted—posture stiffening, jaw tightening.

Tara, sipping from her Starbucks, noticed immediately.

“Oh, shit,” Tara muttered under her breath.

Naomi, completely oblivious, tapped her pen against the page. “Oh! And should we, uh… you know, say hi to—”

Jodi snapped the notebook shut.

“No.”

Naomi blinked. “What?”

Jodi looked her dead in the eyes. “We’re not talking about Avril.”

Naomi, suddenly very aware that she had made a mistake, slowly nodded. “…Okay?”

Tara, watching the tension roll off Jodi in waves, carefully cleared her throat. “So. Dog sledding, huh? You think we get to pick our own pack leader? ‘Cause I’m calling dibs on the fastest one.”

Jodi exhaled slowly, forcing herself to relax. “Yeah. I want one of the psychotic ones.”

Naomi, still processing the mood shift, hesitantly wrote down DO NOT TALK ABOUT AVRIL in her notes.

Lesson learned.

### Tromsø Bound: One More Flight to Go

As their boarding call echoed through the terminal, Naomi sighed. “Okay. I just realized something.”

“What?” Jodi asked, standing up and stretching.

Naomi gestured dramatically at herself. “I am a tiny, fragile Californian woman who has never experienced real winter before. I’m going to die in Tromsø.”

Tara clapped her on the back. “Oh, absolutely.”

Jodi smirked. “Don’t worry. If you freeze to death, we’ll take turns carrying your body through the fjords like a Nordic funeral procession.”

Naomi groaned. “I hate you both.”

As they headed for the gate, Jodi’s phone buzzed again.

Jeff: You on your way to Tromsø?

Jodi: Yep. Also, reminder: your countdown starts the second I land.

Jeff: You’re ridiculous.

Jodi: You knew what this was.

As they filed onto the plane, Tara cracked her knuckles. “Alright, ladies. This is it. We survive one more flight, and then it’s Viking time.”

Naomi frowned. “That’s… not really what we’re doing.”

Tara grinned. “It is if we commit to the bit.”

Jodi laughed, settling into her seat. Norway was officially happening.

And in less than two hours, she’d be back in Jeff’s arms.

If she didn’t die of frostbite first.

### Tromsø Airport: Where Things Get Very, Very Weird

The private jet from Tromsø landed smoothly on the icy tarmac, its sleek black exterior gleaming under the dim, perpetual twilight of a March in Northern Norway. Jodi, Tara, and Naomi stood just outside the terminal, wrapped in coats that absolutely weren’t prepared for this level of cold.

Naomi shivered violently. “Why is the air so aggressive?”

Tara, unfazed, pulled out a cigarette. “It’s Norway, baby. You either adapt or you perish.”

Jodi wasn’t paying attention to either of them. Her eyes were locked on the Gulfstream’s door, fully expecting Jeff to emerge, striding toward her, ready to put his money where his mouth was regarding The Countdown.

Instead…

Kirsten. Freaking. Dunst.

Naomi gasped so hard she choked on air. “OH MY GOD.”

Tara blinked. “Huh.”

Jodi? Jodi had stopped breathing.

Kirsten Dunst, in an impossibly chic winter coat, casually stepped off the jet like she owned Norway itself. She paused for a moment, scanning the terminal area. Then, with a grin—she made a direct beeline for Jodi.

Jodi’s brain: Error 404.

Naomi’s brain: AUTOPILOT PANIC.

Tara’s brain: Hmm. Suspicious.

As Kirsten approached, Naomi smacked Jodi’s arm repeatedly. “Do you see this?! DO YOU UNDERSTAND WHAT IS HAPPENING RIGHT NOW?!”

Jodi—who, to be clear, had been mentally preparing for Jeff and not the literal star of Bring It On—could only manage a confused, “Uh…?”

Kirsten beamed. “Jodi, right?”

Jodi blinked. “Yes?”

“I’ve been dying to talk to you,” Kirsten said, tucking her hands into her coat pockets.

Tara and Naomi, simultaneously, turned to size up Jodi standing next to Kirsten.

And neither of them wanted to say it out loud.

But.

There was something deeply unsettling about seeing them side by side.

Jodi looked… kinda like Kirsten Dunst.

Not identical, obviously—Jodi was her own person. But the resemblance? Uncomfortable.

Tara internally: Well. That’s interesting.

Naomi internally: OH MY GOD DOES JEFF HAVE A TYPE.

Jodi, meanwhile, was far too overwhelmed to notice the existential crisis happening to her left and right.

“Uh,” she managed, “I… what?”

Kirsten grinned. “I saw your Instagram post the other day—loved it. And Jeff never shut up about you.”

Jodi froze.

Naomi was still struggling. “Wait. Wait. WAIT. HOW DO YOU—”

Tara, voice completely neutral: “Ah. So Jeff has a type.”

Jodi’s eye twitched.

This was NOT how she expected Tromsø to start.

Kirsten gave Jodi a once-over, her expression unreadable. Then, leaning in just slightly, she said, “Hey, can I steal you for a second? Just the two of us?”

Tara and Naomi immediately snapped their attention to Jodi.

Jodi, for her part, was trying not to look as thrown as she felt.

“…Yeah,” she said slowly. “Sure.”

Naomi, internally screaming.

Tara, lighting a cigarette and waiting for the fallout.

Jodi had no idea what was about to happen.

But whatever it was, it wasn't going to be normal.

### Tromsø Airport – The Conversation Jodi Was Not Prepared For

Jodi followed Kirsten away from the terminal, the cold air biting at her face as they stopped near a set of sleek, black SUVs waiting in the arrivals lane. Kirsten, ever poised, tucked her hands into her coat pockets and exhaled slowly, like she was carefully measuring her next words.

Jodi, for her part, was still in mild shock.

First, Jeff had invited Kirsten Dunst to Norway.

Second, Kirsten Dunst had just casually told her that Jeff never shut up about her.

And third, this was now her actual life.

“…So,” Jodi said finally, watching her breath fog in the frigid air, “you’re about to say something that I probably don’t want to hear, huh?”

Kirsten smiled, a little rueful, a little amused. “Something like that.”

Jodi sighed, stuffing her gloved hands into her coat. “Alright. Hit me.”

Kirsten studied her for a beat, then nodded. “Alright. The short version? Since you hadn’t planned on coming to Norway while Jeff and the band were recording, he invited me to stay with him.”

Jodi didn’t flinch, but she did blink—once, slowly.

“Uh-huh,” she said, carefully neutral.

Kirsten, reading her like a damn book, pressed on. “I stayed for three days. Before you made your Instagram post—the, uh…” she gestured vaguely, “the cleavage post.”

Jodi smirked. “I do good work.”

Kirsten let out a small laugh. “Yeah. You do.”

Then, softer, “As soon as it became clear you were coming to Norway, we stopped.”

There it was.

Jodi wasn’t shocked. This was The System—the carefully built, painfully specific arrangement that she and Jeff had spent fifteen years fine-tuning.

Jeff didn’t lie. Jeff didn’t cheat. Jeff never crossed a line unless they’d agreed there wasn’t a line.

And yet.

Hearing it out loud, from Kirsten Dunst’s actual mouth, was still a hell of a thing.

Kirsten kept going, watching Jodi’s reaction carefully. “I figured it’d be weird—not to mention dishonest—not to tell you.”

Jodi actually appreciated that.

Kirsten shrugged, a little wry. “But what really got me was—the second Jeff scheduled your flight, he wouldn’t shut up about you.”

Jodi snorted. “That bad?”

“That bad.” Kirsten smiled, but there was something genuinely curious in her expression. “So, I was kind of wondering—who the hell is this woman from Ohio that managed to kick me out of his bedroom?”

Jodi exhaled, shaking her head with pure amusement.

“You know,” she said, “this is adorably too much information.”

Kirsten let out a short laugh. “Yeah, well. It felt important.”

And—it was.

Jodi could have been mad.

She could have been territorial.

She could have decided to take a big, dramatic stance over it.

But this was the life they’d built. This was what had worked for her and Jeff for more than a decade. This was their system, and—damn it, it still worked.

So instead of getting upset, Jodi did the only thing that made sense.

She smirked.

“Alright,” she said, clapping her hands together. “Two questions.”

Kirsten arched an eyebrow. “Shoot.”

“Did he fuck you in Scotland?”

Kirsten shook her head immediately. “Nope.”

Jodi nodded. “Okay. And—” she tilted her head, genuinely curious—“he really never stopped talking about me?”

Kirsten laughed, shaking her head in pure exasperation.

“Not for a goddamn second.”

Jodi grinned. That was all she needed.

“Well,” she said, adjusting her coat, “I’d say it was nice to meet you, but that would be dishonest.”

Kirsten actually laughed at that. “Fair enough.”

They stood there for a beat, an odd mutual respect settling in the air between them.

Then, Kirsten smirked. “Have fun, Jodi.”

“Oh, I will,” Jodi said, grinning like a woman who was about to ruin a man’s life in the best way possible.

And with that, Kirsten Dunst walked off into the Norwegian night, leaving Jodi with a newfound certainty that she was about to make Jeff work for it.

Because The Countdown was still very, very real.

### Tromsø Airport – The Humble Brag of the Century

Jodi ambled back toward Tara and Naomi, her hands tucked deep into her coat pockets, her breath fogging in the frigid air. She took her time, savoring the moment, while her two friends frothed like rabid dogs waiting for information.

Tara had a cigarette clenched between her teeth, her arms crossed tight like she was physically restraining herself from shaking Jodi down for intel.

Naomi, meanwhile, was practically vibrating, her eyes darting between Jodi and the direction Kirsten had just walked.

“Okay, okay,” Naomi burst out, unable to contain herself any longer. “What. The. Hell.”

Tara nodded, exhaling a slow, deadly drag of smoke. “Yeah, I second that.”

Jodi stopped in front of them, rocking on her heels. “Well,” she said casually, as if she hadn’t just had an absolutely insane conversation with a literal A-list celebrity, “apparently, Jeff did invite her to Norway.”

Tara squinted. “No shit.”

Jodi continued, deliberately drawing it out.

“She stayed for three days. You know, before I posted my very tasteful Instagram thirst trap.”

Naomi gasped dramatically. “Oh my GOD.”

Jodi inspected her fingernails. “And—” she paused for effect, “—as soon as it was clear that I was coming to Norway? She got the boot.”

Tara blinked.

Naomi blinked harder.

They both turned—slowly—to glance at the very chipper, very unbothered Kirsten Dunst heading toward the airport’s main terminal.

Then, just as slowly, they turned back to Jodi.

Tara broke first. “Are you humble-bragging that Jeff kicked a literal movie star out of his room for you?”

Jodi grinned. “Not just that,” she said, her voice positively dripping with smug satisfaction. “Apparently, as soon as he booked my flight, he never shut up about me.”

Naomi gasped so hard she nearly passed out.

Tara exhaled a long, slow whistle.

“That’s big,” Tara admitted, finally flicking her cigarette away.

Naomi, still struggling to process reality, let out a deeply cautious breath. “Okay, but… uh…” she glanced at Tara for moral support, then turned back to Jodi. “You do realize you and Kirsten look a lot alike, right?”

Jodi froze for exactly one second.

Then, without missing a beat, she grabbed her own boobs, lifted them like she was weighing produce, and said, “Yeah? Did you see what she was packing versus these guns?”

Then, as if to further demonstrate her point, she made a comic boxing move, throwing a few fake punches at the air.

Tara burst out laughing so hard she actually wheezed.

Naomi hid her face in her scarf, shaking with suppressed horrified giggles.

Jodi grinned wickedly.

Tara, still dying, wiped a tear from her eye. “Okay, I will never be able to unsee that.”

Naomi whimpered into her scarf. “I need to go lie down.”

Jodi, now fully in her element, stretched her arms behind her head, looking disgustingly pleased with herself.

“Anyway,” she said far too casually, “who’s ready for vacation?”

Naomi squeaked.

Tara just laughed.

And with that, they stepped towards Tromsø, where Jeff—utterly unaware of what was coming for him—was waiting.
