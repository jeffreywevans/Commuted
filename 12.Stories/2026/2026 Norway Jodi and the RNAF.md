#### Jodi and the Royal Norwegian Air Force: Part I


### The Longest Morning Ever

Jodi sat at her desk, tapping her pen against her planner while half-listening to a client drone on about a minor discrepancy in their transaction records. It wasn’t that she didn’t care—she was a professional, after all—but her brain was already halfway across the Atlantic.

Norway. Norway!

She and Tara had been texting all morning about it, sending each other articles, travel tips, and increasingly absurd theories about whether or not the Norwegian Air Force would make a dramatic appearance while they were on their fjord cruise.

I’m just saying, Tara had typed earlier, if they don’t do at least one high-speed flyover, I’m demanding a refund.

They don’t even charge for that! Jodi had replied.

EXACTLY, Tara had sent back. I am OWED this spectacle.

Now, as Jodi attempted to focus, she saw another message pop up from Naomi.

Naomi: So hypothetically, if one were to ‘accidentally’ be left behind in Norway to live out their Scandinavian dream, how much trouble would they be in?

Jodi smirked.

Jodi: Depends. Are you planning to run off with a fisherman or a Viking re-enactor?

Naomi: Listen, I am an equal-opportunity impulsive decision-maker.

Jodi was about to respond when Tara strolled into her office, a folder in hand, looking far too pleased with herself.

“They just put out the HR sign-up sheet,” Tara announced, dropping the folder onto Jodi’s desk.

Jodi blinked. “Already? I thought the morale event wasn’t until after vacation.”

“Oh, it is,” Tara said, leaning against the desk, “but apparently, corporate wants to make sure people sign up early. You know, so HR can assess the risk factors and, I don’t know, draft liability waivers.”

Jodi groaned. “Why does everything fun require legal clearance in this office?”

Tara smirked. “Because they know what happens when we’re left to our own devices.”

Fair point.

Jodi flipped through the sign-up sheet, scanning the legalese surrounding her own event. “Why is there an entire clause about ‘moderate-to-severe’ goat-related incidents?”

“Oh, that one’s definitely because of last year’s charity petting zoo,” Tara said. “You remember. Ralph from accounting got a little too friendly with that one goat, and HR decided we could never be trusted again.”

Jodi sighed, shaking her head. “Unbelievable.”

Tara grinned. “So. You signing up?”

“Oh, absolutely.” Jodi grabbed a pen and scrawled her name across the list. “If I’m planning it, I might as well be legally responsible for it.”

Tara reached over and signed up, too. “I love that you accept your fate.”

### Surprise! You're The Center of Attention

By the time lunchtime rolled around, Jodi, Tara, and Naomi were already discussing exactly how they’d react if a fleet of Norwegian fighter jets appeared overhead during their vacation.

Naomi was fully committed to dramatically fake-fainting.
Tara planned to salute them and demand honorary citizenship.
Jodi had decided she’d just hold up a sign that read “Finally, the Air Show I Deserve.”

Unfortunately, they never made it to the break room to finish their debate—because the entire bank was waiting for them.

“Surprise!” a chorus of voices called as they stepped inside.

Jodi stopped short, blinking at the sight of balloons, a cake with their faces poorly printed on it, and at least twenty-five coworkers standing around looking far too pleased with themselves.

“Oh. Oh no,” Jodi muttered under her breath.

Naomi immediately turned on her heel. “Nope. I refuse. We are not doing this.”

Tara, however, looked delighted. “Oh, we are absolutely doing this,” she said, clapping Jodi and Naomi on the back before striding toward the cake.

The branch manager, Gary, beamed at them. “We wanted to send you three off in style! You know, let you know how much we’ll miss you.”

“You mean you’ll miss our labor,” Naomi deadpanned.

Gary ignored that, reaching for a paper plate. “Come on, cut the cake! It’s tradition!”

Jodi sighed but stepped forward, accepting the knife. “Fine, fine. But if someone starts singing, I’m walking out that door.”

Jodi smirked. “You know what? That one’s fair.”

Jodi sighed dramatically but took the pen. “Fine, fine. But if I find out you specifically drafted this with us in mind—”

“I absolutely did,” Mike said before she could finish.

Jodi groaned but signed anyway.

### End of the Workday: Chaos and Norway Await

By the time they clocked out, the three of them were exhausted but buzzing with excitement.

One more day. One more day, and they’d be on a plane to Norway, debating whether or not they could bribe a pilot to let them into the cockpit.

As they stepped into the parking lot, Tara stretched and yawned. “So. If we do see the Norwegian Air Force, how much trouble do you think we’d be in if we attempted to join them?”

Naomi considered this. “Like, just run onto the tarmac and throw ourselves at their mercy?”

Jodi smirked. “Honestly? Worth it.”

And with that, they went their separate ways for the night, knowing full well that tomorrow would be the longest day of work ever.

### T-Minus Departure: The Last Workday & Airport Chaos

By the time their final workday rolled around, the three of them were utterly useless.

“Oh, you mean I was supposed to be productive today?” Tara drawled, flipping a page in her planner that had nothing but Norway Prep: **DO NOT DIE** written in red ink.

Naomi, vibrating with nervous energy, glanced at her open spreadsheet and winced. “I think I just formatted an entire report in Wingdings.”

Jodi, for her part, was mostly focused. She had travel logistics to double-check, a Jeff-based mission to execute, and one singular goal: make it through this workday without doing something so unhinged that HR was forced to intervene.

…Which meant that HR absolutely intervened.

At 11:43 AM, one of the HR reps stopped by, carrying a clipboard and an expression of weary inevitability.

“Okay,” she sighed. “I need you three to sign this.”

Jodi took the clipboard without looking up. “What is it this time?”

“It’s a preemptive liability waiver,” the HR rep said, rubbing her temples. “We’ve done… research. We know something is going to happen while you’re overseas, and we refuse to be held responsible for it.”

Jodi flipped to the second page. “This clause literally says, ‘If international law enforcement gets involved, the company will deny all knowledge of the employee’s existence.’”

Tara whistled. “Damn. You really don’t trust us.”

Naomi, meanwhile, had gone pale. “Wait, why would international law enforcement get involved?”

Tara patted her shoulder. “Oh, sweet summer child. Give it time.”

### Welcome to the Gulfstream, or ‘Who Let Us Have Nice Things?’

A sensible company retreat would involve economy-class suffering and a flight where Naomi would be strategically sandwiched between a crying baby and a man who smelled like regret.

Instead, because this particular branch’s leadership had Poor Decision-Making Syndrome, they’d chartered a Gulfstream—which meant fully reclining seats, a stocked bar, and a level of luxury they absolutely did not deserve.

The moment they stepped onboard, Tara let out a low, reverent whistle. “This,” she said, running a hand over one of the leather seats, “is how corrupt hedge fund executives must feel every day.”

Naomi, still reeling from Bloody Mary-induced trauma, whispered, “I don’t think I belong here.”

Jodi, already half-done with a glass of champagne, grinned. “Oh, you don’t. None of us do. Enjoy it anyway.”

The flight itself was shockingly uneventful—until they neared the North Sea.

Then, over the intercom, the pilot’s voice crackled:

“Ladies and gentlemen, we are approaching Norwegian airspace. It appears we are being intercepted by military aircraft. Please remain seated.”

There was a beat of silence. Then—

Tara let out an ear-splitting whoop.

“FINALLY!” she yelled. “NORWAY GETS IT.”

Naomi, meanwhile, looked moments away from a full nervous breakdown.

“Are we… are we going to be forced to land?!”

Jodi, grinning wildly, pulled out her phone and snapped a photo of the fighter jets streaking alongside them.

“Not unless we piss them off,” she said, opening up her messages.

Jodi → Jeff: I don’t want to brag, but we’re getting a fighter jet escort.

Jeff: You’re a menace.

Jodi: You love it.

The Gulfstream continued its approach, escorted in regal, completely unnecessary style.

And with that, their adventure in Norway had officially begun.
