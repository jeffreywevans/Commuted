**6:00 a.m.**  
The alarm shrieks. Sarah slams it quiet.  
She sits up. Groans. Her stomach twists.  
She mutters, *"Fuck, I think I'm gonna throw up."*  
The phone buzzes in the distance.  
Emails. Texts. Missed calls.  
No peace. No mercy.  
Just another goddamn day.

MICHAEL studies her a beat longer than usual. He knows that look—Sarah the executive, Sarah the hard-ass, Sarah the gladiator—has a hairline fracture this morning. He sets his mug down carefully, like noise would break her further.

**MICHAEL:**  
"That's not nothing, Sarah. You don't usually say that out loud."

**SARAH:**  
"Yeah, well. Guess I’m evolving. Or unraveling. Jury’s out."

She walks past him, opens the cabinet, and pulls out her usual mug. Not the nice one, not the one she bought in Tokyo, not the one MICHAEL gave her at Christmas. Just the chipped one. The one that works.

**SARAH:**  
"I’ve got no appetite. My skin feels weird. My brain feels…wet."

**MICHAEL:**  
"That's poetic. Also concerning."

He steps toward her, just a little. He doesn’t touch her yet.

**MICHAEL:**  
"Maybe call the doctor again. Move the appointment up?"

**SARAH:**  
"It’s in ten days. I’m not about to tell a receptionist I’m bleeding from my soul and need triage."

She pours coffee. No cream. No sugar. Just the bitterness.

**SARAH (softer):**  
"I need to get through this week. Then we see."

Beat.

**MICHAEL:**  
"I’ll be here. If you need a hand. Or a wall to hit."

That earns the tiniest smirk. The barest flicker of her old self, behind tired eyes. Then it’s gone, tucked behind the sip of too-hot coffee.

**MICHAEL:**  
"I’m not saying quit, Sarah. I’m saying… one day. One single day off the battlefield."

She stares him down over the rim of her coffee, eyes dark and flat. Then she looks away.

**SARAH:**  
"You don’t understand. Taking a day off is how people lose the thread. I’m holding too many goddamn threads."

**MICHAEL (gently):**  
"And what happens if you drop? You think Island Records is going to fly a flag at half-mast? You think Commuted’s gonna write a sad little ballad? You’re the one holding the threads. That’s the whole point. Take care of your hands."

Sarah opens the fridge. Closes it. Doesn’t take anything out.

**SARAH:**  
"I don’t need metaphors today, MICHAEL. I need caffeine and rage and a tightly managed inbox."

She sets her mug down a little too hard.

**SARAH:**  
"And for the record, this house? These kids? This life? You did your share. You always have. But now I’m up to my neck in fame, lawyers, trauma recovery, social media meltdowns, *and* a possible international incident."

**MICHAEL (quietly):**  
"And I’m just trying to keep you alive through it."

Silence.

**SARAH (muttering):**  
"Yeah. Well. File that under ‘Good luck, champ.’"

**INT. ISLAND RECORDS \- EXECUTIVE CONFERENCE ROOM \- EARLY MORNING**

**SARAH** 

UGH.

She closes her eyes, grips the edge of the seat with one hand, and presses her other palm flat to her sternum—like maybe she can push the nausea back down. Her laptop sits closed on her lap for once. Notifications buzz on her phone. Emails from Legal. From PR. From Bennett King. From Cremeans. From the band. From the label’s Tokyo office, of all places.

She doesn’t touch a single one.

Her mouth waters with that acidic pre-vomit warning. She forces a slow breath through her nose. The train lurches. Her stomach roils.

**SARAH** 

**(in her head)**  
*This is temporary. I’ve had worse. I’ve handled worse.*

She used to like the chaos of the train—the weirdness, the overheard conversations, the oddball energy that made New York, New York. But right now, it’s all noise. Too much noise.

She cracks open one eye. The guy across from her is watching TikToks without headphones. Someone three rows back is eating something with tuna in it. It’s 7:50 a.m. and the whole damn train smells like a cafeteria trash bin in July.

**SARAH** 

**(thinking)**  
*I was going to conquer the world this morning.*

She swallows hard. Closes her eyes again. Rides the wave.

**SARAH** 

**(barely above a whisper)**  
Not like this.

She barely makes it to the door before tossing her bag onto the nearest chair, heel-clicking across the hardwood floor of her office with none of the grace she normally carries. The lights are still dim—early enough that no assistants have dared bother her yet. Good.

She shuts the bathroom door behind her with a slam that echoes through the suite, drops to her knees like a marionette whose strings got cut, and hurls.

It’s violent. Loud. A gut-punch of bile and acid and rage.

The first wave hits the porcelain with a splash. She heaves again, harder, her shoulders shaking. Her palm slaps against the cold tile wall to brace herself.

This isn't from last night’s dinner. This isn't a bug. It’s something deeper. Something wrong.

When it's done—for now—she coughs, wipes her mouth on a paper towel, and flushes like she’s trying to erase the whole moment from memory.

She stares at her reflection in the mirror. Pale. Hollow-eyed. Hair immaculate, because she always gets the hair right. But the rest?

**SARAH** 

(quietly)  
"This is not sustainable."

Her phone vibrates on her desk just outside. She closes her eyes and counts to five.

By six, she’s already washing her hands. By seven, she’s painting on the face.

**The show must go on.**

Sarah leans back in the conference chair, fingers steepled under her chin, staring out at nothing. The muted buzz of the office surrounds her like static. Cremeans is thumbing through messages, drinking a Mountain Dew like it's holy water, oblivious to her slipping inward.

She lets herself slip, just for a second.

**If only Gilley were alive.**

Would he have seen it? The storm coming? Gilley always had a sixth sense for that kind of thing. The calm in the chaos. The bastard could read Avril like sheet music. Knew when she was fraying at the edges. Knew when to push and when to let her unravel.

And he knew Sarah. In ways no one else ever had. No one else had ever sat across from her in bed, telling her the gossip *before* it exploded. No one had ever made her laugh after she’d burned bridges at the label, or made her come so hard she forgot her title. He’d been her spy. Her release valve. Her wildcard.

Now?

**Now it’s just her.**  
And Jeff fucking Cremeans, loyal and tired and scraping on empty.

She takes a deep breath and pinches the bridge of her nose. Her stomach flips, not just nausea this time. Grief. Maybe rage. Maybe both.

She closes her eyes. Just for a moment. Then opens them again.

She’s still here.

Gilley’s not.

And Avril’s out there—*somewhere between a patient and a powder keg.*

Time to stop spiraling.

Time to fight.

**CREMEANS (CONT.)**

**SARAH**

(skeptically)

**CREMEANS** 

Tim’s already up there. “Producing a jazz fusion album and doing sound design for a Norwegian horror film. Guy’s got half a studio built in an old observatory. He’s *hungry*, Sarah. He’s finally hungry again.

Sarah crosses her arms. She's not buying the poetry yet. Not fully.

**CREMEANS** 

(generously)  
And let’s be real—getting the band to focus in LA or New York right now? Suicidal. Paparazzi. Leaks. Lawyers. Tension. Norway gives us distance. Focus. Cheap labor. And no one’s watching.

A beat.

**CREMEANS** **(CONT.)**

Besides, what the heck else is Madeline gonna do in Tromsø? Start a war with Finland?

Sarah lets out a breath she didn’t know she was holding. She hates to admit it, but there’s weird logic to it. Cremeans always plays the long game.

She nods once.

**SARAH**

(tightly)

Fine.  But if this turns into *Tromsø: The Musical*, I’m blaming you.

Cremeans grins, the first real grin she’s seen from him in 24 hours.

**CREMEANS**

Wouldn’t have it any other way.
