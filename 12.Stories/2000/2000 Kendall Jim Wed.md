---


## **INT. GUND ARENA – LUXURY SUITE TURNED WEDDING VENUE – SEPTEMBER 2, 2000 – 11:11 P.M.**

The lights outside are still pulsing from the after-party—stadium emptied, city glowing like an open wound, Cleveland humming with late-night heat.

Inside?

Velvet ropes. Crystal ashtrays overflowing with cigarette butts and lipsticked cocktail straws. Champagne flutes clinking against glass tabletops that cost more than Kendall’s first car.

This isn’t a church gymnasium. This is *Kendall Weatherby’s wedding* to Jim fucking Brown.

And nobody’s behaving.

---

The scene opens mid-mayhem.

[[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|Gilley]] is standing on a catering table, shirtless, drunkenly leading a group chant of “**WE GOT HER! WE GOT HER!**” while pouring champagne directly into Bill’s mouth. Jeff Evans—suit jacket long gone, tie around his forehead like a casualty of love—is trying and failing to light a cigar with a butane torch. [[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|Jeremy]], the designated driver of the apocalypse, is sitting in the corner with two cups of coffee and a look of calm, quiet horror. Kathy? Oh, Kathy is in the thick of it—heels in hand, eyeliner smudged like war paint, dragging [[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill]] into a conga line that nobody agreed to start.

And the bride? Oh, the bride.

Kendall Weatherby—barefoot, veil discarded, her silk gown unzipped halfway down her spine, garter proudly displayed like a trophy—is standing on Jim Brown’s drum riser, microphone in hand, slurring her vows into the PA like she’s testifying in front of God and every woman Jim has ever disappointed.

**[[5.5 Kendall Weatherby|KENDALL]] (into mic, triumphant, tequila-drenched):**  
I told all of you.  I told you.  I don’t do fairytales.  I do fucking *folk legends*.

She raises her glass, and the room howls.

Jim stands below her, tie askew, his face split wide with the dumbest, happiest grin in recorded human history. He’s still got his drumsticks tucked in his belt like weapons.

**[[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|JIM]] (yelling up at her):**  
Are you gonna come down here and kiss your husband or do I have to climb up there and drag you off the goddamn stage?

**KENDALL (grinning like a wolf):**  
Try it, motherfucker. I *dare* you.

And he does. He climbs. In front of everyone. Scales the drum riser like it’s Mount Olympus and she’s the only fire left worth stealing. He grabs her by the waist—hands rough, wedding band glinting—and yanks her into a kiss so filthy it should’ve come with a warning label. The crowd loses its mind. Glass shatters. Someone faints.

And *then*—oh, *then*—they disappear.

## **INT. GUND ARENA – SERVICE ELEVATOR – MINUTES LATER**

The door slams shut. The elevator shudders.

It’s just them now. Her lipstick smeared across his mouth, her dress pushed up around her thighs, his hands everywhere at once—ravenous, reverent.

She’s laughing. That feral, unstoppable laugh.

**KENDALL (panting, between kisses):**  
I married the drummer.

**JIM (growling, voice thick with lust):**  
You married a goddamn disaster.

**KENDALL:**  
Same thing.

They don’t make it to their floor. He drops to his knees—there, in the humming, swaying metal box of sin—and disappears beneath the folds of silk and lace like a man in prayer.

Her hands slam against the wall. She doesn’t scream. She *laughs*. Loud. Glorious. Profane.

**KENDALL (moaning, breathless, shouting into the empty elevator):**  
I WIN.  I FUCKING WIN.

Kathy watches them pass—mouth open, eyes wide, clutching her champagne flute like it’s the last lifeboat on the Titanic.

She turns to Jeff, who’s nursing a bruised ego and an empty whiskey glass.

**[[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|KATHY]] (whispering, shaken):**
