# **🎸 *Rolling Stone, June 2000*

### **“We Were Wrong About *69 Reasons*”**

**By Jayson Corrick, Senior Critic**

---

It’s easy to pretend we always knew. To claim we recognized the storm forming the moment *Commuted* dropped *69 Reasons* in spring of 1992 — back when grunge was king, Bush the Elder was president, and alternative music was busy making angst profitable.

But here’s the truth:  **We didn’t get it.**  We called it “pornographic shock-rock.” We gave it 2.5 stars. We winced at the profanity, furrowed our brows at the liner notes, and muttered something about performance art gone too far.

And while we clutched our Pearl Jam bootlegs and deconstructed Eddie Vedder’s growl, **the world was busy memorizing the words to “Initiating Connection” and “Scientific Method.”**

Eight years later, it's time to say it out loud:

> ***69 Reasons*** is one of the most essential, dangerous, and emotionally articulate albums of the 1990s.   And we flat-out blew it.

---

### 🔥 It Wasn’t About Sex. It Was About Sovereignty.

When Kathy Espich moaned her way through synthwave choruses and screamed across dive-bar mathcore, she wasn’t selling titillation. She was **wrestling back the narrative**.

At a time when women in rock were either ingenues, waifs, or ball-busters, Kathy was something else entirely:  A **siren with teeth**. A **confessor without apology**. A ***person*** who’d lived every lyric and dared us to look away.

> “Come Inside Me” wasn’t crude — it was *a covenant*.  
> “Not Taken Kindly” wasn’t a sex jam — it was *an emotional reckoning*.  
> “The Left Hand” didn’t seduce — it *exorcised*.

---

### 🎧 Genre Wasn’t the Point. **Truth Was.**

In hindsight, the genre-hopping on *69 Reasons* looks prophetic. It foreshadowed everything from St. Vincent’s art-punk sexuality to Mitski’s genre-leaping grief-core. Commuted *blurred sonic lines* with no loyalty to scene or radio format — only to **emotional urgency**.

What we once called “chaotic,” we now call *deep architecture*.

---

### 🏆 From Infamy to Canon

By 1995, it was clear the audience had spoken louder than we had:

- **15 million copies sold**
- **4 international reissues**
- **Banned in 6 countries**
- **Covered by artists from PJ Harvey to Outkast**

And now, in the year 2000, we place *69 Reasons* where it always belonged: **On our Top 10 Albums of the 1990s** list. Right alongside *OK Computer*, *Nevermind*, *The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill*, and *Aquemini*.

Because great music doesn’t ask permission. It **bleeds**, **moans**, **erupts**, and then leaves you different.

---

### 🙏 Our Final Word

To Kathy Espich, wherever you are:  We weren’t ready.  We judged the skin and missed the soul.  We called it filth when it was **fury**.

And to Commuted — thank you for the **album that dared us to look**. We finally are.
