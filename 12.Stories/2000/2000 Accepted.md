# **ACCEPTED**

---

**INT. HOTEL SUITE – PHOENIX, ARIZONA – 2:43 A.M. – SUMMER 2000**

The air conditioner’s been losing a battle against the desert all night. The suite is stuck at 79 degrees, damp with sweat and beer breath, and every window is a painting of neon. There's a room service tray shoved under the writing desk, empty save for two uneaten potato wedges and a heap of blue matchbooks stolen from the lobby bar.

Kathy’s on the floor, one leg up on the leather ottoman, bottle of Campari sweating in her lap. Her hair’s a mess—half-pulled back with a pen that used to belong to the manager of the stadium they just torched. She’s barefoot, tank top clinging to her ribs, eyeliner not even pretending to behave.

She’s already smoked two cigarettes just telling a story about Jeff missing his own soundcheck because he was having a philosophical debate with a harmonics pedal. Gilley’s passed out face-first into a Gideon Bible. Jim’s not here. Bill’s in another room programming a synth loop he swears “sounds like fucking midwestern asphalt.”

Me, lucky fool, are the last one still upright. And for reasons I don’t fully understand—maybe it’s the heat, maybe it’s the fact that I'm the only one who didn’t argue with her onstage tonight—Kathy’s talking.

And drinking.

And maybe a little sentimental.

---

**KENDALL**  
What’s the one album you never talk about but still know by heart?

**KATHY** (snorts, sips, doesn’t even pretend to think)  
*Tusk.*

**KENDALL**  
Fleetwood Mac?

**KATHY** (grinning like she caught you underestimating her)  
Yeah, that one. The one with the marching band. The one that tanked after *Rumours.* The weird, feral little sibling. The one that bit back.

She stretches her leg and winces, bruises from tonight’s stage dive starting to bloom. Then she leans forward, serious now, elbows on knees, voice going low.

**KATHY**  
Everyone thinks *Rumours* is the breakup album, right? The divorce drama. The soap opera with perfect harmonies. But *Tusk*? *Tusk* is what happens after the funeral. That’s the album where nobody’s pretending anymore. Nobody’s trying to make it pretty. It’s just rot and jealousy and obsession and cocaine and—God, the *boredom*. The boredom of being stuck in your own legend.

**KENDALL**  
You sound like you love it.

**KATHY**  
I do. I fucking love it. I love how petty it is. How *Lindsey Buckingham* decided to burn down their mansion with a four-track recorder and a snarl. I love that Stevie’s voice is *better* when she’s backed into a corner. That Christine McVie sounds like she’s flirting with cyanide. I love that they all sound like they hate each other and still wanted to make *something* worth keeping.

She grabs the bottle again, takes a long sip. Her eyes don’t leave yours.

**KATHY** (softer now)  
That’s what being in a band *really* is. Not the applause. Not the interviews. It’s putting your heart in someone else’s song and hoping they don’t use it as a kick drum.

**KENDALL**  
So it’s therapy?

**KATHY** (shaking her head, laughing dry)  
No. *Rumours* was therapy. *Tusk* is the relapse.

She fishes a cigarette from the bedside ashtray—already half-smoked, ash smudged at the tip—and lights it with the kind of practiced indifference that means she quit trying to quit. She lies back on the carpet, stares at the stucco ceiling like it might blink.

**KATHY**  
There’s this one track—"[Beautiful Child](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YulbXpakoTI)." Stevie sings it like she’s remembering someone who broke her but she still wants him to call. Like if he showed up right now, she’d throw away every boundary she ever bled to build. And she *knows it.* She knows she’d do it again. That’s the part that fucks me up. That she doesn’t even lie to herself.

**KENDALL**
You’ve played that one on piano. In soundcheck, once.

**KATHY**  
Yeah. Once. Never again. Not unless I want to cry on a Steinway in front of a thousand strangers.

**KENDALL**
You’re allowed to cry, you know.

**KATHY** (eyes sharp now)  
Not like that.

Beat.

She puts the cigarette out against the Campari bottle, smearing the label. She’s starting to drift now, the heat and alcohol and ghosts all piling into her bloodstream. But she sits up one more time, propped on her elbow, deadly sincere.

**KATHY**  
You want to know why I love *Tusk*?

**KENDALL**
Yes.

**KATHY**  
Because it’s an album that *refused to heal.* It didn’t resolve. It didn’t explain itself. It got raw, got weird, lost money, and *still mattered*. It’s not clean. It’s not polite. It’s not done.

She exhales.

**KATHY** (whispered, barely)  
Neither am I.

---

I say nothing. She leans back again, head tilted, breathing evening out.

And there, in a too-hot Phoenix hotel room, with a stolen pen in her hair and old synth wax on her wrist, Kathy Espich falls asleep.

Dreaming, probably, of marching bands. Or knives.
