**INT. SERVICE HALLWAY – M&T STADIUM – LATE NIGHT**

The concert’s over. The gear’s packed. Baltimore’s asleep.

And here I am, Kendall Weatherby, lurking in the fluorescent guts of the stadium, tracing the last footsteps of ghosts. I’ve still got my red nail polish chipped from that night. Still got my tape recorder. And still, in my lungs, the static-charged air of *that* show.

I found him exactly where I knew he’d be.

**EMILIO**—lean, quiet, mid-40s maybe. His hands are veined and oil-stained, like he’s been working on muscle cars in a noir film. He wears a faded Pere Ubu shirt under a grease-blackened flannel. No stage name. No flash. Just **Emilio**.

He’s coiling cables with a Zen calm, humming some fucked-up version of “*Speeding*.”

I clear my throat.

**KENDALL**  
Late-night Zen session, Emilio? You almost became [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff Evans]] tonight.

He doesn’t look up, just tugs the coil tighter.

**EMILIO**  
Depends who’s asking.

**KENDALL**  
You almost replaced Jeff on bass tonight.

That earns me a half-smile, sharp and sly.

**EMILIO**  
Esa no fue mi decision.

**KENDALL**  
No, but it almost *was.* Bill was ready to throw you onstage like a Molotov cocktail.

He laughs, a low, rasping thing.

**EMILIO**  
Bill’s always been a fan of the nuclear option.

He tugs one more loop, then finally stands, wiping his hands on a towel.

**KENDALL** (grinning lightly):
You’d have to update your résumé. From ‘gear engineer and equipment savior’ to ‘bassist, emergency disaster response’.

**EMILIO** (deadpan, almost amused):
I’m not interested. Bass players have to answer questions from obsessive archivists who think editing reality is part of their job description.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
But, you really want to know how I got here?

**KENDALL**  
Obviously.

He leans against a road case, eyes gleaming under the buzzing lights.

**EMILIO**  
It’s boring. You sure?

**KENDALL**  
Boring doesn’t scare me. I’ve slept with Jim Brown.

He snorts—full-on bark of laughter.

**EMILIO**  
Fair enough.

Beat.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
’94. I was living in Cleveland. Working at a shop called *Tone Bastard*. Total dive. We fixed pedals, rewired guitars, and re-coned speakers. Mostly we dealt with drunk garage rockers who thought feedback was a personality trait.

One day, this kid wanders in—scrawny, pissed-off, reeking of booze. It’s Jeremy Evans.

**KENDALL**  
Of course it is.

**EMILIO**  
He’s holding this Frankenstein of a Strat—looked like it had been dropped off a building. Says, “Can you make this fucker scream again?” Dije seguro. Took me four hours. Didn’t charge him full rate.

Week later, he’s back. Brings Jeff with him. Jeff doesn’t say shit, just stares at me like I’m a fucking matemáticas problem.

Turns out Jeff liked the tone. Said it was “structurally elegant.” They hired me to tour with ’em for the fall leg.

**KENDALL**  
Just like that?

**EMILIO**  
Just like that. I could tune a guitar in the dark, I didn’t steal gear, and I didn’t talk too much. That was enough.

He shrugs, easy.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
Been with ’em ever since. I’m not family. I’m lastre. I keep the ship from tipping.

**KENDALL**  
You know every bass line by heart.

**EMILIO**  
Occupational hazard. I don’t like surprises.

Beat.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
But I wasn’t gonna take Jeff’s spot. That’s a sacred seat, no matter how fucked-up he gets.

**KENDALL**  
Even if it meant saving the show?

He holds my gaze, steady.

**EMILIO**  
I’d rather the ship sink than sail with the wrong captain.

Silence.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
Besides. Jeff came back. He always does.

He flashes me a grin—crooked, tired, true.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
That’s Commuted. You can’t replace a ghost while it’s still haunting the room.

He shoulders his gear and walks away, humming that weird, mangled Beatles melody again. I don’t stop him.  Some myths deserve to stay unfinished.

**INT. SERVICE HALLWAY – M&T STADIUM – LATER**

He’s about to slip away. But my curiosity itches harder than my professionalism.

**KENDALL**  
Still out there making '*Jessie’s Girl*' palatable for wedding parties?

He stops, mid-step. Turns slowly. No offense taken. No ego either.

**EMILIO**  
I do.

(Shrugs like it’s nothing.)

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
I front a cover band. Weekend gigs. Weddings. Some bar nights.

I blinked, startled. He says it without shame, without apology.

**KENDALL**  
Wait, *seriously?* You—with that ear, that precision—you’re out there playing… what? “*Jessie’s Girl*”?

**EMILIO**  
And “*Crazy Train*.” And “*Man in the Box*.” Whatever pays.

He smiles calmly and utterly unbothered by the contrast.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
I’m not inventive. Never claimed to be. I’m not here to rewrite the rules. My brain’s wired for replication. I can memorize anything you put in front of me. Play it clean. Perfect pitch. Always could.

He leans in just a little, voice dropping, almost conspiratorial.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
Plus, I build things. Pedals. Boards. Custom wiring rigs. You want your amp to sound like it’s bleeding out from a gutshot slowly? I can make that happen.

I laugh, but there’s a knot twisting under it now.

**KENDALL**  
You could’ve been an engineer.

**EMILIO**  
I *am* an engineer. I just wear denim and drink Shiner Bock at load-ins.

That grin again. The calmness of someone who knows exactly what niche they fit.  And then—he tilts his head, studies me the way a snake might study a flickering match.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
But that’s not what scares you about me.

My breath catches—he *sees* it.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
You’re scared because I listen to Jeff. Really *listen*. Every word. Every mutter. Every half-assed lyric he throws away in soundcheck.

(He taps his temple, slow.)

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
And I remember them.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
I know what he means even when he doesn’t. I’ve been inside his phrasing long enough, I can predict where he’s gonna bend a note before he even thinks about it.

He doesn’t smile. He doesn’t gloat. He just states it as fact.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
That’s what you’re afraid of, Kendall.

He steps back, hoists his gear again, and this time I let him go.  I watch him disappear down the hall, back straight, steps steady. Not a rock star. Not a genius. Something worse.
A man with no delusions about who he is, or what he knows. Someone who could burn this whole fucking mythology to the ground, if he ever felt like talking.

**INT. SAME SERVICE HALLWAY – M&T STADIUM – MOMENTS LATER**

I can’t let it end there. I call after him.

**KENDALL**  
You think that scares me?

He stops, glancing over his shoulder. No smile this time—just calm curiosity.

**KENDALL (CONT’D)**  
You’re right, by the way. It *does* scare me. You’re the only person around here who actually understands Jeff without needing an interpreter. How the hell do you even process that?

He turns fully now, walking back toward me, the hum of distant generators thick between us.

**EMILIO**  
Simple.

(He lifts one hand, taps his temple again.)

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
It’s turtles.

Beat.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
All the way down.

He *cracks up*, sudden and loud, the laugh of a man who knows every bad cosmic joke and still thinks it’s funny every damn time. The kind of laugh that makes the air shimmer around it.

I can’t help it. I laugh too—sharp, high, half-horrified.

**KENDALL**  
Christ almighty, you’re worse than him.

He wipes a tear from his eye, still chuckling.

**EMILIO**  
You ever tried explaining empathy to a man who calibrates his relationships by *tuning fork*?

I snort, helpless now.

**KENDALL**  
Jeff Evans—Bass Guitar: Human Monolith of Emotional Detachment.

**EMILIO**  
He’s not detached. He’s just tuned to a frequency nobody else can pick up.

(He pauses, tilts his head, thoughtful now.)

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
You don’t *feel* Jeff. You *decode* him. Like a telegraph operator.

**KENDALL**  
That’s bleak.

**EMILIO**  
Nah. It’s clean.

He leans back against a pillar, suddenly dead-serious but still gentle about it.

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
Jeff isn’t cruel. He’s not out to hurt anybody. He just… doesn’t make room for the noise.

(Beat.)

**EMILIO (CONT’D)**  
You either accept it, or you spend your whole life waiting for him to give a fuck about everything he physically can’t process.

I swallow hard. That hits too close.

**KENDALL**  
And you? You just… accept that?

His face softens, almost kind.

**EMILIO**  
I accept my job. I tune his guitars. I play the notes. I protect the gear.

(Shrugs, simple as gravity.)

**EMILIO**  *(calmly, tapping his temple)*  
It’s turtles, Kendall.

I groan softly, eyes rolling upward—recognition and exasperation mingling perfectly.

**KENDALL**  
Please tell me you’re not quoting Jeff right now. He still hasn’t figured that one out.

**EMILIO**  *(chuckles, warmly amused)*  
"Nope, he’s convinced that’s a legit religious teaching. Something about turtles carrying Earth through space”

**KENDALL**  *(cutting in, mock horror)*  
"And under each turtle is another turtle, eternally, infinitely—Jeff’s personal brand of cosmic wisdom."

He nods slowly, knowingly, his smile quietly affectionate.

**EMILIO**  
"Exactly. All the way down."

My laughter bubbles up unexpectedly, involuntarily warm and genuine, and surprising me a little because it feels good. Comfortable. Familiar.

**KENDALL**  *(through laughter, shaking her head)*  
"Fucking turtles."

Emilio smiles again, gentle in the fluorescent hum, completely unbothered by our strange, shared absurdities. When he finally shoulders his gear, disappearing back into the shadows of the stadium, I’m left smiling faintly to myself, this little inside joke echoing warmly in my chest.

He’s right. Of course he is. It’s always been turtles. And maybe, strangely enough, that’s exactly why it works, why we’ve both stuck around, quietly doing our jobs, collecting absurdities, keeping these weird myths alive just a little bit longe
