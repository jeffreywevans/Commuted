# SCENE: 'Missing Time' – Kathy Espich (Denver Hotel, 1998)

**INT. DENVER HOTEL ROOM – LATE AFTERNOON**

The room is still, but not peaceful—quiet in the way a chapel feels after a funeral. KATHY ESPICH lies on the floor, half-covered by a stranger’s T-shirt. One sock. A dried smear of blood on her arm. The curtains are drawn. Light slices through the slats like knives.

She wakes slowly. Not startled—just there. Consciousness drips back in like a leaky faucet.

Kathy doesn’t sit up. Just stares at the ceiling. Counts her fingers out loud.

**KATHY** (whispers)  
One. Two. Three. Four. Five.

**VOICEOVER – KATHY (V.O.)**

I got what I wanted. I got the silence. No voices. No [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]. No stage. No fucking guitar chords banging around my head like iron pipes. Just space. It was perfect. Like falling into warm velvet and not having to do anything but exist. No proof. No fight. I was… me. But without the volume. I don’t remember coming back to the room. I don’t remember who turned off the lights. Maybe I did. Maybe I didn’t. And I don’t care.

(laughs softly) 
That’s the terrifying part. I’m supposed to care. But I don’t.  Twenty-five hours. Gone. But I wasn’t screaming. I wasn’t writing lyrics on the back of receipts.  I wasn’t trying to make anyone else feel something. I was just quiet.

(pause) 
God, I’ve never loved anything more.

Kathy finally sits up. Looks at herself in the mirror. Her face is wrecked, but soft. Like someone who finally slept. She walks to the window. Pulls the curtain back. The city stares back, indifferent. She doesn’t mind. Not today.
