# 🎙️ ET LIVE BACKSTAGE AT THE GRAMMYS – Feb 2014

**Host (Jessica Lin):**  
We’re here with Commuted, surprise winners of Album of the Year and Song of the Year for their haunting and explosive album *Be First Be Smarter or Cheat*. First off—congratulations, you guys!

Dead silence. Gilley is holding a slice of pizza he may have just stolen. Jeff is wearing sunglasses indoors. Jeremy has a Bloody Mary in hand. Jim is seated like a statue. Bill is texting someone, likely a therapist.

**Jessica:**  
You didn’t collect your awards in person—was that a statement?

**Jeff:**  
Yeah. The statement was: don’t hand us trophies for a death.

**Jessica:**  
Okay… well. John Legend and Avril Lavigne gave beautiful speeches in your place—

**Jeremy:**  
We told ‘em to wing it. They did great. No one's dead in their bands.

**Jessica** (shaken, trying):  
“*Snowglobe*” has really resonated with fans. Do you remember where you were when you wrote it?

**Gilley** (muttering):  
In a room where she hadn’t died yet.

**Jessica**:  
What would Kathy think of this moment?

**Jim**:  
She’d think awards are stupid and this interview is hell.

**Jessica**:  
Harsh.

**Jim**:  
Real.

**Jessica**:  
And Jeremy, you were seen with Gwyneth Paltrow last night, what happened there?

**Jeremy**:  
I asked her if she knew where my other shoe was. She said no. We kissed. I think.

**Bill** (finally looking up):  
Stop asking about Gwyneth. Ask us why we keep writing songs when it hurts.

**Jessica**:  
Okay… why?

**Bill**:  
Because the only thing worse than pain is silence.

**Jessica**:  
There was an altercation backstage. Something involving a phone?

All five men fall silent.

**Jessica:**  
Area code 859?

Jim stands and walks off. Jeff lights a cigarette indoors. No one stops him. Jeremy whispers: “Jesus.” Bill mutters: “You don’t bring that up.”  Gilley just smiles. And does not stop smiling.

**Jessica** (voice-over, cutting back to studio):  
That was Commuted. Winners. Survivors. Barely.

# Rolling Stone | March 2025

*“Five Men, Twp Grammys, One Near-Death Experience:  My 2014 Interview With Commuted”*

By Jessica Lin

I’ve interviewed war criminals.  I’ve been vomited on by a Jonas brother.  I once had to keep a straight face while Chris Martin explained Coldplay’s "spiritual geometry."  But nothing—nothing—comes close to the six minutes I spent backstage with Commuted after the 2014 Grammy Awards.

They’d just won Album of the Year and Song of the Year—and they were so furious, drunk, fractured, and utterly radioactive that the crew whispered, “maybe we shouldn’t air this.”

We aired it.  Why?  Because we’re masochists.  Because we didn’t know what else to do.  Because honestly? I thought they might kill me if I tried to leave.

**<u>Jeff Evans: Warpath in Waiting</u>**

Jeff never spoke. He sat there punching his own palm, rhythmically. Hard. Over and over.  He stared at me the way some people stare at prison walls.  Like if I said the wrong thing, he would give me a black eye without blinking.  I asked him a question about “*Snowglobe*.” He didn’t answer.  He just exhaled through his nose like a bull and tightened his jaw.  I could feel the air in the room go thinner when he moved.

<u>Gilley: Unhinged Libido With a Pulse</u>

Then there was [[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|Gilley]]. If Jeff wanted to kill me, Gilley wanted to mentally devour me.  
The way he stared—I felt like clothing was a suggestion he was erasing in real-time.
He ate pizza during the interview.  He never looked away.  He never blinked. One of the camera crew said afterward, “I think he made eye contact with my soul.”

[[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|Jeremy Evans]] was drunk. [[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill Davenport]] was dead behind the eyes. [[4.4 JIM BROWN character bible|Jim Brown]] was made of war.  Jeremy talked in circles about Gwyneth Paltrow and toaster ovens.  Bill wouldn’t speak unless it was to shut Jeremy up.  And Jim? Jim looked like he was preparing for a Gitmo military tribunal.

The moment I said “859,” he stood up and left.  Jeff lit a cigarette indoors.  Gilley grinned wider.  Bill whispered, “You shouldn’t have said that.”

I’ve covered thirty Grammy nights. That was the only one where I walked away thinking: If someone had handed Jeff Evans a gun, he would’ve used it.  On himself, or me, or the room—I still don’t know.

I never requested another Commuted interview.  I don’t regret it.

They were geniuses.  They were grieving.  They were loaded weapons in suits.  And I was just another warm body in their warpath.
