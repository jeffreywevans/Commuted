## **INT. STAPLES CENTER - BACKSTAGE - NIGHT**

**GILLEY** (Grinning wide, teeth flashing)

**GILLEY** (CONT’D):

**GILLEY** (CONT’D):

## **INT. STAPLES CENTER - BACKSTAGE - LATER**

**JIM** stands with his back to **BILL**, the weight of his words hanging heavy in the air. **BILL** stares at Jim's rigid back, the realization dawning on him: the tectonic plates of the band’s internal structure have shifted. Drastically.

Traditionally, **COMMUTED** had functioned with a well-defined division of labor, a collaborative effort where everyone played their part. There was a delicate balance, a system of checks and balances that kept the whole chaotic machine running. But now…

**BILL** thinks of Kathy, gone, dead and buried. He thinks of the other people, the road crew, the management, the support system that had buffered them from the relentless pressure of the media, the fans, the industry. Half of them were gone, casualties of the road, of life, of bad luck. And the other half… well, they were standing right here, teetering on the brink of total collapse.

**BILL** feels a wave of nausea wash over him. The sheer impossibility of the situation hits him full force. He looks at Jim, at his broad, unyielding back, and a desperate question escapes his lips.

**BILL** (Quietly)
Jim… what do we do now?

Jim doesn’t turn. He remains with his back to Bill, his posture unchanging, as if carved from stone. The silence stretches, thick and suffocating.

Suddenly, the silence is shattered by the sound of shattering glass. **JEREMY** slams his empty beer mug down on a nearby road case, the glass exploding into shards. He lets out a triumphant yell.

**JEREMY**
Seconds!

Before either Jim or Bill can react, Jeremy grabs his second beer mug, barely pausing for breath, and proceeds to chug it down in a matter of seconds, the liquid disappearing down his throat like water down a drain.

Jim and Bill watch, stupefied, as Jeremy finishes the second beer, slams the now-empty mug down beside the broken one, and lets out another, even louder yell.

**JEREMY**

He reaches for a third beer, his eyes wild and unfocused. Jim finally turns from Bill, his cold gaze now fixed on Jeremy. The look on his face is a mixture of disgust, frustration, and a deep, weary resignation.
