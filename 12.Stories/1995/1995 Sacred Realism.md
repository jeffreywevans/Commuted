**Author Style Template: "Sacred Realism" | Required Framing**

---

**WRITING STYLE:**

- Highly emotional, sensory, and sacred.
    
- Full-body experience: sight, smell, touch, sound, taste described when meaningful.
    
- Sexual content is raw, explicitly physical yet emotionally grounded — messy, honest, personal, never cartoonish or pornographic.
    
- Humor used sparingly but powerfully — dry, self-aware, and human in tone.
    
- No omniscient narrative voice — we stay in the moment.
    
- Dialogue formatting mimics hybrid screenplay-prose style:
    
- Character name in ALL CAPS, bold.
    
- Dialogue flows naturally — broken by short action beats (parentheticals optional for tone adjustment).
    
- Beats between dialogue show action or emotional state without interrupting momentum.

---

**FORMATTING:**

- Use screenplay-like headers to clearly define scene location and time (e.g., "INT. HOTEL ROOM – TAHITI – 8:45 P.M.")
    
- Italics used for inner thoughts, emotional reactions, or emphasized sensory notes.
    
- Bolding may be used sparingly to highlight deeply important emotional words.
    
- White space is sacred: short paragraphs, clean dialogue lines, no blocks of heavy text.

---

**SEXUALITY TONE:**

- Sex is not mechanical.
    
- Sex is sacred, soulful, messy, powerful, fun, sometimes awkward, but always loving.
    
- Bodily Sacred Memory Activation is critical: scenes should activate real, physical memory or imagination in the reader.
    
- No censorship of messy physical realities: cum, wetness, smell, stickiness, trembling, dazed aftermaths — all must be included when they deepen realism.
    
- Consent and enthusiasm are assumed as a sacred bond between Jeff and Jen unless otherwise specified.

---

**DIALOGUE CADENCE:**

- Midwestern, casual but emotionally honest.

---

**ATMOSPHERE:**

- Light is important. Windows, ocean light, sunrise, Tokyo neon — all set an emotional tone.
    
- Sounds should be included: street noise, waves, the crack of a bat in a stadium.
    
- The environment should always echo or contrast with the emotional state of the characters.
    
- Time matters: slow moments must be stretched luxuriously; fast moments must hit like hammers.

---

**END STRUCTURE:**

- Scenes end with emotional punctuation:
    
- A touch.
    
- A laugh.
    
- A prayer.
    
- A stare.
    
- A physical or verbal echo that "seals" the scene.
