# **📖 Animage (December 1995)**

## **Hidden Import Review –Sweet Memories: The Dive Bar Sessions**  
By Aki Nishimura

---
## Rating: 3.5/5 Stars  
## 🌀 Visuals: Bleeding dream logic  
## 🎸 Music: Dangerous and sexual  
## 🍷 Plot: Like losing your virginity inside a haunted wine bottle

---

There are rumors in Tokyo’s underground video trade: a mysterious VHS imported from America, animated under pseudonyms, wrapped in scandal and scored by a band that reportedly got banned from three separate television programs for being “unmanageable.”

That VHS is Sweet Memories: The Dive Bar Sessions—a crude, psychedelic, aggressively Southern lesbian bar-fight opera based on a concept album by a Dayton, Ohio band called Commuted.

It is not for everyone.  But if your idea of cinema involves frogs performing metaphysical piercings, a bisexual bachelorette party collapsing into divine vengeance, and Jerry Seinfeld as a God-entity in a haunted lounge suit—you may be the target demographic.

---

## ✨ Style & Influence

Imagine Two Moon Junction got thrown into a blender with Kuttsukiboshi, then struck by lightning and edited by someone who hates coherent transitions but loves horny chaos.

The animation veers wildly from elegant still frames soaked in candlelight to violent, sketchy orgies with inconsistent frame rates. A scene where a succubus is defeated mid-thrust by a wine-fueled gender transformation plays like Belladonna of Sadness rewritten by a drunk theater major.

---

## 💫 Plot (allegedly)

There is a character named Anna, who may or may not be a bride, and may or may not be any gender depending on which wine bottle she drinks from. She is surrounded by chaotic friends: Sherilyn (an unhinged comedy goddess), Dani (a war-goddess of queer wrath), and Leanne (the soft-spoken oracle of alcohol).

They meet:

- A succubus
- Carl Sagan
- And eventually, Kermit the Frog, who performs a supernatural clitoral piercing that causes an orgasm strong enough to rupture reality.

This is not a joke. This is a major plot point.

---

## 🧠 Themes

The film is about:

- Gender
- Power
- Sexuality as salvation
- And how trauma can be repurposed into joy if you’re brave enough to scream about it with eyeliner on

Also, there are codpieces. Mandated by law.

---

## 🔊 Music

The soundtrack is raw and seductive—half garage gospel, half softcore apocalypse. The original Commuted tracks are intact, often playing over dialogue-free sex montages that manage to feel more sacramental than pornographic.

The standout track?

“Wanna Tell Me About Your Big Ideas, Baby?” — a whispered ballad sung over a montage of furious orgasms and legislative reform.

---

## 🌀 Final Thoughts

This is an anime nobody asked for, based on an album no one in Japan ever heard, animated in what appears to be an abandoned ramen shop. And somehow?  It is staggeringly intimate.  If you can find a copy—and if your soul can handle it—*Sweet Memories: The Dive Bar Sessions* will leave you changed. Confused, yes. But seen.
