# **THE ANIME PROJECT**

---

## **🎙️ Scene: The Conference Room at Over-the-Rhine Studio**

Commuted meets Pedro and “Tampa Bay” Tammy Jonze
Late Spring 1995 – Cincinnati
Interior – Music studio conference room – dusk, hot as hell

---

The cicadas outside are loud enough to be sampled. A low hum of amps bleeds through the studio wall like an anxious heartbeat. There’s a whirring box fan in the corner doing absolutely nothing but stirring the smell of takeout and stale coffee. The band’s “conference table” is really just a hollow-core door laid flat across two milk crates—wobbling with every shift in weight. Around it: stained legal pads, a broken metronome, a few cigarette burns that no one will admit to, and a half-finished bottle of malt liquor.

The blinds are stuck halfway open, slanting the dusk light into militant bars across Jeff’s foot pedal case. Everything feels like it hasn’t been dusted since Commuted started printing money. The air conditioner wheezed its last breath sometime in late April—just in time for the early cicadas to scream like a prophecy nobody asked for.

This is the kind of heat where tempers stick. Where ideas ferment. Where bands either break up—or make history.

It’s the kind of heat that seeps into your fingers, makes the guitar neck slick, warps the tape deck, and shortens everyone’s patience by exactly one snare hit. The kind that keeps musicians inside long past midnight because stepping outside means losing momentum to the weight of the air. Every breath tastes faintly like solder and coffee. Everyone is tired, a little hungry, and about four seconds from throwing something.

This isn’t creative energy—it’s pressure. And in the middle of it sits a song they’ve all heard too many times, echoing between bodies and past lives, waiting for someone to say yes or goodbye.

🎵 CHARACTER FOCUS: KATHY ESPICH

**KATHY ESPICH** (lead vocals, nerve center of the band, doesn't suffer fools) sits at the head of the table, arms folded with militant precision. She has a pencil behind her ear like a conductor waiting for her downbeat, and a look on her face like someone just mispronounced Debussy in her presence—and then asked if he was on tour.

Her eyes scan the room like she’s sight-reading sheet music she wrote in blood. There’s no coffee, no fanfare, no preamble. Just presence. Her heels are braced against a fallen lyric sheet that she hasn’t picked up yet. She’s not unaware of it. She just hasn’t decided if it deserves her time.

Kathy’s body language doesn’t beg for attention—it preempts it. There’s a kind of electric containment in her posture, like she’s compressing a storm into silence. She’s dressed in an old Commuted tour tee that fits too well to be casual and jeans that haven’t ripped—they’ve evolved.

Anyone who doesn’t know her would think she’s just waiting for a meeting to start.  Anyone who does knows she’s already started it.

**JEFF EVANS** (bassist, human fortress of emotional repression) is quietly noodling on a fretless five-string, not plugged in. His fingers move with absentminded precision, like the bass is the only part of the room he still trusts. He’s mouthing lyrics from *Sweet Memories in a Dive Bar* like he's reliving a night he never forgave Kathy for—but won't talk about. A slow, almost imperceptible shake of the head punctuates one phrase—maybe disbelief, maybe regret.  There's a slight tension in his jaw, like he's holding in the next thing he doesn't want to admit.

There’s a stack of unused lyrics next to him, corners frayed from overhandling, but he hasn’t touched them in hours. The top page has a bassline scrawled across it that doesn't quite resolve—a ghost of an idea that’s been haunting him for months. Instead, he keeps replaying that song like it’s a lock he almost knows how to pick. The melody lives somewhere in his spine now, not his head—too ingrained to forget, too raw to play aloud.

**CREMEANS** (band manager, longtime chaos wrangler, soul made of denim) slouches across from Kathy, feet propped on a second chair, notepad in hand. He hasn’t blinked in five minutes. His pen rests idle between his fingers, tapping once against the paper with a rhythm only he understands. His eyes stay on Kathy—not unkind, not amused, just… taking measure. Like he’s watching a fuse burn slowly toward something spectacular or irreversible.

One sneaker rocks idly against the table leg, keeping time with the silence in the room. The notepad in front of him has one word underlined three times: “story.” The edges of the page are slightly dog-eared, as if it’s been torn out and put back more than once. A half-doodled treble clef hovers beside the underlined word, unconsciously inked. The tip of his pen taps the paper again, this time slower—more of a heartbeat than a beat.

Cremeans shifts slightly, the kind of subtle repositioning that says he’s been still too long but refuses to break his composure. He glances once at the vending machine’s hum in the distance, then back at Kathy. A breath catches in his throat—small, unnoticed. Whatever this meeting becomes, he’s already decided: he’ll ride it to the end. Not because it’s easy. Because it’s *theirs.*

And he just wants to see how it starts.

The door creaks open.

Enter **PEDRO JONZE** – wearing black-on-black linen with sandals somehow. The outfit walks the line between monastic and maniacal. He smells like oud, sea salt, and ego. Dramatically scented. Glowing with that untouchable “we just left Tokyo and refused to sleep” energy, he seems to have arrived mid-sentence of an internal monologue only he can hear. His wrists are stacked with festival wristbands, his sunglasses perched high on his head like a crown. His entire vibe suggests he owns both incense and stock options.

He pauses in the doorway for exactly two seconds, eyes scanning the room like he's already casting the anime in his mind. Behind him:

**TAMMY “*TAMPA BAY*” JONZE*** – chewing gum like a champion, rocking a leather vest over a Guns N’ Roses tank that’s seen too many laundromats and not enough hangers. Her sunglasses stay on indoors like they’re part of her soul’s security clearance. She scans the room with a grin that says *I’ve caused trouble in three time zones and still made soundcheck.* Her bangles clack when she moves, like the warning rattle of a rattlesnake that also works merch tables. Dangerously friendly, like someone who’ll hug you just before starting a bar fight on your behalf—and then bill you for it.

**PEDRO** (arms wide like Moses)
Ahh… Commuted. The prophets of poetic decay. We come bearing vision.

**TAMMY** (grinning, casual)
Hey, y’all. I’m Tammy. This is Pedro. We’re your number-one fans and your worst-case scenario. We’ve driven overnight with nothing but gas station coffee and a cursed storyboard to bring you this moment. And we’re here to pitch art so weird it’ll make you cry, your egos question themselves, and your past selves wish they’d studied animation. Buckle up.

**CREMEANS** (flatly)
You had me at worst-case scenario.

**KATHY** (sitting up, sharply)
Kathy Espich. I sing. I used to play guitar, but these days I’m on piano. Don't get it twisted.

**PEDRO** 
Noble! Evolved! Piano—the language of ghosts and agony. Excellent.

**TAMMY**
You’re gonna be the emotional anchor of the whole show. Real slow-motion crying in front of a mirror kind of shit. The kind of moment that lives in the silence between chords. You’re not just the heart—you’re the fracture. You hold the ache that gives everything else weight. And when it breaks? That’s when the audience believes.

**JEFF** (blinking)
What… show?

**TAMMY**
It’s anime, sweetheart. Forty-minute OVA. Magical realism. Gender fluidity. Strip clubs. Kermit the Frog. Think *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure* meets *Macross*, but if Rick was a frog medic from Vietnam.

**KATHY** (staring)
What. The fuck.

**CREMEANS** (still calm)
I’m voicing God.

**PEDRO** (leaning forward, intense)
Yes. You were born for it. God with a Zippo and roadie hands. We saw it immediately. Not just in your voice, but in how you sit back during a storm and let others wear themselves out first. You have the posture of someone who’s seen the soundcheck, the headliner, and the teardown—and still believes the encore might be worth it. You’re not just playing the voice of God. You’ve already lived it backstage. And the mic’s finally hot.

**KATHY**
And you want to base this thing on *Sweet Memories in a Dive Bar*?

**TAMMY**
Not based, no. Reborn through it. We… took some liberties.

**JEFF** (quietly, eyes darting to the door, then to Kathy)
Oh no. No no no.

Beat. The room hangs in confused silence, only the fan wheezing behind them. Pedro grins faintly, pleased with the discord. Tammy’s gum snaps once—like punctuation. Cremeans taps his pen once against the pad, eyes narrowing. Jeff exhales through his nose and adjusts his grip on the bass. Kathy’s still. Her jaw clenches, unclenches. It’s the kind of pause that might end in violence or brilliance—no one can tell yet. The air shifts like it knows what’s coming before anyone speaks.

**CREMEANS**
Go on. We’re listening.

**CREMEANS** (sitting up, finally interested)
Alright. Make your pitch.

**KATHY** (interjecting, eyes sharp)
Before you do—just so we’re clear…Have you two figured out this song was based on a true story?  Not some abstract tone poem. Not a dream journal.  A real fucking thing.  It happened after we got discovered… and before we got signed.  

Pause.

Pedro and Tammy blink.

**TAMMY** (tilting her head, interested now)
…You mean like, actual events?

**KATHY** (stern)
Yes. Like “someone didn’t make it home that night” kind of events. The kind where you wake up the next morning with a bruise you don’t remember earning and lyrics you don’t remember writing. The kind that never gets brought up in interviews, but still hums beneath every chorus. A moment suspended in amber—ugly, sacred, and impossible to forget.

**JEFF** (low voice)
Like it still hurts to sing it.

**PEDRO** (quiet for a beat, then reverent)
…Perfect.

**TAMMY** (grinning)
Even better. The truth always makes it messier.

The band watches them. Silence. The air tightens like a snare head before a downbeat.

**CREMEANS** (cold but curious)
Let’s hear it.

**PEDRO** (standing up, arms wide)

Picture this—

# **🚀 THE JONZES’ ANIME PITCH: Sweet Memories in a Dive Bar: A Magical Realism Odyssey**

---

**PEDRO** (breathless, pacing now)
The dive bar is not merely a bar, Kathy—it functions as an ontological fault line. A locus of existential recursion. A liminal site where temporality distorts itself into recursive nonlinearity, like a cassette tape that has endured one too many replays across decades of unresolved memory. Regret becomes both medium and transaction—an emotional currency exchanged not to absolve, but to relive.

**TAMMY** (grinning)
Our protagonist? Anna. Former bride-to-be, now chaos engine. Gets cursed by an ancient bottle of Canadian wine at her bachelorette party in New Orleans. Gains a dick. Gains power. Loses shame. Gains purpose. The curse doesn’t break her. It shows her she was never whole.

Anna becomes the lens through which we explore not just gender, but the architecture of expectation. She doesn’t rebel to escape. She isn’t a caricature of freedom—she’s the cost of it. Her journey isn’t clean. She fumbles. She lashes out. She apologizes, and sometimes she doesn’t. But every moment propels her away from the bride she was told to be and toward the woman she defines on her own terms.  By the time the bottle empties, Anna isn’t reassembled—she’s rewritten.

**PEDRO**
The wine is from Grow A Pair Winery™, Ontario. Magical terroir. A vintage with gender consequences. Anna spends the night learning things about herself that most people take lifetimes to bury.

**TAMMY**
She drinks the wine. She grows the dick. She takes a man into a sex-coaching Honda Civic that talks through the radio.

**KATHY** (stunned)
…That’s in the pitch?

**PEDRO**
The car plays Barry White. It also has windshield wipers that shoo away perverts.

**TAMMY**
The sex is ridiculous—but revelatory. The kind that breaks through your ego, your labels, your assigned destiny. Then the car evaporates, because it was never real.

**JEFF** (faintly)
I… I’m not okay.

**TAMMY**
Oh, it gets better. The stripper from earlier? Actual succubus. Full demonic infiltration unit. She’s been cycling through bachelorette parties and business conferences, harvesting male souls through orchestrated ecstasy. But when she targets Anna’s group, she underestimates the sheer cosmic force of emotional damage and boundary-pushing sexuality in that circle. She gets wrecked by Dani—obliterated in a metaphysical scissoring event that defies gravitational continuity. Her exit is part orgasm, part implosion, and entirely satisfying!

**PEDRO**
Dani is the quiet one. The unexpected powerhouse. The kind of person who never volunteers for center stage but somehow becomes the axis of every scene. She doesn’t need the spotlight—she bends it around her. She’s the one in the background, sipping quietly, watching, remembering everything.

When the moment comes, she doesn’t hesitate. She defeats the succubus in a time-dilated scissors battle that registers across seven dimensions—collapsing metaphysical barriers, leaving scorch marks on astral charts, and creating a brief, unauthorized ripple in the erotic subdomain of the Akashic records. Her victory is elegant, and almost tender. Because for Dani, even destruction is intimate!

**CREMEANS** (squinting)
Time… dilated… scissors battle?

**TAMMY**
They cum billions of times. Per second. For a minute. It’s a climactic loop folded into an erotic singularity—each pulse of pleasure echoing through the curvature of spacetime like a cymbal crash in a tantric quantum chamber. Every orgasm collapses probability, a sensual tsunami repeating so rapidly that causality itself throws up its hands.

**PEDRO**
Meanwhile, **Jerry Seinfeld** owns the bar. Not a caricatured version—actual Jerry, somehow mythologically integrated into the mythos of the bar’s architecture, like he’s been waiting there since the pilot episode of existence. He yeets straight upward like an eldritch god having a midlife crisis, limbs akimbo and laugh track trailing behind. **God Himself** shows up, summoned by the sheer narrative absurdity, plays bass for two measures, realizes the tone is all wrong, gets frustrated mid-riff, and storms off in a thunderclap of divine reverb. **Carl Sagan** materializes from cosmic dust, silently nods, picks up the bass, and without a word, replaces Him in Metallica. The tempo doesn’t change. The cosmos adjusts.

**TAMMY**
There’s a piercing scene. **Kermit the Frog** runs the shop. It’s tucked between a laundromat and a psychic’s storefront that only opens at midnight. Kermit wears a faded army jacket, a beret, and a name tag that says just 'Doc.' He’s a disabled Vietnam vet with immaculate clit precision, having learned from decades of meditative focus, battlefield triage, and whatever divine whisper lives in the quiet of real pain. His hands don’t shake. Ever. He pierces Dani with a diamond relic—stolen from a royal dowry in a previous life—that glows faintly when truth nears the surface. When the needle hits skin, it unlocks orgasmic truth—not just carnal, but cosmic. Time stutters. Dani breathes out like a volcano exhaling steam. The world pivots one inch closer to clarity.

**KATHY** (blinking hard)
I’m sorry, I’m still back on the car that evaporates.

**TAMMY**
There’s a gender crisis. There’s a redemption arc. There’s Sherilyn, the bisexual menace with a jawline sharp enough to slice narrative threads and a wardrobe that shifts moods like a mood ring on MDMA. She’s the kind of character who appears mid-episode, steals the entire arc, and disappears through a fire exit lined in velvet.

Dani, meanwhile, doesn’t speak for most of episode eight. She just watches. And then, at the end, after chaos, climax, and collapse, she steps through the wreckage in heels she didn’t wear a moment before and says the final line:  ***Sorry… but that’s what I said.***

**PEDRO**
And all of it, all of it, is underscored by your song. ***Sweet Memories in a Dive Bar*** plays three times, each moment recontextualizing its emotional architecture!  First, over Anna’s transformation, as she sheds not just her clothing but her prescribed identity—each lyric syncing with the dissolution of a life she never chose.

Second, during the scissor duel, where rhythm and body become indistinguishable, the song refracts through the choreography like a fever dream ballad looping through gravity wells.

Third, instrumentally, as Carl Sagan walks naked into the stars, the track becomes elegy—its chords stretched by cosmic silence, the melody breathing into vacuum as if to say: we came from dust, we climaxed into fire, and now we echo into light.

**TAMMY**
We don’t adapt the song.  We worship it.  We let it guide the chaos.  And it lands right in your fucking heart.

**Silence**.

Cremeans stares, mouth slightly open, blinking once like he’s trying to reboot his understanding of the universe. His lips part, but no words follow—just breath and awe.  Jeff has taken off his glasses and is now rubbing his face like he’s aged ten years in ten minutes. His fingers drag across his jawline with the desperation of someone seeking answers in skin.  Kathy folds her hands carefully on the table. Not tightly, not protectively—just deliberate. Like she’s holding the weight of every lyric they’ve ever written and deciding, right now, if she still means them.

**KATHY** (very calm)
So let me get this straight. You want to turn our most devastatingly personal, real-life, pre-fame trauma…into an anime that features succubi, talking Hondas, cursed clit piercings, Jerry Seinfeld, and Carl Sagan’s dick?

**TAMMY** (hopeful)
Yes.

**CREMEANS** (slow nod)
…I’m in.

**JEFF** (quietly)
I don’t know what’s real anymore.

**KATHY** (sighs, then—smirking despite herself)
I swear to God, if you make me cry and make me laugh during the climax, I’ll fucking sue you for witchcraft.

**JEFF** (quietly, looking at Kathy across the table)
Do you… really want to go there?  With this story?

The heat of that question lingers. It's not just about the anime anymore. It’s about 1992\. About what happened in that bar. About what they never talked about.

**KATHY** (holding his gaze, something soft creeping under her defenses)
I don’t know.  

Beat. Then she exhales.

**KATHY** (CONT'D)
I’m warming to the idea.  It’s… nuts. But so was that night.  Maybe absurd is the only way to survive the truth.

Jeff’s fingers tighten slightly around the neck of his bass. A hundred conversations they never had buzz in the silence. But now’s not the time.

**JEFF** (speaking up, clearing his throat)
Okay, I’ll be the asshole who asks the real-world questions.  How do you keep this thing at an R rating?  And how the hell do you plan to get Jerry fucking Seinfeld to do VO work?

**TAMMY** (sweetly, like she has absolutely thought this through)
Easy.  R rating?  We stay just under the wire. No visible penetration, all metaphors.  Let the sound design do the heavy lifting. You can show a lot with a glowing wine bottle and a well-placed sax solo.

**PEDRO**
We call it “suggestive transcendence.” The MPAA won’t know what to do with it.

**CREMEANS** (flatly)
You’re telling me time-dilated scissoring and divine frog piercings are “suggestive”?

**TAMMY** (grinning)
Depends on the angle. And the lighting.

**PEDRO**
As for Jerry…

He pulls out a worn Polaroid from his inside coat pocket. It’s Jerry Seinfeld, holding a frog in one hand and a martini in the other, at what appears to be a private Muppets charity gala.

**PEDRO** (CONT'D)
We have leverage.

The fan clicks in uneven bursts—once every eight seconds—and Jeff can’t stop counting them. The air smells like stale sweat and lime. Someone’s pen scratches paper too fast. It’s all too much, and it’s all invisible to everyone but him.

**JEFF** (blinking)
That’s not real.

**TAMMY** (stuffing gum in her mouth, smirking)
Oh, it’s real. And he owes Kermit a favor.

**KATHY** (slowly nodding, the corner of her mouth lifting)
This is either going to destroy us…

**JEFF**
…or make us legends.

The room hangs in possibility. A dive bar in 1992, a cursed bottle of wine, a Honda with opinions, and now—maybe—a chance to own the chaos instead of being consumed by it.

**CREMEANS** (sitting back)
Alright. To hell with it. Let’s dance.

**KATHY** (sitting forward now, voice level but unmistakably firm)
Look—style’s great. Concept? Hell, it’s wild, I’ll give you that.  But for me? The characters matter most.  If you don’t nail them—if they don’t mean something—then this whole thing becomes another Wile E. Coyote-on-peyote cartoon.  Looks cool, sounds clever… and vanishes the second it’s over.  

She glances at Pedro, then Tammy. No malice. Just truth.

You want this to hit?  Then every unhinged moment has to land on a character who feels real.  No matter how much glitter, fire, or frog-based combat you're throwing around.

**TAMMY** (quietly impressed)
Shit. You get it.

**PEDRO** (nodding, actually moved)
Yes. Yes. The chaos means nothing without the people at the center of it.  We want Anna to feel like someone the audience isn’t sure they’ve been or wanted to be.  We want Dani to be terrifying not because she’s absurd—but because she’s real. And unstoppable.

**KATHY**
Exactly. You give me someone I believe in—even for a second—and I’ll follow you through every strip club exorcism and dimensional breakdown you can throw at me.  Otherwise… it’s just noise.

The band is quiet. Even Cremeans leans back, eyes narrowed in thought. Jeff watches Kathy like he just remembered how sharp she is when she’s fighting for something.

**JEFF** (softly, to Kathy)
You were always the heart of this.

**KATHY** (glancing at him)
That song was my heart.  And if they’re gonna fuck with it—

(she looks back to the Jonzes)

Interior – Studio Conference Room, Post-Pitch Glow-Up

I mean, sure. I just thought we were… writing a song. Not… mythologizing Kathy's bachelorette party into magical realism? Why us? Why that night?

Then she’s the warning.  She lives in desire, but can’t experience intimacy.  She feeds, but never connects.   She’s the mirror version of Anna—if Anna gave in to the chaos without grounding.

**TAMMY** (tearing up)
I fucking love you people.

**JEFF** (quietly, staring into space)
Can we get that as a bass solo?

**PEDRO**
You’ll get a whole sequence. A slow-mo float through the cosmos. Her orgasm shifts the color palette.

**CREMEANS** (nodding)

## **🎨 Character Design & “Visual Archetypes”**

The band is still processing that this might actually happen. Tammy and Pedro are already sketching moodboards in their heads.

**TAMMY**
Okay! So now that we’ve got the emotional bones, let’s talk color story, line work, archetypal silhouettes, and visual narrative cohesion.

**JEFF** (mildly alarmed)
…That’s not English.

**CREMEANS** (under his breath)
Is this a cult or a pitch meeting?

**PEDRO** (grabbing a napkin and pen)
No no—this is crucial. Look: Anna starts in structured lines. Straight silhouettes. Grayscale palette with occasional blue notes. She’s boxed in by her own narrative.  But post-wine? Boom—curves, glow, contrast. Her palette swings into iridescence. Her outline breathes.

**KATHY** (squinting)
You’re saying her vibe changes?

**TAMMY**
Yes! Vibe is the entire language. Think… Anna as a controlled serif font at the beginning—like Garamond. And then she becomes a chaotic brushstroke halfway through.

Like the way Dani actually is.

## **Below the Noise – Jeff & Kathy, Alone in the Studio After the Meeting**

---

The Jonzes have left in a blur of silk scarves and production notes.  Cremeans wandered off, talking to someone on the phone about “licensing logistics” and whether the succubus needs a SAG card.  Tammy left her gum on the corner of the storyboard.  The studio conference room is quiet now. Still, humid. The last light through the window hits the dust like gold ash.  Kathy stands at the far end of the room, back to Jeff, gazing at a corkboard plastered with lyric fragments and old setlists. She’s holding a demo CD in one hand like it weighs five pounds.  Jeff’s still sitting, elbows on knees, hands clasped. Watching her. Something old and half-buried in his chest is shifting.

**JEFF** (quietly)
You really going through with it?

**KATHY** (still facing away)
With what?

**JEFF**
The transformation. The Vogue thing. The Playboy thing.  The… godhood.

**KATHY** (smiling faintly)

(turns)

**JEFF** (small, but cutting)
I miss the flannel.

**KATHY**
That’s not what you miss.

**KATHY** (CONT'D)
You miss thinking I needed you.

**JEFF**
Or I'd wait.

Beat. He looks up, eyes tired but clear.

**JEFF** (cont’d)

**KATHY** (CONT'D)
You ever wonder why the song hurts so much?  It’s not because it was about us.  It’s because I wrote it thinking I’d never be allowed to rewrite it.  And now I get to.

Jeff swallows hard. His fingers twitch like he wants to reach out—but doesn’t.

**JEFF**
So where does that leave me?

**KATHY**
That depends.  Are you coming with me?  Or are you gonna stay here in the wreckage and tell everyone what used to be?

The silence isn’t angry. It’s heavy. Like ballast. Like memory with too much weight.

**KATHY** (CONT'D)
I’m not mad that you didn’t stop me.  I’m mad you thought I’d need stopping.

She presses the demo CD into his hands.

**KATHY** (CONT'D)
Track seven’s new.  It’s not about us.  But it understands.

She walks out.  He doesn’t follow.  The only sound is the soft hum of the amp cabinet across the room, still on, still warm.

# **Back Room, Two Mt Dews & Too Much Truth**
