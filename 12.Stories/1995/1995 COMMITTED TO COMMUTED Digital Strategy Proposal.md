Hi Wyatt,

So, here's the pitch. You’re not gonna love all of it, but stay with me.

After the *Sweet Memories* Japan thing cratered stateside (RIP to whatever PR plan that was), we still have ***forty-seven original recordings*** from Commuted's sessions. Only half made it to vinyl, obviously. But the **rest?** They're *weird*, raw, evocative, and ***absolutely dripping in post-120-Minutes cult juice***.

Here's my actual idea:  We build a **Commuted fanzine**.  Online.  Yes, *on the Internet.*

We’ve got a dev in Hoboken who can do it in *frames*. And I’m talking ***MP3s, lyrics, scans of Kathy’s journal*****, weird 240x320 photos from the studio — the works.**

They ***don’t want to be on AOL*** (direct quote: “That’s for my mom and her tarot chain letters”), but I think we can ***host it independently*** and plug through alt.music message boards, zines, and one of those new *webring* things the kids are into. Think: ***digital zine for the freaks.***

If we pace out the vault stuff — demos, early cuts, that 14-minute version of Come Inside Me with the Jeff monologue still intact — we’ll have ***content until Y2K***.

And when people finally realize what this band actually was?  
We’ll ***own the archive.***

Let me know if Legal needs to see it before we drop the FTP.

Best,  
**Sarah Malcolm**  
Assistant Director, Digital Strategy  
Island Records, East Coast Office
