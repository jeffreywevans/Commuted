# **The Name – *Commuted***

---

It started with a joke. Or maybe a threat.

It was one of those endless, airless July nights in Bethel Township, the kind that make you forget the rest of the world exists. The band—though they didn’t yet call themselves that—had been rehearsing for four hours. [[4.5 KATHY ESPICH character bible|Kathy]] was bleeding from her cuticle. Jim’s snare was slipping again. [[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]] had stopped talking entirely. [[4.6 JEREMY EVANS character bible|Jeremy Evans]] had played the same riff thirteen different ways. And [[4.2 JEREMY GILLEY character bible|Gilley]], tall and boiling over, threw down his mic and shouted:

> *“This feels like doing time!”*

No one laughed. Because it did.  There was silence. Then Kathy, quietly, from the corner, voice flat and precise:

> “We should call it *Commuted.*”

No one responded at first. Jeff just raised an eyebrow. [[4.1 BILL DAVENPORT character bible|Bill]] leaned back against his amp. Jim nodded without knowing why. It stuck. And like most real names, it stuck because it was true.

They were all serving something—family expectations, factory-town inertia, teenage mythologies, Midwest geography—and what they wanted more than fame, or girls, or even applause… was  *out*. But not out cleanly. Not absolved. They weren’t innocent. They’d missed school, broken windows, lied to their parents, and skipped church. They were guilty in a thousand small, unremarkable ways. They just didn’t want to serve the full sentence.

*Commuted*.

The name was procedural, not poetic. It had weight. It had process. It had law in it. Jeff liked it because it was precise. Kathy liked it because it was ironic. Gilley liked it because he could snarl it into a mic and it sounded like a command. Bill said nothing, which meant he approved. And Jim? Jim just said, “Yeah, okay,” and hit the kick drum like a gavel.

It never changed.

Over time, people asked what it meant. Interviewers would lean in, hungry for some clever backstory—maybe something to do with traveling, or escaping a small town. Gilley would make something up. Jeff wouldn’t answer. Kathy would shrug.

But every now and then—usually backstage, usually when they were bone-tired and someone’s hand was shaking from holding too much silence—someone would say it again:

> *“This feels like doing time.”*

And someone else would whisper it back:

> “*Commuted.*”
