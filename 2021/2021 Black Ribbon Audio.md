## **INT. RECORDING STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE – EARLY AUTUMN EVENING, 2021**

The golden light of a Midwestern sunset slants through dusty warehouse windows, casting long amber rays across rows of vintage gear and sleek new tech. Somewhere in the rafters, an old joist creaks like it’s remembering other tenants. Outside, a streetcar bell clangs, muffled by brick.

The space is half reclaimed century-old brick and half futuristic rigging—like Muscle Shoals mated with Electric Lady Studios and raised the child in Berlin.

BILL DAVENPORT stands near the modular synth wall, sipping a black coffee gone tepid. He’s dressed in charcoal jeans and a faded *Blue Angels* morale shirt, sleeves dying before his elbows. His hair’s pulled back into a ponytail, streaked with silver now, and his stubble looks intentional, not lazy. His eyes scan a set of LED readouts on a rack-mount compressor with the same reverence other men reserve for scripture.

The floor beneath him is warm hardwood salvaged from an old bowling alley in Kentucky. The smell of solder, cold pizza, and aged wood hangs in the air like a memory that refuses to leave.

Across the room, KENDALL WEATHERBY slouches in a beat-up Eames knockoff chair, cradling a leather journal like it’s a therapy animal. Her jeans are ragged, one button unfastened. A small amp hums beside her, its tubes glowing faintly orange. She squints at the walls around them—unfinished drywall in some places, exposed brick in others, filled with racks of gear, stacked mics, and hand-labeled tape boxes.

**KENDALL** (looking around)
This place is like if Abbey Road got drunk, threw up in Detroit, and woke up in a Cincinnati art crawl.

**BILL** (flatly)
Thank you?

**KENDALL** 
I mean it as a compliment.

>Bill grunts. He sets his coffee down on a patched-up Rhodes keyboard. Then he paces. Thinking.

**BILL**  
I want it to feel like… *Snap* in New York. Or Real World. Peter Gabriel’s spot in England…

>He absently twists a patch cable between his fingers, the copper core catching a shard of sunlight.

**KENDALL**  
You mean “less furniture that costs more than a guitar.” Got it.

>Bill glows.

**BILL**
Exactly.

>He walks over to a low-slung rack and flicks on a reel-to-reel. The soft whirr of analog tape winding fills the room, a noise so specific it stirs something ancestral in any real musician. He turns to Jeremy, eyes thoughtful.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
So. Names.

**KENDALL** 
God. *Yes*. Let’s go.

## **INT. RECORDING STUDIO – OVER-THE-RHINE, CINCINNATI – EARLY EVENING, SEPTEMBER 2021**

The warehouse had good bones. Red brick, timber rafters, concrete floors worn smooth by decades of freight and labor. Now it was the hum of gear that filled the space instead of forklifts. The walls were a patchwork of acoustic foam, sound diffusers made from scrap maple, and a sprawling hand-painted mural of cassette tapes swirling into space.

**BILL DAVENPORT** stood near the mixing console, fingers brushing the smooth surface like he was tuning an instrument made of ghosts. His long, calloused hands hovered over the faders of a vintage Neve board, half-rebuilt from parts scrounged off Craigslist and flown in from Denmark.

>The place had no sign yet. No website. But it buzzed—like a held breath, like something waiting.

**KENDALL WEATHERBY** sat cross-legged on a dusty rug near a tangle of guitar pedals, an iPad in her lap, lazily doodling through a half-finished riff for *pardontheconvicted.com.*

**KENDALL** (staring up at the ceiling)
This place feels like… I dunno. Like someone took Sunset Sound and fed it steroids and espresso. But made it moody, Midwestern. Like it smokes weed and listens to *OK Computer* on reel-to-reel.

**BILL** (grinning)
That’s the goal. Sunset for sad bastards.

>He flicked a switch on the rack-mounted LA-2A, its glowing VU meter twitching to life like a heartbeat restarting.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
You ever been to Ocean Way?

**KENDALL**
Only in dreams and documentaries.

**BILL**
Ocean Way had this smell. Old wood, tube heat, and Chanel No. 5. I was there once in 1993. Björk was in Studio A. I was in B with a trip-hop producer from Iceland. Total genius. Barefoot. Ate nothing but oranges and smoked joints the size of trombones.

>Kendall chuckled.

**KENDALL**
So that’s the bar?

**BILL**
No. This place isn’t Ocean Way. It’s not Electric Lady either. It’s closer to Sound City—before they turned it into a damn museum.

**KENDALL**
Oh yeah. Concrete floors, blown speakers, magic in the walls?

**BILL**
Exactly. The ghost of grit. But here, we’re adding intention. Philosophy. A Stoic noise floor.

>Kendall looked around again. The studio wasn’t just gear. It was texture. There was a rusted stop sign hanging near the vocal booth with a bullet hole through the ‘O.’ A broken tambourine nailed to the door. Framed liner notes signed by nobody famous.

>The iso room had blackout curtains and a single red lamp that made everything feel like a secret. The walls had been tuned with precision, thanks to Bill’s obsession with frequency curves. The control room, meanwhile, was a mix of tactile analog and slick digital interface—Pro Tools on one screen, a modular synth system snarling in a corner like a sleeping dragon.

**KENDALL**
This place has soul. But it’s… still undefined.

**BILL**
Which is why you’re here. I need help naming it.

>Kendall raised an eyebrow.

**KENDALL**
We’re really doing this? Naming it tonight?

**BILL**
I’m fifty now. If I wait any longer, I’ll call it something dumb like *Sound Dad.*

>Kendall laughed.

**KENDALL**
Okay. Fine. Let’s do it.

>She stood, slinging the notebook into her backpack, and began pacing like she was onstage trying to burn off nerves.

**KENDALL (CONT’D)**
Alright, what’s the vibe? Like, do we lean mystical? Mechanical? Nostalgic?

**BILL**
All of the above, maybe. It’s about tension. This place is meant to catch lightning in a bottle, not polish turds.

**KENDALL**
Okay, so nothing like *Platinum Soundworks.*

**BILL**
God no. If it sounds like a gym for EDM producers, it’s off the list.

>Kendall snapped her fingers.

**KENDALL**
What about *Third Rail?*

>Bill leans back, takes a slow sip from his cold coffee. A single LED from the rack reflects in his eyes.

**BILL**
Danger. Power. Subtle New York nod. I like that.

**KENDALL**
Also, you touch it wrong, and you die.

Bill beamed.

**BILL**
Yeah. Like pushing a mix too far. Good. Keep going.

>Kendall climbed onto the edge of the drum riser.

**KENDALL**
*Fifty Hertz.*

**BILL**
Because I’m fifty?

**KENDALL**
No—well, yes—but also the European electrical frequency. Half the world’s energy hums at fifty hertz. It’s low, constant, reliable. Like you.

**BILL**
Hmm. Stoic. Technical. Subtle brag. You’re not wrong.

>He jotted it on a sticky note and slapped it onto the wall where a few others were already posted.

**KENDALL**
How about *CTRL Room?*

**BILL**
Clever. A little modern. Feels like a lo-fi beat album waiting to happen.

**KENDALL**
*Temple of Tape?*

>Bill raised an eyebrow.

**BILL**
That’s hot. Sounds like the kind of place where Tame Impala would get lost for six months.

**KENDALL**
Or where Rick Rubin would build a fire and make us listen to our childhood fears on vinyl.

**BILL**
You’re good at this. I hate how good you are at this.

>They both laughed. The sun had dipped below the skyline now, casting the whole studio in a warm twilight. The gear glowed softly—blinking LEDs, backlit buttons, and the eternal low thrum of possibility.

**KENDALL**
You gonna open this place to the locals full time?

**BILL**
That’s the plan. Not just for touring acts or indie darlings. I want some seventeen-year-old from Covington to wander in with a cracked Strat and walk out with a goddamn classic.

>Kendall nodded slowly.

**KENDALL**
That’s righteous. Like the old Compass Point vibe—recording Nina Simone one day, some broke jazz trio the next.

**BILL**
Yeah. Only here, they get chili on spaghetti and bad parking tickets.

>Kendall pointed at one of the scribbled names.

**KENDALL**
That one. *Black Ribbon Audio.* That’s the one you hang a shingle on.

>Bill considered it. It felt vintage, tactile. Like something that belonged on an old vinyl pressing plant or a jazz box set. It had gravity.

**BILL**
*Black Ribbon Audio.* Yeah.

>He looked around the room—at the patch cables like vines, the hiss of tape loops running silent, the scent of wood and circuitry and ambition.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
Let’s make this place useful. 

**KENDALL**
Only if we you also make it loud.

**BILL**
Hells to the yeah.

They bumped fists without ceremony. Outside, a streetcar rumbled past. Inside, a kick drum sample echoed off the far wall—raw, unquantized, perfect in its imperfection. The studio didn’t have a sign yet. But it had a name. And soon, it would have a sound.

## **INT. BLACK RIBBON AUDIO – CONTROL ROOM – EARLY NIGHT, FALL 2021**

The sun has sunk below the horizon, leaving only the soft orange wash of streetlamps filtering through the studio windows. The blinking LEDs of dormant equipment are the only things moving in the room now—tiny, unblinking eyes watching over silence.

**BILL** sits at the console, hunched forward, elbows on his knees, coffee cooling untouched beside him. His face is shadowed, stubble heavier than usual. He looks tired in the way men do when they’ve been too strong for too long.

**KENDALL** leans against the wall, fingers tapping on her iPad. Her eyes trace the spines of old tape reels labeled with sharpie: *That Look of Release*, *Be First*, *Shall I Beg?* They feel like relics now. Bones of something bigger.

**BILL** (quietly)
I got the property tax bill last week.

**KENDALL** (grimaces)
Oof.

**BILL**
Yeah. Not catastrophic. But it’s enough to ask the question: Why am I still doing this?

**KENDALL**
Because you love it?

**BILL** (nods slowly)
I do. God help me, I do. But love don’t pay quarterly assessments. And we haven’t tracked anything in four years. Not even scratch vocals.

**KENDALL**
You’re saying it out loud now.

**BILL**
I’m saying it because if I don’t, this place is gonna bleed me out.

(He gestures vaguely to the walls, to the gear, to the quiet.)
It’s hard to justify holding this ground when there’s no one showing up to fight in it.

> Kendall exhales hard, runs a hand through her coppery-red hair.

**KENDALL**
Jeff’s been circling the drain since 2012. Kathy’s death hit him harder than any of us. He’s not even angry anymore. He’s…so quiet.

**BILL** (sighs)
That’s what scares me.

> The air hangs heavy.

**KENDALL**
I think he wants to end it, Bill. Commuted. For good.

**BILL** (quietly, almost to himself)
Maybe Gilley’s passing *was* the end.

>Kendall doesn’t interrupt. Bill stares at the mixing board like it might answer him.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
I don’t *feel* like calling it a day. Not in my bones. But there’s this moment, right? When you have to look it in the eye… all of it. The grief, the silence, the empty inbox, the rooms that used to be loud—and just say…

>He swallows.

We lost. It’s over.

> Kendall leans forward, arms crossed.

**KENDALL**
You used to fill stadiums. Gilley used to eat up entire rooms with that ridiculous grin. Kathy would turn microphones into razors. And you, Bill? You made the whole damn circus sound like church.

**BILL**
Not anymore.

**KENDALL** (sits down beside him)
No. Not anymore.
>Bill slowly reaches over and powers off the console. The room goes a little darker.

**BILL**
I wanted this place to be our resurrection. You know?

**KENDALL**
Yeah. I did too.

**BILL**
But it’s hard building a temple when the gods are gone.

**KENDALL** (smiles faintly)
Even the angry ones?

**BILL**
Especially the angry ones.

>Silence again. The studio creaks faintly as the building settles.

**KENDALL**
So… what do we do?

**BILL**
I don’t know. But I know this—if we really are done, I don’t want it to end in a fucking whimper.

**KENDALL**
No. If Commuted’s going out… it should leave a crater.

**BILL** (nods slowly)
Then maybe we give it one more swing.

>Kendall pushes her hair behind one ear, leaning forward now like she’s catching the scent of something burning.

**KENDALL**
Even if it fails?

>Bill flips the console’s power toggle — the hum returns, soft but definite.

**BILL**
Especially if it fails.

>They sit in the low tide, two old architects of noise staring down the void.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
Tomorrow, I’ll put a call out. See if some locals want to track demos. Pay a little rent. Keep the lights on.

**KENDALL**
And maybe… maybe Jeff comes around.

**BILL**
Maybe.

**KENDALL**
And if he doesn’t?

**BILL**
Then I write something new.  Without him if I have to.

>Kendall’s eyes widen slightly. It’s not a threat. It’s not defiance. It’s survival.

**KENDALL**
You’d really do that?

**BILL**
I don’t know. But I can’t sit in this silence forever.

>A long, thoughtful pause.

**KENDALL**
Neither can I.

>Bill nods. He finally takes a sip of the cold coffee. Grimaces.

**BILL**
Jesus. That’s terrible.

**KENDALL**
So’s quitting.

They share a tired smile. The console stays dark. But maybe not for long.

## **INT. BLACK RIBBON AUDIO – CONTROL ROOM – LATE MORNING**

The sunlight outside is bright and ordinary, casting sharp lines through the tall studio windows. Inside, it’s quiet. Too quiet. A faint hum from the preamps. Dust curling in a golden shaft of light. On the far wall, a whiteboard reads in block letters:

**"NAME: BLACK RIBBON AUDIO.**
**NEXT STEP: SIGNAGE / LOGO.**
**FIND DESIGNER."**

BILL DAVENPORT sits alone on the rolling chair behind the console, cell phone resting on the armrest, speaker on. A few business cards are spread on the desk—local sign shops, freelance graphic designers, a scribbled sketch of a ribbon microphone with angel wings.

He’s dialing.

**PHONE: RINGING. ONCE. TWICE. THREE TIMES.**
Then—

**CREMEANS (V.O.)** (into speaker, voice hoarse)
Hey, Bill.

**BILL** (relieved to hear him)
Hey, man. I was hoping to catch you. Got a minute?

>An unfillable beat.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Yeah. I’ve… actually been meaning to talk to you.

**BILL**
Okay. Yeah, cool. So—listen, I’ve been here since seven. Trying to scope out signage, maybe bring someone in to do a logo. Make it real. Make it visible. You know?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Right. Makes sense.

> His voice is soft. Careful.

**BILL**
I figured once we get a couple local kids in here cutting demos, we could maybe build momentum, maybe—hell, I don’t know. Reboot the machine. I mean, we can’t keep letting this place sit, right? Something’s got to give.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)** (long pause)
Bill… yeah. That’s actually why I’m calling. I need to tell you something.

> Bill stops rolling. He sits up, eyes narrowing, sensing it now—that tremor under the surface.

**BILL**
Okay… shoot.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)** (slow inhale)
I’ve been holding off. Because, you know—the pandemic. And Gilley. I didn’t want to drop anything heavier. I thought I could handle it. Keep things going. But… I can’t.

**CREMEANS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
I’m stepping back. From all of it. From Commuted. From managing. From the business.

> The air in the room changes. Bill’s breath catches.

**BILL**
Wait, ***what***?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
I’m sorry. I didn’t want to blindside you. But I’ve hit the wall.

**BILL** (a touch incredulous)
What do you mean, “stepping back”? Like… like a leave of absence?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Not exactly. Maybe more like… an exit. At least for a while.

**BILL**
Come on, man. We've been dead in the water for four years. You know that. We’ve been keeping the lights on with duct tape and old memories. And now you're telling me you're out?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
It’s not about the band.

**BILL**
Then what is it?

> A long silence.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Jen.

>Bill’s gaze shifts automatically to the far window. A pigeon lifts off the sill, wings clapping once against the quiet. 
>
>That one name hits harder than Bill expects. It wipes the irritation right out of his throat.

**CREMEANS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
She’s… it’s not good. We’re stopping treatment.

**BILL** (quietly)
Jesus.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
The cancer spread fast. It’s in her liver now. Her lungs. The doctors are saying it’s…palliative from here on out.

> The room seems to fold inward. Bill closes his eyes.

**BILL**
I—I had no idea.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
That’s not your fault. We didn’t tell many people. She didn’t want the sympathy. You know her. Proud as hell.

> Bill nods, though there’s no one to see it. His hands clench in his lap.

**CREMEANS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
We’re focusing on comfort now. Time. Not years. Months, maybe. And I’ve got the girls to think about. My youngest is sixteen. A sophomore.

**BILL** (quiet, stunned)
You’re saying… you’re stepping away for… years?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
If that’s what it takes. Yeah.

**BILL**
We haven’t recorded since 2017. And now we’re looking at three more years before you even consider coming back?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
I’m sorry, Bill. I know how that sounds. I know what this means for the studio. For the band. But I can’t risk missing what little time I have left with her. Or with the girls. I can’t.

> Bill looks at the window. There’s a bird on the power line. Perfectly still.

**BILL**
You’re saying goodbye.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
I’m saying I love this work, and I love you guys, but this isn’t where I’m needed right now.

> Another long silence.

**CREMEANS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
Jen wants to make it to her birthday in December. That’s her goal. We’ll see. And our youngest—if I can help her finish high school without unraveling completely? That’s what matters now.

> Bill exhales hard through his nose, presses the heel of his hand to his eye.

**BILL**
I’m sorry, man. I didn’t know. I should’ve—fuck, I should’ve called more.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
No. This isn’t about guilt. We’ve all been in our caves. Everyone’s been grieving in their own way.

**BILL** (small voice)
Is she in pain?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Sometimes. But she smiles through it. She’s tougher than all of us.

>Bill nods again, eyes shining now.

**BILL**
She always was.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Look, I’m not disappearing. You need me for something, I’ll answer. But I won’t be managing tours. I won’t be running PR. I won’t be keeping the band afloat. I can’t. I’ve got to be a husband. I’ve got to be a dad.

**BILL** (voice cracking)
I get it. I do.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Tell the others when you're ready. I haven’t called Jeff yet. I can’t bring myself to do it today.

**BILL**
Yeah. Okay. I’ll handle it.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Thanks.

**BILL**
Give her my love, alright?

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
I will.

**BILL**
And if you need *anything*—for the girls, for yourself—you call me. No excuses.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
I know. Thank you, Bill.

**BILL** (swallows hard)
Alright. I’ll let you go.

**CREMEANS (V.O.)**
Take care.

**CLICK.**

The line goes dead. Bill doesn’t move. He just sits there. In the half-lit control room. Surrounded by silence. Then, without thinking, he reaches across the desk and erases the whiteboard.

## **INT. BLACK RIBBON AUDIO – CONTROL ROOM – EARLY EVENING**

The light is dying fast now. Outside, the first shadows of night stretch long across Over-the-Rhine, casting the street into an amber hush. Inside, the control room’s glow is static and unnatural—pale blue from the monitor, faint green from the rack gear. All the warmth is gone from the sun and from the day. The silence feels like it’s leaning in on Bill’s shoulders.

He stands near the console, motionless, phone in his hand, thumb hovering above Jeff Evans’ contact.

**BILL** (to no one)
Ten years ago, I would’ve known where you were.

> His voice echoes slightly in the empty studio.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
Down to five hundred yards. Easy. Wouldn’t even have to ask.

> He exhales sharply, shakes his head.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
Now? You could be in Fort Ancient, Ohio. Fort Worth, Texas. Or fucking Santiago, Chile.

>A dry, humorless laugh.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
Hell, maybe you *are* in Santiago. Would explain the radio silence.

> He sits slowly on the padded stool by the keyboard rack, hands on his knees. Phone still in one hand, like it weighs more than it should.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
I go weeks now without hearing from you. No calls. No texts. Just… vapor. You used to be the guy who couldn’t let a track sit without sending three mixes and asking for notes by 3 a.m.

> He swipes through his recent calls: no Jeff. Just Cremeans. Jeremy. A Lyft receipt.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
I’m going to call him.

> His voice is firmer now. He says it like it’s a commitment.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
I’m going to call Jeff, and I’m going to tell him exactly what happened. Not soften it. Not couch it. Not wrap it in poetic bullshit.

> He stands again, pacing now. One loop. Two. Three.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
I’m going to say: Cremeans is out. Jen is dying. They’ve stopped treatment. He’s stepping away. Family first.

> He stops in the middle of the room, the words hanging in the air like smoke.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
And I’m going to say it like a grown-up. And he’s going to treat me like a grown-up.

>A beat.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
Pigs might fly, but that’s what I want to do.

> He runs his hand down his face, dragging at the fatigue clinging to his jawline.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
You don’t get to disappear, Jeff. Not now. Not with everything falling apart. I know we’re all fucked up in our own way. I know Gilley wrecked something in you when he died. I know Kathy dying left a hole none of us know how to patch.

But we’ve *all* been carrying this thing. Commuted. And when one shoulder gives out, the rest of us pick it up.

(softer now)
We always did.

> He stares at Jeff’s name on his screen. A single, static image of him from god knows how long ago—sunglasses, that half-smirk, stage lighting behind him like a halo. It's infuriating and tender at once.

**BILL (CONT’D)**
So, yeah. I’m going to call. And if you treat me like a child again? If you go cold? Say something cryptic and vanish again? I’ll call again next week. And the week after that. 
Because someone in this band still has to *act* like we’re a band. 

>He doesn’t press the button yet. He just stands there. Phone glowing in his palm. A whole world flickering behind one name. And outside, Cincinnati keeps moving. Cars roll by. A siren echoes faintly. Some kid is playing trap beats too loud through Bluetooth speakers down the street.

But in here, it’s just Bill. And the call he knows he has to make. And the grown-up conversation he’s finally ready to have—Even if it breaks him.

## **INT. BLACK RIBBON AUDIO – CONTROL ROOM – NIGHT**

The studio is a cocoon of low light and quiet decay. Outside, the city murmurs. Inside, Bill leans back in the cracked leather chair, phone pressed to his ear. The screen says “**JEFF EVANS – CONNECTING…**”

Then—

**JEFF (V.O.)** (slight reverb, West Coast wind in the background)
Yo.

**BILL**
Jeff. Hey. You alive out there?

**JEFF (V.O.)**
Barely. But yeah. Hey, Bill. Good to hear from you, man. You sound… earthy.

**BILL**
You sound like a beach commercial. Where the hell are you?

**JEFF (V.O.)**
Santa Cruz. Maybe? Somewhere near it. Been consulting for this studio the last few weeks.

**BILL** (surprised)
Studio?

**JEFF (V.O.)**
Video game company, not music. Helping them out with the sound design on this new shooter.

**BILL** (cynical)
Sound effects? Really? You couldn’t tell a kick drum from a goddamn church bell half the time.

**JEFF (V.O.)** (laughs)
Correct. But my specialty is “big.” Big sound. Loud sound. Bass-heavy, teeth-rattling sound. You know—***MEGA KILL!***

>Bill chuckles in spite of himself.

**JEFF (V.O.) (CONT’D)**
Like those old FPS games from back in the day. Doom clones. Explosions, synth choirs, sub drops that make tweens shit themselves. That’s me now. The low-end philosopher of carnage.

**BILL**
You’re gonna give yourself tinnitus from your own soundtrack.

**JEFF (V.O.)**
Too late. What's up, man? Kinda rare to get a call. You okay?

>Bill’s smile fades.

**BILL**
Yeah. No. I don’t know. I’m calling because I think you need to come home.

>A pause.

**JEFF (V.O.)**
Home? What happened?

**BILL**
It’s Cremeans. He just put in for a leave of absence.

**JEFF (V.O.)**
Wait. Why? Did something—

**BILL**
Jen’s dying. Breast cancer. It’s everywhere now. They’ve stopped treatment. She’s not gonna make it.

>Silence.
> 
>Bill doesn’t fill it. He lets it sit. Cold and full.

**JEFF (V.O.)** (low)
God.

**BILL**
He’s stepping away from everything. He’s focusing on his daughters. Especially the youngest. She’s sixteen. He wants to be there, you know, make sure she finishes high school without her whole world caving in.

**JEFF (V.O.)**

He’s been the one holding the fucking ropes the last few years. Touring. Paperwork. Tax filings. You and me? We’ve been half-present. Jim’s buried in side projects. And Jeremy—

## **INT. BLACK RIBBON AUDIO – CONTROL ROOM – NIGHT**

The hum of the mixing console is the only sound in the room now. Bill stands near the racks, arms crossed, trying to figure out how to reach Jim Brown without it turning into another emotionally fraught hour-long conversation. His fingers hover over his phone. Do you call Jim cold? Do you text? Send him a track as a warm-up? He hasn’t decided.

**iMessage from JEFF EVANS:**
> Don’t contact Jeremy. I’ll handle it. He’ll be in touch with you in a couple of days.

**BILL** (reading aloud)
“The hell do you mean, don’t contact Jeremy?”

> Don’t contact Jeremy. I’ll handle it.

And then:
> Step 7 out of 12.

He opens Safari. Types: **"12 steps AA step 7."**

Search result: **"Humbly asked Him to remove our shortcomings."**

Jesus.

Bill leans back in the chair and closes his eyes. For the first time that day, he doesn’t feel like everything is falling apart. Not exactly. Not completely. Something’s shifting. And maybe that’s enough. For now.

## **INT. BLACK RIBBON AUDIO – CONTROL ROOM – NIGHT**

The city outside has gone to sleep, but the studio is still awake. Bill sits cross-legged on a rolling chair, a takeout box of half-eaten Pad Thai abandoned on a nearby amp case. The phone is pressed to his ear again, still warm from earlier calls.

He scrolls to **JIM BROWN**, taps the number, and listens to the ringing. He expects Springboro. He expects windshield wipers and highway hum, maybe kids yelling in the background.

>The voice is salt and sunlight over the line — so bright it almost feels wrong against the dim control room.
