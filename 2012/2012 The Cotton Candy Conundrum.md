(SOUND: The synth loop playing a sugary, sparkling progression with faint delay effects. A faint, metronome-like drum pattern pulses underneath.)

BILL, hunched over the keys, fidgets with a modulation wheel, tweaking the sound ever so slightly. His wavy hair falls over his face, lost in concentration.

JEFF (V.O.) (Internal monologue, sardonic)
It's… it's good. Shit, it's really good. But it sounds like we should be playing between innings at a Dayton Dragons game.

(Jeff exhales, finally speaking aloud.)

JEFF (Flatly)
Okay, Bill. Stop. Pause it.

(Bill hits a key. The synth loop freezes mid-decay.)

BILL (Grinning)
You hate it.

JEFF (Slowly) 
No. It's brilliant… I just… it feels too… pink. Like, those awful pink Starbursts.

 **INT. BILL'S OTR STUDIO – LATER (The Stink-Eye Monologue)**

The synth loop hums faintly in the background, barely audible over Kathy's sharp voice. She paces, the lyrics still clenched in her hand, as Jeff and Bill sit on the worn-out couch, clearly enjoying themselves too much.

KATHY (Scathing, reading from the lyrics)
So let me get this straight… "*Gonna break all my rules, bathed in moonlight*"? Oh, that's rich, Jeff. What am I, some backup dancer in a perfume commercial?

(Jeff opens his mouth, but Kathy cuts him off, voice rising.)

KATHY
And this line — "*Double entendres flowed like wine from our glass*." Seriously? It's like you’re writing dialogue for a bad rom-com.

(Jeff presses his lips together, trying not to laugh. Bill snorts.)

BILL (Barely containing laughter)
Yeah, Jeff. What *exactly* was the inspiration here?

(Jeff finally breaks, chuckling.)

JEFF (Grinning)
Okay, okay! So maybe it *was* inspired by… someone specific.

(Kathy freezes, eyes narrowing further.)

KATHY (Wicked grin)
Oh, now *this* I have to hear. Who’s the muse for this masterpiece?

(Jeff fidgets, looking suddenly awkward.)

JEFF (Mumbling)
It's…uh…Jodi. She called me "Inspector #12" once. Said I…overanalyze things too much. Before we, uh…

(Bill bursts out laughing, nearly doubling over. Kathy just stares, then smirks.)

KATHY (Smiling, but still deadpan)
So… you want me to sing a tribute to your girlfriend about how thorough you are in bed?

(Beat. Jeff and Bill exchange glances and crack up.)

KATHY (Grinning wider)
Fine. If you're so proud of these lyrics, why don't *you* sing them?

(Jeff freezes, his expression a mix of horror and defiance. Bill's grin widens.)

JEFF (Trying to keep it together)
Alright. Fine. If you're good singing it as written, I'll… I'll do it.

(Kathy raises an eyebrow, clearly daring him.)

BILL (Leaning in, whispering to Kathy)
This is gonna be like pairing a maestro with a moose getting eaten by a bear.

(SOUND: Jeff clears his throat, the synth loop swells. He begins singing horribly off-key, making Bill collapse into laughter while Kathy shakes her head, smirking.)

**INT. BILL'S OTR STUDIO – MOMENTS LATER (The Vocal Gauntlet)**

The synth loop fades out as Jeff stares, mortified, clutching the lyrics sheet. Kathy leans on the mic stand, arms crossed, clearly enjoying his discomfort.

KATHY
(Smiling wickedly)
Alright, Jeff. Let's try that again. This time… really sell it. Remember, you're the passionate 'Inspector #12.'

(Jeff groans. Bill, still chuckling, cues the synth loop back up. Jeff takes a deep breath and attempts the first verse again — still painfully off-key.)

BILL (Grinning)
Wow, buddy. That was… ambitious.

 (Jeff flushes red. Kathy steps forward, now circling him like a predator.)

KATHY (Feigning sympathy)
You know, maybe you just need… guidance.

(She gently takes the lyrics sheet from him and starts singing the line, perfectly on pitch, with sultry flair.)

KATHY (Singing softly)
*“He walked in, flashing that grin…”*

(She pauses, fixing Jeff with a pointed look.)

KATHY (Continuing)
See? It's not hard when you *mean it*. Your turn.

(Jeff exhales sharply, tightening his grip on the mic. He tries again, better but still rough. Bill nods encouragingly, but Kathy shakes her head, smirking.)

KATHY (Playful, twisting the knife)
Okay, maybe if you picture Jodi while you sing it. That might help.

(Jeff facepalms as Bill loses it completely, laughing out loud.)

**INT. BILL'S OTR STUDIO – EVENING (The Uvula Incident)**

The synth loop continues softly in the background. Jeff, face flushed and voice cracking, manages to power through the chorus — still not good, but not as painfully bad as before. Kathy leans back, arms folded, giving a slow, sarcastic clap.

BILL (Grinning, stepping forward)

Dude! That…wasn't awful!

(He high-fives Jeff, who immediately collapses into a chair, clutching his throat.)

JEFF (Hoarsely)
I think… I sprained my uvula.

(Kathy snorts, shaking her head but visibly impressed.)

KATHY(Grinning)
Alright, credit where it's due. You survived.

(She steps up and high-fives Bill, then turns to Jeff, wagging a finger.)

KATHY
But never… *ever*… make me sing your love letter to Jodi again.

(Jeff, still nursing his throat, manages a weak thumbs-up. Bill can't stop grinning.)

BILL (Laughing)
Well, at least we know who's not our lead vocalist!

(SOUND: The synth fades out gently as the laughter lingers.)

**INT. BILL'S OTR STUDIO – NIGHT (The Champagne Session)**

The room is filled with warm laughter and the clinking of glasses. Jeff, Bill, and Kathy sit sprawled around the studio, a half-empty bottle of champagne resting on the console. The earlier tension has melted into playful camaraderie.

BILL (Raising his glass)
To Jeff's uvula… a brave soldier who fought valiantly tonight.

(They all toast. Jeff still holds his throat in mock pain.)

JEFF (Hoarsely, grinning)
It'll never be the same. But worth it. That was fun.

KATHY (Guffawing)
Oh, it was worth it. I haven't laughed that hard in weeks!

(She leans back thoughtfully, glass in hand, eyes drifting to the lyrics sheet still sitting on the piano.)

KATHY (Softly)
You know, I've been thinking about these lyrics all night… There's something deeper here.

(Jeff and Bill exchange curious glances. Kathy sits up, setting her glass down.)

KATHY (Explaining)
Yeah, it's flirty and playful, but there's a vulnerability too. It's like… you're trying to break free from self-imposed rules, Jeff. Not just some joke about Jodi.

(Bill nods thoughtfully, Jeff shifting slightly, caught off guard.)

JEFF (Awkwardly)
Uh… yeah. Maybe… probably.

KATHY (Smiling)
Alright. I'll sing it. But on one condition.

BILL (Raising an eyebrow)
Oh…

It’s like watching someone walk a tightrope without a safety net—except the stakes are even higher because we’re dealing with music, egos, and a ton of unresolved creative tension. Jeff swirls the last bit of champagne in his glass, watching the bubbles rise and pop as if they held the secrets to his thoughts. His expression is distant, eyes flickering between introspection and hesitation. He takes a slow, measured breath, finally breaking the silence that had stretched long enough to become tangible. His voice, when it comes, is soft but weighted, each word feeling like a step across a fragile bridge.

**INT. BILL'S OTR STUDIO – LATER NIGHT (The Revelation)**

JEFF (Softly)
Alright… So, the truth about "Inspector #12." It wasn't just some random nickname.

(Bill and Kathy lean in, curious. Jeff clears his throat.)

JEFF (revealing)
Jodi had been through some rough relationships… guys who only wanted one thing. She told me about all of them when we started dating. So when things started getting serious with us… you know, when that part of the relationship comes up…

(He shrugs, uncomfortable but smiling faintly.)

JEFF
I guess I overthought it. I wanted to make sure she was…comfortable. I wanted every little detail to be just right, down to the way I set the mood and picked the words I used. But apparently, I was a little too thorough, to the point where it felt less like romance and more like I was performing some sort of scientific experiment. She said it felt like I was, uh, inspecting her… analyzing everything as if I was trying to create this perfect formula for affection. Honestly, I didn’t even realize I was doing it at first. I thought I was being attentive, considerate, you know? But instead of letting things happen naturally, I was caught up in making sure it all met some impossible standard I had built in my head. She told me that what she needed wasn’t perfection, but presence—for me to just be in the moment with her and not get stuck in my own head. It was a wake-up call, really.

(Kathy groans and rolls her eyes, setting her glass down.)

KATHY
Ugh. Been there. Done that. Seriously. It’s like, every single time, there’s this unspoken rule that no one talks about how clumsy and awkward it all feels. And yet, here we are, pretending it’s some grand, poetic moment when really it’s just nerves, miscommunication, and hoping the other person doesn’t notice when you fumble your way through it.

(Bill chuckles, nodding knowingly.)
  
BILL
Yeah, I think we've *all* been that guy at least once.

(Jeff laughs a little, more relaxed now. Kathy leans back, processing it.)

KATHY
Well, at least it came from a good place. And hey… it made for a catchy song, right?

(Bill nods, standing and stretching.)

BILL
Absolutely. I can't wait to see how Jeremy and Jim help flesh this out. It's gonna be killer.

(The trio exchange smiles, the tension fully dissolved. The synth loop fades out gently.)

The sun casts a golden hue through the windshield. Jodi drives with one hand on the wheel, the other occasionally petting Gracie's head, who lounges in the back seat. Jeff sits in the passenger seat, scrolling through his phone absentmindedly.

JODI (Perking up)
Hey…wait. Is this… one of your songs?

(Jeff glances up, startled but trying to play it cool.)

JEFF (Nonchalant)
Uh… yeah. New single. Just dropped last week.
 
(Jodi turns the volume way up, nodding along. Gracie perks her ears up at the sudden sound boost.)

(SOUND: The song's chorus swells, the lyrics unmistakably echoing a playful, romantic story.)

JODI (Hearing the lyrics, eyes narrowing)
Hold up… Is this…

(She glances at the car's sound system display. The title: *Inspector #12.*

JODI (Realization hitting, half-smiling)

**INT. JODI'S CAMRY – MOMENTS LATER (The Scoop, Boop)**

JODI (Giggling, voice teasing)

JEFF (Mumbling)

It's… kinda… you know, about us?

(Jodi raises an eyebrow, grinning wide.)

JODI (Smiling broadly)
Oh, *kinda*? Come on, spill.

(Jeff sighs, leaning back in the seat as the song fades out, still smiling despite himself.)

**INT. JODI'S CAMRY – MOMENTS LATER (The Farmhouse Story)**

JODI (Playful, raising an eyebrow)
So… you're telling me you turned *that* night — our epic four-hour talk at my house, into a pop song?

JEFF (Shrugging, awkward)
I mean… when you put it that way… maybe?

(Jodi narrows her eyes, mock serious, leaning closer.)

JODI (grinning)
We talked so long it was practically morning before any clothes came off, Jeff.

(Jeff clears his throat, clearly flustered, fumbling for words.)

JEFF (Defensive, but grinning)
Hey, it was… it was kinda poetic? Emotional depth and all that…

(Jodi shakes her head, then, with a soft smile, leans over and gives Jeff a quick peck on the cheek.)

JODI (Tenderly)
This is exactly why I took you to bed in the first place.

JODI (Teasing)
Just so we're clear… if my mom asks when we visit, the song is *definitely* not about me.

JEFF (Chuckling)
Oh, that's hysterical. I can live with that.
