In shadows deep where madness cries,  
A trembling heart beneath dark skies,  
He held me close, a fleeting prayer,  
With breath like storms that waged despair.  

His arms like chains, entwined in dread,  
I felt the pulse of life and death;  
In whispered tones so low and raw,  
“Say you want to live,” was the law.  

What worth is life when joy’s a curse?  
If he should take the path of worse,  
Would absence pierce more than desire?  
While limbs ignite in reckless fire.  

Each fractious touch a fervent plea,  
As lust and ruin danced with glee;  
I clutched his arms—infleshed allure—  
Yet knew this urgency was not pure.  

The edge we danced upon was fraught,  
Two souls entwined in battles wrought;  
The world's decay outside our plight,  
With whispers beckoning from the night.  

Apocalypse lingered at our gates,  
Yet here we spun amidst the fates;  
With every thrust and breathless moan,  
Our bodies claimed a world undone.  

Madness echoed through throbbing veins,  
While yearning thrived on tangled chains;  
And dare I chase that final wave?  
To taste the rush that chaos gave?  

Yet still I hungered for more depth—   
To lose myself in smoldering clefts;   
But fear had gripped my mind anew—   
What freedom gave might still construe.  

Then foam would rise on battle's brink,   
As desperate hope began to sink—   
“Live!” he urged within fierce embrace,   
But darkness blurred the path to grace.   

As agony coiled ‘round pleasure's beam,   
The tempest writhed—a choking scream;   
And every pulse became my grave—   
Did living matter if not to save?   

So closed I eyes ‘gainst final light—    
My body wracked beneath the fight;    
The edge grew sharper—the climax found—    
In throes entwined on sacred ground.   

And there we stood—a breath away—    
Two silhouettes of doom and sway;    
My last heartbeat against his skin—    
In somber union fading thin.   

When did lust become despair’s own hand?    
What forces thrashed upon this land?    
Between each gasp where silence shattered 
Loomed hopelessness that fate had scattered.    

The waters rose—all truth laid bare—    
In lost embraces of haunted air;    
A final cry whispered what dreams bled:    
For all I sought now danced with dread.
