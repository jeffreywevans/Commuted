Write a review.  I have a suggested format below.

---

# **Introduction and summary**

**Bibliographical information:** Include the book's title, author, and time period. 

- **Introduction:** Briefly introduce the book and its topic or genre. 
- **Concise summary:** Provide a brief overview of the plot or main argument without revealing major spoilers. 

# **Analysis and evaluation**

- **Purpose and audience:** Discuss the author's goals and who the book is intended for. 
- **Critical analysis:** Examine the book's strengths and weaknesses, discussing elements like plot, character development, writing style, and arguments. 
- **Supporting evidence:** Back up your claims with specific examples or quotes from the text. 
- **Context:** Place the book within a larger context, such as its contribution to a genre or its relation to other similar works. 

# **Conclusion and recommendation**

- **Comparables**: "Read this if you like..." and then provide three comparable titles.
- **Audience fit**: Who it’s _not_ for
- **Synthesis:** Connect the analytical threads.  How do the book's formal choices (structure, style, pacing) serve or undermine its thematic and narrative ambitions? What does this book ultimately achieve?
- **Recommendation:** State who you would recommend the book to and why, or whether you would recommend it at all.
