**(Intro music fades)**

**JOE** 
Welcome back to the JRE, folks! We’re back with another member of the legendary Commuted. Ten days ago, we had Bill, their keyboardist and sound engineer, on the show, and it was a fantastic conversation. Today, we’ve got the man behind the kit, the backbone of the band, the one and only Jim Brown! Jim, welcome to the show!

**JIM** (In a surprisingly energetic, spot-on James Brown voice) 
I feel good!

**(The entire studio erupts in laughter. Joe, Jamie, and the other crew members are doubled over, tears streaming down their faces.)**

**JOE** (Gasping for air) 
Oh… my… god!

**JAMIE** (Wiping his eyes) 
I did not see that coming!

**(The laughter continues for a good few seconds, slowly subsiding.)**

**JOE** (Still chuckling) 
Oh, man. Jim… that was… unexpected.

**JIM** (Back to his normal, slightly more reserved speaking voice, with a hint of a smile) 
Had to do it. Couldn’t resist.

**JOE** (Shaking his head in disbelief) 
I mean… we were all expecting… you know… the stereotypical rock drummer. Taciturn. Silent. Maybe a bit grumpy.

**JIM** (Grinning) 
Nah. That’s not my style.

**JOE** 
You absolutely shattered that stereotype in ten seconds flat. That was brilliant.

**JAMIE** (From off-mic) 
Best intro ever!

**JOE** 
Seriously! That was amazing. So, Jim, for those who don’t know, you’re the drummer for Commuted. You’ve been with the band since… well, since almost the beginning, right?

**JIM** 
Pretty much. I joined a couple of hours after they formed in high school.

**JOE** 
So, you’ve seen it all. You’ve been through all the ups and downs, all the lineup changes, all the… everything.

**JIM** 
Yeah, you could say that.

**JOE** 
We had Bill on the show ten days ago, and we talked about a lot of things. We talked about the band’s history, we talked about their influences, we talked about… well, we talked about a lot of stuff.

**JIM** 
I heard. Bill called me after. Said he had a blast.

**JOE** 
He did. He mentioned that you guys are a bit more… reserved than he is.

**JIM** (Nods) 
That’s fair. Bill’s always been the most… outgoing of the bunch.

**JOE** 
But that James Brown impression… that was gold!

**JIM** (Shrugs modestly) 
Just a little something I’ve been working on.

**(The studio erupts in another round of smaller laughter.)**

**JOE** 
Man, that was fantastic. So, Jim, I’m really excited to have you here. I’m sure you’ve got some great stories to tell.

**JIM** 
I’ve got a few, yeah.

**JOE** 
I can’t wait to hear them. But seriously, that intro… you set the bar pretty high.

**JIM** 
Just wanted to start things off on the right foot. Or should I say… the right beat?

**(Another round of chuckles.)**

**JOE** (Smiling) 
Alright, Jim. Let’s get into it.

**(The conversation begins, the initial burst of energy from Jim’s intro setting a lighthearted and engaging tone for the rest of the interview.)**

**JOE** (Looks down at his notepad, flips it shut with a dramatic *thwack*) 
You know what, Jim? This interview has taken a delightfully unexpected turn. I had a whole list of questions prepared, but after that intro… I think we need to address the elephant in the room. Or, should I say, the *other* Godfather of Soul.

**(A ripple of laughter goes through the studio.)**

**JOE** 
I mean, you’re Jim Brown. In the world of rock and roll. That’s… a name that comes with some serious baggage.

**JIM** (Chuckles) 
You could say that.

**JOE** 
So, the obvious question is… what’s it like? What’s it like being *the other* James Brown in the music world? Especially considering… well, the *other* James Brown lived a long and incredibly influential life, had a stellar, legendary career… before you even picked up a pair of drumsticks.

**JIM** (Shrugs good-naturedly) 
It’s… interesting. It’s definitely something I’ve had to deal with my whole life.

**JOE** 
I can imagine. Did you get a lot of comparisons early on?

**JIM** 
Oh, yeah. All the time. Especially when I first started playing in bands. “Jim Brown? Like *the* James Brown?” It was always the first thing people said.

**JOE** 
Did it ever bother you?

**JIM** 
At first, a little, maybe. It felt like I was living in someone else’s shadow. But I quickly realized there was no point in trying to compete with that. I mean, he’s a legend. There’s only one Godfather of Soul.

**JOE** 
Exactly.

**JIM** 
So, I just embraced it. I figured, if I’m going to share a name with one of the greatest performers of all time, I might as well own it.

**JOE** 
That’s a great attitude.

**JIM** 
What else can you do? Change my name? That seemed a little drastic.

**(A few chuckles from the crew.)**

**JOE** 
Did you ever meet him? The *other* James Brown?

**JIM** 
No, unfortunately, I never had that honor. I would have loved to. I’ve seen him perform countless times on video, though. He was an absolute force of nature.

**JOE** 
Unbelievable. The energy, the showmanship… nobody has ever come close.

**JIM** 
No one. He was one of a kind.

**JOE** 
So, you just decided to carve your own path?

**JIM** 
Exactly. I’m Jim Brown, the drummer for Commuted. I’m not trying to be the next James Brown, the singer. I’m just trying to be the best drummer I can be.

**JOE** 
And you’ve done a damn good job of it. You’re known for your solid, powerful style. You’re the backbone of that band.

**JIM** 
Thanks, Joe. I appreciate that.

**JOE** 
So, did the name ever cause any… mix-ups? Any funny incidents?

**JIM** Oh, plenty. There was this one time, early in my career, we were playing a small club, and the promoter accidentally put “James Brown” on the marquee. People showed up expecting to see the Godfather of Soul. They were… slightly disappointed.

**(The studio erupts in laughter.)**

**JOE** 
I can only imagine.

**JIM** 
Yeah, it was a bit of a chaotic night. But it’s a funny story now.

**JOE** 
Definitely. So, you’ve just embraced it. You’re Jim Brown, the drummer.

**JIM** 
Yep. And I’m proud of it.

**JOE** 
Good for you, man. That’s a great way to handle it.

**(The conversation shifts to other topics, but the lighthearted and engaging tone established by Jim’s initial James Brown impression continues throughout the interview.)**

**JOE** 
So, Jim, you’re clearly a charismatic guy. You’ve got a great sense of humor, and for a drummer, you’ve got an incredible baritone voice. You could be doing voiceovers! Why don’t we see you out there more? Why aren’t you doing more interviews, more media stuff? You’ve got a real gift for it.

**JIM** (Shrugs) 
I don’t know, really. I guess I just… never felt the need.

**JOE** 
But you’re so good at it! You’re a natural.

**JIM** 
I appreciate that, Joe. But it was always… handled.

**JOE** 
Handled?

**JIM** Yeah. By Kathy and Gilley. They were the media people in the band. They loved that side of things.

**JOE** So, they just took it upon themselves?

**JIM** Pretty much. Gilley… he loved the scene. He loved being out there, talking to people, schmoozing with the industry folks. He was like… our Mephistopheles.

**JOE** (Raises an eyebrow) 
Mephistopheles?

**JIM** Yeah, you know, the Faustian bargain guy. He loved the… deal-making, the behind-the-scenes stuff. He thrived on it.

**JOE** Interesting. And Kathy?

**JIM** Kathy was different. She loved dealing with the media because it gave her… power.

**JOE** Power?

**JIM** Yeah. She saw it as a way to influence people, to shape the narrative. She was very strategic about it. She knew how to use the media to her advantage.

**JOE** So, they had completely different motivations.

**JIM** Exactly. Gilley loved the game, Kathy loved the control. And the rest of us… we were happy to let them handle it.

**JOE** That’s fascinating. So, you guys just focused on the music.

**JIM** Yeah. That’s where we were most comfortable. We’re musicians, not… publicists.

**JOE** But now that they’re gone… things have changed.

**JIM** Yeah. It’s… different. We’re having to step up, to take on those roles ourselves. It’s a learning process.

**JOE** But you’re doing great! You’re a natural. You should do this more often.

**JIM** (Smiles) 
Thanks, Joe. I appreciate that. But I still prefer being behind the drums.

**JOE** I get that. But it’s interesting how different people approach the media, the public eye. You’ve got Gilley, who loved the social aspect, the networking. Then you’ve got Kathy, who saw it as a tool for influence. And then you’ve got you, who’s just… naturally charismatic, but prefers to stay out of the spotlight.

**JIM** Yeah, we’re a diverse bunch.

**JOE** That’s what makes a band interesting, right? All those different personalities coming together to create something unique.

**JIM** Absolutely.

**(The conversation continues, with Joe clearly intrigued by the different motivations within the band and the dynamic between the members.)**

**JOE** So, this “Mephistopheles” thing with Gilley… where did that come from? Was that like… an act he put on?

**JIM** (Shakes his head) 
No, no. It wasn’t an act. That’s the thing. That was… him.

**JOE** Really?

**JIM** Yeah. I know what you’re thinking. Like, U2, back in the day, with their whole… what was it, the Zoo TV tour?

**JOE** Oh, man, Zoo TV! I saw that! It was incredible. Bono with the Fly persona, the irony, the satire…

**JIM** Exactly. It was a whole shtick. Bono playing a character on a concert stage. We loved it.

**JOE** It was brilliant.

**JIM** But that was… a performance. Gilley was like that every moment of every day. All day. Forever. He never tired of it.

**JOE** So, he was just… naturally like that?

**JIM** Yeah. He was just… wired that way. He loved the game. He loved the… maneuvering, the strategizing. He loved being the one who knew people, who made connections. He just genuinely enjoyed the social and business aspects of the music industry.

**JOE** So, it wasn’t a facade.

**JIM** Not at all. It was just… him. He was just incredibly charismatic.

**JOE** You’ve mentioned his charisma a couple of times. How charismatic are we talking?

**JIM** (Pauses, considering his words) 
Look, I’ve met a lot of people in my life, Joe. I’ve traveled the world, I’ve been in this business for a long time. I’ve seen all kinds of personalities. And I can honestly say… Jeremy Gilley had more charisma than any other man I’ve ever met.

**JOE** Wow. That’s a powerful statement.

**JIM** It’s true. He just had this… presence about him. He could walk into a room and instantly command attention. He had this way of making everyone feel like they were the most important person in the world.

**JOE** It’s a rare gift.

**JIM** It is. And he used it… not in a manipulative way, necessarily. He just genuinely enjoyed connecting with people. He loved building relationships. He loved being the one who could make things happen.

**JOE** So, the “Mephistopheles” comparison… it wasn’t meant to be negative?

**JIM** No, not at all. It was more of an observation. He was the one who was always… negotiating, making deals, working behind the scenes. He was the one who knew all the right people. It was just his role in the band’s ecosystem.

**JOE** That’s fascinating. It sounds like he was a real force of nature.

**JIM** He was. He’s deeply missed.

**(The conversation continues, with Joe clearly impressed by Jim’s description of Gilley and the impact he had on the band.)**

**JOE** So, you’re saying Gilley was this charismatic, deal-making, behind-the-scenes guy. But… was there another side to him?

**JIM** (Lets out a small chuckle) 
Oh, yeah. There was definitely another side.

**JOE** What was that?

**JIM** Well… Gilley had a… prodigious appetite for women.

**(Joe raises his eyebrows, intrigued.)**

**JIM** It was something else. It was… really fun to watch, from a safe distance. Like, a mile away. Or, preferably, in a different hotel.

**(Joe laughs.)**

**JOE** So, it was… intense?

**JIM** It was… relentless. He just… he couldn’t help himself. It was like a force of nature.

**JOE** How crazy was it dealing with that? I mean, being in a band with a guy who’s constantly… you know…

**JIM** (Interrupts slightly) 
See, that’s the thing, Joe. I wouldn’t describe it as… “womanizing.”

**JOE** Really?

**JIM** Yeah. It wasn’t like he was… manipulating women or leading them on. He was just… having a lot of sex with a lot of women.

**JOE** (Pauses, considering this) 
Is there a real distinction there?

**JIM** I think there is. Because after the first year or so, everyone knew what they were getting into. It wasn’t like he was pretending to be something he wasn’t. He was very upfront about it.

**JOE** So, it was more… open?

**JIM** Yeah. It was… consensual, enthusiastic… on both sides. Plus, Gilley… he loved everyone and everything. He was just a very… generous person.

**JOE** Generous how?

**JIM** In every way. He was generous with his time, his money, his attention… he just wanted everyone to be happy.

**JOE** That sounds… almost naive.

**JIM** In some ways, he was. That was his vulnerability. He was so open, so trusting, that it sometimes left him open to exploitation.

**JOE** Exploitation? How so?

**JIM** Because he was so generous, so eager to please, some people would take advantage of him. They’d take his money, his time, his resources… knowing he wouldn’t say no.

**JOE** That’s rough.

**JIM** It was. But that was Gilley. He was a complex guy. He had this incredible charisma, this infectious energy, but he was also incredibly vulnerable.

**JOE** It sounds like he lived life to the fullest.

**JIM** He did. He definitely did. He packed about five lifetimes into one.

**(The conversation continues, with Joe clearly fascinated by the complex and contradictory nature of Gilley’s personality.)**

**JOE** So, is Jeff… the same way with women?

**(Jim winces slightly, a subtle shift in his demeanor.)**

**JIM** No. No, Jeff is… different.

**JOE** Different how?

**JIM** Jeff isn’t… he doesn’t have that same kind of raw charisma that Gilley had. He’s not the kind of guy who can walk into a room and instantly charm everyone.

**JOE** Okay.

**JIM** Jeff is… more… internal. He’s more focused on his own world, his own thoughts, his own creative process.

**JOE** So, he’s not as outwardly social?

**JIM** Not in the same way. He’s not the type to go out and actively pursue… connections, let’s say.

**JOE** But Bill mentioned he’s… you know… with lots of different women.

**JIM** (Sighs) 
Yeah. He… he does. But it’s… different. It’s not the same kind of… energy as Gilley.

**JOE** How so?

**JIM** (Pauses, searching for the right words) 
It’s… it’s more like… he has a collection.

**JOE** A collection?

**JIM** Yeah. Like… a collection of rare books.

**(Joe raises his eyebrows, intrigued by the unusual analogy.)**

**JIM** He appreciates… the unique qualities of each one. He studies them, he explores them… but it’s more of an intellectual pursuit than a… social one.

**JOE** (Leans forward, fascinated) 
So, he sees women as… rare books?

**JIM** (Hesitates) 
It’s not quite that… cold. But there’s an element of… appreciation for the unique… experiences that each woman brings. It’s not about… conquest, or… scoring points. It’s more about… experiencing something different, something new.

**JOE** (Pauses, considering this) 
I don’t know, man. That sounds a little… clinical.

**JIM** (Shrugs)
I’m not saying it’s perfect. I’m just trying to explain the difference between him and Gilley. Gilley was all about connection, about building relationships. Jeff is more… solitary in his pursuits.

**JOE** But he’s still… sleeping around a lot.

**JIM** (Nods) 
Yes. But it’s not… the same. It’s not driven by the same… need for validation, or… social connection. It’s more… internal. It’s more about… personal exploration.

**JOE** (Still not entirely convinced) 
I don’t know, Jim. That “rare books” analogy… I’m not sure I’m buying it. It sounds like a way to… justify something.

**JIM** (Sighs again) 
Look, I’m not trying to justify anything. I’m just trying to explain the difference. Jeff is a complex guy. He’s not easy to understand.

**JOE** That’s for sure.

**(The conversation continues, with Joe still pondering the unusual analogy and trying to reconcile it with the image of Jeff that Bill had previously painted.)**

**JOE** So, this brings up something else. Jeff wrote that book… what was it called? *Tour Bus Confessions*?

**JIM** (Winces visibly, a reaction mirrored by an off-mic sound from presumably Jamie) 
Yeah… that one.

**JOE** 
Yeah. In that book, he… all but admits to sleeping with Kathy for two decaces. Which, you know… is what it is. But it makes me wonder… how many of you guys in the band… you know…

**(Joe makes a vague gesture with his hand.)**

**JIM** (Takes a deep breath, looks Joe directly in the eye) 
At least nine.

**(Joe’s eyebrows shoot up. He looks genuinely confused.)**

**JOE** Nine?

**JIM** Yeah.

**JOE** But… wait a second. There’s… Jeff, you, Jeremy, Gilley… when he was alive… and Bill. That’s five. How does that… how does that go to nine?

**JIM** (A small, almost rueful smile plays on his lips) 
You’re forgetting… the road crew.

**(Joe’s eyes widen in understanding.)**

**JOE** 
Oh.

**JIM** 
Yeah.

**JOE** 
Ohhhhh. Okay. I see.

**JIM** 
It wasn’t just the band. Kathy was… she was very… popular.

**JOE** (Nods slowly) 
Okay. That makes… more sense. Though, still… nine. That’s… a lot.

**JIM** 
It was a different time.

**JOE** 
Yeah, I guess it was. But still… that’s a lot of… internal band dynamics to navigate.

**JIM** (Shrugs) 
It was what it was. It was… complicated.

**JOE** I can only imagine. I mean, working with people you’re that close to, day in and day out, on the road, in the studio… and then adding that layer of… intimacy…

**JIM** It definitely added a layer of… complexity. It created some… interesting dynamics within the band.

**JOE** Interesting is one word for it.

**JIM** (Nods) 
It could be… tense at times. But for the most part, we managed to make it work. We were all there for the music. That was the priority.

**JOE** But that book… that must have stirred things up.

**JIM** (Lets out a long sigh) 
That book… yeah. That book caused a lot of problems.

**JOE** 
I can imagine. Airing all that… dirty laundry…

**JIM** It wasn’t just the… “dirty laundry.” It was the way he wrote about it. It was… disrespectful, in some ways. To Kathy, to the other women involved… to all of us.

**JOE** So, it created a rift?

**JIM** Yeah. It took a long time for some of us to… forgive him. Or at least… to move past it.

**JOE** That’s understandable. It’s a pretty personal thing to put out there for the world to see.

**(The conversation continues, the atmosphere now slightly more somber as they discuss the impact of Jeff’s book and the complex relationships within the band.)**

**JOE** So, if I manage to get Jeff on the podcast… should I avoid that book? Should I just pretend it doesn’t exist? Or should I dig into it?

**JIM** (Pauses, considering his words carefully) 
That’s… a tough one. I have my own opinions about that book. But… if you really want to know Jeff… then you should read the damn book.

**JOE** 
Really? Even with all the… baggage?

**JIM** 
Yeah. Because it’s… it’s him. It’s Jeff unfiltered. It’s his perspective, his version of events. It’s… starkly honest, from his point of view.

**JOE** 
So, you’re saying it’s… entirely truthful?

**(Before Jim can answer, a voice from off-mic, presumably Bill, interjects sharply.)**

**Bill (O.S.):** 
Not in the least bit!

**(Jim turns his head slightly towards where Bill is sitting, a flicker of annoyance crossing his face.)**

**JOE** (Looks from Jim to the off-mic voice, intrigued) 
What was that?

**JIM** (Sighs) 
That’s Bill.

**JOE** 
He said it’s not truthful.

**JIM** 
Yeah. Because it’s… Jeff’s point of view. It’s his interpretation of events. It’s not necessarily… objective truth.

**JOE** 
Ah, okay. I get it. So, it’s his… subjective reality.

**JIM** 
Exactly. He’s telling the story as he remembers it, as he experienced it. It’s not necessarily how everyone else remembers it.

**JOE** So, there are… discrepancies?

**JIM** (Nods) 
There are… different perspectives. Different interpretations. Different… memories.

**JOE** 
So, if I bring it up with Jeff, it could get… interesting.

**JIM** (A small smile plays on his lips) 
It could. It definitely could.

**JOE** 
So, you’re recommending I read it, but take it with a grain of salt.

**JIM** Yeah. Read it. Get Jeff’s perspective. But also… remember that there are other sides to the story.

**JOE** That’s good advice. I appreciate that. It sounds like it could be a pretty… explosive conversation.

**JIM** (Shrugs) It could be. But that’s Jeff. He’s not afraid to be… controversial. He’s not afraid to stir things up.

**JOE** Well, I’m definitely intrigued. I’ll have to check that book out.

**(The conversation continues, with Joe now having a clearer understanding of the complexities surrounding Jeff’s book and the potential for a future interview with him.)**

**JOE** So, I noticed you came with Bill Davenport today. You guys seem pretty tight.

**JIM** Yeah, Bill and I… we go way back. We’ve been through a lot together.

**JOE** He was on the show a few days ago. He mentioned something about… well, he said he still has some… resentments about the “Be First, Be Smarter, Or Cheat” album.

**(Jim lets out a short, sharp laugh.)**

**JOE** He said it was a really difficult time for him. That he was working crazy hours, that it was emotionally draining.

**JIM** (Nods, a hint of steel entering his voice) Yeah. He did work hard.

**JOE** He said he was working 100-hour weeks for a month.

**JIM** (Shrugs, unapologetically) 
He needed to.

**JOE** (Raises an eyebrow) 
He *needed* to?

**JIM** 
Absolutely.

**JOE** 
Why?

**JIM** 
Because if I hadn’t pushed him like that, that album would still be unfinished. Bill has a tendency to… get lost in the details. He’s a perfectionist. He can spend hours, days, even weeks tweaking a single sound, a single effect.

**JOE** 
He mentioned that.

**JIM** 
And with that album… with everything that was going on… we didn’t have time for that. We needed to get it done. We needed to get it out there.

**JOE** 
So, you were… pushing him to meet a deadline?

**JIM** 
It wasn’t just about a deadline. It was about… focus. It was about keeping him grounded. Bill has a tendency to… go off on these flights of fancy. He gets these ideas in his head, these elaborate sonic landscapes he wants to create, and he can get completely lost in them.

**JOE** 
And you didn’t think that was appropriate for that album?

**JIM** 
Not at all. That album needed to be raw, it needed to be honest, it needed to be… direct. It couldn’t be bogged down by Bill’s… sonic explorations.

**JOE** 
So, you were intentionally… working him down to the bone?

**JIM** (Nods, firmly) 
Absolutely. I’m not apologetic about it. It was what he needed. It was what the album needed.

**JOE** 
Wow. That’s… pretty intense.

**JIM** 
It was an intense time. We were all dealing with a lot. But I knew that if I didn’t push Bill, that album would never see the light of day.

**JOE** 
So, you saw it as a necessary evil?

**JIM** 
Yeah. It was tough. It was stressful. But it was necessary. And in the end… the album is what it is because of it.

**JOE** 
Bill didn’t seem to see it that way.

**JIM** (Shrugs again) 
Bill has his perspective. I have mine.

**(The conversation continues, with Joe now understanding the tension and the different perspectives surrounding the creation of “Be First, Be Smarter, Or Cheat.”)**

**JOE** 
So, if Jeff was so… particular about his vision for that album, why did he come to *you* to be the project leader? Why not just do it all himself? Or go to a professional producer? It seems like an odd choice. Or was it… desperation?

**JIM** (Pauses, considering the question) 
It was a combination of things. It wasn’t desperation, exactly. Though, he was definitely… lost.

**JOE** 
Lost how?

**JIM** 
He was grieving. He was… struggling to process everything that had happened. He wasn’t in a place where he could make clear decisions, where he could… manage a project like that.

**JOE** 
So, he needed someone to take the reins.

**JIM** 
Yeah. And he trusted me. He knew I would… get it done. He knew I wouldn’t let him down.

**JOE** 
But why you specifically? You’re the drummer. Not usually the guy who’s running the show.

**JIM** (Shrugs) 
I’m organized. I’m methodical. I’m good at… logistics. And I’m good at… dealing with people. Especially Jeff.

**JOE** 
So, you were the… steady hand?

**JIM** 
Yeah. I was the one who could keep things on track, who could make sure things were moving forward. I was the one who could… translate Jeff’s… sometimes… erratic ideas into something concrete.

**JOE** 
So, it was a practical decision.

**JIM** 
Partly. But it was also… personal. Jeff knew I cared about him. He knew I cared about the music. He knew I would do everything I could to make it the best it could be.

**(Joe glances off-mic, noticing Bill’s continued steely silence. He can sense the lingering tension between the two bandmates.)**

**JOE** (Turning back to Jim) 
Bill seems… still pretty… affected by all this.

**JIM** (Nods slowly, his expression softening slightly) 
Yeah. It was a tough time for all of us. But… it was especially hard on Bill. He took it… personally.

**JOE** 
It sounds like it.

**JIM** 
He felt like he was being… controlled, manipulated. He felt like his creative input was being… stifled.

**JOE** 
And was it?

**JIM** (Pauses again, choosing his words carefully) 
It was… focused. It was… directed. It wasn’t about stifling his creativity. It was about… channeling it. It was about making sure it served the overall vision of the album.

**JOE** 
But he didn’t see it that way.

**JIM** 
No. He didn’t. And that’s… understandable. But I still believe I did what was necessary.
