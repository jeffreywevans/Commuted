### TIM, THE PERSIAN GULF, AND A CORPSMAN’S JOB

Jodi, leaning on the bar, eyeing Jeff.

“You’ve been dodging bringing up what happened with Tim.”

Jeff, exhaling, rubbing his jaw.

Then, quietly—

“Yeah.”

Jodi, watching him carefully. “…Well?”

Jeff, sighing, shifting in his seat.

Then—“Tim was a Persian Gulf War vet.”

Jodi, blinking. “…Wait. What?”

Jeff, nodding. “Navy.”

Jodi, processing. “…Holy shit.”

Jeff, glancing at her. “You know what a corpsman does, right?”

Jodi, slowly, feeling something heavy settling in her gut.

“…Combat medic.”

Jeff, nodding.

Jodi, softly. “…Oh.”

The bartender, sensing the shift in mood, quietly sips his drink, giving them space.

Jeff, fingering the rim of his glass. “Yeah. Oh.”

Jodi, exhaling, suddenly understanding why Jeff had hesitated to tell her.

Jeff, quietly. “That’s the real reason he can go for hours.”

Jodi, brow furrowing. “…What?”

Jeff, still staring at his drink. “Because he learned to ignore pain. Learned to shut it off. It’s why sometimes he can’t finish—his body’s spent years disconnecting signals.”

Jodi, staring, feeling the weight of it. “…Jesus Christ.”

Jeff, nodding. “Yeah.”

The bar is quiet for a long moment.

Jodi, softly. “Did he tell you that?”

Jeff, shaking his head. “Nah. But I figured it out.”

Jodi, exhaling, rubbing her forehead. “…That poor bastard.”

Jeff, softly. “Yeah.”

And for once?

Neither of them had a joke to crack.

### TIM, STEROIDS, AND THE UGLY EXIT

Jeff, exhaling, shaking his head.

“It gets worse.”

Jodi, already feeling the weight of this, bracing herself. “Of course it does.”

Jeff, rubbing his face. “He got caught stealing steroids.”

Jodi, blinking. “…Wait. What?”

Jeff, nodding grimly. “Yeah. He was using gobs of them after he got back stateside. Shooting up directly into the head of his dick.”

Jodi, staring. “What the fuck.”

Jeff, nodding. “Yeah. Got caught. The Navy fucked up the bust, so they didn’t court-martial him. Instead? They just threw a general discharge at him and said, ‘Have a nice life.’”

Jodi, staring. “…They kicked him out.”

Jeff, nodding. “Slammed the fucking door in his face.”

Jodi, silent, absorbing it.

Jeff, voice quieter now. “And you know what he did before all that?”

Jodi, bracing herself. “…Tell me.”

Jeff, leaning on the bar, voice dark.

“He treated shipmates that were literally cooked by a steam leak in a boiler room.”

Jodi, eyes widening.

Jeff, nodding. “Then? He spent six months in southern Iraq after the shooting war, taking care of the Marsh Arabs.”

Jodi, exhaling sharply. “…And his ship left without him.”

Jeff, nodding, jaw tight. “Yep. His ship sailed home, got a fucking hero’s welcome—and he wasn’t on it.”

Jodi, staring, speechless.

Jeff, lifting his glass. “Because, you know. America. Fuck yeah.”

Jodi, softly, after a long pause. “…Jesus Christ.”

Jeff, quiet.

The bartender, who had been listening, exhales deeply and mutters something in Norwegian.

And just like that?

The mood in the bar shifts.

No more jokes.

No more lighthearted banter.

Just the cold weight of a fucked-up story hanging between them.

### TIM, MUTANT DICK, AND THE LONG ROAD TO NOW

Jodi, exhaling, running a hand through her hair. “Jesus. I had no idea.”

She shakes her head.

“I’ll never call him Mutant Dick again.”

Jeff, grinning slightly, though it doesn’t quite reach his eyes. “Babe—if you’d done that in the ‘90s, Tim would’ve been pissed.”

Jodi, watching him. “…But now?”

Jeff, shrugging. “He’s come to terms.”

A pause.

Then Jeff, quietly. “He was married for the longest time. Then his wife dumped him when their kid went to college.”

Jodi, closing her eyes briefly. “Fucking hell.”

Jeff, nodding. “Yeah. He was a jerk a lot, but she cheated on him constantly. Repeatedly. And yeah…”

His jaw tightens slightly.

“It still makes me hot under the collar, even after all these years.”

Jodi, watching him. “You two were close, huh?”

Jeff, nodding. “Yeah. Different lives, but still.”

Then, grinning faintly. “And for the record? Mutant Dick is still okay.”

Jodi, blinking. “…Really?”

Jeff, nodding. “Yeah. He owns it now.”

A pause.

Then Jeff, rubbing his face. “Am I making sense or just rambling again?”

Jodi, grinning faintly, nudging him. “A little of both.”

Jeff, grinning. “That’s what I do.”

The bartender, who has been quietly listening, finally shakes his head and mutters—

“Jesus Christ, you Americans have stories.”

Jeff and Jodi, exhaling at the same time.

Yeah.

They really, really do.
