#  **2026 F0: Like Father, Like Daughter**

## **INT. JEFF’S MANSION – KITCHEN – 12:42 A.M. – MAY 2026**

**AVRIL** stands at the stove, barefoot, wearing Jeff’s T-shirt, four sizes too big. The hem grazes mid-buttock. She’s brewing tea, softly humming “Yellow” under her breath.

Enter: **JILL** (20) — hoodie up, cargo shorts, muddy boots, duffel slung over one shoulder. She pauses in the doorway. Silent.

**AVRIL** senses her. Turns. Smiles. A little guilty, a little brave.

**AVRIL**  
Didn’t mean to wake anyone.

**JILL**  
You didn’t. The engine noise outside did. Nice shirt.

**AVRIL** grips the mug a little tighter.

**AVRIL**  
Jeff said I could crash after soundcheck. Long day. You must be Jill.

**JILL**  
Wow. Context clues, much. And you must be Cunt.

**AVRIL**  
That’s not… entirely unexpected.

**JILL**  
No. It wouldn’t be. Not from me.

(**JILL** walks past her, opens the fridge. Grabs a can of Starbucks Cold Brew. Cracks it open.)

**JILL**  
Tell me something. Did you wear that shirt because it’s soft, or because it says “I win” without having to say anything?

**AVRIL**  
I wore it because it was hanging on the chair, and I was cold. Nothing symbolic. I’ve moved past that.

**JILL**  
But you knew I might come home.

**AVRIL**  
I didn’t think you were due home until next week.

**JILL**  
So you calculated low odds. Not zero. You’re a pattern, Avril. You don’t get to be a surprise.

**AVRIL**  
I’m sober now. I’ve changed. I was invited.

**JILL**  
You’re not being accused of breaking in. You’re being accused of not knowing what a clean entry actually looks like.

**AVRIL**  
I care about your father.

**JILL**  
We all care about him. That’s why we’re not in his bed.

(long silence)

**JILL**  
You know what I did last week?

**AVRIL** shakes her head.

**JILL**  
Chased a mile-wide supercell across I-35. Wind gusts topping 120\. It flipped a cattle trailer. We watched it bounce. I’m twenty. I forecasted it twelve hours before the Storm Prediction Center across the street.

(sips the Starbucks Cold Brew)
That’s what I do. I see pressure building. I see heat moving the wrong way. And I know where it’ll break.

(She stares Avril dead in the face.)

**JILL**  
You’re not a risk. You’re a forecast.

**AVRIL**  
You think I came here to break something?

**JILL**  
I think you came here thinking you wouldn’t. And that’s what makes you dangerous.

**AVRIL**  
I’m not that woman anymore.

**JILL**  
Then why are you wearing her shirt?

(long silence)

**JILL**  
I’m gone in six hours. But just know, if he starts drinking again, or skipping his meds, or playing shows like they’re funerals?  I won’t come for you on social media. I’ll come in person. Try not to haunt anyone before then.

---
## **INT. JEFF’S MANSION – GARAGE – 6:05 A.M. – NEXT MORNING**

Light is barely up.  **JEFF** stands by the workbench, holding a socket wrench, not using it. The **Jeep** is parked at an angle.  A white and maroon sticker from OU's School of Meteorology has place of pride on the driver's side of the bumper.

The **back hatch is open**.  **JILL** is strapping down her duffel, Oklahoma storm maps spilling from a folder beside her.

She doesn't look up.

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]**  
I didn’t know you were already up.

**JILL**  
Didn’t sleep.

He nods. Tries casual.

**JEFF**  
Back to Oklahoma today?

**JILL**  
Internship starts tomorrow. They’re putting me on the school's core team.

(beat)
Your little girl doesn’t flinch during hail.

Jeff smiles faintly. She’s always had bite. He sets the wrench down.

**JEFF**  
I didn’t plan for you to walk in on her.

**JILL**  
I know.

(she closes the hatch, forcefully)
That’s the problem.

Jeff winces. Stays still.

**JEFF**  
She’s not— It’s not like that anymore.

**JILL**  
I’m not twelve, Dad.

(she steps around the Jeep, faces him)
But you taught me what *not like that* looks like. And it doesn’t look like tea at midnight in your shirt. 

He exhales. She’s right. She always is.

**JILL**  
You told me people don’t get to come back into our lives unless they bring *clarity*.  Unless they prove they can survive silence.  Unless they *stay gone* long enough to deserve return.
So what did she prove?

Jeff doesn’t answer.

**JILL**  
Was it worth me remembering her name?

That cuts deeper than she meant it to. She softens. A little.

**JILL**  
You deserve joy. You do. But I can’t protect you if you keep inviting the things that hurt you to sit at the table. You want to let her in? Fine. But don’t ask me to call her safe.

She walks toward the Jeep. Stops at the door.

**JILL**  
You’re not weak, Dad.  But sometimes? You’re lonely enough to fake strength when you’re actually just tired.

Jeff closes his eyes. She opens the door.

**JILL**  
You don’t have to answer now. But next time I come home? Make sure it’s someone I’d bring into the storm with me.

She gets in.  Starts the engine.  Leaves the garage open. Jeff watches her tail lights vanish into the horizon line. And doesn’t pick the wrench back up.

---
## **INT. JEFF’S MANSION – KITCHEN – 7:14 A.M.**

**AVRIL** steps in slowly, hoodie over pajamas, eyes puffy but awake. She’s not hungover—just drained.  **JEFF** is at the counter.  French press.  Cast-iron skillet.  Eggs and spinach on the plate, toast cut diagonally. Two mugs of black coffee. A bowl of blueberries. No phones. No news on.

Outside, the **Little Miami River** moves like something eternal. Green. Silent. Untouched.

**AVRIL**  
You didn’t have to do this.

**JEFF**  
Did it anyway.

Avril sits. Slowly. Gratefully. The toast crunches perfectly when she bites it.

**AVRIL**  
She came at me hard.

Jeff nods.  Doesn’t explain her. Doesn’t excuse her.

**JEFF**  
She gets that from me.  But she aims better.

Avril takes a sip of coffee. Swallows.

**AVRIL**  
You know what scared me most last night?

Jeff looks at her.

**AVRIL**  
She didn’t need to raise her voice.  She didn’t cry.  She didn’t shake. She just… *saw me*. Like I was a forecast. Not a woman.

Jeff sets his fork down. Folds his hands.

**JEFF**  
She was angry.

**AVRIL**  
No. She was *correct*.

She picks up a blueberry. Stares at it.

**AVRIL**  
You’ve got a kitchen like this, a view like that… and you let *me* walk around in your shirt.

**JEFF**  
Because I knew what you needed wasn’t a bed.  It was a room where no one was selling you silence.

(beat)
And because I knew she’d tell the truth.  Faster than I ever could.

Avril looks up.

**AVRIL**  
You served me breakfast.  Why?

**JEFF**  
Because if you stay or go— I don’t want the last thing I gave you to be **ambivalence**. I want it to be eggs. Coffee. Riverlight. Something **undeniably gentle.**

Avril stares at the plate. Then slowly, without a word, she begins to eat. Outside, the river keeps moving.

---
## **INT. JEFF’S MANSION – KITCHEN – MID-MORNING**

The plates are cleared. The view of the river has softened. They’re not touching. Not avoiding. Just… occupying silence like a worn-out hotel room. JEFF leans against the counter, mug in hand.

**JEFF**  
I’m pretty sure this has to be the very last time we do this.

**AVRIL** doesn’t flinch. Doesn’t answer right away. She finishes the last sip of coffee.  Looks out at the water like it’s a mirror she can almost tolerate.

**AVRIL**  
You say that every third time.

**JEFF**  
Yeah.  But this time I believe it. Not because I don’t care.  Because I can’t keep risking her for clarity I already have.

**AVRIL**  (hollow smile)  
So noble, Jeff.  Really makes the T-shirt moment feel like an accident.

**JEFF**  
You weren’t a mistake.  But you were a pattern.

**AVRIL**  
And you were a forecast.  Always moving east. But don’t write about this.

**JEFF**  
I wasn’t going to.

**AVRIL**  
You were thinking about it. You’re always writing in your head.  Just… not this one.

He doesn’t deny it. She stands. Grabs her jacket.

**AVRIL**  
I might be back.

**JEFF**  
You always say that.

**AVRIL**  
I always mean it.

Then:

**AVRIL**  
But two Evanses in the same place at the same time?

(soft)
Never again.

She looks at him, just for a second too long.

**AVRIL**  
I’d hate for Jill to hate me. She probably already does. But I don’t want to help her build the case.

She walks out. No slam. Just door click, and the sound of tires on gravel. Jeff doesn’t move. He just stares at the empty chair. Then at the coffee ring on the table.
