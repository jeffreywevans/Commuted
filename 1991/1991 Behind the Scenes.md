# **COMMUTED: THE SCOUTING REPORT MONTAGE**

**(SOUND):**  
A relentless, rhythmic instrumental. Drums and bass, a low synth undercurrent. No lyrics, no gloss—just momentum.

**(VISUAL—clean, observational):**  
Shots move slow, deliberate. Focus on hands: pens scribbling, ticket stubs traded, sweat sliding down a glass. Bar lights bouncing off old wood.

---

## **(1) W.O. WRIGHT’S, BEAVERCREEK, OH – INITIAL ASSESSMENT (NIGHT)**

Nick Stewart edges through the doorway—sport coat soft at the seams, boots broken in, pen already out. No flash, just focus. He’s not here to be noticed; he’s here to *notice*.

**WIDE—BAR INTERIOR:**  
W.O. Wright’s at capacity, thick with anticipation and domestic beer. The air is humid with pre-show nerves. Every surface is taken: elbows, pint glasses, scuffed Nikes. Stage lights haze the front row in red and blue.

**(SOUND):**  
Crowd talk—hyped, impatient. Drums thumping through the floor during soundcheck, an accidental pulse in everyone’s shoes.

**AT THE SOUNDBOARD:**  
Cremeans is a one-man mission control—clipboard in hand, lips tight with calculation. Kathy tests her mic with a practiced flick. Bill fine-tunes a synth patch, not looking up. Jeremy bends to his pedalboard, precise and private.

Nick checks his watch—*analog, naturally.*  Jots: “7:04 p.m.—band nearly set.”  
He scans the crowd, notes the shape of anticipation:

- Heads craned toward the stage
- Laughter at the bar, dying off as amps warm
- That particular hush right before something begins

**IN THE CROWD:**  
A college kid, beer sloshed on his sleeves, elbows a friend:  
“Dude, if they do ‘Wow Finish’ second again, I’m done for.”

A girl in a cut-up jacket, hair in her eyes:  
“Heard there’s a new song. Gilley’s supposed to shred tonight.”

Nick, barely blinking, writes:  
“Repeat faces. Anticipation: feverish. Word-of-mouth: everywhere.”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Not just fans. Believers. There’s an edge to it—something I haven’t seen outside of the city, not this far from the coasts.

**ON STAGE:**  
The band shares a quick laugh—no nerves showing, just the ritual. Cremeans whispers something that makes Kathy smirk. Gilley shakes out his hands, rolling his shoulders like a prizefighter.

Nick logs it:  
“Chemistry: unforced. Leadership: visible.”

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
Lights drop a shade. The temperature in the room changes—everybody closer, everything louder. The air pulls tight.

Nick leans in, not out. The notebook never leaves his hand.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
This isn’t just a set. It’s ignition. I’m here to see if it burns.

## **(2) W.O. WRIGHT’S: PERFORMANCE DATA (NIGHT)**

Commuted takes the stage. Nick Stewart’s attention sharpens, pen and stopwatch in hand, every detail a number waiting to be logged.

**(SOUND):**  
Commuted launches—guitars jagged, drums sharp, crowd roar cresting over the band. For a bar, it’s a stadium’s worth of noise.

**(VISUAL):**  
Every motion tracked—Nick’s eyes flick from the stage to the room to the clock. A documentarian’s gaze: dispassionate, hungry.

**CLOSE-UP – NICK’S NOTEBOOK:**  
Setlist recorded with precision:

1. “Not Taken Kindly” – 4:18

2. “The Left Hand” – 3:46
   
3. “Payment in Kind” – 4:05
   
4. “Sweet Memories in a Dive Bar” – 4:41
   
5. “It Has a Wow Finish” – 3:33
   
6. “Come Inside Me” – 11:02
   
Transitions noted: “Clean segues. No dead air. Tight.”

**WIDE—THE CROWD:**  
It’s kinetic—bodies pressed tight, fists in the air, two couples spinning slow in the back. One guy up on a chair, arms wide, singing along, already lost his voice.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
This is where the real numbers live—not on the soundboard, but in the crowd. Applause per square foot. Sweat per ounce. You don’t measure heat by the thermostat—you measure it by the fever in the room.

**Nick logs:**  
“Dance floor packed by song two. Applause peaks at 2:03—‘Wow Finish.’ Zero early exits before encore.”

**AT THE BAR:**  
Nick slips sideways, unobtrusive. Counts glasses—bartender pulling drafts with the urgency of a pit crew. He times a round: watches the order, the pour, the delivery. Chalkboard scrawled:  “$3 cover. $1.50 PBR. $2.50 house whiskey. Miller Lite sold out by song three.”

Scribbles: “Revenue potential: high. Engagement = consumption.”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Forget the guys in the glass offices. This is the market, right here—noisier, messier, more honest. It drinks cheap beer and sings back every line.

**POST-SHOW:**  
House lights up. The crowd, sweat-stained and elated, floods the front of the stage. Kathy’s surrounded, signing arms, setlists, and a torn concert flyer. Jeremy’s got three new friends and a Sharpie. Bill autographs a synth key. Gilley’s mobbed by three girls with banana bras and too much lip gloss. Cremeans—already rolling cables, one eye on the exit.

**Nick tallies:**  
“Fan interactions: 37. Age range: 18–34. High lyric recall. Gear pack-out: 27 minutes.”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Liked is good. Remembered is everything. And tonight, nobody’s forgetting.

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
Nick watches as the crowd thins but the energy lingers. Kathy’s at the bar, water bottle in one hand, someone’s phone number scrawled up her arm. Cremeans hauls the last amp. Nick closes his notebook. Clicks his stopwatch. Allows himself a thin, satisfied smile.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Data never lies. And tonight, the numbers sang.

## **(3) BOGART’S, CINCINNATI, OH: SCALING UP (NIGHT)**

*Scaling up. That’s the test. No more safety net. If Commuted’s just a bar band, tonight’s the night the cracks show.*

**(SOUND):**  
A deep, relentless groove. Bottles, voices, footsteps on old concrete. The crowd is heavier—older, sharper, more deliberate.

**(VISUAL):**  
Camera glides along the perimeter—Bogart’s: 1,500 capacity, all brick and balcony, stained history in every light rig. A real stage. You feel the money in the air.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
W.O. Wright’s was a pressure cooker. Bogart’s is a proving ground. Different scale. Different gravity.

**NOTES:**  
Venue Capacity: 1,500  
Acoustics: natural, little slapback  
Demographics: skewing older, city crowd, less small-town sweat

Nick’s thumb flicks the mechanical clicker. Each head through the door, another point on the graph. Click. Click. Click.

*“Expanded crowd. College, late-30s, hipsters. Leather. Piercings. More city than county. No dead air in the lobby.”*

**TICKET WINDOW (EXT. BOGART’S):**

Nick leans on the glass, voice low.

**NICK** (quiet, easy)  
How’s it looking?

**ATTENDANT**  
Sold out. Three days. Balcony too. Not a single walk-up.

Nick nods, notes it:  *“Advance sell-out, full prepay. Market anticipation: real.”*

**INSIDE—SOUND CHECK:**  
Nick crouched behind the sound booth, decibel meter out—old-school needle, no flash.  Reading: 107 dB, full spectrum. Vocals ride the top, synths punch through. It’s clean. Cleaner than expected.

**SHOWTIME—STAGE:**  
Lights snap on—real white, hard and surgical. Kathy steps in, center frame, owns it with a look.  Gilley cuts through the haze with lead lines that don’t meander.  Jeff’s bass is subsonic, rattling the floor.  Bill’s synth is a *wall.*  
Jim’s snare doesn’t crack—it pronounces.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
No distortion. No mistakes. Too tight for a band this young.  “Adaptation: confirmed. Fear: not observed.”

Lighting—notes:  “Pro rig, quick response. Band using full stage, not hiding.”

**AFTER THE SHOW—LOBBY:**  
No one leaves. The lobby’s a queue—shirts, setlists, records, bodies pressed close just to linger in the afterglow.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Pull isn’t taught. It’s gravity. This band’s got it.

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
Nick against a column, notebook shut, watching the last of the line. There’s no smile. Just calculation—the sense that the next stop won’t be a bar, or even a city this size.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
They didn’t just fill the room. They filled the space. This might be the last time it’s easy to get a ticket.

## **(4) BOGART’S: REVENUE ANALYSIS (NIGHT)**

Second night. Same room. Same fire. *The question isn’t whether Commuted can fill Bogart’s. It’s how much they wring out of every ticket, every body, every second onstage.*

**(SOUND):**  
Crowd at fever pitch. Cash drawers snapping. Bottles clinking in time with “It Has a Wow Finish.”  The room isn’t just full—it’s restless, greedy, *primed*.

**(VISUAL):**  
Fast, data-hungry intercuts:  Nick in constant motion, timing bar lines, counting heads at merch, jotting in his battered notebook. His focus: predatory, clinical, unwavering.

**BAR REVENUE TRACKING:**  
He lingers near the bar, blending in with the noise.

- Four bartenders, hands blurred.
- Orders: Coors, gin, Jack.
- Nine-minute wait at the peak, during “Sweet Memories in a Dive Bar.”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Forget the setlist. Watch the pour speed. That’s your ticker. That’s *real-time market sentiment.*

**MERCHANDISE SALES:**  
The merch table is a feeding frenzy.

- Shirts: mediums and larges, sold out.
- Cassettes: gone before encore.
- Hand-numbered lyric zines: line eighteen deep, two bucks a pop.

Nick’s tally: “Merch Estimate: $4,100. Time: 9:32 PM.”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
This isn’t a crowd, it’s a market. They aren’t just watching—they’re buying. They want a piece.

**POST-SHOW—REVENUE ANALYSIS:**  
Nick corners the manager, quiet but direct.

**MANAGER (low, half-amazed):**  
Bar’s up forty percent. Never seen a merch line like that—ever.

Nick’s only answer: a checkmark in the notebook. *Viable. Very.*

**NICK (V.O.):**  
This isn’t just music. It’s conversion.  Fans to dollars. Noise to margin.

He flips to his Beavercreek notes—checks a box, draws a line.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Scaling’s not hope. It’s math. Tonight, it adds up.

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
Nick posts up at a steel column, running his totals.  Behind him, the band’s gear slides out the door—methodical, machine-tight, nobody lingering.  The room smells like sweat, money, and the start of something big.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
*Now we’re in business.*

## **(5) THE SMOKY DIVE: ATMOSPHERE AND AMBIENCE (NIGHT)**

**INT. STILLWRIGHTS BAR – COLUMBUS, OH – NIGHT**

*If ambiance could puke, it would do it here.*

Stillwrights is a shoebox of bad decisions—plasterboard and plywood, everything lacquered with cigarette tar and regret. The band’s stage is barely a step up from the floor, haloed in red from a busted bulb.

**(SOUND):**  
Thick guitar buzz, glasses clinking, low mutter of the crowd, the occasional sharp crack of pool balls. Somewhere, a fryer sizzles.

**(VISUAL):**  
The lens loses the battle with smoke—faces smear into color fields, everything with a cheap noir blur.  
Neon throws bar code over sweat-stained faces. Every inch is friction.

**NICK’S TABLE:**  
Nick hunched over a sticky two-top, wedged next to a jukebox wheezing out “Turn the Page.”  Legal pad open, corner stained dark, pen moving with grim intent.

**INSERT—LEGAL PAD NOTES:**

- Ambient noise: “ludicrous.”  
- Visibility: “smoke index high, light index low.”
- Floor spill: “3:17—record.”
- Food orders: “Pickles 8, Nachos 3, Mozzarella 1 (regret).”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Atmosphere. Everyone calls it “vibe.” Really it’s just math—particles per million, grease per cubic foot.

He counts food baskets: one, two, three… makes a note.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
If they’re eating, they’re planning to stay. Or at least not bolt before the set’s done. That’s staying power.

**CAMERA POV:**  
A pint goes over at table 7—spreads like an oil slick.  A girl chain-smokes with two lighters going.  Guy in Megadeth sleeveless yells, “Play the bridge!” Nobody listens.

Nick checks his watch. Sighs. Writes:  “Crowd density: low. Enthusiasm: stubborn. Acoustics: disastrous.”

He sips bourbon, tastes futility. Makes a face.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
It’s ugly, but ugly’s honest. These places teach you what matters. No room for fake.

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
The pad, corners curling.  The band in the red light, wringing every ounce out of cheap gear and shit sound.  A mozzarella stick dying a slow, sticky death on the linoleum.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
They don’t flinch. That’s the tell. That’s what makes them worth betting on.

## **(6) THE EMPTY BAR: MINIMAL VIABILITY (NIGHT)**

**INT. IDORU MUSIC BAR – SPRINGFIELD, OH – NIGHT**

*It’s a weeknight that barely deserves the name. Even the jukebox would rather stay quiet.*

**(SOUND):**  
Guitar and snare echo off too much empty brick. The relentless buzz of the lights.  The hollow thunk of a single bottle, set down with nothing to toast.

**(VISUAL):**  
Tracking across the bar’s void—eight patrons, four alive, two lost, one asleep, one debatable.  The bartender’s polish is eternal.  Light: too white, too honest.

**NICK’S TABLE:**  
Nick alone, slumped but still upright. Notebook looks abused, dog-eared, half-mashed.  He accepts the house draft. He didn’t ask for it. He doesn’t mind.

**INSERT – NOTES:**

- *Occupancy: 8%*
- *Revenue: “theoretical at best”*
- *Existential dread: palpable*
- *Band: full set. No shortcuts. No grumbling.*

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Nobody puts this test in the brochure. Not the festival. Not the sold-out night.  
This is the graveyard shift of the music business. Where you learn who’s bluffing, and who’s addicted.

He watches—Kathy giving it everything, Gilley soloing like the walls are full, Bill sweating through his shirt.  Nobody phones it in. Nobody looks for the door.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Here’s the truth: Weekend warriors bail when it rains. Lifers play through the drought.  Minimal viability isn’t about the crowd. It’s about never giving up the setlist.

He checks his notes. Three songs extra.  No complaints, no shortcuts.

**(CAMERA POV):**  
One man at the bar flicks a peanut shell, unimpressed.  Nick clicks his stopwatch. For the first time tonight, he grins.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
They passed. God help me, that’s all it takes.

## **(7) THE ROWDY CROWD: BEHAVIORAL ANALYSIS (NIGHT)**

**INT. THE PIT – TOLEDO, OH – NIGHT**

There’s a line out front. There’s carnage inside.  Between them: the full moon, the sweat, the charge in the air—ready to turn the stage into kindling.

**(SOUND):**  
Bass slamming off cinderblock.  Glass shattering, metal chairs doubling as percussion.  Laughter, jeers, a bottle ricochets and explodes.

**(VISUAL):**  
The camera swims in strobe and sweat, catching a shirt in flight, bodies colliding.  Nick sits at the eye of the storm, calm as a bookie, notes flying.

**INSERT – NICK’S NOTEBOOK:**

- Intoxication index: 9.3/10
- Volatility: “fluid, potentially explosive”
- Fight clock: running
- Viral potential: “disturbingly high”
   
**STAGE LEFT:**  
Kathy’s vocals spike, riding the room’s madness.  Jim’s hitting like he wants to break something—cymbal blows out, nobody misses a beat.  The crowd howls.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Crowd dynamics: herding cats, if the cats were half-naked, hammered, and maybe missing parole.

**11 MINUTES, 42 SECONDS:**  
Push, spill, shove—fight erupts right on time.  
Nick marks it, almost bored. Took longer than expected.

**STAGE CENTER:**  
Gilley clocks the fight—doesn’t blink.  Leans into the mic, dry as dust:

**GILLEY** (deadpan):  
Let’s all take a breath and remember—we’re here to *love* each other. Or at least keep it above the belt.

Room laughs, some groans, energy pivots.  He hammers into the next riff—crowd follows.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
That’s command. Not by yelling, but by daring the room to follow his tempo instead of their fists.

**CAMERA SWEEP:**  
The brawl fizzles. The pulse returns.  Bodies move—not to fight, but to music.

**INSERT—NOTEBOOK:**  
*Command* (underlined).

**NICK (V.O.):**  
You don’t just survive a crowd like this.  You own it.

## **(8) THE IMPROMPTU JAM SESSION: UNEXPECTED DATA (NIGHT)**

**INT. CANAL STREET TAVERN – DAYTON, OH – VERY LATE NIGHT**

The show’s long over. What’s left isn’t a crowd—it’s the afterglow.  Nick hunches in the corner, half-lit, notebook resting easy, as if the night might take notes on him, too.

**(SOUND):**  
Acoustic strings stretching into tune.  Pint glasses clinking, voices blending with last-call hush.  A tambourine keeping time for nobody in particular.

**(VISUAL):**  
Soft gold and tobacco haze.  Kathy barefoot, Gilley strapping on a battered Strat, Jim tapping out jazz-funk on a pint glass.  Jeremy rides a bassline thick as river fog.  Locals join—one in a battered Stooges shirt, another in steel-toed boots and hope.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
This isn’t data you can schedule. This is jazz in the machine—joy as method, the mess as the metric.

**INSERT—NICK’S NOTES:**

- Improvisation: “innate, playful”
- Style blending: “fearless, not precious”
- Social cohesion: “unforced”
- Jam start: 1:17 AM
- Time now: 2:43 AM
- Audience retention: “83% (Red’s dog doesn’t count)”
   
They roll from blues into something psychedelic and messy, Kathy harmonizing with a local folkie in a Bauhaus tee, Gilley grinning like he’s home.

Jeff’s trading licks with a dude who probably did acid at Monterey Pop.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
You don’t plan for this. It’s not on any checklist.  You just have to see it, and know it’s the thing.

Nick’s posture changes: notebook still open, but now forgotten.  He’s not analyzing, just—witnessing.  Remembering why he does this job, and who he’d be if he ever forgot.

**SLOW PAN:**  
The last notes ring.  Room exhales.  Kathy raises her glass. Gilley bows, grinning.  Someone cheers. Someone else just sighs, content.

Nick underlines one phrase, almost sheepish, almost grateful.

**INSERT—NOTEBOOK:**  
This is why we sign bands.

Just offscreen, a flash of a newcomer’s face—watching from the bar, a red spiral notebook under her arm. Sarah, but not named yet. Not Nick’s problem… until it is.

## **(9) THE DOUBLE SCOUT: COMPARING NOTES – NIGHT**

**INT. INDIE MUSIC CLUB – INDIANAPOLIS, IN – NIGHT**

A tight, low-ceiling box with more attitude than wattage.  Exposed brick, condensation on the ductwork, stage barely a foot off the sticky dance floor.  
A room built for making—or breaking—reputations.

**(SOUND):**  
Muted bass, glasses clinking, crowd chatter.  Commuted on stage—looser, louder, hungry.

**(VISUAL):**  
Camera glides over a crowd dense with hope and skepticism:  bands with crossed arms, college radio nerds, curious scene kids.  Then lands on *two* note-takers, one at each side of the house.

**NICK STEWART:**  
Jacket off, sleeves up, pen tapping a beat—old-school confidence, no wasted movement.

**SARAH MALCOLM:**  
Straight from NYU, but hiding the nerves.  Leather notebook, red pen, sharp blazer over jeans—watching everything, *especially* what Nick ignores.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
She catches what I miss.  I see what she doesn’t know to value—yet.  Double coverage, same game.

Gilley launches into a solo that sounds like he’s playing three guitars at once—possibly by accident.

**SARAH** (dry, notebook open, not looking up):  
“He’s either brilliant… or stoned.”

**NICK** (without missing a beat):  
“Not mutually exclusive.”

**INSERT – NICK’S NOTES:**

- Crowd: 82
- Song lengths: inconsistent
- Set pacing: loose, pulls you in
- Acoustics: middling, but energy covers the gaps

**INSERT – SARAH’S NOTES:**

- Kathy: frontwoman, needs polish
- Gilley: “chaos, but watch him”
- Fashion: “Grunge before grunge, plus thrift-store rhinestones”
   
During break, they migrate to a corner by the faded merch table.  Red wine in cheap plastic.  Whispers just above the hum.

**SARAH (V.O.):**  
He plays by feel.  I run the numbers.  But even a spreadsheet knows the story isn’t always in the stats.

**SARAH** (raising an eyebrow, pen as pointer):  
“Kathy’s the one. Give her a stylist and a little vocal coaching? Whole damn package.”

**NICK** (nods):  
“She keeps them in orbit. Can’t buy that.”

They swap notebooks, scan, nod.

**SARAH** (smiling):  
“We might just agree.”

**NICK** (toasting, almost surprised):  
“Miracles happen.”

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
Back to the stage—second set.  Commuted, suddenly tighter, sharper.  Nick and Sarah, both pens at the ready.  Two scouts, one target, the band in their crosshairs.

## **(10) THE DIFFERENT PERSPECTIVES: CONFLICTING DATA – NIGHT**

**INT. UPSCALE BAR & VENUE – COLUMBUS, OH – NIGHT**

This is not a dive.  House lights soft as butter. Cocktails with names and garnish.  A stage so polished you could check your eyeliner in it.

**(SOUND):**  
Crystal-clear mix, glassware clinks, a sax solo slips under idle bar chatter.  
Even the applause is polite—measured, never rowdy.

**(VISUAL):**  
Nick and Sarah at a small round table, backs almost touching but looking at the stage from angles as different as their philosophies.

Cut between their notebooks:

**INSERT – NICK’S NOTES:**

- “Timing on ‘It Has a Wow Finish’: off by five seconds. Kathy covers—solid pivot.”
- “Jeremy pedal fix mid-verse—professional.”
- “Bridge/chorus structure: tighter than Dayton.”

**INSERT – SARAH’S NOTES:**

- “Gilley’s hair: ‘haunted prom date’—needs stylist.”
- “Kathy: star quality. Wardrobe? Thrift store, heavy on the rebellion.”
- “Audience connection: weak. No eye contact. Introspective.”

**NICK (V.O.):**  
They’re musicians, not mannequins.  Nobody ever asked The Clash to color-coordinate.

**SARAH (V.O.):**  
You don’t sell records to musicians. You sell them to dreamers.  No one buys a poster of a band that looks like Wednesday morning.

**DURING SET BREAK:**  
Sarah angles her notebook toward Nick, a half-grin daring him to challenge her.

**SARAH:**  
“They sound like pros. But they look like they got dressed in the dark—twice.”

**NICK:**  
“Would you prefer eyeliner and dance moves? This isn’t New Kids on the Block.”

**SARAH:**  
“I’d prefer polish. Presence. Something that says they belong on a magazine cover, not just a playlist.”

**NICK:**  
“That tight turnaround between chorus and bridge? That’s the real presence.”

They exchange a look—not quite irritation, more like respect with a dash of “we’re not there yet.”

**(VISUAL):**  
After the show:  Sarah  the bar, stopwatch in her hand, tracking how long Kathy lingers with fans.  Nick at a distance, arms folded, watching the band crack up over a gear mishap.

**NICK (V.O.):**  
Subjectivity. Like static on a clean feed.  You learn to work around it—or learn from it.

**SARAH (V.O.):**  
Tight sets are great, but no one hums a bassline in their sleep.  They remember the feeling. The look. The presence.

**(FINAL SHOT):**  
The band pushes their battered gear out to the van under the soft neon “CRAFT BEER & KARAOKE” sign.  Nick and Sarah each close their notebooks—satisfied, unresolved.  Two scouts, two visions, one target.

Tonight?  The music’s tight.  The brand is still a question mark.

## **(11) GILLEY’S “SUCCESS”: UNINTENDED CONSEQUENCES – NIGHT/MORNING**

**INT. CLUB – GREEN ROOM – NIGHT**

**GILLEY** leans against the faux-brick, beer in hand, grin at half-mast—the kind of confidence that only comes from a total lack of self-doubt. Across from him, **Sarah Malcolm**—newly-minted analyst, professional, unflappable—meets his banter with an arched eyebrow and an actual laugh.

**(VISUAL):**  
Extreme close-up: Sarah’s lips parting in a slow, genuine smile.  
Gilley’s finger tracing the rim of his bottle.  
Their conversation is a low hum, just under the static of the after-show room.

**(SOUND):**  
Muted thump of a closing amp case. Glasses clinking. Sarah’s laugh—quick, bright, just slightly surprised.

---

**CUT TO:**

**INT. HOTEL – HALLWAY – EARLY MORNING**

Harsh yellow light. Silence, except for the whir of the vending machine.

**GILLEY** emerges from a room, hair a disaster, t-shirt one size too small (not his), shoes in hand. He closes the door with the care of a man sneaking past a sleeping dragon.

**(VISUAL):**  
A single, manicured hand waves lazily from under crisp sheets.  Sarah’s silhouette barely visible, a tangle of hair and satisfied anonymity.

**CUT TO: ACROSS THE HALL – NICK’S HOTEL ROOM – DOOR AJAR**

**Nick Stewart**, fully dressed, coffee in hand, leans in his doorway. He watches the parade, sighs like a man who’s seen it all and still can’t believe it.

He writes, deadpan:

**INSERT – NICK’S NOTES:**  
*“Extracurricular activity: observed.*  
*Duration: 7h12m.*  
*Potential effect on cohesion: unknown. Possible scenario: drama.*  
*Plan B: poach a rhythm guitarist from the openers?”*

**NICK (V.O.):**  
There’s no spreadsheet formula for what happens when libido meets logistics.  
But maybe there should be.

**NICK** (dry, muttering):  
“Day one, Gilley. Of course.”

**GILLEY (V.O.):**
