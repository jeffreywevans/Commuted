**Name and Basic Information**

 Bill Davenport, born March 12, 1972, is a multi-instrumentalist and co-founder of Commuted. His versatility and technical expertise have made him an essential part of the band’s sound, both in the studio and on stage. Known for his philosophical outlook and embrace of technology, Bill has carved out a dual career as a musician and independent producer.

**Physical Description**
 Bill has a perpetual suntan and three-day stubble. By his mid-40s, he grew his hair longer, reflecting the big hair aesthetic of 1980s rock bands. His casual yet polished style often features dark blue or black clothing, reflecting his admiration for the New York Yankees baseball team.

**Background and History**
 Bill’s musical journey began in high school, where he played horns in the marching band. His adaptability allowed him to transition to synthesizer during Commuted’s early days. A lifelong fan of Van Halen, Bill helped establish the band’s initial tradition of using "*Jump*" as their pre-concert sound cue. His career evolved beyond the band, leading him to co-own a recording studio in Cincinnati’s Over-the-Rhine district and produce music within Europe’s electronic scene. Bill’s admiration for the Blue Angels culminated in a ride-along in 2012, which he considers a highlight of his life.

**Personality and Traits**
 Bill is pragmatic, stoic, and deeply philosophical, often referencing Stoic philosophy to navigate challenges. He is a pioneer of music technology, advocating for tools like Autotune and Pro Tools. His easygoing demeanor contrasts with his intensity in the studio, where he is known for his meticulous attention to detail. Bill enjoys unwinding with a drink, often in Jeff’s company, where their conversations range from the mundane to the profound.
