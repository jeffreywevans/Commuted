# **SLEEP WITH THE GOSHDARN FISHES**

---

## **SCENE: “THE STRENGTH OF AN ARMY”**

**INT. SOUNDSTAGE – NIGHT – OCTOBER 2025**  
Avril’s alone with an acoustic. She starts to softly sing the opening bars of *Snowglobe*.

Bill enters. Doesn’t speak.  Just waits until she stops.

**BILL (quiet):**  
“Don’t dig up things that wanted to stay buried.”

**AVRIL (after a pause):**  
“You mean Kathy?”

**BILL:**  
“…I mean *you*.”

---

## **SCENE: “WOMEN AND CHILDREN CAN BE CARELESS”**

**INT. BILL’S MUSIC STUDIO – OVER THE RHINE – CINCINNATI – NIGHT (OCTOBER 2025)**

Rain taps on the old factory windows like it’s trying to tune in.  The table is littered with coffee cups, empty LaCroix cans, and two copies of *Potential Setlist (DRAFT – NOT FINAL)*.

Cremeans stands with a dry erase marker like a preacher about to rewrite doctrine.  
Avril leans on her six-string like it’s a statement piece. She just said it.

**AVRIL:**  
“So what if we bring back some of the *Be First, Be Smarter, Or Cheat* material? Four of those tracks hit the top five. Two went number one.  You guys *own* that sound. Why not honor it?”

Silence.  Cremeans blinks.  Jeff’s lip twitches.  Jeremy looks like Morgan Freeman learning what was in the box. was  him a secret.  Jim exhales like he’s trying not to laugh or cry.

Then—

**BILL:**  
*“You’ve got to be fucking kidding me.”*

**[[4.3 JEFF EVANS character bible|Jeff]]:**  
*“Are you high? That album is a grave marker.”*

**JEREMY:**  
*“Jesus CHRIST, Avril—read the fucking room!”*

**JIM:**  
*“You wanna open a set with ‘Snowglobe? Maybe drape your face in a hospital sheet while you’re at it?”*

Avril doesn’t flinch.  She tightens her grip on the neck of her guitar.  Eyes sharp.  No apology.

**AVRIL (calmly):**  
“I helped with two of those songs. I *sang* on them. They meant something to people. You *don’t* get to gatekeep grief and call it closure.”

Bill **SLAMS** his hand on the table.  The coffee cups jump.  A string on her guitar hums like it’s scared.

**BILL:**  
“That record killed people. That record made liars of all of us. You think just because you survived your little subway stunt, you can exorcise *that* album onstage like it's therapy?!”

Silence again.  Cremeans quietly caps the marker.  Still says nothing.

**AVRIL:**  
“That album made me want to join this band.”

Jeff stands.  Shaking. Not from rage—from memory.

**JEFF:**  
*“That album made me want to fucking die.”*

He storms outside into a downpour.  The rain gets louder.  He slams the door.

Cremeans finally speaks.

**CREMEANS:**  
“…We’re not there yet.”

Avril nods.  Just once. Fade to black.

---

## **SCENE: “SOMETHING UNHOLY AND EVIL"**

Jeff storms out.  The door slams.  Rain punches the windows harder. Avril still hasn’t moved.  She’s gripping her guitar like a flotation device on the Titanic.  Bill watches the empty space where Jeff was.  Then turns to her. Calm. Measured. And absolutely done.

**BILL (quietly, but lethal):**  
“Look what you just did.”  

*(beat)*  
“You chased Jeff off. Usually takes a little more to piss him off. But hey—credit where it’s due.”

Avril opens her mouth, but no words come out.

**BILL (stepping toward the door):**  
“Well… I promised Cremeans I wouldn’t break another TV.”  

*(he gestures vaguely toward the flatscreen near the soundboard)*  
“And I respect him. So I’m just gonna leave.”

He doesn’t yell.  Doesn’t slam anything.  He just walks out.

*The door clicks shut.*

Avril stands alone, the weight of four ghosts and a banned album pressing on her lungs. She finally exhales.  Just one word:

**AVRIL (to herself):**  
“What the fuck just happened?”

---

## **SCENE: "DON'T TAKE SIDES AGAINST THE FAMILY"**

**INT. BILL’S STUDIO – MOMENTS LATER**

Avril is standing, stunned. Still holding the guitar.  The echo of Bill’s “I respect him” line is still bouncing off the walls.  Jeremy’s been watching silently, half-sitting on the arm of the couch, sipping a La Croix like it’s whiskey in a Western.

He stands.  Walks over slowly.  Points at the now-vacant door Jeff disappeared through.

**JEREMY:**  
“Y’know, I’ve been thinking about what to do for the last thirty seconds.”

Avril raises an eyebrow.

**JEREMY (deadpan):**  
“And I remembered something.”  

*(beat)*  
“Blood’s thicker than water.”

He raises the can.  **Squirts** the last of the **La Croix** in her face with all the gravitas of a man delivering a sermon via citrus mist.

**AVRIL (blinking, stunned):**  
“You did *not* just—”

**JEREMY (already walking out):**  
“You can play your ghosts without me.”

He tosses the empty can toward the trash. Misses. Doesn’t look back.  Avril stands frozen.  She wipes the citrus sting from her face with the back of her hand.  Slow. Mechanical. Like she’s not sure if she’s wiping water, or anger.

**Door slams behind him.**

Avril stands there, *face wet, ego cracked*, absolutely incandescent with fury.

**AVRIL (to herself):**  
“You people are out of your fucking minds.”

The studio hums.  The ghost of *Be First, Be Smarter, Or Cheat* plays faintly in the silence.  
No one’s left to listen to it.

---

## **SCENE: "I MAKE NO APOLOGIES FOR THAT"**

**INT. BILL’S STUDIO – CONTINUOUS**

Avril is frozen, half-covered in La Croix, half-burning with rage.  Jeremy just pulled a third-grade stunt.  The air smells like grapefruit sadness.

Then—  **Jim** rises slowly from the far end of the studio.  He’s been quiet this whole time.  
Notebook closed. Arms folded. He crosses the room with **controlled weight**, the kind of movement that says *don’t mistake my calm for apathy.*

**JIM (deep, deliberate):**  
“I’d like to apologize for Jeremy’s… *toddler moment.*”  

*(beat)*  
“But you’re still an asshole, Avril.”

Avril’s face drops. She’s had enough. Or thought she had.

**JIM:**  
“You didn’t just read the room wrong.  You set the room on fire and acted surprised when people ran.”

She opens her mouth to fire back—  But Jim keeps going. Low. Measured. Final.

**JIM:**  
“You want to understand what this band is?  Go read Jeff’s book. Then maybe you’ll understand why we *don’t* sing that album.”

He heads for the door.  Doesn’t yell. Doesn’t break anything.  Just stops at the threshold.

**JIM (without turning around):**  
“Next time you want to pick a setlist, don’t do it with gasoline and a lighter.”

He’s gone.  **Avril is left alone.**  Face still wet.  Hands clenched.  Guitar untouched. A silence you can’t record.

---

## **SCENE: “CHRIST FORGIVES; CREMEANS DOES NOT

**INT. BILL’S STUDIO – MOMENTS LATER**

Silence.

The door creaks as Cremeans steps forward from where he’s been standing all along — unnoticed, unmoved, watching like a quiet judge at a trial no one wanted.

Avril looks up at him.  Tired.  Exhausted.  Still clinging to pride like it’s a warm blanket in a house fire.

Cremeans folds his arms.  Pauses.  Then speaks in that patient, resonant baritone that usually calms the room — but now carries the force of divine reckoning.

**CREMEANS:**  
“Do you understand *why* now, Avril?”  

*(beat)*  
“Ever?”

Avril doesn’t answer. She doesn’t need to.

**CREMEANS (more intense):**  
“If you don’t, let me put it to you *plain*.”

*(He steps closer. Not threatening — just unshakably firm.)*

“If you *ever* suggest to my guys that we *ever* play that material again…So help me God—
You. Will. Sleep. With. The. Gosh. Darn. Fishes.”

He locks eyes with her.  Not angry. Just deadly sincere.  Then — with surreal calm — he picks up his half-empty bottle of **Mountain Dew**, cracks his **spare can** with a cold *pssst*, and waltzes out of the room like it’s Sunday morning and he’s already forgiven her.

**DOOR: SLAMS GENTLY.**  

**AVRIL: ALONE.**
